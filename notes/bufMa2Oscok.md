### 荷兰盾的兴衰

**Host**: 历史告诉我们，没有哪种货币能永远处于顶峰。最先是**荷兰盾**（Dutch Gilder），然后是**英镑**（British Pound），现在全世界都在问，**美元**（US Dollar）是否会是下一个。一场所谓的“抛售美国”的交易。美元兑欧元跌至四年多来的最低点。对美元资产信心的缓慢侵蚀。那么，**特朗普**（Trump）是否终于在推动世界放弃美元作为首选货币？这将是一个重大的转变。特朗普自己也曾说过，如果你想沦为第三世界国家，就失去你的**储备货币**（reserve currency）。但这是真的吗？英国和荷兰早已失去了**储备货币**的地位，但它们至今仍是富裕国家。虽然可能容易被遗忘，但我们其实曾经历过类似的情况。在 20 世纪 70 年代，许多经济学家认为**美元**已时日无多，将被**黄金**（gold）或其他货币篮子取代。然而，它反而比以往任何时候都更强大地回归了。今天，美元会重蹈覆辙吗？如果美元真的下跌，我们又该期待什么？为了回答这些问题，我们不能只看这个频道通常关注的最新数据。我们别无选择，只能回到 400 年前，详细追踪**英镑**的崛起、生存、复兴与衰落，以及**美元**的崛起、衰落与复兴。首先是第一章：**荷兰盾**的兴衰。

**Host**: History tells us that no currency remains at the top forever. First it was the **Dutch Gilder**, then the **British pound**, and now the world is asking if the **US dollar** is next. A so-called sell America trade. The American currency slipped to its lowest level against the euro in more than four years. A slow erosion of confidence in dollarbased assets. So is **Trump** finally pushing the world to move away from using the dollar as their currency of choice? This would be a massive deal. Even Trump himself has said that if you want to go to third world status, lose your **reserve currency**. But is it actually true? Both Britain and the Netherlands have lost their **reserve currency** status a while ago. And yet they are still wealthy countries today. And while perhaps easily forgotten, we have actually been here before. In the 1970s, many economists thought that the US dollar was done for and would be replaced by **gold** or a basket of other currencies. yet it came back stronger than ever. Could the same thing happen to the dollar today? And if the dollar does fall, what can we expect? To answer these questions, we could not just look at the latest data like we normally do on this channel. Instead, we had no choice but to go back 400 years in time to track in detail the rise, survival, resurgence, and fall of the British pound, the rise, fall, and resurgence of the US dollar. And first chapter one, the rise and fall of the **Dutch Gilder**.

</details>

**Host**: 年份是 1609 年。新成立的**荷兰共和国**迅速成为全球贸易强国，**阿姆斯特丹**（Amsterdam）是其中心。作为少数受法律保护的地区之一，即使你的国家在与共和国作战，你的钱在阿姆斯特丹也依然安全。因此，欧洲各地的银币都涌向了后来成为所有中央银行模板的**阿姆斯特丹银行**（Bank of Amsterdam）。官方而言，**阿姆斯特丹银行**承诺所有存款都由金库中的银币作为后盾。但后来人们发现，事实并非完全如此。事实上，该行将储备用于向当时最大的公司——**荷兰东印度公司**（Dutch East India Company）——提供几乎免费的廉价贷款，以及向**阿姆斯特丹**市提供贷款。这意味着由**东印度公司**和荷兰政府管理的荷兰殖民努力，有效地享有了法国后来称之为“**过高特权**”（exorbitant privilege）的待遇。发行**储备货币**使荷兰比法国和英格兰等规模大得多的竞争对手拥有三个巨大的优势。首先，荷兰的借贷利率比否则要低。例如，荷兰省份通常只需支付 2% 到 3% 的利息，而规模大得多的英国则需要支付 5% 到 6%，不可靠的法国则要支付 10% 到 12%。其次，荷兰可以借到比否则更多的钱，这让他们能够资助一支庞大的海军，尽管**荷兰共和国**只是一个小国，却称霸了海洋。最后，在大规模地缘政治危机期间，资金常常流向阿姆斯特丹这个避风港，而不是流出。因此，虽然欧洲大多数国家的借贷成本通常很高，但在它们最需要的时候，至关重要的是，荷兰可以在危机时期以极低的利率借款。这在所谓的“灾难年”（Year of Disaster），即“Rampjaar”时至关重要，当时这个小小的共和国被其两个大得多的竞争对手——法国和英格兰——以及两个德国主教的联军入侵。尽管军队迅速逼近**阿姆斯特丹银行**，荷兰各省仍能以远低于入侵者的利率借款。这种特权使这个小国能够雇佣德国雇佣兵，并同时资助西班牙和奥地利哈布斯堡王朝加入战争。与此同时，英格兰国王查理二世（King Charles II）的资金非常匮乏，不得不部分违约，导致伦敦发生重大金融危机。因此，“**过高特权**”可以说 literally 地拯救了**荷兰共和国**。

<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: The year is 1609. The newly established **Dutch Republic** is quickly becoming the global trading powerhouse with the city of **Amsterdam** at its center. As one of the few places where money was protected by the rule of law, even if your country was literally waging war against the republic, your money would still be safe in Amsterdam. As a consequence, silver coins from all over Europe were flooding to the bank that would later become the template for all central banks, the **Bank of Amsterdam**. Officially, the **Bank of Amsterdam** promised that all deposits there were backed by silver coins in the vault. But it would later be revealed that this was not fully the case. In fact, the bank was using its silver to provide basically for free cheap loans to the biggest corporation of the time, the **Dutch East India Company**. and the city of Amsterdam itself. This meant that the Dutch colonial efforts managed by the East India Company and the Dutch government effectively enjoyed something the French would later call **exorbitant privilege**. Issuing the reserve currency gave the Dutch three massive advantages over much larger rivals such as France and England. First, the Dutch could borrow at a lower interest rate than they otherwise could. For example, Dutch provinces typically had to pay around 2 to 3% while the much larger British had to pay 5 to 6% and the unreliable French 10 to 12%. Second, the Dutch could borrow much more than they otherwise could, allowing them to fund a massive navy that dominated the seas despite the Dutch Republic being a tiny country. Finally, during a massive geopolitical crisis, money often flowed to the safe haven of Amsterdam rather than away from it. Therefore, while borrowing was often extra expensive for most European states, at the moment that they needed it most, crucially, the Dutch could borrow at extra low rates in times of a crisis. This was crucial during the so-called year of disaster, Rampia, when the tiny republic was invaded by its two much larger rivals, France and England, plus two German bishop bras. Despite troops rapidly closing in on the Bank of Amsterdam, the Dutch provinces could still borrow at rates much lower than the invaders. This privilege allowed the tiny country to hire German mercenaries and simultaneously subsidize the Spanish and Austrian Habsburgs to enter the war on its side. Meanwhile, King Charles II of England was so low on money that he was forced to partially default on his debts, leading to a major financial crisis in London. So exorbitant privilege quite literally saved the Dutch Republic.

</details>

**Host**: 然而，所有这些钱在某种程度上也导致了黑暗的一面。也许我们应该称之为“**过高的负担**”（exorbitant burden）。其症结在于，资金变得过于容易获得。这鼓励了省政府和**东印度公司**的过度借贷。容易获得的资金也助长了猖獗的房地产投机，以及**阿姆斯特丹银行**的过度扩张，后者极大地增加了对资金过剩的**荷兰东印度公司**的贷款。最后，这些金融利润大部分都流向了日益富裕的金融精英，加剧了不平等。**Yandere** 和 **Atanderada** 等经济学家认为，这种“**过高的负担**”使**荷兰共和国**越来越专注于全球银行业，从而挤压了使共和国伟大的贸易和手工业。讽刺的是，荷兰的金融精英却资助了那些后来超越共和国的国家——法国、美国，尤其是英格兰——的工业崛起。首先，荷兰继续投资英格兰叛乱的美洲殖民地，这导致了第四次英荷战争，这场战争摧毁了荷兰经济，并导致**东印度公司**破产。这反过来又导致人们对**阿姆斯特丹银行**的信任度大幅下降，并使得他们发行的货币的价值低于他们金库中本应持有的货币。然而，尽管明显处于衰落之中，荷兰的金融体系却因荷兰已将其投资多元化，向法国王室提供了大量贷款而部分得以维持。但讽刺的是，这很快就导致了他们的最终灭亡，因为法国国王在一次暴力革命中被推翻，这进一步削弱了荷兰，以至于在 1795 年被法国革命军轻易征服，法国革命军将荷兰的财富视为资助其军队的绝佳途径。然而，具有讽刺意味的是，这主要有利于他们的宿敌英国，因为从那时起，资金不再储存在阿姆斯特丹，巩固了伦敦作为阿姆斯特丹继承者的地位。即使在从法国解放后，荷兰也负债累累，而伦敦此时已如此强大，以至于荷兰再也无法重新获得其“**过高特权**”。他们花费了一个多世纪的时间来偿还国际债务，导致了一个世纪的经济停滞。并且在借款时，荷兰现在要比英国支付更高的价格。尤其是在危机时期，荷兰不得不勒紧裤腰带，因为资金现在流向了伟大的大英帝国。

<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: However, at some point all of that money did lead to a dark side as well. Perhaps we should call this the **exorbitant burden**. The crux of it is that money had essentially gotten too easy. This encouraged over borrowing by both the provincial governments and the East India Company. Easy money also contributed to rampant real estate speculation and to overreach by the bank of Amsterdam which massively increased its loans to the overstretched Dutch East India Company. Finally, most of these financial profits went mostly to an increasingly rich financial elite increasing inequality. Economists like **Yandere** and **Atanderada** have argued that this exorbitant burden made the Dutch Republic increasingly specialized in global banking which then crowded out the trading and crafts that had made the republic great in the first place. Then ironically the Dutch financial elite got rich funding the industrial rise of the very nations that would overtake the republic, France, the US and especially England. First, the Dutch continued investments to England's rebellious American colonies and this then led to the fourth Anglo Dutch war which ruined the Dutch economy and led to the bankruptcy of the East India Company. This in turn caused a major loss of trust in the Bank of Amsterdam and this made money issued by them worth less than the money they supposedly had in their vault. However, while clearly on the decline, the Dutch financial system managed to cling on partially because the Dutch had diversified their investments by lending a lot to the French crown. But ironically, this would soon lead to their final demise as the French king was overthrown in a violent revolution, further weakening the Dutch so much that it was easily conquered by the French revolutionary forces in 1795, who saw Dutch wealth as a great way to fund their armies. However, ironically, this would mostly benefit their arch rival, the British, as from that point on, money was no longer saved in Amsterdam, cementing the status of London as Amsterdam's successor. And even after being liberated from the French, the Dutch were in so much debt, and London was now so strong that the Dutch would never again regain their exorbitant privilege. They spent well over a century paying back their international debts, leading to a century of economic stagnation. And when borrowing, the Dutch paid a premium now over the English. And especially in times of crisis, the Dutch had to tighten their belt as money now flowed to the great British Empire instead.

</details>

**Host**: 但在我们讲述那个故事之前，让我们先回顾一下**荷兰共和国**的经历与今天的**美元**（US Dollar）状况有多么相似。首先，今天的美国也被认为拥有“**过高特权**”（exorbitant privilege）。尽管美国负债累累，政府赤字高企，但其借贷的实际利率却接近 2%，确实非常低。更重要的是，在大危机期间，例如 2007-2008 年，随着全球资金涌向纽约市寻求安全，美国能够以越来越低的成本借款。但就像**荷兰共和国**一样，许多人认为美国也正面临政府过度扩张、金融泡沫增多以及不平等加剧的问题。就像**荷兰**精英资助了法国和尤其是英国的崛起一样，中国的崛起很大程度上也是得益于**美元**体系。在其“**过高特权**”的末期，荷兰经历了一系列金融危机和冲击。他们衰落的第一个信号是，在第四次英荷战争或法国入侵等危机期间，他们不再能够获得更多借款。而这正是特朗普“解放日”之后，市场首次出现恐慌的原因，因为资金从美国流出，而不是流入。失去这一部分“**过高特权**”literally 地标志着**荷兰共和国**末路的开端。仅仅几年后，阿姆斯特丹就被法国占领了。当然，当年的微小**荷兰共和国**与今天的美国至少有两处巨大的不同。第一点显而易见：今天的美国并未面临被入侵的风险。但更重要的是，第二点不同在于，今天的**美元**不像**荷兰盾**那样与贵金属挂钩。第三，当年荷兰陷入困境时，存在明确的替代方案。英国拥有一个经济体量大得多的国家，并且得益于近期荷兰银行家（他们帮助建立了**英格兰银行**，Bank of England）的涌入，英国是显而易见的替代选择。英国随后获得了“**过高特权**”。今天，美国的状况远不如**荷兰共和国**那样清晰。目前没有明确的替代方案。因此，要弄清楚**美元**的实际命运，我们需要一个更具可比性的例子，这便引出了**英镑**的崛起与衰落。

**Host**: But before getting to that story, let's first review how similar the experience of the Dutch Republic is to the dollar situation today. First, the US today is also said to have an **exorbitant privilege**. Despite having some of the highest debts in the world and one of the highest government deficits in the world right now, the US can borrow at real interest rates that are close to 2%. Very low indeed. On top of that, during big crisis such as during 20072008, the Americans could borrow more and more cheaply as money from all around the world wanted to go back to the safety of New York City. But just as the Dutch Republic, many have argued that the US is now also suffering from everincreasing government overreach, more and more financial bubbles, and increased inequality. And just like the Dutch elites funded the rise of France and especially Britain, so did China's rise mostly come about thanks to the US dollar system. Near the end of their exorbitant privilege, the Dutch faced a series of financial crisis and shocks. The first signal of their decline was that they could no longer borrow more during crisis such as the fourth Anglo Dutch war or French invasion. And this is why after Trump's liberation day, for the first time ever, markets were panicked because money flowed out of the US during this crisis rather than into it. Losing this part of exorbitant privilege was literally the beginning of the end for the Dutch Republic. Just a couple of years later, Amsterdam was occupied by the French. But of course, there are at least two very big differences between the tiny Dutch Republic back then and the US today. The first is obvious. America today is not at risk of an invasion. But more importantly for our story, the second difference is that the dollar today is not tied to a metal like the Dutch guilder was. Thirdly, where the Dutch were in trouble, there was a clear alternative. Britain had a far bigger economy and thanks to a recent influx of Dutch bankers who helped establish the Bank of England, it was the clear alternative. Britain would go on to have the exorbitant privilege next. Today, America's situation looks much less clear-cut than that of the Dutch Republic. There is no clear alternative right now. So to find out what will actually happen to the dollar, we need a more comparable example, which leads us to the rise and fall of the British pound.

### 英镑的崛起与衰落

**Host**: 让我们回到 1799 年。**拿破仑**（Napoleon）刚刚在法国掌权，这意味着现在有两个新兴大国在争夺成为新的全球霸主。法国拥有最强大的军队，并迅速扩张领土，但英国拥有最强大的海军，使其能够主导全球贸易。虽然法国占领了富裕的**荷兰共和国**，但这样做，法国可以说给了英国最大的代价——**过高特权**。英国的借贷利率可以降至 3%，而法国则需要支付 10% 或更高。利用这一特权，英国实际上是通过资助奥地利、普鲁士和俄国来雇佣欧洲军队对抗法国。此外，他们利用其庞大的海军封锁了法国及其盟友。但为此，英国国家不得不以前所未有的水平借款，几乎达到了 GDP 的 200%。这导致**英格兰银行**（Bank of England）挤兑，意味着他们不得不暂停英镑兑换**黄金**（gold）和白银的能力，实际上使英镑成为一种“**法定货币**”（fiat currency），与今天的**美元**（US Dollar）非常相似。令所有人惊讶的是，这并没有让英镑变得一文不值。如果一种货币不以贵金属为后盾，它还能有什么价值？答案很简单：**英镑**得到了强大的英国国家的支撑。你总是可以用它在英国支付税款。反过来，这意味着你总是可以用它来支付当时世界上最能干的英国商人。那么，这个时代的英镑和今天的**美元**一样吗？不完全是。因为与今天的**美元**不同，**英格兰银行**承诺，一旦战争结束，英镑将再次与**黄金**一样有价值。他们也兑现了这一承诺。在**拿破仑**战败后，英国已成为无可争议的霸主，每个人都想在那里储存他们的钱。无论是在**英格兰银行**、伦敦证券交易所，还是英国的银行，利率都比以往任何时候都低。虽然这在微小的**荷兰共和国**助长了挥霍和泡沫，但在
