### 预算总览

**主持人（The Rundown）**: 今天在皇后公园，财政部长 **Peter Bethlenfalvy** 发布了安大略省2026年预算，内容庞大且关乎数十亿美元开支，经济前景不确定，我们来逐项解读给观众。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host (The Rundown)**: Today was a big day at Queen's Park as Finance Minister, Peter Bethlenfalvy, delivered Ontario's 2026 budget. It's a big document with some even bigger numbers, billions in spending, all at a time when the economic outlook is uncertain. We're going to look at what the budget tells us about the province's finances, about the government's priorities and what it could all mean for you. Welcome to The Rundown.
</details>

### 关键数字：赤字与财政路径

**John Michael McGrath**: 今年的最大数字是赤字 — 现在预计今年为13.8亿美元，而此前他们曾预计更小（约7亿美元）；明年预计6.1亿美元赤字，有可能在后续财政年度收支平衡（若实现的话将是自2018年以来保守党首次平衡预算）。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**John Michael McGrath**: So the biggest number coming out of this year's budget has to be the deficit. The government had been projecting a smaller deficit this year than they've ended up with. We are now looking at a $13.8 billion deficit for this year. That is not just larger than they were projecting — they were projecting like a $7 billion deficit — but it's also larger than last year's. They are now projecting a $6.1 billion deficit for next year. We might be balanced by the following year. That would be the 2028-29 fiscal year. If the government accomplishes that, it might sound kind of churlish, but it would be the first time that the PCs have presented a balanced budget since they won in 2018.
</details>

### 赤字驱动因素：利息与医疗

**John Michael McGrath**: 利息支出与新增开支推动赤字，其中最大的新支出来源是医疗保健；过去两年卫生支出增加了100亿美元，本年度医疗开支将超过1000亿美元。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**John Michael McGrath**: Interest on debt is one of the big drivers of the deficit. The government says that they've accumulated a lot of deficits — some of that was COVID related, some of that has been the result of tax cuts and other spending priorities. But overwhelmingly, the biggest driver of new spending has been health care. We've got an aging population in Ontario. The spending on health care just in the last two years has increased by $10 billion. This year will be over $100 billion just in health care spending alone.
</details>

### 政府宣称优先项与财政路径

**主持人**: 政府拥抱赤字一年，意在保持财政弹性并应对不确定性；他们选择中间谨慎路径，把赤字目标较去年预测的更低。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: Budgets are financial documents and they're obviously political ones too. How healthy are our finances as we head into a period of even greater economic uncertainty? What does this budget tell us about the government's priorities? ... The government is embracing deficits for another year. It's $2.3 billion higher for '25-26 increases to... Kicking a balanced budget down the line a little bit further. They're trying to keep up with inflation pressure and population growth on some files. They're going for a prudent middle ground.
</details>

### 投资与“Protect Ontario”基金

**John Michael McGrath**: 一个新的亮点是“Protect Ontario Account Investment Fund”——把此前未用的5亿中的4亿作为种子资本，旨在吸引私营资本，投资于供应链韧性与未来面向的产业（例如AI与先进制造）。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**John Michael McGrath**: It's the Protect Ontario Account Investment Fund. Previously, the government had announced the Protect Ontario Fund, a $5 billion fund to assist businesses and employers in response to Trump's tariffs. Only $1 billion of that $5 billion got used. So they're now announcing this investment fund that will use the remaining $4 billion as seed capital. They want to try and encourage private capital to come in. They're going to look around for more future-facing investment opportunities. They want to work on supply chain resilience. You could probably think of like AI and other more sort of advanced industries. That $4 billion will be the seed capital with which they will do that.
</details>

### 刺激新房建设：扩大HST返税

**主持人**: 政府扩大对新房建设的HST返税，目标是恢复住房开工，但专家警告短期内效果有限（预算里对今年年初新开工预测被下修、未来数年也有下调）。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: One of the big announcements yesterday was the expanded HST rebate for new home construction. They're hoping to resuscitate the province's housing numbers. The government's estimate is that this HST rebate, expanded from last year's measure, they expect that to lead to the construction of 8,000 new homes between now and 2031. ... The estimates for new housing starts for this year in this budget were revised downwards by 10,000 units, and a further 20,000 units were lost in downward revisions for next year and the year after that.
</details>

### 住房政策的批判与分歧

**Ricardo Tranjan (CCPA)**: 把1.4亿用于对买新房的税收返还是一种“企业福利”，对真正改善住房安全性与供给帮助有限；若把资金用于社会住房或收紧租房管制，能更直接缓解危机。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ricardo Tranjan**: I'm not thinking of a least effective way to improve housing security. Because we're spending $1.4 billion to provide a tax rebate to folks buying newly built homes. And it can be anyone — it can be your first house, it can be your second house, it can be your 10th house. If we were to focus on social housing and tenancy regulation, we could do a lot with those two areas. With $1.4 million we could build a significant number of social housing units. With $0 we could tighten rent controls and improve affordability for a third of Ontarians. We're providing a tax base that likely will fail on its own alleged goal, which is to improve supply. The previous measure didn't achieve that goal, and we're not sure it even achieved its stated goal. It's an awful policy in housing. This is not housing policy. This is simply corporate welfare.
</details>

**Rosalie W**: HST返税可能刺激新建和使个别购买更便宜，但若目标是整体供给与住房安全，需要更长期且针对性的供给与社会住房投资；一些开发商对前景更乐观，但我对此仍较悲观。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rosalie W**: I think it's intended to stimulate new builds more than it is really around housing affordability. So if it's going to be effective around the total supply of housing, then that will take some years to show up. But also those that are currently going to planning to buy a new house — it will make it more affordable for them. Whether they needed that affordability break or not, it does make it cheaper. Some developer groups are much more positive about this outlook. They welcome these changes. They say it could help the housing sector turn the corner. They may know better than me. I'm kind of pessimistic on it so far.
</details>

### 小微企业：税率与现金流缓解

**主持人**: 省府把小企业税率从3.2%降到2.2%，目标是给现金匮乏的小企业减负，促进投资与保岗。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: Ontario plans to cut the tax rate for small business to 2.2% from 3.2% on par with... If we look at other provinces across the country, how significant is that to a small business? Will it help them hire new staff, expand?
</details>

**Ricardo Tranjan**: 小企业普遍“现金匮乏”，税收减免能提高流动性，帮助它们维持运营并可能保住或扩大雇佣，但重要的是配套的贸易与供应链政策以减少经营冲击。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ricardo Tranjan**: One of the main things you see across all small businesses and the surveys across provinces is that they're continually cash poor, as in they're strapped for additional resources. So giving them some tax relief, particularly when their costs are increasing in ways that they can't manage the same way that big businesses do — I think relief for small businesses, what they will spend that on will be investing in their businesses and keeping themselves afloat. Hopefully they'll be able to grow and expand and hire new people, but it will help if they're facing tough times to make sure that they don't have to reduce their business or reduce their staff.
</details>

### 卫生与长期照护：投入与担忧

**主持人**: 政府承诺未来十年在医疗基础设施投入约640亿美元、增加约50家医院床位；但财政问责办公室警告：在现有资金模式下，医护人力与护理层级可能供给不足。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: The government says there will be $64 billion over the next ten years for health care infrastructure. That includes about 50 hospitals to increase their beds. The Financial Accountability Office of Ontario has warned about health care staffing levels at the current funding model. What could the government have done to address that?
</details>

**Rosalie W**: 需要关注的是“获得何种床位”与资金去向；更多社区与居家照护（home care）投资有助于减少医院不必要住院并延缓进入长期照护，当前家护、长期照护等待名单仍很长，单年投入不足以全部解决但方向正确。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rosalie W**: It matters more what kind of beds they are, because there is the risk that we have high volumes of alternate level of care patients. Those are patients that could be better cared for in long-term care in the community, but they're in hospital beds. Depending on the hospital, those rates could be 10% up to 40% of the beds are currently occupied by those types of patients. ... If we could increase home care services, we could likely delay people requiring LTC. Investing in home care is absolutely a good place to do it and that we still have an aging population. So I would agree that going back down is actually more of a concern. But the investment this year is welcome.
</details>

**Ricardo Tranjan**: 实际上按通胀、人口增长和老龄化校正后的“实际分配”今年仅小幅增长0.5%，明年反而有实质削减（1.6%），表明在真实购买力上并未充分投资；另需警惕对私营化、以补贴私营照护为导向的倾斜。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ricardo Tranjan**: My main concern here is that when you adjust the current figures for inflation, for population growth and for aging, which is really important in health, we have only a 0.5% increase in the real allocation this year, and that drops next year. Next year, we have a 1.6% cut in real health spending as forecast right now. So we're not investing nearly enough. The second piece of this, it also matters where this funding is going. We are starting to pay more and more for or subsidize in different ways for profit care. That's a slippery slope. We know that funding private care won't buy us as much health in return.
</details>

### 教育、职业培训与长期人力

**主持人**: 在教育方面，省预算在K–12与高等教育投入并未显著提升，托育是唯一推动投资上升的项目；但对职业培训与后中等教育支援（影响护理等专业供给）投入不足，引发对未来人力供给的担忧。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: In education, the only thing driving investment up is the childcare program — a federal initiative. We haven't seen the same level of investment at K to 12 schools or in post-secondary training. Post-secondary education is becoming more expensive, and a lot of the professionals who could be the potential professionals, the workers who could be looking at training — the right investment here would have been post-secondary education, college training of all sorts.
</details>

**Ricardo Tranjan**: 政府削减或限制学生资助（如OSAP 改变）会让想成为护士等职业的人在决定是否投入培训时更加犹豫，长期不利于医疗与护理人力供给。  

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ricardo Tranjan**: This government has recently made some very draconian changes to OSAP. We also have seen that this government has not come through for post-secondary institutions, including colleges, since the cap on international students. So overall, we have a system where post-secondary education is becoming more expensive, be it university, be it college, their class assistance for students to go through. A lot of them right now are second guessing. Should I invest? Can I afford how much debt will I have to take in order to become a nurse or to become a health professional? The right investment here would have been on post-secondary education.
</details>
