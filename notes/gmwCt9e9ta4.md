### 无人机的技术与应用

**[主持人]**: 你的公司生产无人驾驶飞行器，即 **UAVs**。请帮我理解一下，那究竟是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Host]**: Your company manufactures unmanned aerial vehicles, better known as UAVs. Help me understand. What exactly is that?

</details>

**[Kathe Ensign]**: 它们就是无人机。我们的无人机比较高级。通常我们认为无人机是某种没有飞行员的飞行物。本质上，我们的产品看起来更像传统的没有飞行员的飞行器。我们的产品被称为**固定翼无人机**（fixed wing UAV），这意味着它有类似飞机的机翼，这让它具有 DJI 这类四旋翼摄影无人机所不具备的航程。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Kathe Ensign]**: They're drones. Ours are a little bit fancier. Generally, we think of drones as being, you know, something, a flying thing that doesn't have pilots. Essentially ours look a little bit more like the traditional flying things that don't have pilots. So ours are what's called a fixed wing UAV, meaning that it has aeroplane like arms, which gives it a distance that, say, a quadcopter DJI type videography drone wouldn't, wouldn't have.

</details>

**[Kathe Ensign]**: 以我们的 **Recom 3.2** 为例，它的翼展为 3.2 米，机身长度约 2 米。这赋予了它出色的航程和续航能力。它的飞行时间约为 5 小时，飞行速度超过每小时 100 公里，航程超过 500 公里。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Kathe Ensign]**: So our recom 3.2 that's what's on the screen right now is a 3.2m wingspan drone. So somewhere around 12ft if you're thinking in freedom units and about a two metre length fuselage. What this does is that this gives it distance and endurance. So when we think about how long this thing can actually stay in the air, it's around five hours operating over 100km an hour, which gives it a range of over 500km.

</details>

**[主持人]**: 与仅用于摄影的消费级无人机不同，这些无人机有什么用途？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Host]**: Unlike the consumer drones, which are there for really videography, looking at views, what are these drones used for?

</details>

**[Kathe Ensign]**: 它们的应用范围很广，从侦察、诱饵、电子战到打击等作战应用。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Kathe Ensign]**: They can be used for a wide variety of applications, so everything from surveillance to decoy to electronic warfare, or even for combat applications like strike.

</details>

<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Host]**: Your background isn't in drones necessarily. You have a pharmacology background as well. How did you get into this space?

<summary>Original English</summary>

My brother has always been much more defence focussed. He became much more entrenched in the goings on of the conflict... where I really started to get excited was in the scale of the business and the potential applications of the type of work that he was doing across NATO. So I became involved just over a year ago.

### 探访加拿大坦克博物馆

**[主持人]**: 你知道位于奥沙瓦的 **加拿大坦克博物馆** 拥有全国最大的历史军事车辆收藏吗？Rundown 的制片人 Brit Weaver 进行了实地探访，了解了该地区对过去战争努力的支持，以及这些坦克如何诉说着加拿大的军事遗产。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Host]**: Did you know the Canadian Tank Museum in Oshawa has the country's largest collection of historical military vehicles? Rundown producer Brit Weaver visited to learn about how the region supported past war efforts and what these tanks tell us about Canada's military heritage.

</details>

**[Jeremy Blowers]**: 我是加拿大坦克博物馆的执行董事 **Jeremy Blowers**。博物馆的宗旨不是美化战争，而是让人们了解我们过去的退伍军人所使用的装备，以及这些系统的运作方式，从而尊重他们的服务和牺牲。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Jeremy Blowers]**: I'm Jeremy Blowers, I'm the executive director of the Canadian Tank Museum. It's very important that people understand that our museum and the things that we're doing educationally in the shows is not to in any way glorify war or promote conflict. The whole purpose is so that people can have an understanding of what our veterans in the past and today served on what protects them, how the systems work. And it's more to honour their service and sacrifice.

</details>

**[Jeremy Blowers]**: 我们的收藏包括 163 件军事装备，其中超过 150 件是可操作的。我们拥有世界上规模最大的运行中军事车辆车队之一，涵盖了从第二次世界大战到当今战场上使用的坦克，以及各种先进的实验室技术。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Jeremy Blowers]**: Our collection has 163 pieces of actual military equipment. Over 150 of them are operational. That is one of the largest fleets of running military vehicles in the world. We have pieces of equipment that cover the entire mechanisation of military hardware. So everything from jeeps to trucks to engineering vehicles to armoured personnel carriers, infantry fighting vehicles, tanks from the Second World War to ones that are being used on battlefields today.

</details>

**[Jeremy Blowers]**: 我们有在 1943 年奥沙瓦制造的 **General Motors Fox Mark one** 装甲侦察车。这是战争期间制造的 1400 多辆中仅存的七辆之一。它曾被送往海外参与意大利战役，80 年后在 2023 年被遣返回加拿大坦克博物馆。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Jeremy Blowers]**: We have the General Motors Fox Mark one built right here in Oshawa, Ontario, in 1943. This is just one of seven that remains of the over 1400 that were built during the war. What's more interesting is that this isn't just an example of a General Motors fox. This is a veteran that was produced in 43, sent overseas to serve in the Italian campaign. And exactly 80 years later, in 2023, it was repatriated to the Canadian Tank Museum.

</details>

**[Jeremy Blowers]**: 奥沙瓦和达勒姆地区一直是一个军事生产走廊，拥有铁路、航空基础设施、港口以及工业生产能力和专业知识。这也是博物馆现在能够通过志愿者来运营的原因，我们有 208 名志愿者，其中 50% 是加拿大武装部队或盟军的退伍军人，这让他们在退役后找到了归属感。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Jeremy Blowers]**: So in Oshawa and Durham region, this really is a hub where we have rail, we have air infrastructure, we have ports, and we also have that industrial infrastructure and know how... we have 208 volunteers on the books. About 50% of them are former serving members of the Canadian Armed Forces or our allied forces.

</details>
