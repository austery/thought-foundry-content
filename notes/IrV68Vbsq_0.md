### 食品银行的困境

**Will Jarvis**: 随着伊朗战争看不到尽头，且美国燃油价格仍远高于每加仑4美元，该国的食品银行正开始受到挤压。每年有多达5000万美国人求助于食品储藏室或救济厨房，而在通胀上升的背景下，这些组织已经在努力应对需求。现在，这些团体表示，他们被迫做出痛苦的权衡：支付运送食品的燃料费，还是支付购买食品本身的费用。俄勒冈州的一家食品银行表示，其不得不为卡车车队编制额外的2万美元月度燃料预算。蒙大拿州的一个食品银行网络表示，它正在减少订单量，其他食品银行则表示，他们不得不将供应内容转换为更便宜的选择，例如用低成本农产品代替肉类和其他蛋白质。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Will Jarvis**: And with no end in sight to the war in Iran and the cost of fuel still well over $4 a gallon in the US, the country's food banks are starting to get squeezed. As many as 50 million Americans turn to food pantries or soup kitchens every year, and the organizations had already been struggling to keep up with demand amid rising inflation. Now, the groups say they're being forced to make a painful trade-off: pay for the fuel to deliver food, or pay for the food itself. A food bank network in Montana said it's reducing how much food it orders, and other food banks say they've had to shift what they offer to cheaper options like substituting meat and other protein with low-cost produce.
