### 电影叙事的织物语言

**旁白**: 服装设计是用织物进行的叙事。每一次素描和每一针缝补，设计师都在通过服装转化角色。像**阿德里安 (Adrian)** 这样的设计师，他是好莱坞华丽风格的先驱，并创造了影史上最著名的鞋子——**《绿野仙踪》(The Wizard of Oz)** 中桃乐丝的**红宝石鞋 (ruby slippers)**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**NARRATOR**: Costume design is storytelling in fabric form. With every sketch and stitch, designers craft outfits that transform characters. Designers such as Adrian, a pioneer of Hollywood glamour and creator of the most famous shoes in cinema history, Dorothy's ruby slippers in The Wizard of Oz, set the standard for iconic costume design.

</details>

**旁白**: **艾琳·夏拉夫 (Irene Sharaff)** 为**伊丽莎白·泰勒 (Elizabeth Taylor)** 在《埃及艳后》中设计的60多套服装为她赢得了一座实至名归的奥斯卡奖。**特拉维斯·班顿 (Travis Banton)** 为**玛琳·黛德丽 (Marlene Dietrich)** 设计的难忘装束。**特拉维拉 (Travilla)** 为**玛丽莲·梦露 (Marilyn Monroe)** 设计的经典粉色礼服。还有**让·路易 (Jean Louis)** 为**丽塔·海华丝 (Rita Hayworth)** 在《吉尔达》中设计的精巧长裙，以及由年轻的**鲍勃·麦基 (Bob Mackie)** 勾勒、至今仍是红毯焦点的璀璨杰作。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**NARRATOR**: Irene Sharaff's creations of more than 60 costumes for Elizabeth Taylor in Cleopatra earned her a well-deserved Oscar. Travis Banton's memorable outfits for Marlene Dietrich. Travilla's archetypal pink gown for Marilyn Monroe. And Jean Louis' ingenious engineering for Rita Hayworth's dress in Gilda, along with his sparkling masterpiece sketched by a young Bob Mackie that continues to be a red carpet scene stealer.

</details>

**旁白**: **沃尔特·普伦基特 (Walter Plunkett)** 在**《乱世佳人》(Gone With The Wind)** 中那件由窗帘改制的裙子，不仅是电影史的一部分……服装设计师鲍勃·麦基在1976年创造了美国电视史上最令人难忘的时刻之一，当时**卡罗尔·伯内特 (Carol Burnett)** 穿着恶搞版“窗帘装”走下楼梯。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**NARRATOR**: Walter Plunkett's drape dropping work in Gone With The Wind isn't just part of cinematic history... Costume designer Bob Mackie made it one of the most unforgettable moments in US television history when, in 1976, Carol Burnett swept down the stairs in her parody "Went with the Wind".

</details>

### 服装界的第一夫人：伊迪丝·海德

**旁白**: 然而，谈到服装设计，有一个名字凌驾于所有人之。**伊迪丝·海德 (Edith Head)**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**NARRATOR**: Yet, when it comes to costume design, one name stands above all others. Edith Head.

</details>

**萨莉 (SALLY)**: 伊迪丝·海德当然是好莱坞的首选设计师。她经常同时为50部电影工作。她有一大群人为她工作，供她调遣。有一天，**西奥多拉·范·伦克尔 (Theadora Van Runkle)** 告诉她：“他们正在美国国旗上翻云覆雨。”伊迪丝只回了一句：“嗯，他们穿的是什么？”

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**SALLY**: Edith Head was, of course, the premiere designer as far as Hollywood is concerned. She frequently worked on 50 films at a time. She had vast armies of people who worked with her, and for her. She came in one day, Theadora Van Runkle said to her, "They're fornicating on the American flag." And all Edith said back was, "Hm, what are they wearing?"

</details>

**旁白**: 1938年，伊迪丝·海德成为第一位领导好莱坞大制片厂服装部的女性。她参与了1000多部电影的设计，为**奥黛丽·赫本 (Audrey Hepburn)**、**格蕾丝·凯利 (Grace Kelly)**、伊丽莎白·泰勒和**贝蒂·戴维斯 (Bette Davis)** 等明星创造了标志性造型。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**NARRATOR**: In 1938, Edith Head became the first woman to lead a major Hollywood studio's costume department. She designed on over 1,000 films, creating iconic looks for stars like Audrey Hepburn, Grace Kelly, Elizabeth Taylor, and Bette Davis.

</details>

**基里切蒂 (CHIERICHETTI)**: 伊迪丝·海德曾获得35项学院奖提名，并8次获奖。没有任何其他服装设计师能接近她的记录。她至今仍是奥斯卡历史上获奖最多的女性。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**CHIERICHETTI**: Edith Head was nominated for 35 Academy Awards and she won the award eight times. No other costume designer comes close to Edith Head's record. She remains the most awarded woman in Academy history.

</details>

**伊迪丝·海德**: 当你赢得了一座大型奥斯卡奖，你就有权获得一个我们称之为“缩微版”的奖杯。我记得**沃尔特·迪士尼 (Walt Disney)** 拥有的缩微奖杯最多，他把它们做成了给他妻子的手镯。他说：“你为什么不也做一个呢？”我说：“我不喜欢手镯，它们会叮当作响。”他说：“你某种程度上也是个缝纫师，为什么不做个针垫呢？”所以，这就有了世界上唯一的珠宝针垫。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**EDITH HEAD**: You see, when you will have won a large Oscar, you are entitled to what we call a miniature. Now, I think Walt Disney had the most that I can remember, and he made them into a bracelet for his wife. He said, "Why don't you do something?", and I said, "I don't like bracelets, they jangle." He said, "Well, you're a dressmaker in a way, why don't you make a pin cushion?" So, there's the world's only jewel pin cushion.

</details>

### 服装设计师的专业素养

**伊迪丝·海德**: 服装设计师是做什么的？这是一个漫长的故事。通过衣服这个媒介，我们被要求将男演员、女演员、孩子，甚至动物，转化为他们在电影中扮演的任何角色。很多人把我们和时装设计师混为一谈。我们不是。换句话说，我们完全、绝对是根据一份叫做“剧本”的东西来工作的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**EDITH HEAD**: What does a costume designer do? It's a long story. Through the medium of our clothes we are asked to translate an actor or an actress, or a child, or even an animal, into whatever character they're playing in the film. And so, many people confuse us with fashion designers. That we are not. In other words, we work completely absolutely from something called a script.

</details>

**伊迪丝·海德**: 剧本说女孩很漂亮，她有漂亮的衣服。剧本说女孩一团糟，她看起来像个邋遢鬼。换句话说，我们无法控制自己在做什么。我喜不喜欢这件衣服根本无关紧要。但我们有一种魔力，能让公众相信同一个演员在每次出现在电影中时都是不同的人。对我来说，那是纯粹的魔力。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**EDITH HEAD**: The script says the girl is beautiful, she has lovely clothes. The script says the girl is a mess, she looks like a slob. In other words, we have no control over what we're doing. Whether I like it or not has nothing to do with it. But we have the magic of asking the public to believe the same actor or actress is a different person every time you see them in a film. See, to me, that is pure magic.

</details>

**伊迪丝·海德**: 我认为导演就是这艘船的船长。读完剧本后，我会去找导演，去问**希区柯克 (Hitchcock)** 或**乔治·罗伊·希尔 (George Roy Hill)**，“关于剧本有什么特别的要求吗？”如果是希区柯克，他会说：“亲爱的伊迪丝，读剧本就行了。”

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**EDITH HEAD**: The director, I think, is possibly the captain of the ship as far as I'm concerned. After I will have read the script I will go to a director, to a Hitchcock or George Roy Hill, whoever it might be, and say, "Is there anything particular about the script?" If it's a Hitchcock, he'll say, "My dear Edith, just read the script."

</details>

**旁白**: 制片人会给你一定数额的预算，你必须弄清楚如何用这笔钱完成任务。当伊迪丝·海德为**卡罗尔·贝克 (Carroll Baker)** 制作《地毯袋商》(The Carpetbaggers) 时，她不得不重复使用旧服装，因为他们警告她预算超支了。在制作**《日落大道》(Sunset Boulevard)** 时，伊迪丝找回了一些**格洛丽亚·斯旺森 (Gloria Swanson)** 在20年代穿过的鞋子，并在1949年再次使用了它们。伊迪丝非常擅长达成让每个人都满意的共识。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**NARRATOR**: The producer gives you a certain amount of money and you have to figure out how to do it with that amount of money. When Edith Head was doing The Carpetbaggers with Carroll Baker, she had to reuse old costumes for a couple of the scenes because they warned her she was just going over her budget. When they were doing Sunset Boulevard, Edith went and found some of the shoes that Gloria Swanson had worn in the '20s, and she used them again in 1949. And Edith was great at arriving at a consensus that would please everyone.

</details>

**伊迪丝·海德**: 我会和电影摄影师交谈。我在电视制作中发现，有些东西，比如纯白色，是不行的。我还会了解在某些片段中，比如月光下的场景，他们希望避免某些阴影或蓝色。有时是有技术原因的。所以我向摄影师展示所有的面料样品和草图。显然，我还要和艺术指导合作，因为我不想在一套白色布景前做一套白色西装。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**EDITH**: I talk to the cinematographer. Now, I discovered in television there are certain things, not pure white, still things of that sort. Also I find out in certain sequences, perhaps in a film which might be done at moonlight, they would like to avoid certain shades or blues or somet-- Sometimes there are technical reasons. So I show all samples of fabrics and sketches to the cinematographer. Obviously I work with the art director because, for instance, I wouldn't want to do a white suit and a white set.

</details>

**伊迪丝·海德**: 假设你是一位演员，我不会给你看一张草图然后问：“**罗伯特·雷德福 (Robert Redford)**，你喜欢这个吗？”我会给你看三张草图，然后说：“这张草图会让你看起来更英俊、更有阳刚之气；这张草图更具亲和力；第三张则更富戏剧性。这三张草图中，哪一张能帮助你转化为电影中的角色？”所以他会说：“嗯，伊迪丝，我觉得这张很漂亮，但我更想要开领的。”这样当演员跟我合作完，他们就成了创作的一部分。这就是为什么我以后不会有任何抵触。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**EDITH**: Assuming you were an actor... I would not show you a sketch and say, "Robert Redford, do you like this?" I would show you three sketches and I would say, "This sketch will make you look much more handsome and virile, this sketch is more sympathetic. Third sketch is more dramatic. Which of these three sketches will help you transform yourself into the film you're playing?" So he will say, "Well, Edith, I think this is lovely but I'd rather have an open neck." So, when an actor or actress will have worked with me, they're now part of it. And that's why I don't have any repercussions later on.

</details>

### 从黑白到彩色的挑战

**埃尔维**: 让我们聊聊**《彗星美人》(All About Eve)**，那是部伟大的电影。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**ELWY**: Let's talk All About Eve, great picture.

</details>

**伊迪丝·海德**: 首先，你有一位极其出色的女演员，她确切地知道自己想要如何表现以及需要什么。你有一个极其出色的剧本，当然还有伟大的导演，以及制作精美服装的机会。当你读剧本时，脑子里就会灵光一闪，你说：“啊哈，必须是一件用黑貂皮装饰的棕色裙子，我知道必须是这样。”换句话说，你训练有素，读剧本时就在设计。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**EDITH**: All About Eve, first place, you have a superb actress who knows exactly how she wants to work and what she needs. You have a superb script and, of course, great director, and a chance to do beautiful clothes. As you will read a script... Something clicks and you say, "A-ha, it's gotta be a brown dress trimmed with sable, I know it has to." In other words, you're so trained that as you read a script you design.

</details>

**旁白**: 贝蒂·戴维斯有很多身材问题。她的胸线很低且宽，脖子粗，还有严重的罗圈腿。幸运的是，当时的“新风貌”强调长裙，所以盖住了腿。因为伊迪丝非常擅长伪装问题身材，贝蒂·戴维斯对她非常满意。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**NARRATOR**: Bette Davis had a lot of problems. She had a very big, low bustline, a broad neck, and she had very bowed legs. And, fortunately for Edith, the new look stressed longer skirts than what they'd been doing up to that time, so those covered the legs. Because Edith was really good at camouflaging problem figures Bette Davis was very happy with her.

</details>

**埃尔维**: 为彩色电影设计服装与黑白电影相比，有什么特别的不同吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**ELWY**: Are there very special differences in designing costumes for colour as against black and white?

</details>
