### 儿童疫苗政策面临重大转变

我正在亚特兰大参加一个非常重要委员会的会议。这是一个疫苗委员会，负责为美国成人和儿童接种的所有疫苗提供建议，实质上是制定指导方针。本周，他们似乎即将做出一些重大改变。

<p class="english-text">I'm in Atlanta for the meeting of a very important committee. This is a vaccine committee that makes recommendations, essentially guidelines for all the shots that adults and children in America get. And this week it seems like they are about to make some very big changes.</p>
</details>

我的同事**阿波拉·曼达维尔**（Apora Mandaville）正在报道**CDC**（Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: 美国疾病控制与预防中心）委员会今天的会议，该委员会似乎准备对一项几十年来一直推荐给美国所有新生儿的疫苗——**乙型肝炎疫苗**（Hepatitis B vaccine: 预防乙型肝炎的疫苗）——做出重大调整。这种疫苗可以预防一种高度传染性疾病，该疾病可能严重损害肝脏。自20世纪90年代初婴儿开始接种疫苗以来，儿童急性感染率下降了99%。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">My colleague Apora Mandaville is covering the meeting of a CDC committee today that seems poised to make a major switch up involving a vaccine that's been recommended for all newborns in the US for decades: the hepatitis B vaccine. It protects against the highly contagious disease that can severely damage the liver. And after babies began getting vaccinated in the early 90s, acute infection rates for kids dropped by 99%.</p>

然而，该委员会现在似乎很可能决定推迟接种此疫苗，甚至完全不再向儿童提供，因为卫生部长**小罗伯特·F·肯尼迪**（Robert F. Kennedy Jr.）对此疫苗的必要性提出了质疑。阿波拉表示，他们还将讨论修订其他常见儿童疫苗的使用建议。

<details>

<p class="english-text">But the committee now seems likely to decide that vaccine should be delayed or no longer offered to children at all, as health secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. has cast doubt on whether it's necessary. AORVA says they're also going to discuss revising the use of other common childhood vaccines.</p>

该委员会不制定法律或规则，但他们提出的指导方针非常重要，因为它们实质上决定了保险公司和政府保险项目是否会覆盖这些疫苗的费用。因此，他们的决定可能会影响人们支付这些疫苗的能力，甚至影响他们获得疫苗的途径。

So, what they decide could affect people's ability to afford these shots or even have access to them.</p>
</details>

更广泛地说，我采访的公共卫生专家担心，该委员会正在助长一些已经流传数十年的**反疫苗论**（Anti-vaccine tropes: 质疑疫苗安全性或有效性的观点）。该委员会成员均由小罗伯特·F·肯尼迪亲自挑选，他多年来对疫苗提出了许多毫无根据的主张，声称疫苗未经适当测试、对儿童大脑有毒，并会导致自闭症等疾病。

The committee members were all handpicked by Robert F. Kennedy Jr. who has made many unfounded claims about vaccines over the years that they were not tested properly, that they are toxic to children's brains, that they cause conditions like autism.</p>

### 美国军事行动的伦理质疑

While many use of force experts and lawmakers have criticized that overall campaign as illegal, today the focus will be on one strike in particular.</p>
</details>

9月2日，**海豹突击队第六分队**（Seal Team 6: 美国海军特种作战部队）的队员对一艘载有11人的委内瑞拉船只发动了袭击。硝烟散去后，监控视频显示残骸中有两人仍然生还。据《纽约时报》采访的官员称，军方截获了幸存者的通讯，他们正在向涉嫌贩毒集团成员求助。官员表示，军方将这一求救电话解读为幸存者仍在抵抗。于是，负责此次行动的**弗兰克·布拉德利海军上将**（Admiral Frank Bradley）下令进行后续袭击，导致两人死亡。

Officials say the military interpreted that distress call as meaning the survivors were still in the fight. And Admiral Frank Bradley, who was overseeing the operation, ordered a follow-up strike that killed both people.</p>

### 特朗普政府削弱燃油效率标准

昨天在白宫，**特朗普总统**（President Trump）采取行动，大幅削弱了联邦**燃油效率标准**（Fuel efficiency standards: 衡量汽车燃油消耗效率的规定），废除了拜登时代一项鼓励汽车制造商销售更高效车辆和更多电动汽车的规定。这是本届政府旨在削弱应对气候变化政策、并加倍支持燃油车的最新举措。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At the White House yesterday, President Trump moved to significantly weaken federal fuel efficiency standards, getting rid of a Biden era rule that had encouraged automakers to sell more efficient vehicles and more electric vehicles. It's the administration's latest move to gut policy aimed at addressing climate change and double down on gas powered cars.</p>
</details>

白宫声称，新规定将通过降低制造成本为消费者省钱。经济学家告诉《纽约时报》，这在一定程度上可能实现，但代价是公共健康和环境。此外，特朗普自己的关税政策以及通货膨胀，也助推了许多汽车成本的上涨。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The White House claims the new rules will save consumers money by lowering manufacturing costs. Economists told the Times that could happen to some extent, but it would come at the expense of public health and the environment. Additionally, Trump's own tariff policies along with inflation have helped drive up the cost of many cars.</p>
</details>

当特朗普宣布新的降低标准时，他身旁站着各大汽车公司高管，他们公开庆祝这些变化。然而私下里，许多人对联邦政策的反复无常表示担忧。因为在拜登政府期间，他们已经投入了数十亿美元转向电动汽车。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As Trump announced the new lowered standards, he was flanked by top auto executives who publicly celebrated the changes. In private though, many have expressed concerns about the whiplash of changing federal policies. As during the Biden administration, they already spent billions of dollars pivoting to electric vehicles.</p>
</details>

### AI聊天机器人与健康咨询

今年早些时候，一位26岁的女性收到血液检查结果，显示荷尔蒙失衡，她将完整的医疗报告粘贴到**ChatGPT**（OpenAI开发的大型语言模型）中，询问可能存在的问题。聊天机器人告诉她“很可能与肿瘤有关”。这个回答是错误的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Earlier this year, when a 26-year-old woman got some blood work results back showing hormone imbalances, she pasted her full medical report into chat GPT to ask what the problem could be. The chatbot told her it was quote most likely related to a tumor. That was wrong.</p>
</details>

但当一位63岁的男性在锻炼时感到不适，向ChatGPT寻求建议时，他利用聊天机器人提供的信息说服医生仔细检查他的心脏，结果发现了一处严重堵塞。这种好坏参半的结果，是全球数百万开始使用聊天机器人寻求健康建议的人们所面临的现实。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But when a 63-year-old man who'd been feeling some discomfort while exercising asked ChatGpt for advice, he used the info the chatbot gave him to persuade his doctor to take a closer look at his heart where they found a significant blockage. These kinds of mixed results are the reality for millions of people around the world who have started using chat bots for health advice.</p>
</details>

考虑到聊天机器人在各种问题上都可能出错，专家们担心潜在的错误信息，例如人们在没有病的情况下以为自己快死了，或者在情况严重时却以为不严重。围绕这一趋势的另一个主要担忧是隐私问题。有些人正在将他们的完整病史上传到聊天机器人，其中可能包含大量敏感信息。而**HIPAA**（Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act: 美国联邦医疗信息可携性与责任法案）这项美国联邦健康隐私法，并不适用于运营这些聊天机器人的公司。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

The other big concern around this trend is privacy. Some people are uploading their whole medical histories to chat bots, which can include a ton of sensitive information. And HIPPA, the federal health privacy law in the US, doesn't apply to companies that operate the bots.</p>
</details>

一位专门研究生物医学技术的教授告诉《纽约时报》：“你基本上放弃了你在医疗隐私方面的任何权利。”尽管如此，人们也指出了一些可能意想不到的好处。一位患有转移性阑尾癌的女性告诉《纽约时报》，ChatGPT能即时提供她的检测结果概述，尤其是在结果不好的时候，这很有帮助。她觉得这样可以在就诊前处理毁灭性的消息，然后更有效地利用与医生交流的时间。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

Still, there are some perhaps unexpected benefits that people point to." One woman who has metastatic appendix cancer told the Times it's been helpful to get ChatGpt's immediate overview of her test results, especially when they're bad. She felt like she could process the devastating news before her doctor's visit and then use her time with the doctor more effectively.</p>

### 抢救音乐历史：磁带修复的挑战

最后，一场抢救音乐历史的竞赛正在进行中。许多艺术家的录音室录音最初都是用**磁带**（Magnetic tapes: 用于记录音频或视频信息的存储介质）录制的，而这些磁带现在正在衰变。《纽约时报》追踪了新泽西州的一位名叫**凯利·普里布尔**（Kelly Prible）的男子，他一直走在音频修复的最前沿，致力于抢救**鲍勃·迪伦**（Bob Dylan）和**布鲁斯·斯普林斯汀**（Bruce Springsteen）等标志性艺术家的独特录音。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And finally, there is a race on to save music history. A lot of artists studio recordings were originally captured on magnetic tapes which are now decaying. And the times followed how one man in New Jersey, Kelly Prible, has been at the forefront of audio restoration, working to save unique recordings from icons like Bob Dylan and Bruce Springsteen.</p>
</details>

所有模拟录音都会随着时间变得越来越脆弱，但最易受损的一些录音来自20世纪70年代和80年代，当时制造工艺的变化引入了一些问题，这些问题只是随着时间的推移才变得明显。普里布尔有自己的一套词汇来描述这些问题，从“润滑剂流失综合症”到“粘连综合症”，后者指的是磁带卷轴粘合在一起，变成一大块固体。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">All analog recordings get more and more fragile over time, but some of the most vulnerable recordings are from the 70s and 80s when changes in manufacturing introduced problems that have only become obvious over time. Pribble has his own glossery for how to describe those problems. Everything from loss of lubricant syndrome to adhesion syndrome where spools of tape get fused together into a huge solid chunk.</p>
</details>

普里布尔一直在开创各种**鲁布·戈德堡式**（Rube Goldbergesque: 形容复杂、间接且不必要的装置或方法）的技术来抢救它们，例如使用通常在脱毛沙龙中找到的产品，或者在一个精心倾斜的自制吹风机架上烘干潮湿的磁带。**国会图书馆**（Library of Congress: 美国国家图书馆）的一位音频专家称普里布尔为“魔术师”。他从数百位艺术家那里抢救了磁带。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And Pribble's been pioneering various Rube Goldbergesque techniques to save them using products that you'd usually find in a waxing salon, for example, or drying out wet tape on a handbuilt rack of carefully angled hair dryers. One audio expert at the Library of Congress said Pribble is known as the magician. He salvaged tapes from hundreds of artists.</p>
</details>

但这项工作非常昂贵。一位唱片公司高管，其公司拥有大量旧录音的目录，表示处理和保存所有旧磁带将花费数百万美元。因此，这变成了一个难题：什么值得保存，什么不值得。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But the work is expensive. One record executive whose company owns a huge catalog of old recordings said it would cost millions of dollars to process and preserve all of their old tapes. So, it becomes a difficult question of what is and isn't worth saving.</p>
</details>
