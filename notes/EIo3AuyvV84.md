### 基于能力而非行业

**查尔斯·科克**: 关于早期的业务，分馏塔板业务当时的总裁是一个迷恋**自上而下控制**的人。他每周发备忘录，要求所有人汇报每一分钱是怎么花的，这让员工感到非常沮丧。而且当时的文化很封闭。后来我更换了管理层，改变了理念。首先聚焦于**为客户创造价值**，其次**授权员工**，第三是在意大利建厂。我们开始盈利，并增加了相关产品。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: The first one was uh making fractionating trays. Uh we had a president then who was top down and obsessed with controlling everybody. So they were frustrated. And so I changed the the management and changed the philosophy. Okay, the first thing we're going to focus on is creating value for our customers. And then the second thing we're going to empower our employees so they want to to to do this. And we started growing.

</details>

**戴夫**: 你才 25 岁左右，看到公司亏损、管理混乱，就敢直接更换管理团队。你的信心从何而来？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: Well, it was life or death. And my father said, "You can run this business any way you want. The only thing you need my approval on is to sell." That's how he talked me into coming back.

</details>

**查尔斯·科克**: 我在学习这些不同的原则。我意识到，我们需要**基于能力（capability bounded）**而非**行业限制（industry bounded）**。比如，我们在原油集输领域，大家都说你应该成为一家综合性石油公司，涉足每一个环节。但我应用的是**比较优势下的劳动分工**原则。不，你应该只参与价值链中你能创造比别人更多价值的部分。否则，你注定会失败。我创建了一个叫做“**互惠互利的良性循环**”的原则，这引领我们进入了一个永无止境的增长、创新、成功与失败的循环。如果没有在每一件事上都经历失败，说明你没有做任何尝试。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: I was learning all these different principles and what I saw we were doing is we were building capabilities that is I looked at it we need to be capability bounded not industry bounded. Everybody was saying you need to be an integrated oil company. You need to be in everything. And I was applying divisional labor by comparative advantage. No, you need to be in the part of the value chain where you can create more value than others. Otherwise, you're going to fail. And so I started this created this principle called uh uh uh creating virtuous cycles of mutual benefit. If you're not failing at everything, you're not doing anything new.

</details>

### 失败是最好的老师

**戴夫**: 你人生中第一次重大的失败是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Dave**: What was the first major failure that you know? What was the first punch in the face?

</details>

**查尔斯·科克**: 在炼油过程中我们会产生石油焦，我当时想研究出一种方法把它作为基材来制造**活性炭**。我们为此花了很多钱，但失败了。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: Like I said, okay, we're when we got into refining, we created petroleum coke. So I said, well, let's come up with a a way to to use that as base to make activated carbon. And that was a fair we spent a fair amount of money on that.

</details>

**戴夫**: 很多企业家都会爱上自己的项目。你如何决定什么时候止损离场？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: 当我们意识到自己没有能力为客户创造超额价值，且无法获得相应的回报时，就是离场的时候。这就是为什么我们要从失败中学习。我们现在的业务非常多元化，包括工程项目、太阳能工厂、大宗商品贸易、肥料、精炼产品、化工、玻璃、消费品、四家投资公司、电子产品和管理软件系统。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: Yeah. Well, that's when enough is enough. It's when we decide we don't have the capability to create superior value for our customers and that we're going to be rewarded for. That's what I mean we learned from failure.

</details>

**蔡斯·科克**: 我想深入说明一点。很多人问，科氏到底是什么？这种**基于能力而非行业**的思路是核心区别。如何从俄克拉荷马州的小型原油公司发展到这么多领域？我们鼓励所有管理者从这个角度思考：我已经证明了哪些能为客户创造价值的能力？然后将这些能力指向新行业进行实验。我们另一个原则是**实验性探索**（experimental discovery），不是试图征服世界，而是不断测试：客户是否认可我的产品？我们的核心能力从运营、物流、贸易开始，后来扩展到天然气、化工、肥料。当我们收购**佐治亚太平洋**（Georgia-Pacific）时，虽然看起来是木制品业务，但它背后的核心能力是一致的，后来我们也因此习得了品牌运营的新能力。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Chase Koch**: I just wanted to kind of really drill a point home because this point that is so different about Coke versus almost any other company out there is what my father said on being capability bounded, not industry bounded. I would encourage anyone that's in a business and trying to scale think about it from that lens. What capabilities have I demonstrated that I can add value to customers and then point it at new industries where I can experiment. This is one of our principles as well, experimental discovery.

</details>

### 价值观优先于才华

**查尔斯·科克**: 我们最严重的失败往往是因为违反了这一原则：**招聘时，价值观第一，才华第二**。多年来我一直告诉员工：如果你非要招一个价值观有问题的人，那就招个又慢又笨的，这样我们能很快抓住他并让他滚蛋。如果招了价值观极差的人并让他们成为领导者，后果不堪设想。我们希望员工都是**贡献驱动型**（contribution motivated），通过创造价值来获得回报。但有些价值观有问题的人是**破坏驱动型**的，他们渴望的是权力和控制，会掩盖失败并伪造成功。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: I'll give you our worst failures and what caused it. And it caused us by by violating the principle of of of hiring people first on values and second on talent. I want everybody in the company to be contribution motivated that I want to succeed by by contributing. I want to be rewarded for my contributions. Some of these people were destructively motivated. What they wanted was power or control and and they would hide their failures and uh and make up their successes.

</details>

**蔡斯·科克**: 90 年代后期我们的农业业务有一个战略叫“从天然气到面包”（gas to bread spread）。我们想掌控从天然气开采、转化为肥料、种植作物到最终摆上货架变成面包的每一个环节。这完全违背了原则。我们甚至收购了一个做生猪饲料的业务，却没做任何**尽职调查**。收购几天后才发现我们背负了数亿美元的亏损合同。这对渴望扩张的创始人是一个警示：**不计代价的增长**心态会让你陷入大麻烦。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Chase Koch**: In the late '90s in our a business, we called it the strategy the gas to bread spread. we wanted to basically be in every element of the value chain. It completely violates probably all 41 principles in the book. And we closed that acquisition and within days we found out that we had hundreds of millions of out-of-the-money hog contracts because we didn't even look at the contracts. This growth at all cost mindset is the kind of trouble that you get yourself in.

</details>

### 文化的底层逻辑

**戴夫**: 你如何驱动这种代表原则的文化？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Dave**: How do you drive that culture that represents the principles?

</details>

**查尔斯·科克**: 最初我们尝试过大规模培训（sheep dipping），把所有人叫来开研讨会。但根据迈克尔·波兰尼（Michael Polanyi）的《个人知识》，你需要**重构大脑**才能形成新习惯。就像健身一样，你需要长期高强度的训练。所以我们决定先找那些面临困难、真正感兴趣的团队，对他们进行辅导。当他们取得成功，其他业务部门就会产生**社会模仿**（social mimicry），主动要求学习。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: Because your brain gets habituated. Well your brain's part of your body. So you've got to do the same thing. So we said, "Okay, let's find a group that's really interested in this. And if they succeed, then the other businesses says, "Gosh, I'd like to do that."

</details>

**蔡斯·科克**: 文化的本质是：能否让组织里的每一个人**在没有被明确告知的情况下，都知道该做什么**？大多数公司是自上而下的，有一个标志性的聪明领袖制定策略。我们要把它反转过来，实现**基于原则的底层授权**，利用每个人的集体智慧。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Chase Koch**: The essence of principal-based management is what if you could have a business where everyone knew what to do without being told. It's about bottom-up empowerment with principles.

</details>

### 保持私人持有的优势

**戴夫**: 如果你不打算上市，如何在规模化的组织里让中层管理人员敢于冒险？他们通常倾向于最稳妥的路径以保住奖金。

<summary>Original English</summary>

<details>

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: 这确实是我们的竞争优势。曾有人建议我让公司上市，我说：“**除非我死了**。”如果上市，我们永远无法实现这种“基于能力而非行业限制”的扩张，因为分析师们理解不了。私人持有的身份让我们能够为了长期利益而忍受短期财务损失，并进行这种破坏性创新。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: The main threat we've had is we had people who wanted to take us public and I said it'd be over my dead body. We never would have been able to pull off this capability bounded versus industry bounded because people don't understand it.

**蔡斯·科克**: 关于人才，我们留在威奇托的一个原因是我们可以雇佣“板凳队员”——那些在农场长大、具备**贡献驱动心态**、工作极度努力的孩子，而不是那些带着优越感和权利心态的人。我们的现任 **CIO** 最初只是在我们的停车场画停车位线的，他没有大学学位，但他证明了自己的数据科学能力和贡献动力。我们更看重价值观和能力，而非学历。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Chase Koch**: One of the reasons why we stayed in Witchaw is that we can basically hire the farm team, kids that have grown up on the farm that have that contribution motivated mindset. Our CIO today, he start his first interaction with KO was striping lines in our parking lot. Guy with no college degree. Values first.

</details>

### 社会变革与教育愿景

**戴夫**: 能谈谈你们在社会变革方面的努力吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**查尔斯·科克**: 这始于“人类进步原则”。维克多·弗兰克尔（Viktor Frankl）曾有深刻见解：当今的问题是越来越多的人有了生存手段，却失去了**生存的意义**。如果找不到生命意义，人只有两种选择：追求权力和追求享乐。追求权力会导致成瘾和独裁，追求享乐会导致犯罪和自杀。解决方案是帮助人们找到自己的**天赋**，并以此帮助他人成功。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Charles Koch**: Viktor Frankl has this tremendous insight. He says the problem today is ever more people have the means to live and no meaning to live for. The solution is to help people find their gift and a way to apply it that enables them to succeed by helping others succeed.

</details>

**蔡斯·科克**: 这正是 **Stand Together** 的故事。我们希望将教育从“为考试而教”转变为“**个性化教育**”。疫情让大家看到了系统的弊端，现在 70% 到 80% 的家庭对教育转型持开放态度。我们支持像**萨尔·汗**（Sal Khan）和可汗学院（Khan Academy）这样的合作伙伴，以及通过 Veila 基金资助微型学校。未来的学习应该是基于项目的，应该授权孩子们使用 **AI 模型**，而不是禁止它们。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Chase Koch**: Our vision for education is to go from a teach-to-test model to one that's individualized education. We've helped create and seed over 5,000 schools. The reality is it's project-based learning. You empower them with these AI models.

</details>

### AI 与未来的遗产

**戴夫**: 你如何看待 AI 的未来？它是赋能个人，还是让财富更加集中？
