### 机场管理模式的变革探讨

加拿大各大机场多年来一直遵循相同的运营模式：由**非营利机构**（Non-profit authorities）进行运营管理，但土地及固定资产的所有权仍由联邦政府持有。作为“房东”，联邦政府每年从这些机场机构收取约5.25亿加元的租金。然而，当前的联邦政府正在考虑彻底改变这一方向，探讨包括**机场全面私有化**（Full privatization）在内的各种选项，这与澳大利亚、新西兰以及部分欧盟国家的运营模式类似。目前，这涉及加拿大境内约21至26个主要机场枢纽。

<details><summary>Original English Source</summary>
For decades, Canada's large airports have worked the same way. They're run by non-profit authorities. But the federal government owns the land and fixed assets. Now the Carney government is thinking of going in a new direction... federal government maintained ownership over the land that those airports operated on. And essentially what happens is they now operate as the landlord for those airports. So they collect rent from these airport authorities to the tune of about $525 million a year currently. And now the federal government is discussing fully privatizing those airports similar to other countries like Australia, New Zealand or some of the examples that we see in the European Union.
</details>

### 支持私有化的效率与竞争视角

从支持私有化的角度来看，**Fraser Institute** 的财政研究主任 Jake Fuss 认为，加拿大当前的航空系统运营模式导致了极高的税费，部分原因是机场机构需要弥补支付给联邦政府的巨额租金，例如 Pearson 机场某些航班的机场改进费高达40加元。私有化若配合更广泛的改革（如实施**开放天空协议** Open Skies Agreements、行业去管制及引入低成本航司等新竞争者），有望显著提升运营效率与服务质量。参考澳大利亚和新西兰的经验，尽管私有化后部分机场费用可能上涨，但通过引入竞争，客户最终支付的票价总额在长期内趋于下降，同时能够创造更多的 sector 雇佣机会。

<details><summary>Original English Source</summary>
So in Canada right now, ultimately, we have a system that has very high taxes and fees relative to other countries, in part because these airport authorities have to recoup the revenue that they're losing to the federal government... But what privatization will actually allow you to do is become more efficient, improve service quality. It could potentially lead to lower fees in the long run, and ultimately could also help lower airfares as part of a broader package of reforms. We've seen the examples in Australia, New Zealand and the European Union following those privatization reforms... they were ultimately able to bring down airfares for their customers... it's really a win win benefit.
</details>

### 对私有化问责与安全的深度担忧

**加拿大劳工大会**（Canadian Labour Congress）秘书长 Lily Chang 则强烈质疑私有化提议。她指出，私有化往往会导致问责性降低、可及性变差，并在安全层面产生隐患。在私有企业模式下，公司首要职责是满足股东与投资者的回报需求，这极可能促使其削减员工工资和福利以优化财务底线。她担忧决策过程将从目前的社区参与模式转变为在封闭的corporate boardrooms内进行，导致缺乏公共监督。此外，若为削减成本而牺牲人员资质或运营稳定性，将严重威胁航空安全与大众利益。

<details><summary>Original English Source</summary>
I'm absolutely not convinced. Why not? Because all the examples that I have just in my own backyard in Ontario, for example, tell me that privatization actually leads to less accountability, less accessibility, and worse in terms of safety... I think that it will bring in higher costs, because we will have private corporations that are accountable to their shareholders, to their investors, and they will demand a return on their money. I also think that there will be less public oversight, there will be less accountability. It will be decisions made behind closed doors in a closed corporate boardroom... So I don't think that that would work at all. And also in terms of the safety... are you going to be cutting corners to to make more money for the shareholders, does that going is that going to mean translation to people who are not qualified to do the job?
</details>

### 历史镜像：407高速公路的警示

在辩论中，安大略省的 **Highway 407** 常被视为公共资产私有化的反面教材。该高速由公共资金建成，旨在缓解401公路的拥堵，但省政府将其以30亿加元低价租赁给财团，且该资产目前的实际价值远高于当初的估值。这导致了严重的负面后果： toll费即刻大幅飙升，普通通勤者难以负担，从而未能实现 relief 交通拥堵的目标，反而使得401公路比以往更拥堵。Jake Fuss 承认应引以为戒，并强调私有化*过程*的重要性：必须确保有竞争性的招标过程，售予优秀的管理者，而非仅追求一次性的现金抓取（cash grab）。

<details><summary>Original English Source</summary>
Critics have cited Ontario's Highway 407 as evidence of a bad idea to privatize public assets... drivers have ended up paying billions of dollars so far in tolls. Should we see this as a cautionary tale? Absolutely. I mean, it's a real shame because it was public money that went into building that highway in the first place... the government took a one time cash grab and undervalued the actual worth of that 407 today... competitive bidding process, for instance, is something that the airports could do... to make sure that they're getting a good return. But they're also selling to people that are going to be good managers of those airports in the long run as well.
</details>

### 劳动保障与跨国资本的冲突

Lily Chang 进一步警示了私有化对就业与国家主权的影响。她指出，为了追求财务回报，企业极可能采取外包（contracting out）服务，从而影响解冰维护工人的技术与经验稳定性。她表达了对外国股东优先考虑全球利润而非国内健康与安全的深度担忧，并举了 **Connaught Laboratories** 的案例：该机构曾是国有疫苗实验室，私有化后被跨国制药公司并购，最终导致该国在 Covid-19 疫情期间无法自主制造疫苗。她主张应对企业进行合理的利润征税，利用公共资金进行投资，而非将关键资产交由外国公司控制。

<details><summary>Original English Source</summary>
I certainly believe that it could mean that there would be cuts to jobs. It cuts to the quality of the workers... for example, wages, benefits... I'm very concerned about that. It isn't just the shortage of staff... but also, you know, we believe in good quality jobs. The job that can you can do one job that can support you and your family to have a decent life in this country... Connaught Laboratories, which used to be a Crown owned, public owned vaccine laboratory. And what happened was when this was privatized, it eventually ended up being merged into a multinational pharmaceutical company... prioritized global profits instead of domestic health and safety... When we had Covid 19, we couldn't manufacture our own vaccines.
</details>

### 运营挑战与夏季航行保障

最后，NAV Canada 代表 Jonathan Bagg 就航空业面临的 staffing 限制进行了说明。他表示这不仅是加拿大的问题，而是全球性挑战。NAV Canada 正处于 relatively good position，并通过 record levels 的培训（目前有超过500人在受训）来确保新人增速超过 attrition。针对即将到来的繁忙夏季（包括 World Cup 等国际赛事），NAV Canada 采取了一系列创新举措，包括召回退休控制器（目前已有约50人回归）及 incentivizing 刚离职的操作人员暂时回归。他强调，尽管天气等因素可能导致不可控的延误，但每一项决策都以**安全至上**（safety first and foremost）为原则。尽管达到目标 staffed 水平仍需几年时间，但在 interim，通过科技手段和专业人员的技能，NAV Canada 致力于为旅客提供 smooth 的服务体验。

<details><summary>Original English Source</summary>
Staffing or resource constraints in the air traffic control field is a global issue. It's not unique to Canada. We're in a relatively good position... We're training at record levels today, more than 500 people in our training programs... bringing back retired air traffic controllers. We have about 50 in the workforce currently that have come back on contract. Also, as you mentioned, people who recently left an operational role voluntarily are being incentivized to come back for the summer period... every decision we make is based on safety first and foremost. So in a situation where we have to slow traffic to ensure that it's at a rate that we can effectively and safely manage, we will do so.
</details>
