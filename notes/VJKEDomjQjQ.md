### 公司转型与文化重塑

**Karin Rådström**: 我两年前开始担任 **Daimler Truck** 的 **CEO**。当时，我还没接到 **Morgan Stanley** 的电话，但也许我还在等。当我开始担任新 CEO 时，我做的第一件事，我认为是相当合理的，就是梳理战略。于是，我和我的管理委员会、战略部门以及一些提供建议的咨询公司合作，自上而下、自下而上地引入人才，最终制定出了我和整个董事会都认为非常强大的战略：五大支柱，非常清晰的愿景和使命，我们知道该做什么。

**Karin Rådström**: So, I uh started as the CEO of Daimler Truck two years ago. Um didn't get called actually uh by Morgan Stanley, but maybe I'm still waiting for that. So, I worked with my management board, of course, strategy department, uh some consultants who wanted to give us advice. um top down, bottom up, bringing in the talents and sort of coming out with um what I thought and the whole board thought was a really really strong strategy. **Karin Rådström**: 之后，我们第一次要向公司高层领导会议展示战略，参会的是公司 200 位高层领导。结果在会议前几天，出于好奇，我开始翻看我们过去在这些会议上都说过什么，前几任 CEO 都谈过什么。然后我意识到，当我们翻阅旧的幻灯片时，很多战略内容和我的前任们过去 15 到 20 年一直在谈论的内容大同小异。当然，有一些新元素，比如**数字化**、**AI**、**服务化**等等。但说实话，大部分内容真的非常相似。

<summary>Original English</summary>

Uh a lot of the strategy was kind of the same as what we had been talking about for or not me but my predecessors for like the last 15 uh 20 years. uh for sure there were new elements like digitalization, AI, servitization etc.

**Karin Rådström**: 那时我们就意识到，我们的公司其实并没有战略问题，而是执行问题。这非常棘手，因为正如您在介绍中所说，我们有 130 年历史，一直以卓越工程和创新领先。今天，我们是世界上最大的卡车和巴士公司，显然我们做得对很多事情才能走到今天。但这并不能保证未来的成功，哪怕是下个季度，更不用说未来 10 年、20 年甚至 130 年了。

But if you looked at the bulk of it really to be very honest a lot was quite similar and then we realized that we actually don't really have a strategy problem in our company. we have an execution problem and this is really tricky because as you mentioned in the introduction we're 130 years old uh engineering excellence always innovation leader um today we're the biggest truck and bus company in the world um so obviously we did a lot of things right to get to where we are today but that does not guarantee success not even the next quarter and definitely not for the next 10, 20 or even 130 years.

</details>

**Karin Rådström**: 除此之外，正如我们经常讨论的，世界变化太快了。宏观层面，**AI**、技术、**地缘政治**、全球力量平衡的转移等等都在发生。在我们的行业内部，技术也在经历转型。我们过去 100 多年一直在研发柴油卡车，现在我们关注**电气化**、**氢能源**。我们有**软件定义汽车**，还在大力投入**自动驾驶汽车**。出现了新的商业模式，人们不再想拥有资产。所以，我们的行业也在变化。

But besides that, in our industry, we also have the transformation of technology. Uh we've been developing diesel trucks for the last 100 plus years. Now we're looking at electrification, hydrogen. We have softwaredefined vehicles. Uh we also do a lot of work into self-driving vehicles. There's new business models. People don't want to own the assets anymore. So our industry is also changing.

</details>

**Karin Rådström**: 同时，**Daimler Truck** 本身也在变化。一方面，我们有 130 年历史；另一方面，我们实际上是四年前才从一个庞大的 **Daimler** 集团中分离出来的。所以，从很多方面来说，我们只是一个四岁的公司，正在学习如何成为一家上市公司，以及如何在这样的环境中运作。因此，在不责怪过去的前提下，我们需要更专注于如何推动未来的转型。

<summary>Original English</summary>

So in many ways, we're just a fouryear-old company learning what it's like to be to be a listed company um and and to function in that environment. </details>

**Karin Rådström**: 于是，我们提出了三个简单的词：更简单、更快速、更强大（simpler, faster, stronger）。这是一种我们现在试图融入公司一切事务的心态。这听起来非常简单。有些人也谈论过简化，但我们是德国公司，总部在德国南部，我们为我们的传统和历史感到自豪。但要建立我们强大的历史所依赖的卓越工程，我们必须注重细节。我认为德国的成功建立在强大的结构、清晰的决策和坚定的方向上。所以，这些东西并不容易改变。仅仅在墙上写上“更简单、更快速、更强大”，并期望每个人都能照做是不够的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

And then we came up with these um three kind of simple words simpler, faster, stronger. And that's a mindset that we now try to bring into everything we do in the company. And it's sounds very easy. Someone else also talked about doing things simpler, but I mean we are a German company. I would say we're a global company, but our headquarter is in South Germany and we're very proud of our heritage and what we come from. But for sure we had in order to build the engineering excellence that built our strong history, we needed to pay attention to detail. And I think German success is really built on strong structures, clear decisions, strong direction. So that's not so easy to change. It's not enough to put simpler, faster, stronger on the wall and expect everyone to run.

</details>

**Karin Rådström**: 但我认为它有一个非常积极的方面，那就是它给了公司里的任何人一个机会去质疑我们做事的方式。这可能也不是德国公司的历史优势——人们站出来问“我们为什么要这样做事？”。现在，我听到公司里很多人说：“是啊，有没有一个更简单、更快速的方法来做这件事？”这开启了一种与以往不同的对话，而不是仅仅被视为在抱怨老板。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

And that's maybe also not the historical strengths of German companies that people really stand up and ask why do we do it like that? **Karin Rådström**: 但我们想做得更具体一些。因此，我们开始更广泛地审视**文化**，以及什么是文化。我们在公司里将其定义为：文化是公司所有实践的总和。而实践可以包含几个不同的方面。首先，是价值观——我们墙上的文字，年报中的内容，以及我们声称代表的东西。但实践也包括公司里的所有流程，比如招聘、绩效考核、预算等正式流程。然后，还有所有非正式流程——公司里大家知道“我们这里总是这样做的”，这是公司一直以来的做法。我们开始审视所有这些实践，并发现了一种非常结构化的方法：我们每个月挑选三到四个要改变的实践，然后审视它，描述我们今天如何做，以及未来我们希望如何做。之后，我们分批推出新的实践，大约每隔一到两个月会有一批新的实践上线。我认为这是在一家非常老的公司中推动文化变革的非常具体的方式。有些改变听起来非常基础，但即使是小事也不容易。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Um but we wanted to do it even more let's say concrete. So we started looking more broadly on culture and what is culture and the way we defined it in our company is the culture is the sum of all the practices you have in the company. and practices can be a couple of different things. So, first of all, it's the values. It's kind of the the words on the wall and and what you put in your uh annual reports and and what you say that you stand for. But practices is also all the processes that you have in the company. The formal processes on how you recruit people, how you um pay for performance, budgeting, whatever. And then it's also all the informal processes um which is the way you do things in a company which is actually not a formal process but everyone just kind of knows that's how we always do it here and that's how it always has been done and we started looking at all these practices and actually we found a very structured way that we are picking three to four practices every month that we want to change and we look at it we describe how do we do it today and what's the desired state on how we want to do it in the future and then we're rolling out new practices in waves. So every one to two months we have a wave and there's three to four new practices coming.

</details>

**Karin Rådström**: 比如，现在我们每次会议开始时，都会审视在座的各位。你们为什么在这里？是为了给讨论贡献意见，还是想参与决策？你们只是来收集信息，还是因为这位演讲者在发言，而他是他老板的老板的老板，所以你必须在场以确保你了解情况？通过这样做，我们发现可以请走很多没有真正来贡献成果的人。

**Karin Rådström**: 另外，我刚加入 **Daimler** 时，觉得非常不可思议，几乎所有事情都要用 **PowerPoint**。每次会议我都会收到一份 50 页的 **PowerPoint**，需要提前阅读。我们并没有完全禁止 **PowerPoint**，但我们停止了默认使用 **PowerPoint**。我们发现这能带来完全不同质量的讨论，因为大家真正专注于问题本身和如何解决它，而不是有时花费大量时间描述背景。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Um also I got crazy when I joined Daimler. There's powerpoints for everything. So every meeting I get a PowerPoint. It's like 50 pages. I have to pre-eread. I come in. Um so that's we have not totally banned PowerPoints but we have stopped having powerpoints by default. Um and we see that it drives a completely different quality of discussion because you really focus on the problem and how to solve it instead of sometime spending a lot of time describing all the backgrounds.

</details>

**Karin Rådström**: 这些是一些小方面的改变。还有一些更大的改变，比如我们现在正试图取消预算流程。通常，我们现在就开始规划 2027 年的预算。你知道，它始于预测整体市场容量，宏观经济情况等等，然后自下而上地构建。这是一个庞大的、结构化的流程。现在，我们正在取消预算流程，直到 10 月、11 月才会开始，因为那时我们需要为董事会准备。这是一个非常有趣且根本性的改变，因为它是一项核心流程，有些人还很喜欢它——至少我不是。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Um so those are some small ones. Then there's some bigger ones. uh trying to kill the budget process right now. Um normally we would already now actually start to plan the budget for 2027. Um it's you know starts with what's the total market going to be what's macro etc. And then we build from bottom up. It's a huge process very structured. Um so now we're killing the budget process.

</details>

**Karin Rådström**: 所以，这也许只是关于如何改变文化的一个实际方法。我认为我们才刚刚开始这段旅程。当人们已经开始感到沮丧时，我告诉他们，这不是一个短期活动，而是我们需要真正投入和专注的事情。我认为在我整个 CEO 任期内，我都将致力于推动、推动、再推动，一步一步地前进。但正是这些，让身处这个快速转型、古老行业中变得如此有趣和令人兴奋。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

uh we will not start until October, November because then we have to prepare it for the board and that's a really interesting one um and it's very fundamental um in our company because it's it's one of the real core processes that that some people love. Um so yeah, not me. Um so maybe that's a little bit just the practical approach to to how to change culture and I think we're just getting started on this journey. Um when people already now start to get frustrated I tell them you know this is not a campaign this is really something we need to go out and focus on and I think I will do it for my whole CEO tenure to really push push push push and slowly move step by step by step by step. Um, but that's what makes it so fun and interesting to be in these old industries that are transforming very fast.

</details>
