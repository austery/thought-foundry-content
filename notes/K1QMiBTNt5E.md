### 巴菲特致股东信：童年回忆与奥马哈情缘

前不久，被誉为**奥马哈先知**（Oracle of Omaha: 著名投资人沃伦·巴菲特的昵称）的沃伦·巴菲特发布了他人生中最后一封致股东信，这位执掌**伯克希尔·哈撒韦公司**（Berkshire Hathaway: 巴菲特创建并长期领导的多元化控股公司）整整六十年的商业传奇，以这封信正式告别了半个多世纪的职业生涯。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A few days ago, Warren Buffett, known as the Oracle of Omaha, who has led Berkshire Hathaway for sixty years, released his final letter to shareholders. This letter marks a formal farewell to his half-century-long career, a solemn entrustment of the future of the business empire he built, and even his last words of wisdom to the entire business and investment world.</p>
</details>

这封信承载着一位历经无数商业周期、见过无数行业起伏的老者最真实的思考、最宝贵的经验，以及最坦诚的自我剖析。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It carries the most genuine thoughts, most valuable experiences, and most candid self-analysis of an elder who has navigated countless business cycles and witnessed innumerable industry ups and downs.</p>
</details>

在信中，巴菲特首先回忆了一段发生在1938年的童年经历。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">First, Buffett recalled a childhood experience from 1938.</p>
</details>

那时他年纪尚小，因肚子剧痛，家庭医生哈雷·霍茨（Harley Hotz）上门看诊。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">He was very young then, and due to severe stomach pain, his family doctor, Harley Hotz, came to visit.</p>
</details>

这位友善的天主教医生总是背着一个黑色的医药包，亲切地称巴菲特为“小船长”，且收费不高。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The friendly Catholic doctor always carried a black medical bag, affectionately called Buffett "Little Captain," and charged modestly.</p>
</details>

起初，哈雷·霍茨医生以为巴菲特的肚子疼只是小问题，说第二天早上就会好，但后来他总觉得巴菲特的症状有点古怪，放心不下，当天晚上便将巴菲特送往圣凯瑟琳医院，紧急进行了**阑尾切除手术**（appendectomy: 移除发炎阑尾的外科手术）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Initially, Dr. Harley Hotz thought Buffett's stomach ache was a minor issue, saying it would be better by morning. However, he later felt Buffett's symptoms were peculiar and, uneasy, sent Buffett to St. Catherine's Hospital that same evening for an emergency appendectomy.</p>
</details>

在医院的三周里，巴菲特感觉像置身于修道院，但修女们对他都很友好，他也逐渐喜欢上了那个环境。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">During his three weeks in the hospital, Buffett said he felt like he was in a convent, but the nuns were kind to him, and he gradually grew fond of the environment.</p>
</details>

更让他难忘的是，当时他三年级的老师马德森老师特意让班上30个同学每人给他写了一封信。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">What made it even more memorable was that his third-grade teacher, Miss Madsen, specifically had all 30 classmates write him a letter.</p>
</details>

巴菲特笑着说，他大概把男生们的信都扔了，女生们的信却反复读了很多遍，还调侃住院也是有好处的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Buffett chuckled, saying he probably threw away the boys' letters but reread the girls' letters many times, jokingly adding that being hospitalized had its benefits.</p>
</details>

康复期间最让他开心的是，他亲爱的艾迪姨妈送给他一套看起来非常专业的指纹采集工具。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">What delighted him most during his recovery was a gift from his dear Aunt Eddie: a set of professional-looking fingerprinting tools.</p>
</details>

拿到工具后，巴菲特立刻给照顾他的修女们都采集了指纹，他还调皮地说，自己可能是圣凯瑟琳医院里修女们见过的第一个新教徒孩子，修女们也不知道该对他有什么期待。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Upon receiving the tools, Buffett immediately took the fingerprints of the nuns who cared for him. He mischievously remarked that he was probably the first Protestant child the nuns at St. Catherine's Hospital had ever seen, and they didn't know what to expect from him.</p>
</details>

那时的巴菲特还产生了一个特别天真的幻想：他觉得总有一天会有修女犯了法，而**联邦调查局**（FBI: Federal Bureau of Investigation，美国的主要联邦执法机构）会发现他们居然没有修女的指纹。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At that time, Buffett also harbored a particularly naive fantasy: he imagined that one day a nun would commit a crime, and the FBI would discover they had no fingerprints for her.</p>
</details>

到时候**FBI**局长**埃德加·胡佛**（J. Edgar Hoover: 曾长期担任美国联邦调查局局长）就会亲自来到奥马哈，查看他收藏的这些珍贵指纹，甚至他还能和胡佛一起，找出并逮捕那个失足的修女，从此获得全国性的声誉。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Then, FBI Director J. Edgar Hoover himself would come to Omaha to examine his collection of precious fingerprints. He even imagined he could work with Hoover to find and arrest the errant nun, thereby gaining national renown.</p>
</details>

当然，这个幻想最终没有实现，但巴菲特后来才知道，其实当时他更应该给胡佛本人采集指纹，因为多年后，胡佛因滥用FBI职权而身败名裂。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Of course, this fantasy never materialized, but Buffett later learned that he should have actually taken Hoover's own fingerprints, as years later, Hoover's reputation was ruined due to his abuse of FBI authority.</p>
</details>

这段童年回忆特别有趣，也让我们看到了巴菲特小时候调皮又充满想象力的一面。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This childhood memory is particularly amusing and reveals Buffett's mischievous and imaginative side as a child.</p>
</details>

除了童年趣事，巴菲特还在信里梳理了他和几位奥马哈同乡的缘分，这些人后来都成了他生命中重要的伙伴。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Beyond childhood anecdotes, Buffett also recounted his connections with several fellow Omaha natives in the letter, who later became important partners in his life.</p>
</details>

首先是**查理·芒格**（Charlie Munger: 巴菲特的长期商业伙伴和好友），这位和巴菲特合作了64年的挚友，其实在20世纪30年代的时候，就住在离巴菲特的房子只有一个街区的地方。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">First, there's Charlie Munger, his close friend and partner for 64 years, who actually lived just one block from Buffett's house in the 1930s.</p>
</details>

巴菲特说，他早年差点就和芒格成为朋友。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Buffett said he almost became friends with Munger in their early years.</p>
</details>

1940年夏天，芒格在祖父的杂货店里打工，每天工作10个小时，只能挣2美元，而第二年，巴菲特也在那家店里做过类似的工作。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In the summer of 1940, Munger worked 10 hours a day in his grandfather's grocery store, earning only $2, and the following year, Buffett did similar work at the same store.</p>
</details>

但有意思的是，直到1959年，这两个人才真正见面。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Interestingly, it wasn't until 1959 that the two men actually met.</p>
</details>

当时芒格35岁，巴菲特28岁。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Munger was 35 at the time, and Buffett was 28.</p>
</details>

后来，芒格从哈佛法学院毕业，二战服役结束后就永久定居在了加利福尼亚，但是他一直把自己在奥马哈的早年岁月当成人生的重要阶段。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Later, Munger graduated from Harvard Law School and settled permanently in California after serving in World War II, but he always considered his early years in Omaha an important stage of his life.</p>
</details>

在巴菲特看来，芒格不仅是他的大哥，更是他最好的老师。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In Buffett's view, Munger was not only like an elder brother but also his best teacher.</p>
</details>

六十多年里，他们之间虽然有过分歧，但是从来没有过争执，而且芒格从来不会说“我早就跟你说过”这种事后诸葛亮的话。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Over sixty years, despite disagreements, they never had a quarrel, and Munger would never say "I told you so" in hindsight.</p>
</details>

这种相处模式也成了商界合作的一段佳话。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This mode of interaction has become a celebrated example of business partnership.</p>
</details>

还有**斯坦·利普西**（Stan Lipsey: 曾是《奥马哈太阳报》的出版商，后为伯克希尔哈撒韦旗下报纸高管）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There's also Stan Lipsey.</p>
</details>

1968年的时候，他把《奥马哈太阳报》卖给了伯克希尔。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In 1968, he sold The Omaha Sun to Berkshire.</p>
</details>

十年后，因为伯克希尔旗下一家子公司拥有的《布法罗晚报》，正和当地唯一的周日报纸出版商展开生死较量，而且当时伯克希尔这边正节节败退。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Ten years later, because The Buffalo Evening News, owned by a Berkshire subsidiary, was locked in a life-or-death struggle with the local sole Sunday newspaper publisher, and Berkshire was losing ground.</p>
</details>

巴菲特就请利普西搬到了布法罗，结果利普西不负所望，最终打造出了《布法罗晚报》的《星期日》版产品。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Buffett asked Lipsey to move to Buffalo, and Lipsey, living up to expectations, eventually created The Buffalo Evening News's Sunday edition.</p>
</details>

从那以后，这笔原本每年都在大量亏损的投资，每年税前的回报率都超过了100%，要知道在20世纪80年代初，这笔3300万美元的投资对伯克希尔来说可是一笔非常重要的资金。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">From then on, this investment, which had been losing a significant amount annually, yielded a pre-tax return of over 100% each year. It's worth noting that in the early 1980s, this $33 million investment was very significant for Berkshire.</p>
</details>

巴菲特还提到，利普西就住在他家附近大约五个街区的地方，而利普西的邻居正是**小沃尔特·斯科特**（Walter Scott Jr.: 曾任中美能源公司CEO，后担任伯克希尔董事），他在1999年把**中美能源公司**（MidAmerican Energy: 伯克希尔哈撒韦旗下的能源公司）带到了伯克希尔，后来还担任伯克希尔的董事，直到2021年去世，也是巴菲特的挚友。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Buffett also mentioned that Lipsey lived about five blocks from his home, and Lipsey's neighbor was Walter Scott Jr., who brought MidAmerican Energy to Berkshire in 1999 and later served on Berkshire's board until his death in 2021, also a close friend of Buffett's.</p>
</details>

另外一个不得不提的奥马哈同乡是**唐·基奥**（Don Keough: 曾任可口可乐公司总裁）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Another notable Omaha native is Don Keough.</p>
</details>

1959年的时候，唐·基奥和他的年轻家庭住在离巴菲特家街对面、距离芒格家旧居大约100码远的房子里。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In 1959, Don Keough and his young family lived across the street from Buffett's home, about 100 yards from Munger's old residence.</p>
</details>

当时的基奥还只是一名咖啡推销员，一年挣12000美元，却要和妻子米基一起抚养五个孩子，而且这些孩子都要上学费不菲的天主教学校。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At the time, Keough was just a coffee salesman, earning $12,000 a year, yet he and his wife Mickey had to raise five children, all of whom attended expensive Catholic schools.</p>
</details>

巴菲特说，他们两家很快就成了挚友。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Buffett said their two families quickly became close friends.</p>
</details>

基奥来自爱荷华州西北部的一个农场，毕业于奥马哈的**克雷顿大学**（Creighton University: 位于奥马哈的一所私立天主教大学），早年娶了奥马哈姑娘米基。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Keough came from a farm in northwestern Iowa, graduated from Creighton University in Omaha, and married an Omaha girl, Mickey, in his early years.</p>
</details>

后来加入**可口可乐公司**（Coca-Cola Company: 全球知名的饮料公司）后，很快就在全球声名远扬。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">After joining The Coca-Cola Company, he quickly gained global renown.</p>
</details>

1985年，基奥担任可口可乐公司总裁期间，公司推出了**新可口可乐**（New Coke: 可口可乐公司在1985年推出的一款配方改变的饮料），结果市场反应极差，成了一次命运多舛的尝试。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In 1985, while Keough was president of The Coca-Cola Company, the company launched New Coke, which was met with extremely poor market reaction, becoming a ill-fated attempt.</p>
</details>

面对这次危机，基奥做了一次非常著名的演讲，他公开向公众道歉，并且宣布重新推出**老可口可乐**（Old Coke: 指新可口可乐推出前原有的可口可乐配方）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Facing this crisis, Keough delivered a very famous speech, publicly apologizing to the public and announcing the reintroduction of Old Coke.</p>
</details>

他在演讲里还调侃说，寄给“至高无上的白痴”的信件，会迅速送到他的办公桌上。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In his speech, he even joked that letters addressed to "the supreme idiot" would quickly reach his desk.</p>
</details>

这次撤回演讲后来成了企业危机公关的经典案例，在**YouTube**（YouTube: 全球最大的视频分享平台）上还能看到视频。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This retraction speech later became a classic case study in corporate crisis management, and videos of it can still be seen on YouTube.</p>
</details>

而且演讲之后，可口可乐的销量大幅上升。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Moreover, after the speech, Coca-Cola's sales significantly increased.</p>
</details>

巴菲特还推荐大家去**CharlieRose网站**（CharlieRose website: 查理·罗斯主持的访谈节目官方网站）上看基奥的一次精彩访谈，他说基奥和芒格一样，始终是个地道的中西部男孩，热情、友善，骨子里透着十足的美国味儿。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Buffett also recommended watching an excellent interview with Keough on the CharlieRose website, saying that Keough, like Munger, was always a genuine Midwestern boy, warm, friendly, and thoroughly American at heart.</p>
</details>

更有意思的是，巴菲特还提到了两位后来加入伯克希尔的核心人物：出生于印度并在那里长大的**阿吉特·贾恩**（Ajit Jain: 伯克希尔哈撒韦保险业务副董事长）以及即将接任CEO的加拿大人**格雷格·阿贝尔**（Greg Abel: 伯克希尔哈撒韦非保险业务副董事长，巴菲特的指定继任者）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Even more interestingly, Buffett also mentioned two key figures who later joined Berkshire: Ajit Jain, born and raised in India, and Greg Abel, the Canadian designated to succeed as CEO.</p>
</details>

他们在20世纪末都曾经在奥马哈生活过数年，而且格雷格·阿贝尔在20世纪90年代的时候，就住在离巴菲特家仅几个街区远的**法纳姆街**（Farnam Street: 奥马哈市中心的一条主要街道）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Both lived in Omaha for several years in the late 20th century, and Greg Abel lived just a few blocks from Buffett's home on Farnam Street in the 1990s.</p>
</details>

只不过当时两人从未谋面。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">However, the two never met at the time.</p>
</details>

聊到这些的时候，巴菲特还幽默地问了一句：“难道奥马哈的水里有什么神奇的成分吗？”
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When discussing these connections, Buffett humorously asked, "Is there something magical in Omaha's water?"</p>
</details>

其实这句话背后，更多的是他对奥马哈这个家乡的热爱和感恩。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Behind this remark, there is more of his love and gratitude for his hometown, Omaha.</p>
</details>

虽然他年轻时也在华盛顿特区、纽约生活过，但是1956年，仅仅在纽约待了一年半之后，他还是选择回到了奥马哈，并且从此再也没有离开过。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Although he lived in Washington D.C. and New York in his youth, in 1956, after only a year and a half in New York, he chose to return to Omaha and has never left since.</p>
</details>

巴菲特说，他的三个孩子以及几个孙辈都在奥马哈长大，孩子们一直就读于公立学校，而且和他的父亲、第一任妻子苏西毕业于同一所高中。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Buffett said his three children and several grandchildren grew up in Omaha, attending public schools, and graduated from the same high school as his father and first wife, Susie.</p>
</details>

此外，对伯克希尔发展至关重要的几个人，比如查理·芒格、斯坦·利普西等人，都和奥马哈有着深厚的联系。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Furthermore, several individuals crucial to Berkshire's development, such as Charlie Munger and Stan Lipsey, all have deep connections to Omaha.</p>
</details>

巴菲特承认，美国有很多伟大的公司、学校、医疗机构，每个地方都有独特的优势和才华出众的人。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
