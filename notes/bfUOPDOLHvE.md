### 私募股权表现不佳的现状与原因

今年八月，特朗普总统签署了一项行政命令，允许**私募股权**（Private Equity, PE: 一种投资形式，通过收购非上市公司或上市公司私有化来获取股权）公司进入美国的**401(k)**（401(k): 美国雇主发起的退休金计划）系统。其宣称的目标是实现投资的民主化，即不再过度保护小型投资者，让他们能够与养老基金和主权财富基金经理在同一个领域竞争——但将退休储蓄交给像阿波罗（Apollo）这样的公司，似乎与“民主化”的说法格格不入。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This August, President Trump signed an executive order allowing private equity firms to tap into America’s 401(k) system. The stated goal was to democratize access — to stop babying smaller investors and to let them play in the same sandbox as pension funds and sovereign wealth fund managers - nothing says ‘democratization’ like handing your retirement savings to Apollo.</p>
</details>

那么，为什么今年公开上市的私募股权公司股价表现远逊于大盘呢？人们本应期待一次反弹。然而，**GLPE指数**（GLPE index: 全球上市私募股权指数）今年迄今已下跌近10%，甚至自八月初的公告以来也略有下降。阿波罗（Apollo）和蓝猫头鹰（Blue Owl）今年迄今跌幅超过20%，KKR、Ares和TPG都出现了两位数的跌幅。黑石（Blackstone）也下跌了，只有凯雷（Carlyle）和3i是例外，涨幅超过了**S&P**（S&P: 标准普尔公司，通常指其编制的指数）。事实证明，如果沙盒里装满了“坏掉的玩具”，让散户投资者进入也无济于事。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So why are the shares of publicly listed private equity firms underperforming the broader stock market so badly this year? You’d expect a rally. Instead, the GLPE index is down nearly 10% year to date – and it’s even down slightly since the announcement in early August. Apollo and Blue Owl are off more than 20% year to date - KKR, Ares, and TPG are all in double-digit decline. Blackstone is down too, with Carlyle and 3i being outliers, up more than the S&P. It turns out - that letting retail investors into the sandbox doesn’t help if the sandbox is full of broken toys.</p>
</details>

《华尔街日报》（The Wall Street Journal）指出，部分表现不佳可能反映了过去几年股价上涨的“悬而未决”效应——特别是对于一些在被纳入**S&P 500**（S&P 500: 标普500指数）之前股价大涨的大型公司。但该行业仍存在一些挥之不去的担忧。私募股权公司难以以有吸引力的价格出售之前的投资，而没有退出就意味着没有分配。没有分配，就更难募集新资本。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Wall Street Journal points out that some of the underperformance might reflect an overhang from the run-up in stock prices over the last few years— especially for some of the big firms who rallied ahead of being included in the S&P 500. But there are still concerns hanging over the industry. Private equity firms are struggling to sell prior investments at attractive prices - and without exits, there are no distributions. Without distributions, it’s much harder to raise new capital.</p>
</details>

私募股权仍坐拥数万亿美元的资产。但其“募集、部署、退出、分配、重复”的飞轮模式已不再像过去那样运转。而进军401(k)市场，与其说是创新，不如说是在现有投资者感到沮丧时，寻找新投资者的举动。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Private equity is still sitting on trillions in assets. But the flywheel — raise, deploy, exit, distribute, and repeat — isn’t spinning the way it used to. And the push into 401(k)s looks maybe less like innovation and more like a search for new investors when the existing ones are getting frustrated.</p>
</details>

### 私募股权的运作模式与市场错位

今年S&P 500指数上涨了约14%（如果包括股息），但这并非一次全面上涨。回报集中在少数几家科技巨头——“七巨头”（Magnificent Seven）减去特斯拉（Tesla）加上博通（Broadcom）——它们占据了指数三分之一以上的份额。这些公司盈利增长，现金流充裕，并且杠杆率低于互联网泡沫时期的前辈。投资者一直在为盈利能力买单，市场也一直在奖励他们。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The S&P 500 is up around 14% this year – if we include dividends, but it hasn’t been a broad-based rally. The returns have been concentrated in a handful of tech giants — the Magnificent Seven minus Tesla plus Broadcom — which make up more than a third of the index. These firms are growing earnings, throwing off cash, and doing it with less leverage than their predecessors in the dot-com era. Investors have been paying up for profitability, and the market has been rewarding them.</p>
</details>

私募股权不拥有这些公司，也不拥有任何类似的公司。私募股权公司倾向于拥有规模较小、通常处于增长较慢行业、且通常负债更多的企业。典型的**杠杆收购**（Leveraged Buyout, LBO: 一种收购方式，主要通过借贷资金完成）基金是对小型价值股的杠杆押注——这正是市场中一直滞后的领域。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Private equity doesn’t own these companies – and it doesn’t own anything like them. PE firms tend to own smaller businesses, often in slower-growing sectors, and usually with a lot more debt. The typical buyout fund is a leveraged bet on small-cap value stocks — the exact corner of the market that’s been lagging.</p>
</details>

期望私募股权回报随大盘波动（并根据额外杠杆进行调整）是合理的。但当市场由少数几只私募股权基金没有敞口的超大型科技股驱动时，这种关系就会失效。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It’s not unreasonable to expect private equity returns to rise and fall with the broader market, adjusted for the additional leverage. But that relationship breaks down when the market is being driven by a narrow set of megacap tech stocks that private equity funds have no exposure to.</p>
</details>

**IPO**（IPO: 首次公开募股）市场已经沉寂了相当长一段时间，而私募股权投资组合中的公司类型根本不是公开市场投资者所寻求的。投资者不愿为负债累累、增长缓慢的企业支付高价，这使得私募股权公司更难干净利落地退出。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The IPO market has been dead for quite a while, and the kinds of companies sitting in private equity portfolios simply aren’t what public investors are looking for. Investors are not interested in paying top dollar for slow-growth businesses with heavy debt loads — and that’s making it harder for PE firms to exit cleanly.</p>
</details>

将私募股权与S&P 500指数进行基准比较一直有些牵强。该行业主要投资美国公司，但它们规模较小、周期性更强、杠杆更高。更好的比较对象可能是一篮子小型价值股，并在此基础上叠加几倍的债务。但即便如此也算宽容了。大多数小型价值ETF不收取2%的管理费和20%的业绩提成。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Benchmarking private equity against the S&P 500 has always been a bit of a stretch. The sector invests mostly in U.S. companies, but they’re smaller, more cyclical, and more heavily levered. A better comparison might be a basket of small-cap value stocks with a few turns of debt layered on top. But even that’s generous. Most small-cap value ETFs don’t charge fees of 2 and 20.</p>
</details>

私募股权常被描述为一种资产类别，但它实际上只是一种结构。大部分资本通过杠杆收购基金部署，这些基金通过股权和债务的组合收购私人公司的控股权。债务承担了大部分繁重的工作。一笔典型的交易可能由60%到75%的杠杆融资，远高于上市公司中约30%的**资产负债率**（Debt-to-equity ratio）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Private equity is often described as an asset class, but it’s really just a structure. Most of the capital is deployed through buyout funds, which acquire controlling stakes in private companies using a mix of equity and debt. The debt does most of the heavy lifting. A typical deal might be financed with 60 to 75 percent leverage — far more than you’d find in a publicly listed company, where debt-to-equity ratios tend to hover around 30 percent.</p>
</details>

### 私募股权回报的真实来源：杠杆而非运营改进

私募股权背后的理念很简单：大量借款，收购一家企业，改善其运营（或者至少进行某种程度的有效管理），然后尝试以高于收购价的价格出售。回报应该来自运营改进、估值倍数扩张，以及——最可靠的——杠杆。挑选收购公司的团队通常拥有投资银行或咨询背景。这是否使他们有资格经营一家水暖用品公司，以及他们是否能比其创始人经营得更好，则是另一个问题。但是，嘿，如果你能在Excel中建模杠杆收购，那么经营厕所又能有多难呢？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The idea behind Private Equity is simple: borrow heavily, buy a business, improve it (or at least manage it somewhat efficiently), and then try to sell it later for more than you paid. The returns are supposed to come from operational improvements, multiple expansion, and — most reliably — leverage. The people picking the companies to buy - often have backgrounds in investment banking or consulting. Whether that qualifies them to run a plumbing supply company – and whether they can run it better than its founder did - is a separate question. But hey, if you can model a leveraged buyout in Excel, how hard can toilets be?</p>
</details>

私募股权基金倾向于将赌注集中在规模较小、通常处于成熟行业的公司——原因是稳定（即使无趣）的商业模式更容易获得贷款，没有人愿意向现金流为负甚至高度波动的企业贷款。这使他们接触到学术金融追踪的小盘股和价值股因素——历史上这些因素表现优异，但这种情况已经很久没有发生了。他们还声称提供**非流动性溢价**（Illiquidity Premium: 购买难以出售的资产所获得的额外回报）：即以折扣价购买难以出售的资产，并将投资者资本锁定多年，应该会带来更高的长期回报。这种溢价是否存在尚有争议。明确的是，费用高昂，杠杆巨大，退出路径狭窄。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Private equity funds tend to concentrate their bets in smaller companies, often in mature industries — the reason being that steady, if boring, business models are easier to borrow against – no one wants to lend money to a business with negative – or even highly variable cashflows. That gives them exposure to the small-cap and value factors tracked by academic finance – historically these factors outperformed – but that hasn’t happened for quite some time. They also claim to offer access to the illiquidity premium: the idea is that buying hard-to-sell assets at a discount, and locking up investor capital for years, should result in higher long-term returns. Whether that premium exists is debatable. What’s clear is that the fees are high, the leverage is substantial, and the exit routes are narrow.</p>
</details>

私募股权公司喜欢讲述一个故事。这是一个关于转型的故事——将沉睡、表现不佳的公司转变为精益、高效、高增长的机器。主人公是前投资银行家和顾问，他们手持电子表格和战略愿景，通过卓越的运营解锁隐藏价值。这就是**营运阿尔法**（Operational Alpha: 通过改善公司运营效率而非市场波动带来的超额收益）的神话。但数字却讲述了一个截然不同的故事。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Private equity firms like to tell a story. It’s a story about transformation — of taking sleepy, underperforming companies and turning them into lean, efficient, high-growth machines. The protagonists are former investment bankers and consultants who, armed with spreadsheets and strategic vision, unlock hidden value through operational excellence. This is the myth of operational alpha. But the numbers tell a somewhat different story.</p>
</details>

私募股权的大部分回报并非来自运营改进，而是来自杠杆。典型的杠杆收购由60%至75%的债务融资——远高于上市公司平均30%的水平。当一切顺利时，这种杠杆会放大回报。它也带来了脆弱性，但故事的这一部分通常被省略在推介材料中。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Most of the returns in private equity come not from operational improvements, but from leverage. A typical buyout is financed with 60–75% debt — significantly more than the 30% average for public companies. That leverage amplifies returns when things go well. It also introduces fragility, but that part of the story is usually left out of the pitch deck.</p>
</details>

私募股权经理在经营企业方面拥有独特技能的说法，很难与许多普通合伙人的简历相符。这些人通常拥有并购背景，而非制造业背景。他们知道如何构建交易，但对如何经营一家水暖用品公司却知之甚少。他们用一名初级银行家取代的创始人，可能已经花了30年时间建立起这家企业。新所有者有五年的退出期限，能够借到巨额资金，并且其模式假设利润率会扩张。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The idea that private equity managers are uniquely skilled at running businesses is hard to square with the resumes of many general partners. These are usually people with backgrounds in M&A, not manufacturing. They know how to structure a deal – but next to nothing about how to run a plumbing supply company. The founder they’re replacing with a junior banker - may have spent 30 years building the business in question. The new owners have a five-year exit horizon – the ability to borrow huge sums of money - and a model that assumes margin expansion.</p>
</details>

你可能会惊讶地发现，利润率扩张很少实现。贝恩（Bain）的《2025年全球私募股权报告》显示，在他们收购的软件企业中，过去十年利润增长对价值创造的贡献仅为6%。其余回报来自收入增长和估值倍数扩张。在剥离交易（私募股权公司从大型公司收购非核心部门或子公司）中，情况类似：2012年之前，发起人将利润率提高了29%。此后，这一数字已降至2%。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You may be surprised to hear - that margin expansion rarely materializes. Bain’s 2025 global private equity report shows that in the software businesses that they buy out — margin growth has contributed just 6% to value creation over the last decade. The rest of the return came from revenue growth and multiple expansion. In carve-outs — transactions where a private equity firm acquires a non-core division or subsidiary from a larger corporation — the story is similar: pre-2012, sponsors boosted margins by 29%. Since then, that figure has fallen to 2%.</p>
</details>

即使运营改进是真实的，它们也常常被夸大。正如Ludovic Phalippou等人指出的那样，该行业首选的业绩指标——自成立以来的**内部收益率**（Internal Rate of Return, IRR: 一种投资回报率指标）——对现金流的时机高度敏感，并且可以通过**认购额度**（Subscription Lines: 基金向银行借款以满足资本金催缴）和**过桥贷款**（Bridge Loans: 短期过渡性贷款）进行操纵。它在任何有意义的层面上都不是一个回报率。它是一个营销数字，或者是金融领域的“Instagram滤镜”——讨人喜欢，但往往与现实大相径庭。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Even when operational improvements are real, they’re often overstated. As Ludovic Phalippou and others have pointed out, the industry’s preferred performance metric — since-inception IRR — is highly sensitive to the timing of cash flows and can be gamed through subscription lines and bridge loans. It’s not a rate of return in any meaningful sense. It’s a marketing number or the financial equivalent of Instagram filters — flattering, but often nothing like reality.</p>
</details>

沃伦·巴菲特（Warren Buffett）长期以来一直因此批评私募股权模式。在伯克希尔·哈撒韦公司（Berkshire Hathaway）2019年的会议上，他指出私募股权基金的投资者必须持有备用现金，随时准备被调用。这些现金收益甚微（尤其是在当时的零利率环境下），但这并没有体现在IRR中。结果是一个讨人喜欢的回报数字，却忽略了真实世界的资本成本。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Warren Buffett has long criticized the private equity model for this reason. At Berkshire Hathaway’s 2019 meeting, he noted that investors in PE funds must hold cash on the sidelines, ready to be called. That cash earns very little (especially during the zero-interest rate environment of the time), but that doesn’t show up in the IRR. The result is a flattering returns number that ignores the real-world cost of capital.</p>
</details>

这并不是说私募股权公司从不改善他们收购的企业。有些确实如此。但该行业的总回报——尤其是在近年来——表明运营阿尔法更多是愿望而非现实。私募股权真正的引擎是杠杆。在一个利率更高的世界里，这个引擎开始熄火。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">None of this is to say that private equity firms never improve the businesses they buy. Some do. But the industry’s aggregate returns — especially in recent years — suggest that operational alpha is more aspiration than reality. The real engine of private equity is leverage. And in a world of higher interest rates, that engine is starting to sputter.</p>
</details>

### “波动性洗钱”：私募股权回报的平滑假象

私募股权长期以来一直将自己标榜为稳定性的来源。在一个公共市场剧烈波动的世界里，私募股权似乎提供了平稳、向上倾斜的回报。业绩图表上的线条令人安心地呈线性。但正如AQR的Cliff Asness所言，这并非稳定性，而只是“陈旧的定价”。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Private equity has long sold itself as a source of stability. In a world of whipsawing public markets, Private Equity appears to offer smooth, upward-sloping returns. The lines on the performance charts are reassuringly linear. But as Cliff Asness of AQR famously put it, this isn’t stability — it’s just stale pricing.</p>
</details>

私募股权回报的平滑化绝非缺陷。它是一个特点——而且在相当长一段时间内一直是主要的卖点。由于私人资产不按市价计价，其价格不会像公开上市公司股票那样随每日头条或季度收益而波动。相反，它们偶尔会被估价——通常由基金经理自己或他们聘请的第三方进行。这造成了低波动性的假象——我记得在信贷危机期间与一位私募股权基金经理交谈时，他声称尽管世界上所有资产的价值都崩溃了，但他投资组合中的公司却没有一家下跌。这些投资隐藏其真实波动性的事实，实际上对受偿付能力规则或惩罚回撤的业绩指标约束的机构投资者具有吸引力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The smoothing of returns in private equity is by no means a bug. It’s a feature — and, for quite some time has been a major selling point. Because private assets aren’t marked to market, their prices don’t fluctuate with daily headlines or quarterly earnings like publicly listed stocks do. Instead, they’re occasionally appraised - often by the fund managers themselves or by third parties hired by them. This creates the illusion of low volatility – I remember speaking to a Private Equity fund manager during the credit crunch – who claimed that while every asset in the world had collapsed in value – none of his portfolio companies had even ticked down. The fact that these investments conceal their true volatility is actually appealing to institutional investors bound by solvency rules or performance metrics that penalize drawdowns.</p>
</details>

然而，这种假象是有代价的。正如CFA协会（CFA Institute）网站上的一篇文章所指出的，该行业依赖自成立以来的**内部收益率**（IRR）作为业绩指标，具有严重的误导性。IRR在任何有意义的经济层面上都不是一个回报率——因为它不是任何私募股权投资者实际收到的回报。它更像是一个数学上的产物，对现金流的时机高度敏感，并且很容易通过**认购额度**、**过桥贷款**和延迟资本调用来操纵。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This illusion – however - comes at a cost. As an article on the CFA Institute’s website argues - the industry’s reliance on since-inception internal rate of return (IRR) as a performance metric is deeply misleading. IRR is not a rate of return in any meaningful economic sense – in that it is not a return that any private equity investor receives. It’s more of a mathematical artefact, highly sensitive to the timing of cash flows and easily manipulated through subscription lines, bridge loans, and delayed capital calls.</p>
</details>

考虑一种常见策略：基金识别一笔交易，但延迟向投资者调用资本，而是使用短期贷款为收购融资。这缩短了衡量持有时长并夸大了IRR，即使基础经济状况没有改变。基金看起来实现了25%的回报，而实际上投资者在考虑闲置现金和费用后的实际回报可能接近10%。投资者的资金一直闲置在债券中，等待资本调用，但基金没有调用，而是借款（以更高的利率），只是为了显示一个人为夸大的IRR。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Consider a common tactic: a fund identifies a deal but delays calling capital from investors, instead they use a short-term loan to finance the acquisition. This shortens the measured holding period and inflates the IRR, even though the underlying economics haven’t changed. The fund looks like it’s delivering a 25% return, when in reality the investor’s actual return — accounting for idle cash and fees — may be closer to 10%. The investors money has been sitting in bonds – waiting for the capital call – and instead of taking it – the fund borrows money (at a higher interest rate) just so that they can show an artificially inflated IRR.</p>
</details>

然后是**延续基金**（Continuation Funds: 私募股权公司将资产从一个基金出售给另一个由其管理的基金）的激增。这些工具允许私募股权公司将其一个基金的资产出售给其管理的另一个基金，并收取费用。这笔交易重置了时间，在第一个基金中产生了账面收益，制造了成功退出的假象。但资产并未以任何有意义的方式易手。这有点像以更高的价格将自己的房子卖给自己，然后宣称获利。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Then there’s the proliferation of continuation funds. These are vehicles that allow a PE firm to sell an asset from one of its funds to another fund it also manages – and charges fees on. The transaction resets the clock, generates a paper gain in the first fund, creating the appearance of a successful exit. But the asset hasn’t changed hands in any meaningful way. It’s a bit like selling your house to yourself at a higher price and declaring a profit.</p>
</details>

贝恩2025年报告指出，近30%的杠杆收购投资组合公司经历了某种形式的流动性事件——包括二级市场交易、**股息资本重组**（Dividend Recaps: 公司通过借贷向股权投资者支付股息）和**净资产值贷款**（NAV Loans: 以基金净资产值为抵押的贷款）——但没有真正的退出。这些金融操作产生了现金流和讨人喜欢的指标，但它们不一定反映真正的价值创造。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Bain 2025 report notes that nearly 30% of companies in buyout portfolios have undergone some form of liquidity event — including secondaries, dividend recaps, and NAV loans — without a true exit. These financial maneuvers generate cash flow and flattering metrics, but they don’t necessarily reflect real value creation.</p>
</details>

彭博社（Bloomberg）的这张图表显示，过去几年中，延续基金持有的企业以高于实际退出企业的估值进行转移。投资者开始注意到这一点。**已缴资本分配额**（Distributions to Paid-in Capital, DPI: 衡量投资者实际收回现金的指标）已成为许多有限合伙人首选的更实际的现金回报衡量指标。根据该指标，近年来的基金表现平平。2019年推出的基金刚刚达到盈亏平衡，有些可能永远无法实现盈利。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This chart from Bloomberg shows that over the last few years – the businesses held on to in continuation funds are transferred at higher valuations than the ones actually exited from. Investors are starting to notice. Distributions to Paid-in Capital (DPI) — a more grounded measure of actual cash returned — has become the metric of choice for many limited partners. And by that measure, the recent vintages are underwhelming. Funds launched in 2019 are only just breaking even and some may never get there.</p>
</details>

回报的平滑化可能有助于投资者安然入睡。但它也掩盖了风险，隐藏了表现不佳，并使得区分技能和运气变得更加困难。最终，AQR的Cliff Asness将这种技术称为**波动性洗钱**（Volatility Laundering: 通过不频繁估值来掩盖资产真实波动性的做法），它就是如此：洗钱。最终，它不是真正的稳定性——它是披着复杂外衣的不透明。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The smoothing of returns may help investors sleep at night. But it also obscures risk, conceals underperformance, and makes it harder to distinguish skill from luck. In the end, volatility laundering as this technique has been named by Cliff Asness at AQR - is just that: laundering. In the end, it’s not true stability - it’s opacity dressed up as sophistication.</p>
</details>

### 流动性危机与估值扭曲

私募股权平稳回报的承诺现在正与严峻的现实发生冲突：该行业正在努力将账面收益转化为实际现金。未售出公司的积压已超过3.6万亿美元，涉及近3万家投资组合公司。分配速度已降至极慢。贝恩2025年报告显示，分配占净资产的百分比已降至11%，为十多年来的最低水平。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Private equity’s promise of smooth returns is now colliding with a hard reality: the industry is struggling to convert paper gains into actual cash. The backlog of unsold companies now exceeds $3.6 trillion across nearly 30,000 portfolio firms. Distributions have slowed to a crawl. Bain’s 2025 report shows that distributions as a percentage of net asset value have fallen to 11% — the lowest rate in over a decade.</p>
</details>

IPO市场处于昏迷状态。战略买家持谨慎态度。发起人之间的交易——曾经可靠的退出途径——现在受到怀疑。延续基金、二级市场和NAV贷款已介入提供流动性，但它们更多是会计操作而非真正的退出。流动性挑战也正在扭曲估值。当资产从未真正出售时，你不得不开始怀疑它们的账面价值。二级市场折扣——通常为15%或更多——揭示了投资者真正认为这些资产的价值。这还不包括费用、杠杆和货币时间价值。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The IPO market is comatose. Strategic buyers are cautious. Sponsor-to-sponsor deals — once a reliable exit route — are now viewed with suspicion. Continuation funds, secondaries, and NAV loans have stepped in to provide liquidity, but they’re more accounting maneuvers than genuine exits. Liquidity challenges are also distorting valuations. When assets are never actually sold – you have to start wondering about their carrying value. Secondary market discounts — often 15% or more — reveal what investors really think these assets are worth. And that’s before accounting for fees, leverage, and the time value of money.</p>
</details>

我几周前报道的First Brands的倒闭——一个越来越大的故事——说明了该系统的脆弱性。First Brands大量借款，多次抵押同一抵押品，并依赖不透明的应收账款融资结构。当它失败时，23亿美元的资产凭空消失了。债权人仍在努力追查这笔钱。与格林希尔（Greensill）的相似之处显而易见，且令人不安。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The collapse of First Brands- which I covered a few weeks ago – and which is becoming a bigger and bigger story - illustrates the fragility of the system. First Brands borrowed heavily, pledged the same collateral multiple times, and relied on opaque invoice financing structures. When it failed, $2.3 billion dollars in assets simply vanished. Creditors are still trying to trace the money. The parallels to Greensill are obvious — and troubling.</p>
</details>

**私募信贷**（Private Credit: 非银行机构向企业提供的直接贷款），直到现在一直被宣传为银行贷款的纪律性替代方案，现在看起来更像是一个盲点。那些本应提供灵活性的定制合同和双边关系反而掩盖了问题。投资的养老基金和保险公司现在发现自己面临着他们几乎不理解的风险。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Private credit, which up until now has been marketed as a disciplined alternative to bank lending, now looks more like a blind spot. The bespoke contracts and bilateral relationships that were supposed to offer flexibility instead just concealed problems. Pension funds and insurers who invested - now find themselves exposed to risks they barely understand.</p>
</details>

流动性紧缩也是一场心理上的清算。投资者正在提出尖锐的问题：这些资产到底值多少钱？我什么时候能拿回我的钱？为什么基金在无法向外部投资者出售任何东西的情况下，却一直报告收益？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The liquidity crunch is also a psychological reckoning. Investors are asking hard questions: What are these assets really worth? When will I get my money back? Why does the fund keep reporting gains when they can’t seem to sell anything to outside investors?</p>
</details>

在这些问题得到更好的答案之前，该行业声称的稳定性声誉将继续面临压力。回报在纸面上可能仍然看起来平稳，但实现它们的路径却绝非如此。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Until those questions have better answers, the industry’s reputation for stability will remain under pressure. The returns may still look smooth on paper — but the path to realizing them is anything but.</p>
</details>

### 私募股权的社会影响与反垄断担忧

八月，唐纳德·特朗普（Donald Trump）签署了一项行政命令，可能会重塑数百万美国人的退休格局。该命令允许401(k)计划管理人将私募股权和其他另类资产（如加密货币）纳入**固定缴款退休计划**（Defined Contribution Retirement Plans: 雇主和雇员定期向个人账户缴款的退休计划）。此举受到阿波罗（Apollo）、贝莱德（BlackRock）、凯雷（Carlyle）和其他多年来一直游说进入不断增长的退休金市场的公司的欢迎。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In August, Donald Trump signed an executive order that could reshape the retirement landscape for millions of Americans. The order allows 401(k) plan administrators to include private equity and other alternative assets – like cryptocurrencies - in defined contribution retirement plans. The move was cheered by Apollo, BlackRock, Carlyle, and other firms that have spent years lobbying for access to the growing retirement market.</p>
</details>

他们的说辞是，这些资产提供了多元化、潜在更高的回报以及机构级投资的途径。但风险却很少被广泛宣传。私募股权基金伴随着高额费用、长期锁定期和有限的透明度。它们不每日定价，也不容易退出。对于散户投资者来说，这与购买指数基金是截然不同的主张。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The pitch is that these assets offer diversification, potentially higher returns, and access to institutional-grade investments. But the risks are less widely advertised. Private equity funds come with high fees, long lockups, and limited transparency. They’re not priced daily, and they’re not easy to exit. For retail investors, that’s a very different proposition than buying an index fund.</p>
</details>

该命令背后的游说工作非常激烈。根据财务披露，阿波罗（Apollo）和凯雷（Carlyle）大力推动监管改革。黑石（Blackstone）通过行业协会开展工作。阿波罗的马克·罗文（Marc Rowan）认为，退休金系统过度暴露于超大型科技股——他说：“我们基本上将国家的退休金系统杠杆化到了英伟达（Nvidia）身上。”在他看来，解决方案是向私人市场开放大门。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The lobbying effort behind the order was intense. According to financial disclosures, Apollo and Carlyle pushed hard for regulatory changes. Blackstone worked through trade associations. Marc Rowan of Apollo argued that the retirement system was overexposed to megacap tech stocks — “we’ve basically leveraged the retirement system of the country to Nvidia,” he said. The solution, in his view, was to open the gates to private markets.</p>
</details>

一些公司走得更远。据报道，固定缴款另类投资协会（Defined Contribution Alternatives Association）声称，401(k)计划可能因不提供私募股权投资而面临诉讼。他们的逻辑是——如果私募股权提供更高的回报，那么将其排除在外可能被视为违反**信托责任**（Fiduciary Duty: 法律或道德上要求一方为另一方利益行事的责任）。这个论点在法律上可能站不住脚，但它反映了该行业的信心——或者绝望——这由您来判断。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Some firms went further. The Defined Contribution Alternatives Association reportedly argued that 401(k) plans could be sued for not offering access to private equity. Their logic being - if PE delivers higher returns, then excluding it could be seen as a breach of fiduciary duty. That argument may be legally tenuous, but it reflects either the industry’s confidence — or desperation – I’ll let you be the judge of that.</p>
</details>

贝恩2025年报告指出，零售资本现在有望推动该行业未来的大部分增长。**半流动性产品**（Semiliquid Products: 介于流动和非流动之间的投资产品）、**目标日期基金**（Target-Date Funds: 根据投资者退休日期自动调整资产配置的基金）和模型投资组合正在被设计用于混合公共和私人资产，大型私募股权公司都已宣布与退休金计划提供商建立合作关系。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Bain 2025 report notes that retail capital is now expected to drive much of the industry’s future growth. Semiliquid products, target-date funds, and model portfolios are being designed to mix public and private assets and the big private equity firms have all announced partnerships with retirement plan providers.</p>
</details>

我不确定美国投资者会在多大程度上相信这种说辞——多年来大多数投资者学到的一件事是，高费用投资会降低回报。投资者支付的平均费用率逐年下降，因为投资者拒绝为表现不佳支付高价。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I’m not sure to what extent American investors will fall for this pitch – the one thing most investors have learned over the years is that high fee investments reduce returns. Average expense ratios paid by investors has been falling year after year – as investors refuse to pay up for underperformance.</p>
</details>

该行业进军401(k)市场，与其说是民主化，不如说是在寻找新资本。当现有投资者感到沮丧时，就寻找新的投资者。当金融界人士批评私募股权时，他们通常关注费用和业绩。但在金融界之外，对该行业对社会的影响也有很多批评。当所有权遥远、激励措施不一致、目标是财富攫取而非增长和客户关注时，会发生什么？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The industries push into 401(k)s looks less like democratization and more like a search for new capital. When the existing investors are frustrated, find new ones. When people from the world of finance criticize private equity, they often focus on fees and performance. But outside the world of finance there is plenty of criticism over the industry’s impact on society. What happens when ownership is distant, incentives are misaligned, and the goal is wealth extraction rather than growth and customer focus.</p>
</details>

道德批判集中在金融工程的社会后果上。国会对私募股权拥有的牙科连锁店的调查引发了关于护理质量和为追求利润而向患者推销不必要治疗的问题。批评者认为，追求**EBITDA**（EBITDA: 息税折旧摊销前利润）增长可能导致反社会后果——从人手不足的护理院到被掏空的零售商。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The moral critique centers on the social consequences of financial engineering. Congressional investigations into private equity-owned dental chains have raised questions about care quality and unnecessary procedures being pushed on patients in the pursuit of profits. Critics argue that the pursuit of EBITDA growth can lead to anti-social outcomes — from understaffed care homes to hollowed-out retailers.</p>
</details>

围绕私募股权的“整合策略”（roll-up strategies）也存在**反垄断**（Antitrust: 防止垄断、促进竞争的法律和政策）担忧，即私募股权公司在分散的行业中收购数十家小型企业——如医生诊所、私立学校、餐馆或学生公寓——并将它们整合到**平台公司**（Platform Companies: 私募股权公司收购并整合多个小企业后形成的大型公司）中。结果可能是竞争减少和消费者成本增加。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There is also antitrust concern around private equity roll-up strategies, where PE firms buy dozens of small businesses in fragmented industries — like doctors’ offices, private schools, restaurants, or student housing — and consolidate them into platform companies. The result can be reduced competition and higher costs for consumers.</p>
</details>

### 高利率环境下的私募股权困境

私募股权在廉价债务的世界中蓬勃发展。在其现代历史的大部分时间里，该行业在结构性利率下降的环境中运作。这种顺风现在已经消失了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Private equity thrived in a world of cheap debt. For most of its modern history, the industry operated in a structurally falling interest rate environment. That tailwind is now gone.</p>
</details>

商业模式的一个重要组成部分依赖于杠杆。杠杆收购公司通常用60%至75%的债务为收购融资。当利率接近于零时，这既便宜又有效。但随着过去两年利率上升，债务成本飙升。突然之间，这种计算就不那么奏效了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A huge component of the business model relies on leverage. Buyout firms typically finance acquisitions with 60–75% debt. When rates were near zero, that was cheap and effective. But as interest rates have gone up over the last two years, the cost of debt soared. Suddenly, the math doesn’t work that well.</p>
</details>

更高的利率意味着更高的融资成本、更低的估值和更少的退出。卖家想要昨天的价格，而买家却负担不起。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Higher rates mean higher financing costs, lower valuations, and fewer exits. Sellers want yesterday’s prices - while buyers can’t afford them.</p>
</details>

许多有私募股权支持的公司现在背负着六到八倍于EBITDA的债务——这通常与垃圾债券相关联。当利率上升时，破产风险也随之上升，私募股权模式开始显得更加脆弱。
