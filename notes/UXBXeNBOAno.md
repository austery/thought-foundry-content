### 企业文化：超越创始人的核心竞争力

**大卫·鲁本斯坦**: 我很高兴来到奥斯陆。我来这里并不是为了游说争取诺贝尔和平奖，如果有人想颁发一个给我，我很乐意接受。但如果**阿尔弗雷德·诺贝尔**（Mr. Nobel）今天还活着，我有机会见到他，我会对他说，设立和平奖是一个很好的主意，但为什么不同时也设立一个企业文化奖呢？因为伟大的公司及其创造的优秀文化，正如伟大的和平缔造者一样，同样能够改变世界。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: I'm happy to be in Oslo. I didn't come here to lobby for a Nobel Peace Prize. So, if anybody wants to give me one, I'd be happy to take it. But why don't you also have a peace prize or a prize for corporate culture? Because companies that are great and create good cultures can change the world just the way great peacemakers can.

### 从体育英雄到企业领袖

**大卫·鲁本斯坦**: 当我在马里兰州巴尔的摩长大时，我无法诚实地说我的榜样是一位伟大的企业领袖。我专注于棒球或体育运动，我那个时代的男孩子们都想成为体育英雄。而今天，十几岁的年轻人可能不像以前那么渴望成为体育英雄，他们更想成为企业领袖和企业家，这种文化已经改变了世界。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: Now, when I was a little boy in Baltimore, Maryland, growing up, I can't honestly say that my role model was a great corporate leader. I was focused on baseball or sports or other things. And little boys in my era wanted to grow up to be sports heroes. Today, younger people in their teenage years probably don't want to be sports heroes as much as they want to be corporate leaders and entrepreneurs. And that has changed the culture of the world.

</details>

### 持续经营的关键：不仅仅是产品

**大卫·鲁本斯坦**: 想想这个数据：每年大约有 5000 到 6000 万家新公司诞生。其中美国约有 600 到 700 万家，欧洲约 800 万家，亚洲及其他地区约 2000 到 2500 万家。一年后，这些公司中约有一半会倒闭；再过两年，又有一半会消失；再过一年，约有一半会再次消失。究竟有哪些公司能存活 5 年、10 年甚至 15 年呢？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: Think about this. In any given year, there'll be roughly 50 to 60 million new companies started. 50 to 60 million new companies started around the world. About 6 to 7 million a year in the United States. About 8 million a year or so in Europe and maybe 20 to 25 million in Asia and other parts of the world as well. So 50 some million new companies started. After one year about half of those won't be around and after another two years another half won't be around and after another year about half more won't be around. What companies actually get to be there for 5 years or 10 years or 15 years?

</details>

**大卫·鲁本斯坦**: 这些公司通常拥有富有魅力的领袖。起初，公司可能由一、二甚至三位富有魅力的领袖创立，他们对想要做的事情有着清晰的愿景。他们有自己想卖给人们的产品或服务，并说服人们他们的生活会因为这个产品或服务而变得更好。他们不像赚钱那样专注于让人们买下他们的愿景，他们愿意努力工作以达到目的，并确保他们的产品和服务得到世界的公平对待。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: Well, they're companies that have charismatic leaders. Typically, typically it starts, a company might start with one or two or even three charismatic leaders that have a vision of what they want to do. Uh second, they have a product or a service that they're interested in selling to people and convincing people their lives will be better with that product or service. And they are not as focused on making money as they are on getting people to buy into their vision that this product or this service will make people's lives better. They also have a willingness to try to work hard to get to their end and their willingness to make certain that their product and their service will get a fair hearing from around the world.

</details>

### 苹果公司的启示：文化超越个人

**大卫·鲁本斯坦**: 但是，能够存活 5 年、10 年、15 年甚至 20 年的公司，不仅仅拥有富有魅力的领袖和优质的产品或服务，他们拥有不可思议的**企业文化**。正是文化使得公司能够从一代传承到下一代。正如我们所知，伟大的公司在创始人离世后能够存活的非常少。但**Steve Jobs**就是一个完美的例子。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: But the companies that survive for five years and 10 years and 15 years and 20 years are companies that have more than a charismatic leader and a good product or good service. They have an incredible culture. And it's culture that enables companies to go from one generation to the next generation. As we all know, very rarely do you see a great company survive the founder's demise. It happens. And Steve Jobs is a perfect example of it happening.

</details>

**大卫·鲁本斯坦**: **Steve Jobs**在 20 世纪 70 年代末创立了**Apple**，他实际上被踢出了自己的公司，离开了约 14 年才被请回来。后来，他通过**Apple**开发的一系列产品和服务改变了世界。当他去世时，许多人认为没有创始人，公司将无法存活。但正是因为拥有不可思议的文化，公司戏剧性地增长了。Steve 去世时，公司市值约 3500 亿美元，今天大约是 3.5 万亿美元，增长了约 10 倍。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: Steve Jobs started the company in the late 1970s. Um, and he was actually thrown out of the company. Thrown out of his own company and he was out of the company for about 14 years before he was brought back. And then he changed the world by a number of products and services that Apple developed. And then when he passed away, he was leaving behind a company many people thought couldn't survive without the founder. But because it had an incredible culture, the company dramatically grew. When Steve died, the market capitalization of the company was about $350 billion. Today, it's about three and a half trillion. So, it's up about 10 times.

</details>

**大卫·鲁本斯坦**: 公司在**Tim Cook**的领导下如何达到如此不可思议的高度？这是因为**Steve Jobs**建立的文化被**Tim Cook**扩展了，最终这种文化渗透到了公司的整个员工群体和整个客户群体中。

<details>

**David Rubenstein**: How did the company go under Tim Cook's leadership to such an incredible place in the world? Well, it's because the culture that Steve Jobs had built was expanded by Tim Cook and ultimately that culture permeated the entire employee base and entire customer base of the company.

</details>

### 如何定义优秀的文化

**大卫·鲁本斯坦**: 什么是优秀的文化？它是确保客户至上，确保员工得到公平对待，确保每个人都有成功的机会并被倾听，以及确保生产出人们真正想要且对社会真正有用的产品或服务。塑造这样的文化就像拥有自己的 DNA，父母给你的 DNA 在某种程度上塑造了你，你在某种程度上可以改善父母所做的事情。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: And what is a good culture? It's a good culture that a company has and enables it to survive not more than five or 10 or 15 years but 20 or 25 years or beyond is it's a concern about making certain that the customer comes first. Making certain that the employees are treated fairly. Making certain that everybody has a chance to succeed and has been listened to and make certain that you're producing a product or a service that really people want and really does something useful for society. And producing a culture like that is like your own DNA. Everybody here has the DNA from their parents. And your DNA is really what shapes you to some extent. To some extent you can improve upon what your parents did.

</details>

**大卫·鲁本斯坦**: 存活 25 年、30 年、40 年及以上的公司，是那些人们总是想要模仿并将其作为榜样的公司，因为它们拥有令人钦佩的文化。它们优先考虑客户、员工和供应商。确保你关心别人胜过关心自己，是企业文化中最重要的事情。在企业文化中，最能存活并真正繁荣的公司，不是首席执行官赚最多钱的公司，也不是关心自己赚多少钱的公司。关心别人才是让公司存活并做得如此出色的原因。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: The companies that survive for 25 years and 30 years and 40 years and beyond are the companies that people will always emulate and want to have as role models because they're the ones that have a culture that people admire. They're the ones that put customers first. They're the ones that put employees first. They're the ones that put the people that provide services to the company, the suppliers first. Making certain that you worry about other people more than yourself is the most important thing in corporate culture. And in corporate culture, the companies that survive the most and that really prosper are not the ones where the CEO is making the most amount of money or is worried about the amount of money he or she is making. You worry about worrying about other people is what makes companies survive and do so well.

</details>
