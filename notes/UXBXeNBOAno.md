### 企业文化与诺贝尔奖

**David Rubenstein**: 我很高兴来到奥斯陆。我来这里并不是为了游说争取**诺贝尔和平奖**的。所以，如果有人想给我颁一个，我很乐意接受。但是，如果诺贝尔先生今天还活着，我有机会见到他，我会对他说：“设立和平奖是个好主意，但为什么不同时也设立一个‘企业文化奖’呢？”因为伟大的公司和优秀的文化，和伟大的和平缔造者一样，同样能够改变世界。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: I'm happy to be in Oslo. I didn't come here to lobby for a Nobel Peace Prize. So, if anybody wants to give me one, I'd be happy to take it. But if Mr. Nobel were still alive today and I had a chance to meet with him, I would say to him, "Well, if you're going to have a peace prize, that's a good idea. But why don't you also have a peace prize or a prize for corporate culture?" Because companies that are great and create good cultures can change the world just the way great peacemakers can.

</details>

### 产品与文化的力量

**David Rubenstein**: 想想看，刚才提到的 **iPhone**。它可能是历史上最成功的消费产品之一，并且像所有智能手机一样，以我认为造福我们所有人的方式改变了世界。因此，当一家伟大的公司生产出伟大的产品或提供伟大的服务时，它对世界的帮助，就像伟大的和平事业一样。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: Think about it. The iPhone that was referenced earlier was maybe one of the most successful consumer products ever. And it has changed the world, as have all smartphones as well, in ways that I think benefit all of us. And so, when you have a great company that produces a great product or great service, it can help the world just the way great peacemaking can help the world.

</details>

### 初创企业的挑战与存续

**David Rubenstein**: 当我在马里兰州巴尔的摩长大时，我无法诚实地说我的榜样是伟大的企业领袖。那时候我们专注于棒球、运动或其他东西，我们那个时代的小男孩想长大成为体育英雄。而今天，青少年可能不像渴望成为体育英雄那样，他们更渴望成为企业领袖和企业家。这改变了世界文化。

想想看，每年大约有 5000 万到 6000 万家新公司成立。其中美国约有 600 万到 700 万家，欧洲约有 800 万家，亚洲及世界其他地区约有 2000 万到 2500 万家。然而，一年后，大约有一半的公司会消失；再过两年，剩下的一半又会消失；再过一年，又会消失一半。哪些公司能真正存在 5 年、10 年或 15 年呢？

通常，它们始于一到两名甚至三名具有**魅力型领导力**的创始人，他们对未来有清晰的愿景。其次，他们有志于销售人们需要的产品或服务，他们并不那么专注于赚钱，而是专注于让人们认同他们的愿景，即该产品或服务会让人们的生活变得更好。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: Now, when I was a little boy in Baltimore, Maryland, growing up, I can't honestly say that my role model was a great corporate leader. I was focused on baseball or sports or other things. And little boys in my era wanted to grow up to be sports heroes. Today, younger people in their teenage years probably don't want to be sports heroes as much as they want to be corporate leaders and entrepreneurs. And that has changed the culture of the world.

Think about this. In any given year, there will be roughly 50 to 60 million new companies started around the world. About 6 to 7 million a year in the United States, about 8 million a year or so in Europe, and maybe 20 to 25 million in Asia and other parts of the world as well. After one year, about half of those won't be around; and after another two years, another half won't be around; and after another year, about half more won't be around. What companies actually get to be there for 5 years, or 10 years, or 15 years?

Well, they're companies that have charismatic leaders. Typically, a company might start with one or two or even three charismatic leaders that have a vision of what they want to do. Second, they have a product or a service that they're interested in selling to people and convincing people their lives will be better with that product or service. And they are not as focused on making money as they are on getting people to buy into their vision that this product or this service will make people's lives better.

</details>

### 文化的代际传承：以 Apple 为例

**David Rubenstein**: 但那些存活了 5 年、10 年、15 年甚至 20 年以上的公司，不仅仅有魅力型的领导者和好产品。它们拥有**不可思议的文化**。正是文化使公司能够从一代人传到下一代人。正如我们所知，伟大的公司极少能在创始人去世后依然存活。这种情况确实发生过，**Steve Jobs** 就是完美的例子。

他创立了 **Apple**，后来被赶出了自己的公司，离开了大约 14 年，之后被请了回来。他通过开发的一系列产品和服务改变了世界。当他去世时，许多人认为没有创始人，公司无法存活。但因为拥有令人难以置信的文化，公司戏剧性地成长了。当 Steve 去世时，公司的市值约为 3500 亿美元。今天，大约是 3.5 万亿美元，翻了 10 倍。在 **Tim Cook** 的领导下，公司是如何达到如此惊人的地位的呢？那是因为 Steve Jobs 建立的文化被 Tim Cook 扩展了，最终这种文化渗透到了整个员工群体和客户群体中。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: But the companies that survive for five years, and 10 years, and 15 years, and 20 years are companies that have more than a charismatic leader and a good product or good service. They have an incredible culture. And it's culture that enables companies to go from one generation to the next generation. As we all know, very rarely do you see a great company survive the founder's demise. It happens, and Steve Jobs is a perfect example of it happening.

Steve Jobs started the company in the late 1970s. And he was actually thrown out of the company, thrown out of his own company, and he was out of the company for about 14 years before he was brought back. And then he changed the world by a number of products and services that Apple developed. And then when he passed away, he was leaving behind a company many people thought couldn't survive without the founder. But because it had an incredible culture, the company dramatically grew. When Steve died, the market capitalization of the company was about $350 billion. Today, it's about three and a half trillion, so it's up about 10 times. How did the company go under Tim Cook's leadership to such an incredible place in the world? Well, it's because the culture that Steve Jobs had built was expanded by Tim Cook, and ultimately that culture permeated the entire employee base and entire customer base of the company.

</details>

### 优秀企业文化的定义

**David Rubenstein**: 什么是好的文化？能够使公司存活 20 年、25 年甚至更久的好文化，是对**确保客户至上**的关注。确保员工受到公平对待，确保每个人都有机会成功并被倾听，确保你生产的产品或服务真正是人们想要的，并且对社会真正有用的。

创造这种文化就像你自己的 **DNA**。父母给你的 DNA 塑造了你。在某种程度上，你可以改进父母给你的东西，而伟大的公司可以通过使用创始人留下的 DNA 并在其基础上更进一步。当你观察企业文化时，必须看创始人创造了什么，以及这种文化在创始人离开后是否还能延续？

把他人放在自己之前，是企业文化中最重要的事情。那些真正繁荣的公司，不是 CEO 赚最多钱或担心自己赚多少钱的公司。担心他人，正是使公司存活并如此成功的原因。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: So, what is a good culture? It's a good culture that a company has and enables it to survive not more than five or 10 or 15 years, but 20 or 25 years or beyond. It's a concern about making certain that the customer comes first. Making certain that the employees are treated fairly. Making certain that everybody has a chance to succeed and has been listened to, and making certain that you're producing a product or a service that really people want and really does something useful for society. And producing a culture like that is like your own DNA. Everybody here has the DNA from their parents. And your DNA is really what shapes you to some extent. To some extent, you can improve upon what your parents did. But many companies can improve upon what the founders gave them by using the DNA that the founders gave them, and they can even go beyond that. And so, when you're looking at corporate culture, what you have to do is look at what the founders created. But can that founder's culture go beyond when the founders leave the scene? And those are the great companies.

Making certain that you worry about other people more than yourself is the most important thing in corporate culture. And in corporate culture, the companies that survive the most and that really prosper are not the ones where the CEO is making the most amount of money or is worried about the amount of money he or she is making. You worry about worrying about other people is what makes companies survive and do so well.

</details>

### 总结

**David Rubenstein**: 归根结底，我们都希望将其作为榜样的公司，我们都想构建或投资的公司，都是拥有企业文化的公司：将**客户置于首位**，确保员工得到良好的对待，并对社会有愿景。对赚钱的关注通常不是公司起步时繁荣的原因。

当然，你需要一种真正驱动每个人、让大家团结一致的文化。当我投资公司时，我寻找的是那种人们想来工作、喜欢在那里工作、认为自己正在为社会做有益事情的文化。赚钱是衡量成功的不错指标，但不是公司意义的主要衡量标准。如果你能拥有那种能超越创始人、最终让每个人都认为他们正在生产对世界更有益的产品或服务的文化，这就是基业长青。非常感谢。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**David Rubenstein**: So, as we look at corporate culture and we talk about it today, just think about this. In the end, the companies that we all want to have as role models, the companies that we all would like to build or invest in, the companies we all think that we'd like to have our children build as well if they start their own companies, would be ones that have a corporate culture that puts the customer first, that makes sure the employees are treated well, that has a vision of what they want to do for society to make the company succeed and make the world a slightly better place. The concern about making money is not generally the thing that makes a company prosper at the beginning.

Certainly, you need to have a culture that really drives everybody and having everybody pull together. And that's what, when I'm investing in companies, what I'm looking for is companies that have a culture where people want to come to work. They like working there. They think they're doing something useful for society. And making money is a nice measure of success, but it's not the main measure of what a company's all about. And so, as you think about corporate culture and through this conference today, just think about whether these companies that you're going to hear about are companies that have a culture that you want to have as you want to invest with. They're companies you'd like to work at, you'd be proud to have your children work at, and the kind of companies you wish you had started because those are the kind of companies I think that we all want to have in our portfolio and all the kind of companies we want to have as companies that are trying to make the world a better place. Companies have a good and everlasting corporate culture that really will survive the founders and that will ultimately make everybody think they're producing a product or service that makes the world a better place. Thank you very much.
