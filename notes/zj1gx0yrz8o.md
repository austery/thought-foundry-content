<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Dina Halles**: My name is Dina Halles. I'm from Jordan. I've been a brain trainer for some time and I've seen brain training change so many lives. I've seen kids struggle. Parents cry in front of me just worried about their kids, not sure what to do with them.

</details>

**Dina Halles**: 没有人认为你天生就是运动员，也没有人认为你一开始就能完美地运动。但不知为何，一谈到数学，要么你擅长，要么你不擅长。没有人真正考虑过训练这种技能。所以我想确保把我的想法传达给尽可能多的人：我们是可以采取行动的，我们的**大脑实际上是可以改变的**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nelly Attar**: My name is Nali and I'm from Lebanon. The big idea I want to share with the world is only by taking risks can you find out how far you can go. This idea is so important to me because it's my dad's favorite quote and my dad's no longer here today.

</details>

**Nelly Attar**: 攀登 **K2** 是我最难忘的时刻之一。站在那个顶峰，为阿拉伯地区创造了历史，作为一名女性在男性主导的运动中创造了历史。如果我不一次又一次地冒险，我今天就不会在这里。每个人都试图回避不适，但我认为我生命中最美好的事情都发生在不适之中。一旦你看到恐惧的另一面有伟大的事情，你就再也不想回头了。**Move** 不仅仅是一个舞蹈工作室，它是沙特阿拉伯需要的一场**全国性运动**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nelly Attar**: Climbing K2 was one of my favorite moments. Standing on top of that summit, making history for the Arab region, making history as a woman in a male dominant sport. I wouldn't be here today if I didn't take risks over and over and over and over again. Everyone tries to shy away from discomfort, but I think this is where the best things in my life have happened. Once you see that there's great things on the other side of fear, you never want to go back. Move is not just a dance studio. It was a national movement that Saudi needed.

### 韧性与现实的悖论

**Reema Das**: 这种悖论不仅仅是噪音。它们是一面镜子，折射出塑造我们世界的深层不平等。每一条头条新闻都在残酷地提醒我们，一个家庭回归的退伍英雄，是全球南方另一个地方平民的悲剧。我叫 **Reema Das**。我认为我们地区的每个人都在经历同样的罪恶感——当邻居死于饥饿且我们无能为力时，我们却能在企业界享受美食并雄心勃勃。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Reema Das**: These paradoxes are not just noise. They're a mirror into the deep inequality shaping our world. Every headline news a brutal reminder of that one family returning veteran hero is another civilian tragedy somewhere in the global south. My name is Rema Das. I think everybody in our region is going through the same this guilt of being able to have a meal and think big in the corporate world when really the next door people are dying of hunger and there's nothing we can do about it.

</details>

**Reema Das**: 这种日常生活的悖论正在吞噬我们。当你开始**失去共情**的那一刻，那便成为了我们面临的最大危险。那是自古以来所有不公正的根源。我只想让人们挖掘头条新闻背后的深意，通过你对世界的影响来重新定义成功。当我在企业环境中讲授韧性时，脑海中浮现的第一个画面是精疲力竭。没关系，韧性会帮你重回正轨。**心理安全感**在职场中至关重要，要创造一个让人们感到安全、敢于发声并被看见的环境。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Reema Das**: That paradox of our daily lives is eating us up. The minute you start losing empathy, that becomes the biggest danger that we have. And that is the root cause of all injustices from the beginning of time. I just want people to dig deep beyond the headlines and redefine success to the impact you've done to the world. When I teach resiliency in corporate settings, this is the first image that comes to my mind. Feeling burnt out. It's okay. Resiliency will help you get back on track. Psychological safety in the workplace. Promoting an environment where people feel safe to speak up and be seen.

</details>

### 心理健康的文化根源

**Tala Alud**: 我们使用的关于心理健康的词汇不只是描述现实，它们还在塑造现实。当你使用尊严之词时，你给了人们发声的机会，让他们感到自己被听见了。我叫 **Tala Alud**，来自约旦安曼。我的核心观点是，**心理健康并不是一个外来概念**，也不是西方概念。照顾我们的心理健康深深植根于我们自己的**文化、信仰和诗歌**中。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tala Alud**: Words that we use about mental health don't describe reality, they shape it. When you use words of dignity, you give people voices and you make them feel they are heard. My name is Tala Alud and I'm from Alman, Jordan. My main idea here is that mental health is not really a foreign idea. It's not a western idea. Taking care of our mental well-being and mental health is rooted in our own culture, our faith, and our own poetry.

</details>

**Tala Alud**: 我在心理健康领域工作了 20 多年，目睹了该领域在约旦的发展。我希望人们开始讨论心理健康。我觉得心理健康必须登上这样的大舞台，尤其是在我们这个地区。这是一种真正的通用语言。我曾参加过许多讨论大胆想法、强力声音的会议，但当你提到心理健康时，人们的声音会变小，眼神会低落，羞愧感随之而来。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tala Alud**: I've personally worked in mental health for over 20 years and I've seen the impact of mental health as a field growing in Jordan. I'd like to have people start their discussions and start talking about mental health and I really felt like mental health has to take stage on a big platform like this especially in our region and our area. It's really a universal language. I'd sit in meetings where the people discuss bold ideas, strong voices. But when you mention mental health, voice drops, eyes dropped, and the shame sweeps in.

</details>

Only those who risk going far can find out how far they can go.

**Dina Halles**: 我们生活在 AI 的世界。口袋里的小设备可以在几秒钟内获取任何信息。但我们从未被教导如何使用这些信息，如何运用创意、分析、记忆和专注。我想分享一个学生的故事。他叫 Sami，当时只有 9 岁，有很多学习障碍。他第一次见到我时说：“我很笨，我做不到。”你能想象这有多令人心碎吗？我们每天训练，到最后，那句“我很笨”变成了“**我是个天才**”。请确保像对待肌肉一样对待你的大脑。

<details>

We trained every day and towards the end that "I'm stupid" turned into "I'm a genius." Make sure that you treat your brain like a muscle.
