### 领导者面临的挑战与日常勇气的定义

想象一下，你正在做出一个商业决策：也许是招聘，也许是预算，又或者是就某个社会问题发表看法。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Imagine making a business decision: maybe hiring, maybe budgeting, or maybe speaking up on a social issue.</p>
</details>

这些选择中的任何一个都可能面临公众强烈反对，直接影响你的声誉，甚至动摇投资信心。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Any one of those choices could face public backlash, directly impacting your reputation or even shaking investment confidence.</p>
</details>

这就是当今领导者所面临的现实。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That's the reality for leaders today.</p>
</details>

经济冲击、政治不确定性和两极分化创造了一个环境，即使是日常决策也可能带来真正的风险。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Economic shocks, political uncertainty, and polarization have created an environment where even routine decisions can carry real risk.</p>
</details>

在当今的气候下，专业知识可以帮助你制定战略。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In today's climate, expertise can help you map a strategy.</p>
</details>

但当前路不明朗、风险涉及个人、失败公之于众，而你的团队又指望你提供指导时，**勇气**（Courage: 在面对不确定性、风险或恐惧时，仍能坚持价值观并采取行动的能力）才是能助你渡过难关的关键。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But when the road ahead is unclear, the stakes are personal, the fall is public, and your people are looking to you for guidance, courage is what will carry you through.</p>
</details>

需要明确的是，我所说的并非那种一次性的英勇行为。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">To be clear, I'm not talking about bravery, that one-off heroic act.</p>
</details>

勇气有所不同；它就像一块肌肉，你需要通过践行自己的价值观来锻炼它，即使存在疑虑、风险或恐惧。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Courage is different; it's like a muscle you build by acting on your values, even when there's doubt, risk, or fear.</p>
</details>

我称之为“**日常勇气**”（Everyday Courage: 在不引人注目的日常小事中展现出的勇气，它能塑造组织文化、团队信任和领导者传承）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I call this "Everyday Courage."</p>
</details>

它在那些不会成为头条新闻，但却能塑造你的组织文化、团队信任和你的传承的小时刻中，展现出最强大的力量。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It's most powerful in the small moments that won't make headlines but still shape your organization's culture, your team's trust, and your legacy.</p>
</details>

### 六种日常勇气的类型及培养方法

基于研究和我与资深领导者的合作经验，我总结出了六种日常勇气。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Drawing on research and my work with senior leaders, I've identified six types of everyday courage.</p>
</details>

**1. 道德勇气**：选择原则而非利润。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">**Moral courage** involves choosing principles over profit.</p>
</details>

通过尽早明确你的价值观，并在危机来临之前坚定地承诺这些价值观来培养它。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Build it by clarifying your values early and committing to them before a crisis puts them to the test.</p>
</details>

**2. 发声勇气**：在他人保持沉默时挺身而出。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">**Speaking up courage** means speaking up when others stay silent.</p>
</details>

在那些沉默感觉更安全的会议中培养它，通过点明风险、清晰表达你的观点，并奖励团队中的不同意见。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Build it in meetings where silence feels safer by naming the risk, voicing your perspective clearly, and rewarding dissent on your team.</p>
</details>

**3. 情感勇气**：与艰难情绪保持同在，无论是你自己的还是团队的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">**Emotional courage** involves staying present with hard feelings, both your own and your team's.</p>
</details>

在挑战期间培养它，通过说出你的情绪并以诚实领导，而不是回避它们。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Build it during challenges by naming your emotions and leading with honesty, instead of brushing past them.</p>
</details>

**4. 智力勇气**：质疑自己的假设。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">**Intellectual courage** means questioning your own assumptions.</p>
</details>

通过定期邀请他人挑战你的思维，并在被证明错误时庆祝来培养它。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Build it by regularly inviting others to challenge your thinking and celebrating when you are proven wrong.</p>
</details>

**5. 创造性勇气**：提出大胆且未经检验的想法。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">**Creative courage** involves putting bold and untested ideas on the table.</p>
</details>

通过降低风险、进行小型实验，并在此过程中将“聪明的失败”常态化来培养它。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Build it by lowering the stakes, running smaller experiments, and normalizing smart failures along the way.</p>
</details>

**6. 身体勇气**：在艰难或有风险的条件下出现。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">**Physical courage** means showing up in tough or risky conditions.</p>
</details>

通过练习临在感来培养它，例如与一线团队进行实时交流。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Build it by practicing presence, such as spending real-time with front-line teams.</p>
</details>

这些并非戏剧性、引人注目的行为；它们是日常的选择。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These aren't dramatic, headline-grabbing acts; they're everyday choices.</p>
</details>
