### 绳结理论：从鞋带到宇宙的奥秘

我们大多数人系鞋带的方式都是错的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Most of us tie our shoe laces wrong.</p>
</details>
系鞋带的绳结有两种打法：一种是逆时针绕圈，另一种是顺时针绕圈。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There are two ways to tie a knot in your shoe laces. In one, you go counterclockwise around the loop, and in the other, you go clockwise.</p>
</details>
这两种方法看起来几乎一模一样，但其中一种绳结远优于另一种，它不容易松开或散开。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These two methods look almost identical, but one of these knots is far superior to the other. It doesn't loosen or come untied nearly as easily.</p>
</details>
要理解其中原因，我们需要深入研究**绳结理论**（Knot Theory: 数学的一个分支，旨在识别、分类和理解所有可能存在的绳结）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">To understand why, we need to delve into knot theory. This is a whole branch of mathematics that aims to identify, categorize and understand every possible knot that could ever exist.</p>
</details>
到目前为止，我们已经发现了前352,152,252个绳结，每个都有其独特的性质和特征。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So far, we have discovered the first 352,152,252 knots. Each one has its own particular properties and characteristics.</p>
</details>
我认为存在一个像绳结的“元素周期表”这样的东西非常引人入胜，但这不仅仅是纯粹的数学。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I think it's fascinating that there's something like a periodic table for knots out there, but it's not pure math.</p>
</details>
绳结理论已被证明具有非凡的实用性。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Knot theory has turned out to be remarkably useful.</p>
</details>
它是蛋白质和DNA结构的核心，正在催生可能比**凯夫拉**（Kevlar: 一种高强度合成纤维）更坚固的新材料，甚至被用于开发拯救数百万人生命的药物。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It is core to the structure of proteins and DNA. It's leading to new materials that could be stronger than Kevlar. It's even used to develop medicines that save millions of lives.</p>
</details>
所有这些都源于试图理解这个不起眼的绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">All of this just from trying to understand the humble knot.</p>
</details>

### 绳结的数学定义与等价性问题

那么，什么是绳结呢？
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So what is a knot?</p>
</details>
在日常生活中，我们看到的绳结是这样的或那样的，但如果你想严谨地研究绳结，你需要能够将它们拉开，以便真正看清发生了什么。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Well, in our everyday lives, we see knots like this or this, but if you are trying to rigorously study knots, you want to be able to pull them apart so you can really see what's going on.</p>
</details>
问题在于，像这样的绳结仅靠张力和摩擦力维系，所以如果你拉得太用力，它们就会散开。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The problem is knots like this are held together only by tension and friction. So if you pull on them too hard, they fall apart.</p>
</details>
为了捕捉绳索上的绳结，数学家们想出了一个主意：将两端连接起来。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So in order to capture the knot on the rope, mathematicians got the idea to connect the two ends.</p>
</details>
这样，你就可以将绳结分开来研究它，但它永远不会从根本上改变。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And now, well, you can tease the knot apart to study it, but it will never fundamentally change.</p>
</details>
所以在绳结理论中，所有绳结都存在于闭合的环上。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So in knot theory, all knots exist on closed loops.</p>
</details>
这意味着你能拥有的最简单的绳结就是一个像这样的圆圈。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This means the simplest knot you can have is just a circle like this.</p>
</details>
诚然，这算不上什么绳结，所以它被称为**非结**（Unknot: 最简单的绳结，可以不打断绳索而解开成一个简单的圆圈）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now, admittedly, this is not much of a knot, which is why it is called an unknot.</p>
</details>
这是另一个绳结，同样由一根绳子形成一个闭合的环。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Here is another knot. Again, it's made of a single piece of rope that forms a closed loop.</p>
</details>
这是再一个。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Here is another.</p>
</details>
只有当你无法在不打断环的情况下将一个绳结变成另一个绳结时，它们才被认为是不同的绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Two knots are only different if you can't make one into the other without breaking the loop.</p>
</details>
这是非结之后最简单的绳结，它被称为**三叶结**（Trefoil: 最简单的非平凡绳结，有三个交叉点）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This is the simplest knot after the unknot. It is called the trefoil, and you can see that there's no way for me to turn this back into a circle unless I actually break it open, take out the knot and then close it up again.</p>
</details>
现在我有两个非结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now I have two unknots.</p>
</details>
用肉眼分辨两个绳结是否不同是出奇地困难。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It is surprisingly hard to tell two knots apart by eye.</p>
</details>
这是一个简单的神秘绳结，它是一个非结、三叶结还是两者都不是？
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Here is a simple mystery knot. Is it an unknot, a trefoil or neither?</p>
</details>
我给你一秒钟时间思考。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I'll give you a second to figure it out.</p>
</details>
实际上它是一个三叶结，只要我把它解开一点并重新排列，你就能看出来。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It is in fact a trefoil, which you can see if I just untwist this and rearrange the knot a little bit.</p>
</details>
而我们第一个复杂的绳结，实际上只是一个非结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And our first complicated knot, well, it is actually just the unknot.</p>
</details>
我将尝试解开它，这样你就能看到。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I'm going to try to disentangle it so you can see that.</p>
</details>
看，它只是一个简单的环。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There you go. It was just a single loop.</p>
</details>
事实上，这些都是非结，问题就从这里开始。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In fact, these are all unknots, and this is where the problem begins.</p>
</details>
你不能随便缠绕一些绳子然后连接两端就称之为一个新的数学绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You can't just randomly tangle some rope and connect the ends.</p>
</details>
要制造一个新的数学绳结，你需要证明它不仅仅是另一个绳结的缠绕版本。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">To make a new mathematical knot, you need to prove it's not just a tangled up version of another knot.</p>
</details>
那么，你如何区分两个绳结呢？
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So how do you tell two knots apart?</p>
</details>
这个问题，也被称为**绳结等价问题**（Knot Equivalence Problem: 判断两个绳结是否在拓扑上等价的问题），因其著名的难度推动了整个绳结理论领域发展了150多年。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This one question, also known as the knot equivalence problem, is so famously difficult that it's propelled the entire field of knot theory for over 150 years.</p>
</details>
**艾伦·图灵**（Alan Turing: 英国数学家、逻辑学家和计算机科学家）甚至在他最后的出版物中写道：“目前还没有已知的方法可以系统地判断两个绳结是否相同。关于绳结的判定问题很可能是不可解的。本文的结果为我们纯粹通过推理所能达到的成就设定了某些界限。”
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Alan Turing even wrote in his final publication, "No systematic method is yet known by which one can tell whether two knots are the same. A decision problem which might well be unsolvable is the one concerning knots. The results in this article set certain bounds to what we can hope to achieve purely by reasoning."</p>
</details>

### 绳结理论的早期历史与原子漩涡模型

此前，历史上最著名的绳结问题是**戈尔迪之结**（Gordian Knot: 古代传说中一个极其复杂的绳结）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Previously, the most famous knot problem in history was the Gordian knot.</p>
</details>
据说，谁能解开这团巨大的绳结，谁就注定要统治整个亚洲。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It was said that whoever untangled this massive knotted rope was destined to rule all of Asia.</p>
</details>
传说中，**亚历山大大帝**（Alexander the Great: 古希腊马其顿国王）只是走上前去，一剑将其劈开。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Legend goes, Alexander the Great simply came along and sliced right through it.</p>
</details>
这在绳结理论中可不是一个有效的解决方案。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That would not be a valid solution in knot theory.</p>
</details>
历史上还有其他著名的绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And there are other famous knots of history.</p>
</details>
**无尽结**（Endless Knot: 一种象征永恒和相互关联的绳结图案）早在印度河流域的泥板上就已出现，并被用于中世纪凯尔特设计、中国绳结艺术以及印度教和佛教中。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The endless knot is seen as far back as clay tablets from the Indus Valley, and it was used in Medieval Celtic designs, Chinese knot work and Hinduism and Buddhism.</p>
</details>
在印加文明中，绳结被系在称为**奇普**（Quipu: 印加帝国用于记录信息的一种结绳系统）的绳索上，用于追踪从税收到来年日历的一切事物。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In Incan civilization, knots were tied on chords called quipu to track everything from taxes to calendars.</p>
</details>
你甚至可以在**博罗梅奥家族**（House of Borromeo: 一个自14世纪以来存在的意大利贵族家族）的纹章中找到一个绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You can even find a knot in the coat of arms for the House of Borromeo, an Italian noble family that has existed since the 1300s.</p>
</details>
**博罗梅奥环**（Borromean Rings: 三个相互连接但两两分离的环）在技术上是一个**链环**（Link: 多个闭合绳环的集合），它只是一个由多条绳索环组成的绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Borromeon rings are technically a link, which is just a knot with multiple loops of rope.</p>
</details>
最基本的链环是**非链环**（Unlink: 多个相互不连接的闭合绳环），即两个实际上没有连接的环，很像非结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The most basic link is the unlink, two loops which aren't actually connected, much like the unknot.</p>
</details>
之后是**霍普夫链环**（Hopf Link: 最简单的非平凡链环），再之后是博罗梅奥环等等。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">After that is the Hopf link. Then later, the Borromeon rings and more.</p>
</details>
但绳结等价问题直到几个世纪后才被提出。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But the knot equivalence problem was only encountered centuries later.</p>
</details>
1867年1月，苏格兰物理学家**彼得·格思里·泰特**（Peter Guthrie Tait）向著名科学家**威廉·汤姆森**（William Thomson），即后来的**开尔文勋爵**（Lord Kelvin），展示了他自制的烟雾机。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In January of 1867, Scottish physicist Peter Guthrie Tait showed off his homemade smoke machine to renowned scientist William Thomson, later Lord Kelvin.</p>
</details>
泰特读过一篇论文，其中提到漩涡环在理想流体中应该是永恒稳定的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Tait had read a paper that said a vortex ring should be eternally stable in an ideal fluid.</p>
</details>
于是他产生了兴趣，设置了两个木箱，里面装着氨水、硫酸和盐的混合物。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So intrigued, he set up two wooden boxes containing a kind of toxic mixture of ammonia, sulfuric acid and salt.</p>
</details>
当他轻敲每个箱子背面绷紧的毛巾时，化学烟雾从圆形切口中以完美的环状喷出。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When he tapped a towel stretched across the back of each box, the chemical smoke popped out of a circular cutout in perfect rings.</p>
</details>
开尔文看着这些环，被深深吸引了。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Kelvin, watching, was transfixed by the rings.</p>
</details>
他一直在思考原子组成这个当时的基本问题，突然间他看到了答案。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">He had been pondering the composition of atoms, a fundamental question of the time, and suddenly he saw an answer.</p>
</details>
他宣称原子一定是由**以太**（Ether: 19世纪物理学中假想的无处不在的介质）的漩涡环构成的，以太是一种无形的、无处不在的介质。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">He declared that atoms must be made out of vortex rings of ether, an invisible everywhere medium.</p>
</details>
不同类型的漩涡环绳结将构成不同的元素。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Different knots of vortex rings would make different elements.</p>
</details>
霍普夫链环的形状解释了钠的双重光谱线，简单的非结环则是氢。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The shape of the Hopf link explained the double spectral lines of sodium. The simple unknot ring was hydrogen.</p>
</details>
泰特对此表示怀疑，但随着开尔文的原子漩涡模型成为主流理论，泰特开始认真研究绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Tait was skeptical, but as Kelvin's vortex model of the atom became a leading theory, Tait began investigating knots in earnest.</p>
</details>
在他看来，每发现一个新的绳结，就是在为元素周期表添砖加瓦。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In his mind, creating a periodic table of the elements with every new knot he found.</p>
</details>
**交叉数**（Crossing Number: 绳结在平面投影中最少交叉点的数量）是分类绳结的一种简单方法。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The crossing number is an easy way to categorize knots.</p>
</details>
只需取绳结最简单的形式，即没有额外扭曲或缠结的形式，然后数出所有交叉点。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Just take the simplest form of a knot, one with no extra twists or tangles, and count up all its crossings.</p>
</details>
泰特手工发现了三交叉绳结（三叶结），然后是四交叉绳结（**八字结**，Figure Eight Knot），接着是两个五交叉绳结，三个六交叉绳结和七个七交叉绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">By hand, Tait discovered a three crossing knot, the trefoil, then a four crossing knot, the figure eight, then two five crossing knots, three six crossing knots and seven seven crossing knots.</p>
</details>
一个快速提示：绳结是可加的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">One quick note, knots are additive.</p>
</details>
你可以将多个绳结连接在一起形成一个新的绳结，比如将这两个三叶结组合成一个六交叉绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You can stick multiple knots together to make a new knot, like combining these two trefoils into a six crossing knot.</p>
</details>
这被称为**复合结**（Composite Knot: 可以分解成两个或更多简单绳结的绳结），但有些绳结不能分解成更简单的绳结，这些被称为**素结**（Prime Knot: 不能分解成两个或更多简单绳结的绳结）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This is called a composite knot, but some knots aren't decomposable into simpler knots. These are known as prime knots.</p>
</details>
由于所有复合结都由素结构成，人们主要关注素结的列表。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Since all composite knots are just built from primes, people mainly focus on tabulating prime knots.</p>
</details>
不幸的是，对泰特而言，开尔文勋爵的原子漩涡理论的警钟已经敲响。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Unfortunately for Tait, warning signs were on the horizon for Lord Kelvin's vortex theory of the atom.</p>
</details>
**门捷列夫**（Mendeleev: 俄国化学家，元素周期表的提出者）的第一张元素周期表于1869年发表。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Mendeleev's first periodic table had been published in 1869.</p>
</details>
**迈克尔逊-莫雷实验**（Michelson-Morley experiment: 1887年进行的实验，旨在探测以太的存在）在1887年播下了对以太存在的怀疑种子。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Michelson-Morley experiment sowed seeds of doubt about an ether in 1887,</p>
</details>
而最具毁灭性的结果是**J.J.汤姆森**（J.J. Thomson: 英国物理学家，电子的发现者）在1897年发现了电子。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">and the most damning result was JJ Thomson's discovery of the electron in 1897.</p>
</details>
因此，存在比原子更小的粒子，并且这些粒子存在于原子内部。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So there were particles smaller than the atom which were inside atoms.</p>
</details>
但泰特已经深陷绳结研究，无法停止。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

最后再乘以n。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Cap it off with n again.</p>
</details>
这无疑是我们视频中展示过的最大数字。

### 绳结不变式：区分绳结的关键工具

如果区分两个绳结如此困难，我们是如何列出3.5亿个不同绳结的呢？
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If it's this hard to tell two knots apart, how have we managed to tabulate 350 million different knots?</p>
</details>
嗯，绳结有一些属性是永远不会改变的，无论你如何扭曲或缠绕它。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Well, there are some properties of a knot that never change, no matter how much you twist or tangle it up.</p>
</details>
这些被称为**不变式**（Invariant: 绳结的固有属性，不随扭曲或缠绕而改变），这些不变式对于某些绳结与另一些绳结是不同的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These are called invariants, and these invariants will be different for some knots compared to other ones.</p>
</details>
所以你可以用它们作为特定绳结的标志。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So you can use them as hallmarks of a particular knot.</p>
</details>
它们并非完美无缺，有些绳结会共享不变式，但如果两个绳结有不同的不变式，那么你就可以确定它们是不同的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They're not perfectly discriminating. I mean, some knots will share invariants, but if two knots have different invariants then you know for sure that they are different.</p>
</details>
交叉数本身就是一个不变式。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Crossing number is itself aninvariant.</p>
</details>
如果两个绳结的交叉数不同，它们就不可能是相同的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Two knots can't be identical if they have different crossing numbers.</p>
</details>
但交叉数计算起来出奇地困难。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But the crossing number is surprisingly difficult to calculate.</p>
</details>
你可以在任何绳结中添加额外的交叉点，比如随意添加一堆扭曲。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You can put extra crossings into any knot, like just throw in a bunch of twists.</p>
</details>
同一绳结的不同变体被称为该绳结的不同**投影**（Projection: 绳结在平面上的二维表示）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Different variations of the same knot are known as different projections of that knot.</p>
</details>
交叉数衡量的是绳结可能拥有的最少交叉点数量，但它只适用于绳结最简单的投影，也称为其**简化形式**（Reduced Form: 绳结投影中交叉点数量最少的状态），但很难确保绳结完全简化。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Crossing number measures the least number of crossings a knot can have, but it only works for the simplest projection of a knot, also known as its reduced form, but it's difficult to ensure that a knot is fully reduced.</p>
</details>
相反，我们可以使用另一个不变式，它对于绳结的所有投影都立即成立，因此对于杂乱的三叶结和简化的三叶结都会给出相同的值。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Instead, we can use another invariant, one that's true right away for all projections of a knot. So it'll give the same value both for a messy trefoil and for a reduced trefoil.</p>
</details>
这个第一个不变式是**三色可染性**（Tricolorability: 绳结图是否可以用三种颜色着色，遵循特定规则），即绳结是否可以用三种颜色着色。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This first invariant is tricolorability or whether or knot can be colored in with three colors.</p>
</details>
取一个绳结图，并为每个独立的线段着色。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Take a diagram of a knot and color in each individual segment.</p>
</details>
这些线段只是被下穿交叉点分隔开，就像你会抬起笔离开纸面一样。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These are just separated by under crossings where you would lift your pen off the page.</p>
</details>
三色可染性只有两条规则。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Tricolorability only has two rules.</p>
</details>
首先，你必须使用至少两种颜色，因为任何绳结都可以用一种颜色着色。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">First, you must use at least two colors because you can color any knot in with one color.</p>
</details>
其次，在交叉点处，三根相交的线段必须要么全部颜色相同，要么全部颜色不同，基本上不能出现只有两种颜色的交叉点。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And second, at crossings, the three intersecting strands must either be all the same color or all different colors. Basically no two colored crossings.</p>

这个不变式只有两个类别：绳结是三色可染的，或者不是。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There are just two categories of this invariant, either a knot is tricolorable or it's not.</p>
</details>
相同的绳结必须匹配。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Identical knots must match.</p>
</details>
所以，如果一个绳结是三色可染的，而另一个不是，那么你就知道它们是不同的绳结。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So if one knot is tricolorable and the other one isn't, then you know they're different knots.</p>

很难相信三色可染性对于同一绳结的任何可能投影都是不变的，但由于只需要雷德迈斯特变换就可以在投影之间移动，我们只需要证明它不受雷德迈斯特变换的影响。

扭曲很容易，一切都保持一种颜色。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The twist is easy. Everything's one color already, and it stays that way.</p>

对于戳，两种颜色的交点意味着形成的环必须变成第三种颜色，所以每个交点都有三种颜色。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">With the poke, the intersection of two colors means that the loop formed must become the third color. So we have three colors at every intersection.</p>

对于滑动，你永远不必打破三色可染性，因为你从三个交点处的三种颜色开始，然后将一个交点切换为只有一种颜色。

<p class="english-text">With the slide, you never have to break tricolorability because you start with three colors at three intersections and then switch one intersection to just one color.</p>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So any knot will maintain its tricolorability, no matter what Reidemeister moves you do.</p>
</details>
现在是时候指出，我们从未真正证明三叶结和非结是两个不同的绳结，但我们现在可以用三色可染性来做到这一点。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The crossings all have three colors, so it is tricolorable.</p>

现在我们知道三叶结的每个可能投影都是三色可染的，而非结的每个可能投影都不是。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now we know every possible projection of the trefoil is tricolorable while every possible projection of the unknot isn't.</p>
