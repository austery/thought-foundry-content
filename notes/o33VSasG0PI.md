### 从历史中汲取城市建设经验

**Jan**: 帮助我们了解城市过去是如何建造的，这对我们如何在此基础上更好地建造城市有什么启示？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jan**: Help us use sort of how cities have been built before, help us inform how we can sort of build better in this case.

</details>

**Antonio Gomez Palacio**: 当然。这方面我们确实有一些很好的例子。坦白说，我们祖父母和曾祖父母以前来到的那些城市，它们都是**步行可达**的。他们可以步行去学校。今天有多少孩子步行去学校？他们去杂货店，生活在非常紧凑、精炼的生活模式中。而在第二次世界大战后的城市，我们发明了机器，有了汽车，开始修建道路。当时有一种观念，认为我们可以积累财富，对吧？我们可以通过物质积累来创造各种非常奢华的生活方式。毫不奇怪，这导致了**不可持续的城市**。我们无法支撑那种水平的基础设施。对家庭来说，这也变得**难以负担**。所以，我们需要让发展的钟摆回到更紧凑、更多元化、更**步行可达**的方向，这样我们才能拥有高品质的生活，而无需建造如此多的基础设施，也无需积累如此多的物质。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Antonio Gomez Palacio**: Absolutely. And here is where we actually have some great examples. Frankly, most of the cities where our grandparents and great grandparents came from, right, were walkable. They were able to walk to school. How many kids today walk to school? Right? They went to the groceries and they lived in, in very compact and succinct life forms. Right. And in cities after the Second World War, we invented machines and we had cars and we started to build roads. And, and there was this sense that we could accumulate stuff, right? And we could build our way into all kinds of very luxurious lifestyles. Not surprisingly, that has resulted in unsustainable cities, right? We can't sustain that level of infrastructure. And for households, it's become unaffordable. So we need to bring the pendulum back to more compact, more mixed use, more walkable so that we can have great quality of life and great lifestyles without the need to build so much infrastructure and without the need to accumulate so much stuff.

</details>

### 政府在城市规划中的失误

**Jan**: 我想更好地理解，政府多年来在哪些方面做错了。当我们谈到**城市扩张**（urban sprawl）和建造更好的城市时，在过去几年里，我们听到了很多关于**绿化带**（greenbelt）以及想要开发这些地方的讨论。我们知道，从头开始建设基础设施会非常昂贵。但在您看来，政府多年来在哪些方面做得不对？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jan**: I want to have a better understanding of what have governments gotten wrong over the years. When we talk about sort of, you know, urban sprawl and building better cities, you know, over the past couple of years, we've heard a lot about, you know, the green belt and wanting to develop those places. Those can be costly, as we know, with sort of having to build the infrastructure from the ground up. But what have governments gotten wrong over the years from your view?

</details>

**Antonio Gomez Palacio**: 是的，在某些情况下，**权宜之计**（expediency）妨碍了良好的**长期规划**。一个好的城市是基于 100 年或 1000 年的视野来规划的。为什么？因为 100 到 200 年前的人们做出的决定，我们今天还在承受其后果。例如，为了提供更多住房而蚕食**绿化带**，如果这还伴随着为这些地区提供住房而需要建造大量基础设施的话。这就会造成一种生活方式，人们需要长途开车才能到达。短期内，我们可能会制造出提供**可负担住房**的假象。但实际上，我们只是在扩大一种非常昂贵的城市建设模式和一种非常昂贵的生活方式。所以是的，许多政府在许多情况下都犯了很大的错误。我们现在也在围绕交通枢纽进行建设，在一些关键区域进行建设。所以，在所有错误之中，也有一些很好的事情正在发生。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Antonio Gomez Palacio**: And yeah, in some instances, expediency has gotten in the way of good long term planning, right? Good cities are planned on 100 year or 1000 year type of horizons. Why? Because people made decisions 102 hundred years ago that we're living with the consequences of today. We're also now building around transit centres. We're building in some key areas. So in the midst of all the wrong, there's also some great things that are taking place.

### 方法的即时影响

**Jan**: 显然，在这一切之中，我们确实面临**住房危机**。我们也有，你知道，**生活成本危机**。我想更好地理解。您的方法如何能立即产生影响？因为我认为，当你谈论一个长期计划时，人们确实想要眼前的结果。当然，事情不一定能立刻实现。但您的方法如何能立即影响人们的生活和财务？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jan**: Obviously, in the midst of all of this, we do have a housing crisis. We do have a, you know, a cost of living crisis. I want to have a better understanding. How could your approach have an immediate impact? Because I think that's, you know, when we talk about having this long term plan, but people do want results. Now, it doesn't happen necessarily like that. But how could your approach have an immediate impact on people's lives and finances?

</details>

**Antonio Gomez Palacio**: 是的。我们需要改变政策。我们需要改变我们思考城市的方式。我们需要以一种**综合**（comprehensive）的方式来思考。同时，我们需要帮助那些努力平衡所有这些事情的家庭。所以，如果我们致力于实现**可负担住房**——这是我们绝对需要做的——同时，我们还在建立一些**可负担的交通方式**，并创造获得**可负担娱乐和服务**的机会，等等。如果我们能在每个方面都取得 5% 甚至 10% 的进展，那么我们就能够从不同角度解决那些出现在餐桌上的账单问题。对于一个特定的家庭，我们可能无法解决住房问题，但如果我们解决了交通问题，或者提供了**健康食品**的途径，或者创造了其他方面，这意味着通过从**多管齐下**（multi-pronged）的角度来解决问题，我们就能极大地提高对家庭产生即时影响的能力。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Antonio Gomez Palacio**: Yeah. So the we need to change the policy. We need to change the way in which we think about cities. We need to be thinking about it in a comprehensive way. And at the same time, we need to help families who are trying to balance all of these things out, right? So if we're trying to do affordable housing, which we absolutely need to do, and at the same time, we're building some affordable means of transportation and creating access to affordable recreation and services in these kinds of things. If we can just make a 5% dent in each one of those and a 10% dent, then we're tackling what those bills that show up at the kitchen table from a variety of different perspectives. So one particular family, we may not be able to tackle the housing, but if we've tackled the transportation or access to healthy food, right, or creating other aspects, it means that by approaching the problem from a multi-pronged perspective, we have a much greater ability to have an immediate effect on families.

</details>
