### 数学与现实的脱离：虚数的诞生

数学最初是为了量化我们的世界而诞生的，例如测量土地、预测行星运动以及记录商业交易。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Mathematics began as a way to quantify our world, to measure land, predict the motions of planets, and keep track of commerce.</p>
</details>
然而，一个曾被认为不可能解决的问题出现了。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Then came a problem considered impossible.</p>
</details>
解决这个问题的秘诀在于将数学从现实世界中分离出来，将代数与几何分离，并创造出一些如此奇特以至于被称为**虚数**（Imaginary Numbers: 指形如 bi 的数，其中 b 是实数，i 是虚数单位，i² = -1）的新数字。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The secret to solving it was to separate math from the real world, to split algebra from geometry and to invent new numbers so fanciful they are called imaginary.</p>
</details>
具有讽刺意味的是，四百年后，这些数字竟然出现在我们对宇宙最佳物理理论的核心之中。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Ironically, 400 years later, these very numbers turn up in the heart of our best physical theory of the universe.</p>
</details>
只有放弃数学与现实的联系，我们才能发现现实的真正本质。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Only by abandoning math's connection to reality could we discover reality's true nature.</p>
</details>

### 古代数学的局限：几何与负数

1494年，列奥纳多·达·芬奇的数学老师**卢卡·帕乔利**（Luca Pacioli）出版了《算术、几何、比例总论》（Summa de Arithmetica），这是一本全面总结了文艺复兴时期意大利所有已知数学知识的著作。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In 1494, Luca Pacioli who is Leonardo da Vinci's math teacher publishes "Summa de Arithmetica," a comprehensive summary of all mathematics known in Renaissance Italy at the time.</p>
</details>
书中有一节是关于**三次方程**（Cubic Equation: 指未知数最高次数为三的多项式方程）的，今天我们通常会将其写成 ax³ + bx² + cx + d = 0 的形式。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In it, there's a section on the cubic, any equation which today we would write as ax cubed plus bx squared plus cx plus d equals zero.</p>
</details>
人们至少在四千年前就开始尝试寻找三次方程的通用解法，但每一个遇到它的古代文明，无论是巴比伦人、希腊人、中国人、印度人、埃及人还是波斯人，都一无所获。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">People have been trying to find a general solution to the cubic for at least 4,000 years, but each ancient civilization that encountered it, the Babylonians, Greeks, Chinese, Indians, Egyptians, and Persians, they all came up empty-handed.</p>
</details>
帕乔利的结论是，三次方程的解法是不可能的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Pacioli's conclusion is that a solution to the cubic equation is impossible.</p>
</details>
这至少应该让人感到有些惊讶，因为如果没有 x³ 项，这个方程就只是一个**二次方程**（Quadratic Equation: 指未知数最高次数为二的多项式方程）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now, this should be at least a little surprising, since without the X cubed term, the equation is simply a quadratic.</p>
</details>
许多古代文明早在几千年前就已经解决了二次方程。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And many ancient civilizations had solved quadratics thousands of years earlier.</p>
</details>
今天，任何通过八年级数学的人都知道它的通用解法：负 b 加减根号 b 方减四 ac，再除以二 a。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Today, anyone who's passed eighth grade knows the general solution. It's minus b plus minus root b squared minus four ac all over two a.</p>
</details>
但大多数人只是机械地套用这个公式，完全不了解古代数学家用来推导它的几何原理。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But most people just plug and chug into this formula completely oblivious to the geometry that ancient mathematicians used to derive it.</p>
</details>
要知道，在那个时代，数学不是用方程写下来的，而是用文字和图画来表达的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You know, back in those days, mathematics wasn't written down in equations. It was written with words and pictures.</p>
</details>
以方程 x² + 26x = 27 为例。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Take, for example, the equation x squared plus 26x equals 27.</p>
</details>
古代数学家会将 x² 项想象成一个边长为 x 的正方形。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Ancient mathematicians would think of the x squared term like a literal square with sides of length x.</p>
</details>
而 26x 则是一个长为 26、宽为 x 的矩形，这两个面积加起来等于 27。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And then 26x, well, that would be a rectangle with one side of length 26 and the other side of length x, and these two areas together add to 27.</p>
</details>
那么我们如何求出 x 呢？
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So how do we figure out what x is?</p>
</details>
我们可以把这个 26x 的矩形切成两半。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Well, we can take this 26x rectangle and cut it in half.</p>
</details>
这样我们就有两个 13x 的矩形，我们可以把它们重新排列，使新形成的形状几乎是一个正方形，只是缺少下面这一部分。
<details>

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

### 塔尔塔利亚的几何解法

为了解决这个问题，他将“补全平方”的思想扩展到三维空间。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">To do it, he extends the idea of completing the square into three dimensions.</p>
</details>
以方程 x³ + 9x = 26 为例。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Take the equation x cubed plus nine x equals 26.</p>
</details>
你可以把 x³ 想象成一个边长为 x 的立方体的体积。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You can think of x cubed as the volume of a cube with sides of length x.</p>
</details>
如果你加上一个 9x 的体积，你就得到 26。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And if you add a volume of nine x, you get 26.</p>
</details>
所以就像补全平方一样，我们需要在立方体上增加体积，使其体积增加 9x。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So just like with completing the square, we need to add onto the cube to increase its volume by nine x.</p>
</details>
想象一下将这个立方体的三条边向外延伸距离 y，创建一个边长为 z 的新大立方体。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Imagine extending three sides of this cube out a distance y, creating a new, larger cube with sides of length, call it z.</p>
</details>

### 虚数在量子物理中的核心地位

1925 年，**埃尔温·薛定谔**（Erwin Schrödinger）在**德布罗意**（Louis de Broglie）关于物质由波组成的洞察基础上，正在寻找一个描述**量子粒子**（Quantum Particles: 指在量子力学尺度下表现出波动性和粒子性双重特征的微观粒子）行为的波动方程。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In 1925, Erwin Schrödinger is searching for a wave equation that governs the behavior of quantum particles building on de Broglie's insight that matter consists of waves.</p>
</details>
他提出了物理学中最重要和最著名的方程之一——**薛定谔方程**（Schrödinger Equation: 描述量子力学系统中粒子行为的波动方程）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">He comes up with one of the most important and famous equations in all of physics, the Schrödinger equation.</p>
</details>
其中显著地包含了 i，即负一的平方根。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And featured prominently within it is i, the square root of negative one.</p>
</details>
虽然数学家们已经习惯了虚数，但物理学家们还没有，并且对它出现在如此基础的理论中感到不适。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While mathematicians have grown accustomed to imaginary numbers, physicists have not and are uncomfortable seeing it show up in such a fundamental theory.</p>
</details>
薛定谔本人写道：“这里令人不快，甚至直接遭到反对的是复数的使用。波函数 Psi 肯定从根本上是一个实函数。”
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Schrödinger himself writes "What is unpleasant here, and indeed directly to be objected to, is the use of complex numbers. The wave function Psi is surely fundamentally a real function."</p>
</details>
这似乎是一个合理的反对意见，那么为什么最初出现在三次方程解中的虚数会出现在基础物理学中呢？
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This seems like a fair objection, so why does an imaginary number that first appeared in the solution to the cubic turn up in fundamental physics?</p>
</details>
这是因为虚数具有一些独特的性质。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Well, it's because of some unique properties of imaginary numbers.</p>
</details>
虚数存在于垂直于实数轴的维度上。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Imaginary numbers exist on a dimension perpendicular to the real number line.</p>
</details>
它们共同构成了**复平面**（Complex Plane: 一个二维平面，其横轴表示实数，纵轴表示虚数，用于表示复数）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Together, they form the complex plane.</p>
</details>
看看当我们反复乘以 i 时会发生什么。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Watch what happens when we repeatedly multiply by i.</p>
</details>
从 1 开始。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Starting with one.</p>
</details>
1 乘以 i 是 i，根据定义，i 乘以 i 是负 1。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">One times i is i, i times i is negative one, by definition.</p>
</details>
负 1 乘以 i 是负 i，负 i 乘以 i 是 1。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Negative one times i is negative i, and negative i times i is one.</p>
</details>
我们回到了起点，如果我们继续乘以 i，这个点就会不断旋转。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We've come back to where we started, and if we keep multiplying by i, the point will keep rotating around.</p>
</details>
所以当你乘以 i 时，你实际上是在复平面上旋转 90 度。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The two quintessential functions that describe waves are both contained in e to the ix.</p>
</details>
所以当薛定谔写下波动方程时，他自然假设他的方程的解会看起来像 e 的 ix 次方，特别是 e 的 i(kx - ωt) 次方。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So when Schrodinger goes to write down a wave equation, he naturally assumes that the solutions to his equation will look something like e to the ix, specifically e to the ikx minus omega t.</p>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Plus, since the Schrodinger equation is linear, you can add together an arbitrary number of solutions of this form, creating any sort of wave shape you like, and it too, will be a solution to Schrodinger's equation.</p>
</details>
物理学家**弗里曼·戴森**（Freeman Dyson）后来写道：“薛定谔将负一的平方根放入方程中，突然间它变得有意义了。突然间它变成了一个波动方程，而不是一个热传导方程。薛定谔欣喜地发现，这个方程的解对应于原子**玻尔模型**（Bohr model: 描述原子结构的一种早期量子力学模型）中的量子化轨道。”
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The physicist Freeman Dyson later writes, "Schrödinger put the square root of minus one into the equation, and suddenly it made sense. Suddenly it became a wave equation instead of a heat conduction equation. </details>
事实证明，薛定谔方程正确地描述了我们所知道的关于原子行为的一切。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It turns out that the Schrodinger equation describes correctly everything we know about the behavior of atoms.</p>
</details>
它是所有化学和大部分物理学的基础。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It is the basis of all of chemistry and most of physics.</p>
</details>
而那个负一的平方根意味着自然界是用**复数**而不是实数来运作的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And that square root of minus one means that nature works with complex numbers and not with real numbers.</p>
</details>
这一发现对薛定谔以及其他人来说都是一个完全的惊喜。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This discovery came as a complete surprise, to Schrodinger as well as to everybody else."</p>
