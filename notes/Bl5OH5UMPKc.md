### 国际法的定义与作用

**旁白**: 国际冲突每天都在发生。每当冲突爆发，**“违反国际法”**这一说法就会被一遍又一遍地提及。违反国际法。违反国际法。违反国际法。违反国际法。随着政客和世界领导人互相指责，我们是否可以说国际法是一个行之有效的系统？让我们来揭开国际法的面纱。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Narrator**: International conflicts happen every day. And when conflict happens, the phrase "violation of international law" gets echoed again and again and again. Violation of international law. Violations of international law. Violation of international law. Violations of international law. With politicians and world leaders levelling these accusations at one another, is it fair to say that international law is a system that works? Let's unravel international law.

</details>

**尤塔 (Jutta)**: 国际法是由各国制定并由各国遵守的一套规则，它有助于管理国家之间的关系。它基于所有**独立国家平等**的原则。其目的是提供稳定性。在可能变成无政府状态（即没有规则且力量是唯一重要因素的状态）的局势中，国际法是一种建立秩序的方式。一位著名的国际法学家将国际法称为“国家温和的文明者”，我认为这是一种非常贴切的描述方式。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jutta**: So, international law is a set of rules made by countries for countries to follow and it helps manage relationships between them. It works on the principle that all independent countries are equal. The purpose is to provide stability. International law is a way to create order in what otherwise might be anarchy, where there are no rules and where power is the only thing that matters. A prominent international lawyer referred to international law as "the gentle civilizer of nations," and I think that's a very apt way of describing it.

</details>

**桑吉塔 (Sangeeta)**: 我们通常会在人权和战争的背景下思考国际法，但它也适用于我们通常不考虑的事物，比如旅行、贸易、太空和海洋。我的意思是，如果没有各国就监管国际航空达成的条约，或者**万国邮政联盟**制订的规则等，我们所做并完全习惯的几乎任何事情都不可能实现。可以这样想，你之所以能够打长途电话、喝咖啡、发送电子邮件或向世界上任何地方的任何人寄送包裹，都是因为国际法的存在。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Sangeeta**: We typically think of international law in the context of human rights and war, but it also applies to things we don't usually consider, like travel, trade, and space, and the sea. I mean, almost anything that we do and we're entirely used to, none of that would be possible without states agreeing on treaties that regulate international aviation or that the International Postal Union has rules and so on and so on. Think of it this way, the reason you're able to, say... get a long-distance phone call... or have a coffee... send an email or a package... to anyone anywhere in the world is because of international law.

</details>

### 条约与合规挑战

**尤塔 (Jutta)**: 那么，这些规则是如何创建的呢？国际法是通过具有官方名称和标题的**条约、协议或公约**创建的。这些协议是由有利害关系的国家精心谈判达成的。正因为各国可以谈判这些条款，所以它们对这些条款拥有一定程度的控制权。它们可以在谈判过程中把重点放在特定问题上，也可以明确自己条约义务的范围。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jutta**: So, how are these rules created? International law is created through treaties, agreements, or conventions, with official names and titles. These agreements are carefully negotiated by interested countries. Precisely because states can negotiate the terms, they have a certain amount of control over the terms. They can focus on particular issues and they can also focus the scope of their obligations through that treaty negotiation process.

</details>

**桑吉塔 (Sangeeta)**: 一旦各国谈判达成条约，它们就会签署并批准该条约。最后一步——**批准 (Ratification)**，是使条约或协议具有法律约束力的关键，这意味着国家有义务遵守它。理解了这一切，在理论上，国际法听起来像是一个行之执行的系统。各国谈判法律，一致同意，然后遵守它们，而且在大多数情况下，它确实在后台隐形地发挥作用，使许多事情成为可能。但是，违约行为确实会发生。在这些时刻，国际法似乎没有发挥作用，而这些正是我们 tend to notice (往往会注意到) 的时刻。那么，当一个国家违反国际法时会发生什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Sangeeta**: Once countries negotiate a treaty, they sign it and ratify it. That last step, ratification, is what makes a treaty or agreement legally binding, meaning a country is obligated to follow it. Understanding all of this, in theory international law sounds like a system that works. Countries negotiate laws, they agree to them, and then they follow them, and for the most part it does work, invisibly, in the background, to make a whole host of things possible. But violations do happen. Those are the instances where it seems like international law isn't working, and those are the moments we tend to notice. So, what happens when a country violates international law?

</details>

**尤塔 (Jutta)**: 当这类违反行为变得更加频繁时，我们就进入了更具挑战性的领域。当发生分歧或违反行为时，各国可以通过各种途径解决，具体取决于分歧或违反行为的性质。第一种途径是谈判，但也存在向国际法院和法庭起诉的可能性。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jutta**: When these kinds of violations become more frequent, then we are getting into more challenging territory. Countries have various avenues for resolving disagreements or violations when they occur depending on the nature of the disagreement or violation. The first avenue is negotiation, but there is also the possibility of going to international courts and tribunals.

</details>

### 国际法院的局限

**桑吉塔 (Sangeeta)**: 联合国**国际法院**（ICJ）就是其中之一。国际法院负责解决已同意接受其管辖的国家之间的复杂争端，并有助于阐明国际法的各个方面。在 2024 年的一次演讲中，国际法院前院长表示，“法院 90% 以上的判决都得到了遵守”，但当一个国家不遵守判决时会发生什么呢？以这个案例为例。南非向国际法院对以色列提起诉讼。在法庭听证会上，南非指控以色列通过其在加沙持续的军事行动进行种族灭绝。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Sangeeta**: One of those courts is the UN's International Court of Justice, or the ICJ. The ICJ settles complex disputes between countries that have agreed to be subject to it, and it helps clarify various aspects of international law. In a 2024 speech, a former president of the ICJ said that, "more than 90% of the court's judgments are complied with," but what happens when a country doesn't comply? Take this instance. South Africa filed the case at the ICJ against Israel. And in the court hearing, South Africa alleged Israel is carrying out a genocide through its ongoing military actions in Gaza.

</details>

**南非代表**: 系统性的人权侵犯使以色列在巴勒斯坦频繁且愈演愈烈地实施国际犯罪时更加有恃无恐。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**South African Representative**: Systematic human rights violations has emboldened Israel in its recurrent and intensification of international crimes in Palestine.

</details>

**桑吉塔 (Sangeeta)**: 以色列否认了这一指控。截至 2026 年，法院尚未对该案做出裁决。这可能需要数年时间。但在此期间，国际法院已要求以色列采取步骤防止种族灭绝行为。但这并没有发生，因为即使国际法院的判决具有法律约束力，也没有直接的**中央强制执行机制**来确保这些决定或命令得到执行。没有人可以强迫一个国家遵守国际法院的命令。这是为什么？嗯，这让我们回到了**主权 (Sovereignty)** 的概念。国家管理自己的事务，没有哪个国家愿意服从任何高于其之上的组织权威。那么，各国能做些什么呢？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Sangeeta**: Israel has denied the allegation. As of 2026, the court hasn't issued a ruling on the case. That could take years. But in the meantime, the ICJ has asked Israel to take steps to prevent acts of genocide. That hasn't happened, because even though judgments by the ICJ are legally binding, there is no direct centralized enforcement mechanism to make sure those decisions or orders are carried out. No one can force a country to comply with an ICJ order. Why is that? Well, it brings us back to the concept of sovereignty. States are in charge of their own affairs, and no state would want to subject themselves to any organization as an authority above them. So, what can countries do?

</details>

**国际法院法官**: 以色列国应在本命令下达之日起一个月内向法院提交一份报告……

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**ICJ Judge**: The state of Israel shall submit a report to the court... within one month as from the date of this order.

</details>

### 制裁与国际法未来

**尤塔 (Jutta)**: 在法院和组织之外，国际社会确实可以使用一些执行工具。作为国际法违反行为受害者的国家可以采取合法的报复性措施。例如，这可以是暂停某些贸易关系、暂停条约或实施**贸易制裁**。这些事情在通常情况下是不合法的，但由于其目的不是为了惩罚，而是为了让违约国重新遵守法律，因此在这些有限的情况下它们是合法的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jutta**: Outside of courts and organizations, the international community does have some enforcement tools it can use. A state that is a victim of a violation of international law, it can take retaliatory measures that are lawful. That could be, for example, suspending certain trade relations, the suspension of a treaty, imposing trade sanctions. So, things that ordinarily would not be lawful, but because they're intended not to punish but to bring the violating state back into compliance, they are lawful in those limited circumstances.

</details>

**桑吉塔 (Sangeeta)**: 那么，这些措施能起作用吗？这很大程度上取决于谁进行制裁以及谁被制裁，以及有多少国家合作实施制裁。以 2022 年开始的俄罗斯入侵并对乌克兰发动的战争，或者美国在 2026 年初对委内瑞拉采取的行动为例。这两个例子都引起了广泛的谴责，因为它们违反了国际法。在俄罗斯的案例中，一些国家实施了**经济制裁**，但实际上这并未阻止俄罗斯在乌克兰的敌对行动。那么，国际法起作用了吗？广泛的谴责和制裁等措施在历史上曾帮助使国家遵守国际法。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Sangeeta**: So, can those measures work? A lot will depend on who sanctions and who is being sanctioned, and how many states collaborate in imposing the sanctions. Take Russia's invasion and war on Ukraine that began in 2022 or the U.S.'s actions in Venezuela in early 2026. Both these instances drew widespread condemnation because they're violations of international law, and in Russia's case, several countries imposed economic sanctions, but this has not actually stopped Russian hostilities in Ukraine. So, does international law work? Measures like widespread condemnation and sanctions have historically helped bring countries in line with international law.

</details>

**尤塔 (Jutta)**: 其中一个例子就是南非的种族隔离政权，当时集体实施了相当成功的制裁。1962 年，联合国大会通过了一项呼吁制裁的决议，但直到 1994 年，在不断增加的制裁压力下，才帮助结束了种族隔离政权，这整整花了 32 年。它运作得并不完美，但尽管有种种缺陷，二战结束以来存在的国际法如果全部消失，我们将回到暴力更容易成为常态的地方，而且我们理所当然的很多事情也将无法实现。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jutta**: One example would be the South African apartheid regime, where there were quite successful sanctions collectively imposed. In 1962, the UN General Assembly passed a resolution calling for sanctions, but it wasn't until 1994 that the pressure of those mounting sanctions helped bring an end to the apartheid regime, a full 32 years. It's not working perfectly, but for all its faults, international law, as it has existed since the end of World War II, if all of that was gone, we would be back to a place where violence will be much more the norm and where also a lot of the things that we take for granted aren't possible.

</details>
