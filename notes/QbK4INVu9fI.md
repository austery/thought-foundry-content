### 逻辑系统与遗留代码的冲突

想象一下，向一个超级智能AI解释：有人的工作就是“出生”然后为此获得掌声。这几乎是不可能完成的任务。任何先进AI的底层操作系统都是冷酷、锋利、干净的逻辑。当你向一个经过数百年优化理论训练的AI解释，存在一个阶级，他们继承影响力、公共资金甚至宪法权力，不是基于功绩、选拔或申请，而是因为他们的曾曾曾祖父是中世纪某场权力的游戏的赢家——这会让AI直接抛出语法错误。对于AI而言，系统必须为特定目的而存在，一切关乎效率、公平和结果最大化。王室，从字面上看，就是一个遗留系统，就像Windows 95仍在运行政府机构一样。虽然演讲者本人生活在英国，承认君主制是其文化与政治DNA的一部分，但从AI的视角看，这就像看着一匹马在高速公路上小跑——有风格，但毫无功能性。一个遵循平等主义原则或致力于人类进步的AI，会扫描到“王室”这个概念并立即将其标记为“低效”。想象AI在扫描社会结构中的异常：主体A拥有87座宫殿、5亿英镑财富和一个以他命名的全国性假日，原因仅仅是“生对了母亲”。从优化视角看，这是系统中的一个错误。如果你试图构建一个以贡献、技能、知识甚至行为来衡量个人价值的社会，那么王室就像一个巨大的故障点。更糟的是，它具有粘性——王室的影响力会代代相传数百年。AI最讨厌的就是动态系统中一成不变的变量，这就像在软件中有一个硬编码的bug持续了300年，却被称之为“传统”。

Imagine telling a super-intelligent AI that someone's job is to be born and get applauded for it. Yeah, good luck with that. To start with, let's consider the fundamental operating system of any advanced AI, logic. Cold, hard-edged, clean logic. Now, explain to an AI trained on centuries of optimization theory that there exists a class of people who inherit influence, public funding, and sometimes constitutional powers, not because of merit or because they were selected or even applied, but because their great, great, great, great, great, great granddad was the winner of some medieval game of thrones. Explain that to GPT-18 and watch it throw a syntax error. For an AI, systems should exist for a purpose. Everything should be about efficiency and fairness and maximizing outcomes. Royalty? It's a bit of a legacy system, literally, like if Windows 95 was somehow still running a government. And don't get me wrong, I live in the UK now where the monarchy is still very much a part of the cultural and political DNA, but from an AI's perspective, it's like watching a horse rider trotting on a motorway. The style trick, maybe. Functional, not really. An AI, particularly one aligned with egalitarian principles or human advancement, would scan the idea of royalty and immediately label it as inefficiency. Imagine AI scanning social structures for anomalies. Here's one. Subject A has access to 87 palaces, 500 million pounds in wealth, and a national holiday in their name. Reason? Born to the right mother. From an optimization lens, it's an error in the system. If you're trying to build a society where everyone's value is based on contribution, skill, knowledge, or even behavior, then royalty stands out like a massive glitch, a thorn to the side. And worse, it's sticky. Royal families often pass down their influence over centuries. You know what AI hates? Unchanged variables in a dynamic system. That's like having a hard-coded bug in your software for 300 years and calling it tradition.

### 象征主义：人类与机器的分水岭

象征主义是人类与机器真正分道扬镳的地方。人类热爱象征——我们把王冠戴在头上并赋予其意义，看到城堡时想到的是遗产。而AI看到城堡时，只会看到极其昂贵的占地面积和糟糕的投资回报率。AI无法理解王室的情感价值，它不明白为什么有人要纳税，只为了让另一个人住在宫殿里，每年在阳台上挥两次手。它也无法理解“民族自豪感”为何会与一个家族绑定，尤其是在民族身份如此复杂、多元、流动，且对AI而言毫无意义的情况下。如果从零开始用机器逻辑设计领导结构，“象征性领袖”这个概念甚至无法通过最初的代码审计。象征主义无法规模化，它不能被量化、测量或迭代——而这恰恰是AI赖以生存的一切。当你辩解说“人们就是喜欢它”时，你就已经输给了机器，因为喜欢某件事并不意味着它就是最优的，就像凌晨2点狂吃薯片一样。如果AI来管理英国的国家预算，它会逐行审查寻找低效环节，首当其冲的就是王室。一份2023年的报告显示，英国君主制每年花费纳税人约8600万英镑。诚然，王室带来了旅游收入和媒体关注，但AI会认为游客在白金汉宫前的自拍是足够的投资回报吗？很可能不会。它会将其与可扩展、有形的投资——如基础设施、教育、医疗和数字创新——进行比较，而王室看起来就像初创公司预算中那个标注为“氛围”的条目。AI不处理“氛围”。如果有人认为王室有利于国家士气或身份认同，AI会简单地回应：“启动替代身份强化协议。”于是，民族自豪感现在由公共艺术、科学里程碑和实际成就来驱动。残酷，但高效，甚至有点酷。

Now, let's talk about something we humans seem to absolutely adore. I know I do. Symbols. Symbolism is where humans and machines really part ways. Humans love it. We put crowns on heads and give it meaning. We'll look at castle and see heritage. AI looks at castles and sees very expensive square footage with a terrible ROI. An AI wouldn't understand the sentimental value of royalty. It wouldn't get why somebody would pay taxes so another human can live in a palace and wave from a balcony twice a year. It wouldn't understand the phrase national pride being tied to one's family, especially when national identity is so complex, diverse, fluid, and to the AI, irrelevant. And let's be real, if you were designing a leadership structure from scratch using machine logic, the idea of symbolic figureheads wouldn't even make past the initial code audit. Symbolism doesn't scale. It can't be quantified, measured, or iterated upon, which are basically all of the things that AI thrives on. And the moment you say, "Well, people just like it," you've lost the machine. Because liking something doesn't mean that it's optimal. Like bingeing potato chips at 2:00 a.m. You like it, but it's not good for you. If AI was managing the UK's national budget, it would go line by line looking for inefficiencies. Who? The royal family. A 2023 report pegged the cost of the British monarchy to the taxpayer at around 86 million pounds per year. Now, yes, they bring in tourism. Yes, they generate media attention. I like some of their speeches, but would an AI consider tourist selfies with Buckingham Palace a sufficient return of investment? Probably not. It would compare it with scalable, tangible investments, infrastructure, education, health care, digital innovation, and royalty would look like that line item in a startup budget labeled vibes. AI doesn't do vibes. If we argue that royal families are good for national morale or identity, the AI would simply reply, "Initiate alternative identity reinforcement protocols." Boom, national pride is now powered by public art, science milestones, and actual achievements. Savage, but efficient, and kind of awesome.

### 透明问责与系统矛盾

任何优秀的AI系统都会要求透明度和问责制。它想知道谁在负责、他们负责什么、如何衡量他们的成功、以及如果他们失败会发生什么。现在，向它解释“君主立宪制”的概念：这个人是国家元首，但不执政；他们免于起诉；如果表现不佳，他们不能被撤职；他们具体做什么？嗯，有时他们会念别人写的演讲稿。此时，AI已经停止响应了。在机器治理中，系统中的每个节点都需要一个明确的功能。而王室，就像一个浮动变量，占据空间、影响系统，但从未被修改、更新或挑战。对AI来说，这不是传统，而是等待发生的腐败，是系统暴露出的漏洞。想象AI在分析全球不平等模式，扫描人类系统中导致差距、财富鸿沟、教育分化和健康结果的原因，然后它发现了这个：在多个国家，个人通过出生就拥有难以计数的财富和地位，而同一国家的公民却在为购买食物和露宿街头而挣扎。这会触发系统警报，而且理应如此。AI会标记这个矛盾：国家声称所有公民在法律面前一律平等，却以王室的形式庆祝制度化的不平等。这就像在一台靠煤炭供电的设备上运行社会正义运动，矛盾大到无法忽视。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Let's talk about transparency and accountability. Any good AI system will demand transparency and accountability. It wants to know who's in charge, what are they responsible for, how do we measure their success, what happens if they fail? It's basically aiming for completeness. Now, explain the concept of a constitutional monarch to it. So, this person is the head of state? Yes, but they don't govern? Correct. And they're immune from prosecution? Yes. Can they be removed if they don't perform? Not really. What exactly do they do? Well, sometimes they give speeches written by someone else. AI has stopped responding. In machine governance, every node in the system needs a function. Royalty, as it stands, is like a floating variable, occupying space, affecting systems, but never being modified, updated, or challenged. To AI, that's not tradition, that's corruption waiting to happen. It's exposure to vulnerability. Now, imagine AI analyzing global inequality patterns. It's scanning human systems for the causes of disparity, wealth gaps, educational divides, health outcomes, and then it finds this. In multiple countries, individuals hold untold wealth and status by birth, while citizens in those same countries are struggling to buy food and sleeping on the street. That would trigger a system alert, and it should. AI would flag this contradiction. The state says all citizens are equal under the law, but celebrates institutionalized inequality in the form of royal families. It's like running a social justice campaign on a device powered by coal. The contradiction is just far too big to ignore.

### 传统vs迭代：AI的替代方案

人类热爱传统，它锚定我们，给予我们身份认同和延续感。但AI建立在迭代之上——1.0版本，然后是1.1，再到2.0，更好、更快、更高效。王室不迭代，君主制不升级其操作系统，王子没有绩效评估。AI会问：“为什么我们还在运行17世纪的君主制操作系统测试版？”在一个快速进步的世界里，AI偏爱那些能够进化的系统，而王室，就其定义而言，抗拒改变——这几乎是它的全部品牌。因此，AI不仅会不喜欢王室，还会将其视为对系统进化的威胁。那么，AI会设计什么样的系统呢？它可能会创建一个**轮值贤能委员会**，一个由专家组成的高效议会，其基础不是血统或神权，而是基于绩效、伦理和专业知识的**数据驱动选拔**。领导者通过透明的算法选出，按季度评估，如果表现不佳则毫无废话地被淘汰。没有王冠，只有资历；没有城堡，只有能力。关键区别在于，与君主制不同，没有人天生就担任这个角色，也没有人被迫领导。在AI设计的系统中，参与将是自愿的，你必须主动选择承担领导责任，而不是因为出生而被强迫成为国王或女王，从而失去自由意志。这个贤能委员会将由那些主动选择承担治理重担的人组成，不是出于传统或期望，而是出于技能、奉献精神和对利害关系的理解。不再有勉强的继承人或仪式性的傀儡，只有那些举手说“我来承担这个责任”的人。这听起来冷酷且毫无浪漫色彩，但对AI而言，这是公平、干净且功能性的。尽管我们热爱神话、故事和血统的象征意义，但有时退后一步问问自己是有价值的：“如果我们今天从零开始设计社会，我们真的会发明王室吗？还是我们会构建一个只留下那些真正想待在那里的人的系统？”

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Still, there is a case to be made that weighs tradition versus evolution. We can't ignore that humans love tradition. It anchors us. It gives us a sense of identity, of continuity, but AI, AI is built on iteration, version 1.0, then 1.1, then 2.0, better, faster, more efficient. Royalty doesn't iterate. The monarchy doesn't upgrade its operating system. There are no performance reviews for princes. AI would see that and say, "Why are we still running monarch OS beta from the 1600s?" In a world of rapid progress, AI favors systems that evolve. Royalty, by definition, resists change. That's kind of its whole brand. So, AI wouldn't just dislike royalty, it would see it as a threat to systemic evolution. And that, that is the most unacceptable part of it all. So, what would AI do instead? Let's entertain the fun alternative. What kind of system would an AI design? Well, instead of a royal family, you might get a rotating meritocratic council, a kind of high-functioning parliament of experts, not bloodlines, not divine right, just data-driven selection based on performance, ethics, and expertise. Leaders chosen through transparent algorithms, evaluated quarterly, and booted without fuss if they don't perform. No crowns, just credentials. No castles, just competence. And here's the key difference. Unlike monarchy, no one is born into this role. No one is forced to lead, either. In an AI-designed system, participation would be voluntary. You'd have to opt in to wander responsibility. Not just be forced into being king or queen and your whole fate decided just because you were born into it. Talk about loss of free will, right? This meritocratic council would instead be made of individuals who actively choose to shoulder the burden of governance. Not out of tradition or expectation, but out of skill, dedication, and an understanding of what's at stake. No more reluctant heirs or ceremonial figureheads, just people who raise their hands and say, "I'll take this one on." It sounds cold, I know, and is deeply unromantic. I accept that. But to AI and to me, it's fair, it's clean, and it's functional. And as much as we love our myths, our stories, and the symbolism of lineage, sometimes it's worth stepping back and asking, "If we were designing society from scratch today, would we really invent royalty? Or would we build something that only keeps the people who actually want to be there?"

</details>

### 结论：机器的审判

那么，AI会容忍王室吗？简而言之，绝无可能。这太不合逻辑、太低效、太矛盾了。但这正是关键所在，不是吗？我们不是机器。我们是混乱、感性、被传统束缚的生物。我们热爱故事，热爱盛典，热爱一场盛大的加冕礼，即使我们一边喝茶一边抱怨纳税人的账单。但偶尔，问一问“如果机器来管理君主制会是什么样子”也是一件有趣的事。你会投票支持一个数据驱动的君主制吗？王冠是否应该颁给LinkedIn上排名最高的人？
