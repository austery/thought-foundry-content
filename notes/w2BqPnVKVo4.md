<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: And I remember talking to you offline about this. It was the easier decision for most people in crypto to go to the Zug here in Switzerland or be based in maybe Dubai. And just yolo it and don't worry about regulations and don't worry about audits. You made a different decision. You told me, "No, I'm going to do this buttoned up and proper." And why didn't you go for the quick offshore path?

</details>

**Jeremy Allaire**: 如果你想建立一个全球通用的、互联网原生的金融系统，你必须与现有系统集成，必须与政策制定者合作。我在 2013 年 11 月就在参议院作证了。虽然早期我因此受到了很多仇恨，但如果你想让 **BlackRock**（贝莱德）或全球最大的科技公司使用它，或者让普通人信任数字钱包里的美元，你就需要这种结构来建立**信任**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jeremy Allaire**: If you want to build a new internet native financial system that the whole world uses, you have to integrate with the existing system and you have to work with policy makers to figure that out. I testified to the Senate in November of 2013. I got a lot of hate very early on. But if you want BlackRock to use it or you want the biggest tech companies in the world to use it, you end up needing to have this kind of structure around it for trust.

</details>

**Jason**: 现在的环境已经从威胁变成了机遇，但大银行似乎仍有顾虑，也许是因为你们走得太快、太专业了。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: Now it's gone from a threat to an opportunity, but there is still a little reticence by some of the major banks because they're a little concerned that you're too good at what you do and you might be too far ahead.

</details>

**Jeremy Allaire**: 确实如此。这很像互联网的其他时代。当数字媒体出现时，传统媒体公司说“我们也能做”，但随后出现了一些在软件执行和经济模型上完全不同的公司，比如 **Craigslist**、**Google 广告网络**或 **Amazon**，它们彻底颠覆了行业。我们现在正处于类似的阶段。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jeremy Allaire**: I think that that's there's some truth in that for sure. It's very similar to other eras of the internet. When digital media happened, there obviously were companies that built completely different types of utilities with completely different unit economics that kind of turned the product user experience and the economics upside down. We know those companies like Craigslist, Google ad network, Amazon. We're in a similar place right now.

### 监管法案与市场竞争

**Jason**: 银行现在感到威胁，是因为稳定币能产生奖励或利息。**《天才法案》**（Genius Act）规定可以有奖励但不能有利息，这似乎是立法妥协的结果。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: The banks now are threatened by the concept of a stable coin having the ability to generate points incentives or interest. The Genius Act says hey you can have rewards but you can't have interest. Explain that to the audience.

</details>

**Jeremy Allaire**: 没错。根据《天才法案》，作为支付稳定币发行方，我们被禁止直接向持有者支付利息。欧洲和日本也有类似的规定。但我们是一个商业实体，我们产生收入，并与 **Robinhood**、**Coinbase**、**Visa** 等平台合作。这些平台希望通过奖励或忠诚度计划来激励用户，如果他们通过与我们的合作赚钱，他们就有能力这样做。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jeremy Allaire**: Under the Genius Act, Circle as a payment stable coin issuer we're prohibited from paying interest directly to stable coin holders. It's the same thing in Europe and Japan. But at the same time, we're building a business. We work with everyone from Robinhood to Coinbase to Visa. Those companies want to be able to have the ability to pay rewards or have loyalty programs. And if they're making money from the relationship with us, they want to be able to do that. And that's what the Genius Act captured.

</details>

**Jason**: 你的竞争对手 **Tether** 在离岸运营，规模是你们的几倍。传闻他们要推出美国版并接受审计，你怎么看这种竞争？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: You're up against an offshore platform, Tether, who has maybe three to five times the amount under management. Rumor is they're going to do a US version and try to go legit and audited. How do you think about them as a competitor?

</details>

**Jeremy Allaire**: 《天才法案》的美妙之处在于它创造了一个公平的竞争环境。它定义了联邦法律，你必须接受国家银行监管机构 **OCC** 的监管。**USDC** 已经是最大的受监管稳定币网络。稳定币是**网络业务**，具有极强的网络效应。每当 **Stripe**、**Shopify** 或 **Cash App** 集成 USDC，这个网络的效用就会增加。我们已经在全球建立了深厚的流动性网络，这种护城河是很难逾越的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jeremy Allaire**: The beauty of having the Genius Act is that it creates a level playing field. It defines a federal law that you have to be regulated by the national bank regulator, the OCC. USDC is by far the largest regulated stable coin network. Stable coins are network businesses. Every time an app integrates, like Cash App, Stripe, or Shopify, it adds utility to the network and it adds network effects. We've built out this incredible liquidity network, and that's defensibility.

</details>

### 网络安全：AI 时代的自动化威胁

**Jason**: 接下来我们请到的是 **CrowdStrike** 的首席执行官兼联合创始人 **George Kurtz**。George，在 2026 年，AI 对防御和进攻产生了深远影响。目前最有效的攻击向量是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: George Kurtz is here. He's the CEO and co-founder of CrowdStrike. Speaking of security, AI is going to have a profound impact on defense and also offense. What is the attack vector that AI is most effective at sitting here in 2026?

</details>

**George Kurtz**: AI 正在“批量制造”新的对手。过去你需要深厚的技术知识，现在你只需要询问 **LLM**（大语言模型）。攻击时间线被压缩了，攻击的复杂程度已经达到了国家级水平。我们现在看到的是**自主恶意软件**（Autonomous Malware）。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**George Kurtz**: Essentially you're minting new adversaries because you don't have to have all the knowledge that you had to have in the past. You can ask any number of LLMs. The attack timeline has been compressed. What we're seeing now is autonomous malware.

</details>

**Jason**: 请为观众解释一下什么是自主恶意软件。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: Explain in plain English to the audience what that is.

</details>

**George Kurtz**: 传统的恶意软件需要“打电话回家”（phoning home）接受指令，这会留下特征。但现在的恶意软件只是一个**提示词**（Prompt），它被植入你的电脑后，会自主与 LLM 交互，根据你的电脑环境生成唯一的指纹。它不需要联系控制者就能自主寻找目标，就像一个不需要返回基地的“潜伏特工”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**George Kurtz**: Traditionally, malware would phone home. Now these can run autonomously without ever phoning home, like a sleeper agent. They're prompts, not actually code. It will autonomously interact with an LLM and it will begin to prompt its way to getting to what it needs.

</details>

**Jason**: 这太可怕了。如何应对？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: So scary. How does one counter that?

</details>

**George Kurtz**: 你需要 AI 来对抗 AI。**CrowdStrike** 利用 14 年积累的海量数据集训练我们自己的模型。虽然攻击方式在变，但“抢银行”的本质不变——你总得接触保险箱，总得带走财物。我们训练模型识别这些“攻击指标”（Indicators of Attack），无论你开什么车、穿什么衣服，只要你试图移动财物，我们就能抓住你。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**George Kurtz**: Well, you need AI to counter it. We've leveraged the large data set that we've amassed over 14 years to train our own models. There's only a few ways to break into a bank. At the end of the day, you got to get the money and you got to get out. We've trained our models to look for these indicators of attack.

</details>
