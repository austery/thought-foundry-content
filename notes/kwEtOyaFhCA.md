### 特朗普的资产与利益冲突

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: This was Trump's net worth when he went into office, $2.3 billion. And this is his net worth now, just two years later. $6.5 billion.

</details>

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 是的，我们从未有过一位在任期间经营业务的总统。因此，决策的制定并不是基于对美国人有利，而是基于对他的公司有利。例如，沙特政府为什么要向 **贾里德·库什纳 (Jared Kushner)** 的基金投资 20 亿美元？并不是因为他们只是喜欢库什纳，而是因为他是特朗普的女婿。

And so my biggest concern is the deterioration of American democracy. I mean, it's already happening. Most people think democracies end with tanks in the street or somebody shooting up the presidential palace, but actually in the modern world, they mostly end because someone who is legitimately elected begins to take apart the system. Trump, he has never cared much one way or the other for American democracy. He admires foreign leaders who have no constraints. And I have a goal that is to remind people of why democracy is important and to pay attention to the ways in which it's declining so that we can fight back. ### 民主如何从内部崩坏

**主持人**: 威权领导人瓦解民主有五大核心手段。你能带我们了解一下吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: So there's five core tactics that autocratic leaders use to dismantle a democracy. Could you walk me through the five tactics?

</details>

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 在回答之前，我想先谈谈我的经历。我最初被**苏联**所吸引，在它还存在时以学生的身份去过那里。我有幸目睹了它的解体。当时我是驻华沙的记者，见证了**华沙条约组织**的终结。

在那之后，我花了很多年时间写历史书，试图解释少数人如何维持对如此广阔空间的控制。我一直以为自己是在写关于遥远过去的故事，分析一个已不复存在的系统。但过去十年里，我发现许多我以为已经结束、属于另一个时代的现象又回来了。

现代民主的终结大多是因为合法当选的人拆解体制，剥夺了确保自由选举能够持续的保障。我开始在多个国家同时观察到这种现象，看到了这种**威权本能**的回潮。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: I started out as somebody who was fascinated by the Soviet Union. I went there when it still existed as a student. I was lucky enough to watch it fall apart. I was a journalist based in Warsaw at the time the Warsaw pack came to an end. Then I spent some years writing history books trying to explain how control was maintained over such a large space by so few people. But all that time, I thought that what I was doing was writing stories about the distant past. I was analyzing a system that didn't exist anymore.

What's happened to me in the last decade is that I've discovered that a lot of what I thought was over and done and belonged to some other era has come back. Most people think democracies end with a coup d'etat or, you know, tanks in the street or somebody shooting up the presidential palace. That actually in the modern world they mostly end because someone who is legitimately elected begins to take apart the system and take away the things that ensure free elections can continue and I started watching that happen in multiple countries at the same time and I saw this authoritarian instinct started to come back and that's what I write about now.

</details>

### 威权主义的五大手段：第一手：腐败与制度捕获

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 第一个手段是**腐败**。腐败存在于任何政治体制中，但在威权社会中，腐败更为猖獗，因为法律系统受控。例如，特朗普接管了**美国司法部**，并安插了效忠者，试图以此起诉他的敌人。在正常的民主制度下，司法部和 **FBI** 会调查高层腐败，但现在我们看不到这种情况发生了。

这与过去不同。我们以前从未见过有人试图利用白宫以这种方式赚钱。我们没有一个完全政治化的公务员体系和 FBI 来规避调查。腐败也是一种工具，总统可以向人们提供：顺从我，不批评我，你的生意就会兴旺。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: Well, corruption, we've done that one already. Corruption you have in any political system, and you often have it in democracies, too. But in an autocratic society, you have more corruption because the legal system is controlled. And so what you have for example in the United States the fact that Donald Trump has taken over our department of justice and has installed loyalists who are looking among other things for example to prosecute his enemies just because they're his enemies. That means you have a check and balance. Normally if there was high level corruption in the White House or in the administration you would have people inside the Department of Justice and the FBI who would investigate it. But now we don't have that happening. 

We didn't have anybody try to use the White House to make money in this way before. So, hard for me to say what would have happened in like, I don't know, the Clinton administration, but we didn't have a completely politicized civil service, completely politicized FBI who would avoid, you know, any kind of investigation. And so corruption is a particular symptom of authoritarianism, and it's also a tool. You know, it's something that the president can offer people. You get along with me, you don't criticize me, your business will prosper. You know, you will get government contracts.

</details>

**主持人**: 这就是为什么我们看到那些**科技巨头 CEO** 频繁出入白宫并对他大加赞赏的原因吗？如果我们翻看他们几年前的推文，他们都曾对他发表过极其负面的评价。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: Is that what we're seeing with all these big tech CEOs that seem to be going frequently to the White House and saying wonderful things about him and his support and having dinner with him and none of them speaking out? But if you look at their Twitter feeds a couple years ago, they were all saying the most horrific things about Trump.

</details>

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 是的，他们已经明白，如果这就是美国政府的运作方式，你就必须向总统卑躬屈膝。如果你必须为他的白宫重建基金或就职典礼捐款，你就会去做。

政府监管的受益者本应是普通美国人，而不是总统、他的家人和随从。这是美国政治的一个巨大转变。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: Yes. I mean they've understood that, you know, if this is going to be an American administration that you have to genuflect to the president, you have to be sycophantic to the president in order to get business deals, then they'll do it. If you have to donate to his White House reconstruction fund, which many of them have done, then you'll do it. If you have to donate to his inauguration, you'll do it. It's a question of who is supposed to be the beneficiary of government regulation. It's supposed to be Americans. The beneficiary is not supposed to be, as I said, the president and his family and his entourage. And that is a big shift in American politics.

</details>

### 科技巨头的状态博弈

我猜这是因为这些人的“状态 (Status)”博弈。如果 **Sam Altman** 发表负面言论，不仅会损害业务，更会损害他在同僚中的地位。他会输给 **Anthropic**、**xAI** 或 **Gemini**。在这些科技寡头的圈子里，输掉这场游戏比损失金钱更令他们痛苦。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: When I look back at someone like the CEO of OpenAI's statements on Trump, if you go back to 2016, he said he was an unprecedented threat to America and called him a potential disaster for the American economy. He said he was irresponsible in the way dictators are and compared his rhetoric to the big lie tactics used by historical authoritarians like Hitler. And then I see him side by side at the White House saying nice things about him and saying nothing critical at all.

I think that being rich for these people is actually just a proxy of status. And it's quite clear to me that if someone like Sam Altman was to say anything negative about Trump, it would of course hurt his business, but actually it would hurt something more, which is his status. He would lose to Anthropic and xAI and Gemini. To lose in your category of peers, which all these sort of tech oligarchs are in, would hurt more than anything. I think it would hurt more than losing a gazillion dollars is just like losing the game.

</details>

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 这非常短视。因为最终，如果美国的政治和法律体系发生衰落，受损的将是他们。他们已经习惯了“花钱买路 (Pay to play)”，但这并非长久之计。如果规则改变了呢？

就像俄罗斯的寡头一样，普京可能会说：“我厌倦了这些寡头，我想要另一批。”这种事在中国也发生过。Anthropic 可能已经意识到了这一点，有些律师事务所也意识到了。独立、遵守职业道德、做一个爱国的美国人也是一种竞争优势。

<details>

**Anne Applebaum**: Yeah. There are two things about it. One is it's very shortsighted because ultimately who will suffer if there is a decline in the American political system in the American legal system? I mean it's them. They have gotten used to paying to play in a way, but it's a mug's game. I mean it's fine as long as you're one of the people who are winning, but what if the rules change? 

Oh yeah, like in Russia with the oligarchs. I'm sick of these oligarchs. I want different oligarchs. And that happened in China, too. The second argument is, and I think Anthropic might have figured this out already and some of the law firms have figured it out. There's also a game to be made by saying no I'm independent. We have our own corporate rules. We have our own legal code of ethics and we're going to behave as patriotic Americans and then you attract business.

</details>

### 第二手：干预选举与选区划分

**主持人**: 那第三个手段呢？

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 是对选举的攻击。当你开始看到破坏和左右选举的企图时，你就知道民主陷入了困境。比如对投票资格的争论、试图阻止某些人投票。

一个典型的例子是匈牙利的 **维克多·奥尔班 (Viktor Orbán)**。他在掌握议会三分之二多数后，不断修改宪法以获得选举优势，重新划分选区并改变计票方式。在美国，我们称之为 **杰利蝾螈 (Gerrymandering)**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: When you begin to see attempts to corrupt and shape elections, this is when you know your democracy is in trouble. When the rules of the election are challenged. When there begin to be arguments about who can vote and attempts to make some people not be allowed to vote when you try to alter the result in some way. I mean any an attack on elections is a classic way in which democratically elected leaders undermine democracy. So an example of this okay Victor Orban who just lost an election in Hungary after 16 years he had two-thirds control of in Hungary if you have two-thirds of the parliament then you can change the constitution so he continually altered the Hungarian constitution in order to give himself electoral advantages.

</details>

**主持人**: 什么是杰利蝾螈？

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 这个词来源于 19 世纪初一位名叫杰利 (Gerry) 的国会议员，他画的一张选区地图看起来像一只蝾螈 (Salamander)。杰利蝾螈是指为了偏袒某一政党而改变选区地图。

一旦地图的设计是为了偏袒某一政党，民主就会开始衰落。在美国，还有对 **选民身份证明 (Voter ID)** 的过度要求。虽然大多数美国人都有驾照，但政府试图将其改为必须使用护照或出生证明，而 60% 的美国人没有护照。这显然是为了让某些群体更难投票。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: Gerrymandering is a great word actually. It comes from a congressman named Gerry in the early 19th century who drew an electoral map which looked like a salamander. And a gerrymandered map in US terms is a electoral map that has been altered to favor one political party. Once you have maps that are designed to favor one party or the other, then you begin to get real democratic decline.

In the US, they want to change the law so that you either have to use a passport or a birth certificate. And most Americans don't have passports. I think 60% don't. Many people have lost or never had their birth certificates. If you passed a law like that, it would make it much more difficult for some people to vote, especially certain kinds of people.

</details>

### 第三手：人事操控与政治化官僚机构

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 第三个手段是**人事操控**，即将现代民主国家的公务员体系政治化。

在一个功能正常的民主国家，你需要专家。衡量空气污染的人应该是空气污染专家，而不是总统的亲戚；监管保险市场的人应该是懂保险的人，而不是副总统的好朋友。在腐败的威权体制中，这些职位都是由忠诚者担任的。

我们看到特朗普对 **美联储 (Fed)** 主席 **杰罗姆·鲍威尔 (Jerome Powell)** 施压。他想要的是能代表其个人利益、家庭利益或党派利益的官员，而不是代表全体美国人利益的官员。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: Personnel. This is civil service in a modern democracy. In a 21st century democracy, government does a lot of things. It manages the road system, regulates insurance markets, measures pollution. It's very important that those people be people who know how to do them. You want the person who's measuring air pollution to be an expert in air pollution. You don't want them to be the president's cousin. In corrupt autocracies, that is who gets those jobs.

He doesn't like Jerome Powell in the Fed, right? And so he's tried to undermine Jerome Powell. What Trump wants is to have civil servants who are historically independent, and that includes the chairman of the Fed. In a failing democracy, you have people whose interests are not everybody in the country, but their interests are the president, his family, his party.

</details>

### 第四手：信息管制与媒体所有权

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 第四是**信息管制**。所有独裁政权都寻求控制信息。中国是最极端的例子，自 90 年代起就构建了可以被政府控制的整个互联网。

在美国，虽然是最开放的民主国家，但特朗普政府正试图以新的方式塑造信息空间。如今的媒体控制并不一定是靠审查员划掉报纸上的句子，而是通过**所有权层面的控制**。

在匈牙利或土耳其，领导层会鼓励或帮助亲信商业集团收购媒体。特朗普似乎也在尝试类似的事情，无论是针对 **TikTok**、CBS 还是 CNN。这种非自由的本能是为了控制言论，无论是左翼还是右翼版本，真正的言论自由捍卫者应该是那些愿意在双方都出现问题时都勇于指责的人。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: Information. All dictatorships seek to control information. In China, the entire internet since the 1990s has been constructed so that the government can control it. But even inside the United States, you can see the Trump administration seeking to shape the information space in new ways. Nowadays that's not how media control works. In Orban's Hungary or Erdogan's Turkey what happens is that the leadership encourages or helps business people or groups close to them to acquire media properties. So they do it through the level of media ownership.

And I agree with you that it is an illiberal instinct to try to control speech. And there's a left-wing version of it and there's a right-wing version of it. And the people who are really in favor of free speech are the people who are willing to call it out on both sides.

</details>

### 第五手：物理胁迫与暴力特权

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 第五是**物理胁迫**，即对强力部门和暴力的控制。

大多数威权政权迟早会建立某种物理上的压迫系统。在美国，**移民海关执法局 (ICE)** 的使用方式已经远远超出了以往任何移民机构的范畴。他们戴着面具，穿着军事制服，开着没有标记的车，不对市长或州长负责，而只对国土安全部和总统负责。

当一支武装力量拥有法律豁免权，当它能伤害普通公民而不必付出代价时，它就不再是为美国人服务，而是为执政党的利益服务。这实际上已经变成了一支**准军事力量 (Paramilitary)**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: Control over power ministries and the use of violence. Most autocracies sooner or later want to create some kind of repressive system that's also physical. So it's not just control of the information space. There's also some element of coercion. 

Look at what ICE looks like. They are masked. They are wearing military uniforms. They're not accountable to anybody. They're not accountable to the mayor or to the governor. And that gives them a kind of impunity. When you have a military force that's above the law, then it's really a paramilitary. If you have a police force that can harm ordinary citizens and not pay any price for it and isn't accountable, then you're not serving Americans. You're serving the interests of the ruling party.

</details>

### 全球秩序的瓦解：格陵兰岛阴影

**主持人**: 你认为美国以外的世界正在发生什么？

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 每个人都在**对冲风险 (Hedging)**。世界各地的国家都在寻找替代方案。加拿大正在与欧盟建立安全关系，欧盟和印度签署了贸易协定。

对欧洲许多人来说，转折点是**格陵兰岛事件**。当时特朗普暗示要“入侵”丹麦（以获取格陵兰岛）。这是一个非常亲美的国家，但他们不得不突然开始计划：我们要不要炸毁格陵兰岛的机场？我们要不要射击美国士兵？美国人要向我们开火吗？

那是一个创伤性的时刻，每个人都意识到：这是一个不稳定的强权，他们不可靠，我们需要替代方案。因此，由于美国变得不可靠，全球都在重新排列。这对美国人来说是非常坏的消息，因为美国的繁荣基于其在**全球贸易和安全**中的主导地位。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: What you're watching is everybody all over the world hedging. Everybody is looking for alternatives. I think the breaking point for a lot of people in Europe was Greenland. You had the president of the United States saying he was going to invade Denmark. Trump was kind of hinting it in public. That meant that the Danes said, "Okay, we're preparing for a US invasion." And they had to suddenly imagine a real war with their closest ally. No one has recovered. Everybody remembers that moment and said, "Okay, this is an unstable power. They can't be relied on. We need alternatives."

</details>

### 历史必然性与个人能动性

**主持人**: 有一种观点认为，帝国的寿命大约是 250 年。从 1776 年到现在，美国正好 250 岁。我们是否处于一个预测性的、不可避免的衰落周期中？

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 我非常强烈地不相信**历史必然性**。这种观点非常危险。无论你认为民主一定会崩坏，还是像 90 年代那样认为民主一定会胜利，任何“必然性”都会消解你的行动意愿。

明天和明年会发生什么，完全取决于我们今天做了什么。美国是否能作为民主国家生存下去，取决于美国人的选择、他们的辩论和公民参与程度。

历史中存在很多偶然。如果当年叶利钦选择的不是普京，而是更有开放意识的涅姆佐夫，我们现在的世界会完全不同。我不相信不可逆转的周期。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anne Applebaum**: I don't believe in historical inevitability. And I think is very dangerous. Anytime you think that something is inevitable that takes away your willingness to act. What happens tomorrow and next year is completely dependent on what we do today. Whether the United States survives as a democracy or not depends on choices Americans make, not some historical rule that some very brilliant political scientist invented. I don't believe in inevitable cycles.

</details>

### 结语：守护“水”的鱼

**主持人**: 作为一个生活在民主制度下的普通人，我们能做些什么？

**安妮·阿普尔鲍姆**: 我们非常幸运，生活在可以投票的社会。当人们变得虚无主义，说“他们都一样，投谁都一样”时，这正是威权者希望看到的。普京和中国政府都希望他们的人民远离政治。
