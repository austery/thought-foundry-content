<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Natalie Kitro**: I'm Natalie Kitro. This is the Daily. In the days since deposing Nicholas Maduro, President Trump has given several justifications for his dramatic action in Venezuela.
</details>

**特朗普**: 第一，毒品正大量涌入这个国家。你们知道的。第二，人口正大量涌入这个国家。但特朗普最关注的、他一直强调为其野心核心的是——石油。我们要拿回那些坦白说我们早就该拿回的石油。我们要拿回那些从我们手中被夺走的东西。他们夺走了我们的石油工业。那个石油工业全是我们建立的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Donald Trump**: Number one, the drugs are pouring into the country. You know that. Number two, the people are pouring into the country. But the thing Trump is most focused on, the thing he keeps pointing to as central to his ambitions, is oil. We're going to take back the oil that frankly we should have taken back a long time ago. We're taking back what was taken from us. They took our oil industry. We built that entire oil industry.

**娜塔莉·基特罗**: 具体来说，就是向美国公司开放委内瑞拉广阔的油田。今天，我的同事**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**将解释特朗普声称“这是我们的石油”背后的历史，以及真正拿回它需要付出什么。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

</details>

### 委内瑞拉石油起源

**娜塔莉·基特罗**: 阿纳托利，欢迎回到节目。让我们从一个基本问题开始：为什么特朗普如此关注委内瑞拉的石油？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Natalie Kitro**: Anatoli, welcome back to the show. Let's just start with a basic question, which is why is Trump so focused on Venezuelan oil?
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 委内瑞拉是一个典型的**石油国家**。它可以说是一个由石油建立、通过石油定义自己的国家。如果你是一位通过自然资源、能源地缘政治和资源控制权来看待世界的美国总统，那么委内瑞拉就是你的天然目标。正如我的同事丽贝卡·埃利奥特所写，现在是世界上最大的产油国——美国，瞄准了世界上石油储量最大的国家——委内瑞拉。这在某种程度上是一种天然的关系，一场由戏剧、合作、竞争和各种冲突塑造的“探戈”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: Venezuela is a quintessential petrostate. It is arguably the first petrostate. It is a nation that has been built by oil that defines itself through oil. And if you are an American president who sees the world through the prism of natural resources, who sees the world through the geopolitics of energy and control of energy resources, then Venezuela is your natural target. As my colleague Rebecca Elliot wrote, you have a country that is today the world's largest oil producer, the United States, targeting the country with the world's biggest oil reserves, Venezuela. It is in a way a natural relationship, a natural tango, shaped by drama, cooperation, competition, and all sorts of conflicts.
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 对我们记者来说，整场戏中最令人震惊的元素之一，就是特朗普对自己的动机表现得多么直白。针对马杜罗的整个行动，核心驱动力就是石油。特朗普和他的盟友，比如高级顾问**斯蒂芬·米勒**，一直在说这是美国应得的。米勒曾说：“美国的汗水、智慧和辛劳创造了委内瑞拉的石油工业。其暴政式的征收是有记录以来对美国财富和财产的最大规模窃取。”这种说法虽然夸张且具有煽动性，但确实包含了一丝事实。要理解他的逻辑，你必须回到一个世纪前委内瑞拉石油工业的起点。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: For us as reporters, one of the most shocking elements of this whole drama is just how explicit Trump is being about his motives. The driving aspect of this whole campaign against Maduro is about oil. Trump and his allies, like top advisor Steven Miller, have been saying that the US is reclaiming what's rightfully theirs. Miller said, "American sweat, ingenuity, and toil created the oil industry in Venezuela. Its tyrannical expropriation was the largest recorded theft of American wealth and property." It's wild and incendiary, but it does contain a grain of truth. To understand where he comes from, you have to go back to the beginning of Venezuela's oil industry a century ago.
</details>

### 资源民族主义兴起

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 20世纪20年代，随着美国石油工人的大规模迁入，委内瑞拉的石油工业真正腾飞。美国公司经营油井，美国工人按照美国模式建造城镇——有网格化的街道、郊区住宅、学校、棒球场和教堂。这些城镇逐渐发展成模仿美国生活方式的大都市。委内瑞拉基本上是通过美国石油工人的视角进入了现代性。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: The industry really takes off in the 1920s, propelled by mass migration of American oil workers. American oil companies operate the wells, and those workers started building towns built in the American way with American grids, suburban houses, schools, baseball, and churches. Those towns gradually grew into cities imitating American way of life. Venezuela basically entered modernity through the prism of how American oil workers saw the world.
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 到20世纪50年代，委内瑞拉已成为世界上最大的石油出口国，极其富有。当时的独裁者**马科斯·佩雷斯·希门尼斯**将石油财富投入基础设施，修建了隧道、桥梁和高速公路。委内瑞拉成了该地区的现代性麦加。但随后，委内瑞拉人开始质疑这种现状，即大部分财富流向了外国公司。委内瑞拉由此成为了**资源民族主义**运动的推动者。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: By the 1950s, Venezuela was the world's largest oil exporter, making tremendous amounts of money. The dictator at the time, Marcos Pérez Jiménez, poured that oil wealth into infrastructure, building tunnels, bridges, and highways. Venezuela became this mecca of modernity. But then, Venezuelans began to question the status quo which gives the bulk of the wealth towards the corporations. Venezuela really becomes the driver of a movement called resource nationalism.
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 50年代末，委内瑞拉政治家**胡安·巴勃罗·佩雷斯·阿方索**前往中东，说服其他产油国联合起来维护主权，减少公司的影响。这促成了**欧佩克 (OPEC)** 的诞生。随后在70年代，亲美的总统**卡洛斯·安德烈斯·佩雷斯**决定将石油工业国有化，成立了国有的委内瑞拉国家石油公司，即 **PDVSA**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: In late 50s, a Venezuelan statesman called Juan Pablo Pérez Alfonzo travels to the Middle East and convinces other oil nations that if we bond together, we can assert our sovereignty and minimize the influence of corporations. This becomes OPEC. Then in the 1970s, a pro-US president Carlos Andrés Pérez decides to nationalize Venezuela's oil industry, creating a state-owned company known as PDVSA.
</details>

### 查韦斯与工业国有化

**娜塔莉·基特罗**: 这种国有化听起来很成功，它为委内瑞拉带来了教育和扶贫资金。但你提到这既是福音也是诅咒。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Natalie Kitro**: That nationalization sounds successful, fueling education and poverty alleviation. But you described it as both a blessing and a curse.
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 是的，委内瑞拉对石油产生了依赖。80年代油价暴跌，经济模型出现裂痕。政府的对策是开发**奥里诺科石油带**，那里有世界最大的储备，但石油像淤泥一样难以处理，需要美国公司的尖端技术。于是有了“石油开放”政策，允许外国公司主导开发。但这并未带来持久的经济稳定，反而促成了**乌戈·查韦斯**的崛起。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: Yes, Venezuela became addicted to oil. In the 80s, oil prices dropped and cracks appeared in the economic model. The government's solution was the Orinoco oil belt, which contains the world's largest reserves, but it's locked in sludgy tar that requires cutting-edge technology from American corporations. This led to the "oil opening," allowing foreign companies to take the lead. But economic stability never came back, fueling the rise of Hugo Chávez.
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 查韦斯在1998年上台，他的口号是“委内瑞拉的石油属于委内瑞拉人民”。他解雇了 PDVSA 的专业技术官僚，代之以政治任命人员。2002年的大罢工后，他开除了数万名技术骨干，将 PDVSA 变成了政治工具，甚至让石油公司去开超市、办幼儿园。腐败激增，技术流失，产量开始下降。2007年，他发起了新一轮国有化，强制要求外国公司交出控股权。**埃克森美孚**和**康菲石油**拒绝并撤离，随后发起了索赔数十亿美元的法律诉讼。这就是特朗普所说的“窃取”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: Chávez won in 1998 with the message "Venezuelan oil belongs to Venezuelan people." He fired PDVSA's technocrats and replaced them with political appointees. After the 2002 strike, he fired tens of thousands of skilled workers, turning PDVSA into a political tool that even ran supermarkets and daycares. Corruption increased, expertise was lost, and production declined. In 2007, he launched another wave of nationalizations, forcing foreign companies to give up control. ExxonMobil and ConocoPhillips refused and left, starting legal processes for billions in damages. This is what Trump refers to as "theft."
</details>

### 经济崩溃与工业废墟

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 查韦斯2013年去世后，他建立的脆弱系统像纸牌屋一样倒塌。委内瑞拉经历了现代史上非战争状态下最大的经济危机，通货膨胀率飙升至 700%。石油工业也成了空壳，油井干涸，人们甚至拆除管道和设备当废铁卖。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: When Chávez died in 2013, the rickety system he built fell apart like a house of cards. Venezuela entered the biggest economic crisis in modern history outside a war zone, with inflation hitting 700%. The oil industry became a shell of itself; wells dried up, and people were taking apart infrastructure for scrap.
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 特朗普在第一任期内通过制裁进一步打击了马杜罗政府，使其无法向西方出售石油。这迫使马杜罗改变策略，任命**德尔茜·罗德里格斯**负责经济。她发起了“秘密私有化”：名义上石油仍属于国家，但实际上通过秘密合同让外国公司获得控制权和更大利润。到2025年底，产量回升到了每日120万桶，虽然远低于查韦斯时代前，但已有显著改善。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: Trump's sanctions during his first term further hit Maduro's government, making it illegal to sell oil to the West. This forced Maduro to change tack, entrusting Delcy Rodríguez to fix the economy. She launched "stealth privatization": on paper, oil belongs to the people, but secret contracts give foreign companies control and a bigger share of profits. By late 2025, production reached 1.2 million barrels per day—a fraction of the past, but a significant improvement.
</details>

### 重振工业的巨大挑战

**娜塔莉·基特罗**: 特朗普现在说美国公司将重振委内瑞拉石油工业。这需要什么条件？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Natalie Kitro**: Trump is now saying US companies will revive the industry. What would that take?
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 这需要数百亿美元的投资。德尔茜·罗德里格斯之前只是摘取了“低垂的果实”，修复了容易修复的部分。要进一步提升产量，需要大规模投资。石油工业不是水龙头，一旦设施腐烂，修复起来既昂贵又耗时。而且，美国石油高管们非常谨慎。**雪佛龙**虽然一直留在那里，但埃克森美孚明确表示，委内瑞拉目前“不具备投资价值”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: It would take tens of billions of dollars. Delcy Rodríguez took the "low-hanging fruits." To significantly improve production requires massive investment. The oil industry isn't a spigot; once infrastructure decays, it's expensive and time-consuming to repair. American oil executives are cautious. While Chevron stayed, ExxonMobil said Venezuela today is "uninvestable."
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 挑战是多方面的：首先是**政治稳定性**。德尔茜目前依靠美国军舰的威慑力统治，但国内仍有许多武装组织和政治派系。其次是**法律保障**，公司不希望再次被没收。最后是**能源转型**，公司在考虑10到15年后，世界是否还需要这么多石油。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: The challenges are many: first, political stability. Delcy rules through the threat of US force, but internal rivals remain. Second, legal guarantees—companies don't want to be burned again. Finally, the energy revolution—companies wonder if this oil will even be needed in 15 years.
</details>

### 地缘政治的杠杆作用

**娜塔莉·基特罗**: 如果一切顺利，美国能得到什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Natalie Kitro**: If things work out, what does the US gain?
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 美国将获得巨大的石油来源，这意味着全球油价和美国国内油价都会下降。这对特朗普的国内政治至关重要。在地缘政治上，这给了美国巨大的权力。如果特朗普能通过控制委内瑞拉的石油供应来设定全球油价，**俄罗斯**将很难在不屈服于美国需求的情况下实现其目标。**中国**虽然在转向电动车，但在未来几十年仍将依赖石油进口，如果特朗普控制了来源，他就能迫使北京让步。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: The US would gain a massive source of oil, meaning global and domestic gas prices would fall. This is crucial for Trump's domestic politics. Geopolitically, it gives the US massive power. If Trump can set global oil prices by turning the Venezuelan spigot on and off, Russia will find it difficult to achieve its goals without bowing to US needs. China, while moving to EVs, will remain reliant on oil imports for decades; if Trump controls the source, he can force Beijing to bow.
</details>

**阿纳托利·库尔马纳耶夫**: 目前，委内瑞拉临时政府似乎愿意听从特朗普的吩咐，甚至准备修改法律。但历史表明，这种宏大的长期计划很少能完全按预想实现。委内瑞拉石油工业的未来，可能与华盛顿现在的设想大相径庭。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Anatoli Kurmanaev**: For now, the Venezuelan interim government seems willing to do Trump's bidding, even preparing legal reforms. But history shows that such ambitious, long-term plans rarely turn out as envisioned. The future of Venezuelan oil may look very different from how Washington sees it now.
</details>
