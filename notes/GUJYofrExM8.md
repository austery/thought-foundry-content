**Tracy Mumford**: 随着与伊朗的战争进入第四周，燃料价格高企。来自海湾地区的石油供应基本中断。美国人很快就可能在杂货账单上感受到这种影响。首先要关注的是覆盆子。覆盆子可以说是新鲜农产品中的“戏剧女王”。它们需要快速运输，并全程冷藏。即使是速度更快的飞机，也需要保持低温。这一切使得覆盆子成为杂货店里对燃料最敏感的商品之一，这意味着它们可能成为伊朗战争影响价格的早期信号。覆盆子本已价格不菲，但根据联邦数据显示，自1月份以来，新鲜浆果的批发价已翻倍。美国农业部现在警告称，今年食品账单总额可能上涨多达6%。这不仅仅是运输燃油的问题。战争可能推高塑料包装的成本，而其中很多都以石油为基础。此外，全球大部分化肥也滞留在海湾地区，这也可能影响农作物收成。所有这些都叠加在关税、紧张的劳动力市场以及其他已导致价格上涨的因素之上。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

As the war with Iran hits the 4-we mark, fuel prices are high. The flow of oil from the Gulf remains largely cut off. And Americans will likely start to feel it soon in their grocery bills. Even on planes, which are a faster ride, they need to be kept cool. Raspberries were already expensive, but according to federal data, the wholesale price of fresh berries has doubled since January, and the Department of Agriculture is now warning that food bills overall could climb as much as 6% this year. It's not just about gas for transport. The war could drive up the cost of plastic packaging, a lot of which is petroleum based. Also, a lot of the world's fertilizer is stuck in the Gulf, and that could affect crops, too. All of this comes on top of tariffs, a tight labor market, and other factors that were already contributing to higher prices.

### 科技公司自建数据中心电厂引发担忧

**Rebecca Elliot**: 在美国各地，随着大型科技公司竞相展开一场全面的**AI**冲刺，数据中心如雨后春笋般出现在昔日的牧场、森林和农田上。这些庞大的设施已经引发了关于它们需要多少电力和水资源的担忧。但《纽约时报》也在追踪一种在它们旁边发生的新型争议性建筑。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Across the US, with big tech companies competing in an allout AI sprint, data centers have been popping up on what used to be pastures and forests and farmland. The massive facilities have already raised concerns about how much power and water they need. But the Times has also been tracking a controversial new type of construction that's happening right next to them.

</details>

**Rebecca Elliot**: 让我感到震惊的是，科技公司放弃电网的速度有多快，并说：“你知道吗？我们要建自己的发电厂。” **丽贝卡·艾略特（Rebecca Elliot）**是《纽约时报》商业版块的一名记者。她说，由于连接新数据中心到常规电网需要多年的基础设施建设，公司们正争相建立巨大的燃气轮机和发动机来自己发电。例如，在**俄亥俄州哥伦布市（Columbus, Ohio）**附近，有三座专为数据中心服务的燃气发电厂正在建设中，并且正在规划第四座。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

What was really shocking to me is just how quickly tech companies are ditching the power grid and saying, "You know what? We're going to build our own power plants." Rebecca Elliot is a reporter on the Times business desk. She says that because it can take years to get all the infrastructure set for a new data center to be connected to the normal power grid, companies are rushing to set up giant gas turbines and engines to make their own electricity. Near Columbus, Ohio, for example, three gas fired power plants exclusively for data centers are currently under construction and a fourth one is being planned.

</details>

**Rebecca Elliot**: 这些离网发电厂周边的社区所担忧的一个问题是，这些新的发电设施将如何影响空气质量。在许多情况下，所使用的设备比大型发电厂通常使用的设备污染更严重。除了对空气污染的担忧外，一些工厂旁新建大型发电厂的居民还在抱怨噪音污染。一位密西西比州的居民告诉《纽约时报》，他一直在记录他家内部的测量数据，这些数据相当于24/7不间断的吸尘器噪音。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

One of the concerns that communities around these off-grid power plants have is how these new generating facilities will affect air quality. In many cases, the equipment being used is more polluting than what you would typically find at a big power plant. In addition to worries about air pollution, some of the people who've had these big power plants pop up next door are speaking out about noise pollution, too. One Mississippi resident told the Times that he's been recording measurements inside his house that are roughly equivalent to the sound of a vacuum cleaner going 24/7.

</details>

**Rebecca Elliot**: 面对噪音投诉，建造该发电厂的公司今年早些时候安装了一个价值700万美元的防噪音屏障。
