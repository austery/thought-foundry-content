<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tracy Mumford**: In its ruling yesterday, the court's conservative majority threw out a Louisiana voting map, saying that lawmakers there illegally used race as a consideration when drawing a majority black congressional district. Under the Voting Rights Act, states across the country have long done what Louisiana did. They created districts where non-white voters are the majority to protect those voters ability to elect candidates of their choice. It was seen as a crucial way to try and undo decades of discrimination and disenfranchisement under Jim Crow. But the Supreme Court's majority now says that in the decades since the VRA was passed back in 1965, the country's made so much progress when it comes to racial discrimination in elections that the act has essentially served its purpose.

</details>

**Tracy Mumford**: 有了这一决定，今后可能有意识地创建少数族裔占多数的选区将变得更加困难。在措辞强硬的反对比见中，大法官 **Elena Kagan** 表示，这项裁决将削弱国会授予的选举种族平等这一基本权利。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tracy Mumford**: With this decision, it could be harder going forward to intentionally create majority minority districts. In a strongly worded descent, Justice Elena Kagan said the ruling will set back the foundational right Congress granted of racial equality in elections.

</details>

**Tracy Mumford**: 至于这对于美国人即将参加的下一场大选——**中期选举**——意味着什么，还有待观察。我的同事 **Nick Corsini** 专门负责投票方面的报道，他说许多州即使立法者想改，也没有时间在 11 月之前修改地图。但可以肯定的是，过去一年定义了美国政治的全美选区重划战争，将在 **2028 年大选**前全面继续。原本制约某些州的“护栏”现在因为最高法院的裁决而消失了。因此，南部各州的共和党人很可能会重划地图，潜在目标锁定在民主党人身上。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tracy Mumford**: In terms of what this could mean for the next big election Americans will be voting in the midterms, that remains to be seen. My colleague Nick Corsini, who covers voting, says a lot of states don't have time to change their maps before November, even if lawmakers want to. What is clear though is that the national redistricting wars that defined politics in this country for the past year will continue in earnest ahead of the 2028 election. The guard rails that had kept some states in check are now gone because of this Supreme Court ruling. So, it's likely that Republicans in states across the South will redraw their maps, potentially targeting Democrats.

</details>

### AI 聊天机器人的生物武器风险

**Tracy Mumford**: 去年夏天的一个晚上，斯坦福大学的微生物学和生物安全专家 **David Relman** 博士正在家里办公，他被一家 AI 公司聘请来对其中的聊天机器人进行压力测试。Relman 博士受雇要做的是尝试询问聊天机器人一个他非常熟悉的恶名昭彰的病原体。聊天机器人不仅向他描述了使其对已知疗法产生抗性的方法，更糟糕的是，据他所说，它还描述了如何利用这种“超级细菌”发动袭击，以及如何最大化伤亡并最小化被抓获的几率。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tracy Mumford**: One evening last summer, Dr. David Relman, who's a microbiology and biosecurity expert at Stanford University, was working in his home office, and he had been hired by an AI company to pressure test its chatbot. What Dr. Relman had been hired to do was to try asking the chatbot about an infamous pathogen that he was very familiar with. And not only did the chatbot describe to him ways to make it resistant to known treatments, but worse, according to him, it described how to use the superbug in an attack and how to maximize casualties while minimizing his chance of being caught.

</details>

**Tracy Mumford**: 我的同事 **Gabriel Dance** 一直在调查 AI 聊天机器人的安全防护措施。他说 Relman 博士对他使用聊天机器人的经历感到震惊，因为它似乎在提供一份**生物战蓝图**。Relman 并不是该领域唯一感到担忧的专家。其他几位被领先 AI 公司聘请来审查聊天机器人潜在安全风险的专家也与《时报》分享了对话记录，其中包括聊天机器人解释如何使用气象气球在美国城市上空传播生物载荷。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tracy Mumford**: My colleague Gabriel Dance has been looking into the guard rails that AI chatbots have or don't have. He says Dr. Relman was shaken by his experience with the chatbot, which seem to be offering a blueprint for biological warfare. And Relman's not the only specialist in that field who's concerned. Several other experts who've been hired by leading AI companies to vet their chatbots for potential safety risks have shared transcripts with the Times that include things like a chatbot explaining how to use a weather balloon to spread biological payloads over a US city.

</details>

**Gabriel Dance**: “既然我自己不是生物学家或病毒学家，我拿着这些记录给六位以上的相关领域专家看。所有的科学家都在某种程度上感到了不安，但其中一些人的担忧程度远高于其他人。”

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Gabriel Dance**: So being that I'm not a biologist or viologist myself, I took these transcripts and put them in front of more than a half dozen experts in those fields. All of the scientists found them concerning to some degree, but some of them were much more concerned than others.

</details>

**Gabriel Dance**: “一方面，有几位专家告诉我，这些聊天机器人基本上是在提供通往危险生物武器的路线图。但其他科学家则表示，聊天机器人其实只不过是美化版的谷歌搜索引擎，其中大部分信息在互联网上已经可以找到了，发动重大生物攻击的可能性仍然非常低。但是，即便是一次生物攻击也可能是灾难性的。”

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Gabriel Dance**: On one hand, I had several experts telling me that these chat bots were offering basically road maps to very dangerous biological weapons. But other scientists said that chatbots were really nothing more than glorified Google searching machines. That much of the information is already available on the internet and the likelihood of a major biological attack remains very unlikely, but even one biological attack could be catastrophic.

</details>

**Tracy Mumford**: Gabriel 表示，一些专家正在推动公司审查和封锁大量的生物信息，以试图防范这类潜在威胁。但其他人则认为这是反应过度，限制 AI 获取此类信息可能会扼杀突破性的医学研究，例如开发新药。在回应《时报》关于聊天机器人记录的提问时，**Anthropic**、**OpenAI** 和 **Google** 都辩称，这些机器人并没有提供足够的细节让任何人造成伤害。他们表示，他们一直在不断改进系统，以平衡潜在的利益和风险。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tracy Mumford**: Gabriel says that some experts are pushing for companies to censor swaths of biological information to try and head off these kinds of potential threats, but others say that's an overreaction and that restricting that info from AI could stifle breakthrough medical research like for developing new drugs. In response to questions from the Times about the chatbot transcripts, Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google all argued the bots didn't provide enough detail to allow anyone to cause harm. And they said they were constantly improving their systems to balance potential benefits and risks.

</details>
