### 核心原则一：体面商数 (DQ)

**Ajay Banga**: 所以，首先，我称之为“体面商数”（Decency Quotient，简称 DQ）。我在成长过程中很快就了解到智商（IQ）和情商（EQ）很重要。但我认为，现在领导力所需要的是所谓的“体面商数”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: So first, what I call the decency quotient, DQ. I learned very quickly that IQ and EQ were important while I was growing up. But I believe that now what you need in leadership is what's called the decency quotient.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 我就是这么命名的。我当时在一次全体会议上开玩笑，刚听完 **沃伦·巴菲特 (Warren Buffett)** 谈论 DQ。在他的语境下，DQ 指的是 **Dairy Queen**（一家冰淇淋店）。所以你可以看到我的思维方式。我当时在寻找一些东西来谈论，想告诉人们我的意思，并解释我对文化的理解。这时，“体面商数”这个想法就产生了。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: And that's I I just named it that. I was making a joke in a town hall and I'd just come out of listening to Warren Buffett talk about DQ. In his case, that was Dairy Queen, the ice cream. And so you could see how my mind works. And I was looking for something to talk about to tell people what I meant and to give a word to what I was trying to explain to them about culture. And this idea of decency quotient came.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 但“体面”（decency）并非仅仅是“友善”（being nice）。它实际上是关于“公平”（fair）、“直接”（direct）和“一致”（consistent）。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: But decency is not about being nice. It's actually about being fair, direct, and consistent.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 对你的团队说实话，即使在不舒服的时候。清晰地给出反馈。在困难时发声。尊重他人。所有这些。这实际上并不会减慢组织的步伐。它会起到相反的作用，因为因为人们信任这个系统，它能改进决策，因为人们愿意挑战并被挑战。所以，如果你做得对，它实际上会以正确的方式运作。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: Tell the truth to your people even when it's uncomfortable. Give that feedback with clarity. Speak up when it's difficult. Treat people with respect. All that. It doesn't actually slow the organization down. It does quite the opposite because it allows you to move faster because people trust the system and it improves decisions because people are willing to challenge and to be challenged and so it actually works in the right way if you do it the right way.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 恐惧能驱动行为，但它不能建立判断力、所有权或主动性。体面可以。因此，领导力应该感觉像是一只放在你背上的手，推动你前进，而不是指责你的手指。这就是“体面商数”的全部意义。这是第一点。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: Fear drives behavior for a while but it doesn't build judgment. It doesn't build ownership and it doesn't build initiative. Decency does. And so leadership has to feel like a hand on your back. You got to push people forward but not a finger in their face and that's what decency quotient is all about.

</details>

### 核心原则二：目标清晰 (Clarity of Purpose)

**Ajay Banga**: 第二点是“目标清晰”（clarity of purpose）。在一个大型组织中，我认为最大的文化问题往往不是阻力——詹姆斯（James）可以对此滔滔不绝——而是混乱。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: That's the first thing. The second one is clarity of purpose. In a large organization I think the biggest cultural problem is often not resistance and James can talk to this uh ad nauseum. It's actually confusion more than resistance.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 在 **摩根士丹利 (Morgan Stanley)**，他（詹姆斯）非常清晰地传达了他们的发展重点——即财富管理将是 **摩根士丹利 (Morgan Stanley)** 的增长之道。这改变了一个拥有多种增长想法的组织，使其变得非常专注、非常简单。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: clarity in his case in Morgan Stanley. He very clearly delivered a message of what they were going to focus on which was wealth management as the way that Morgan Stanley would grow.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 我认为简单是关键。而让事情变得简单，实际上是最难做到的。所以，人们认为简单就意味着失去了细节。但实际上，用简单的语言、不带术语地交流，是领导者能做到的最困难的事情。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: Right in and that changed an organization that had multiple ideas of how they would grow to very focused, very simple, very focused. And I think simplicity is the key. And making something simple is actually the hardest thing to do. So people think that in simplicity you've lost detail. Actually conversing in simple terms without jargon is the world's most difficult thing to do as a leader.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 在 **万事达卡 (Mastercard)**，公司不得不重新定义自身，从支付处理器转变为技术平台。我如何简化它？我让所有人围绕一个共同的使命团结起来，那就是“消灭现金 (kill cash)”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: At Mastercard the company had to redefine itself from a payments processor to a technology platform. How did I simplify it? I united everybody around one set of mission and it said kill cash. Just kill cash as compared to thinking about everything else.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 这包括了技术、支付、分析、数据，以及任何你能做的事情，比如金融普惠，帮助弱势群体，将社会保障金发放到他们手中，确定数字身份，所有这些都包含在“消灭现金”的理念中。这给了他们非常明确的方向感。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: And that included technology. It included payments, included analytics, it included data, it included anything that you could do, financial inclusion, working with the downtrodden, getting social security payments into their hands, figuring out digital identities, all came inside the idea of killing cash. It gave them a very clear sense of direction.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 所以，在 **世界银行 (World Bank)**，也面临类似的问题：如何消灭贫困？我逐渐认识到，与其关注许多不同的原因，不如这样做：为气候、为性别平等行动，建造一座医院，建立一个技能培训中心，兴建学校。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: So at the World Bank, similar issue, how do you kill poverty? And I've got come around to saying instead of having many different causes, let's do this for climate, let's do that for gender, let's build a hospital, let's do a skilling center, let's do schools.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 归根结底，你努力消灭贫困，是为了给人一份工作。不一定是在大公司或小公司工作，也不一定是创业，或者仅仅是留在他们的小农场，通过与合作社连接技术而获得生产力，并对此感到满意。我只是给你很多种实现目标的方式。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: At the end of the day, what you're trying to do to kill poverty is to give people a job. not necessarily working for a big company or a small company or as an entrepreneur or just staying in their small farm and being content with the productivity they get by connecting up with technology to a cooperative. I'm just giving you many ways to do this.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 关键在于，赚取一份工作带来的收入是驱动人们希望和尊严的最佳方式。如果你仔细想想，我的一位朋友曾说过，贫困既是一种思维状态，也是一种存在状态。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: The point is a job earning is the best way to drive people's hope and dignity. And if you sort of think about this carefully, a friend of mine once said that poverty is both a state of mind and a state of being.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: “存在”（being）可以通过提供补助金、援助、捐赠、慈善捐款来解决。“思维”（mind）则无法通过这些解决。思维需要你每天早上都感到有成效、乐观，并觉得你有未来。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: Being you can solve by giving grants, by giving aid, by giving donations, by writing philanthropic dollars. Mind doesn't get solved with that. Mind needs you to feel productive every morning, optimistic, feeling that you've got a future.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 所以，我认为最重要的事情是，一份工作既能帮助你摆脱“存在”的状态，又能帮助你形成一种“思维”状态，最终将贫困彻底根除。这就是我所领悟到的清晰目标。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: So I think the most important thing is the job gives you both a way to defeat a state of being and a state of mind to drive a nail into the coffin of poverty. That's the clarity that has settled in.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 我认为一旦你开始这样做，你就会获得人们据此做出决策的“倍增效应”。关于清晰度，最后一点是：永远不要厌倦地讨论你决定的那件非常简单的事情，因为领导者听厌了说同样的话，但组织并不会厌倦听。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: And I think once you start doing that you get this multiplier effect of people making decisions on it. The last thing about clarity, just never tire of discussing what you've decided is very simple because leaders get bored saying the same things. Organizations don't get bored hearing it.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 我认为，如果你希望某件事能够深入人心，你必须一遍又一遍地重复它，直到它体现在人们的决策方式中。这就是第二点。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: And I think if you want something to stick, you got to say it again and again and again and again until it shows up in how people make their decisions. That's the that is the second topic.

</details>

### 私营部门与公共部门的差异

**Ajay Banga**: 现在，所有这些都很棒，但这里是私营部门和公共部门之间的背景差异。在私营部门，压力来自市场、股东、利润动机、绩效、竞争、危机。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: So now all this is lovely but here's the context difference between the private sector and the public sector. In the private sector pressure comes from markets comes from shareholders comes from profit motive performance competition crisis.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 你有明确的杠杆：薪酬、招聘、解雇、资本配置。在 **万事达卡 (Mastercard)**，如果有什么东西不起作用，我可以用所有这些杠杆来调整它，快速修复。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: You got clear levers compensation hiring firing capital allocation. At Mastercard if something wasn't working I could use all those levers jiggle with them fix it quickly.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 在公共部门，挑战则完全不同。我们有一个使命，但我们也有 189 个股东，每个股东都有基于其国内政治的、不同且细致的看法，他们每天都带到我的办公室。你们有长期存在的流程。任期很长。风险规避非常真实。我们有点像一个学术机构，试图完成实际工作。所以，你可以想象那有多复杂。而且你无法在一夜之间重置那个系统。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: In the public sector the challenge is completely different. We've got a mission, but we also have 189 shareholders, each with a different and nuanced way of looking based on their domestic politics, which they bring to my office every day. You've got long-standing processes. Tenure is long. Risk aversion is very real. We're kind of like an academic institution trying to get real work done. So, you can imagine how complex that is. And you cannot reset that system overnight.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 薪酬不是你在私营部门拥有的那种杠杆。所以，你只能利用你拥有的所有杠杆。你围绕成果来协调团队。你简化要说的话。简化结构。你明确什么会得到认可。你明确什么会获得资助。这需要更长时间。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ajay Banga**: And compensation is not a lever of the type that you have in the private sector. So you work all the levers you have. You align teams around the outcomes. You simplify what you say. Simplify structures. You become explicit in what gets recognized. You become explicit in what gets funded. It takes longer.

</details>

**Ajay Banga**: 但原则完全相同。如果你不协调整个系统，文化就不会改变。而协调来自于简单、专注，以及你如何谈论你正在做的事情来领导。
