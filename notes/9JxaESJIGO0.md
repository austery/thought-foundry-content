### 加拿大的战略地位

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: Trump threw the election and then thereafter said that Canada should honestly become our 51st state, which is never going to happen. Pierre Polyv, leader of his majesty's loyal opposition. There's a significant probability that you could be Canada's next leader and your team said I can ask you whatever I want. So it appears that the United States have made the decision to kind of go it alone in the world and that is a very big strategic mistake.

</details>

**皮埃尔·波利耶夫**: 在加拿大的案例中，只要美国把我们当朋友对待，我们拥有他们需要的一切。例如，我们拥有世界第四大的**石油储量**。在排名前五的国家中，你认为美国最能依靠谁？是委内瑞拉、沙特阿拉伯、伊朗还是伊拉克？不，是加拿大。如果美国能采取友好的姿态，并与我们达成完全**零关税**的贸易安排，我们可以更好地合作。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Pierre Poilievre**: In Canada's case, we have everything the United States needs if they treat us like a friend. So, for example, we have the fourth biggest supply of oil. And if you look at the leading five, which of these countries do you think the United States can most rely on? Is it Venezuela, Saudi Arabia, Iran, or Iraq? No, it's Canada. We could cooperate better on this if we got a friendly posture and a fully tariff-free trade arrangement with the US.

</details>

### 自由市场与福利制度

**Steven Bartlett**: 你曾读过**亚当·斯密**的《**道德情操论**》，这如何改变了你的政策观点？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: One of the books that I realized you'd read at that time from some research is this book Adam Smith the theory of moral sentiments. And how did that change your perspective and therefore your policies and your career?

</details>

**皮埃尔·波利耶夫**: 亚当·斯密在《**国富论**》中描述了**看不见的手**，即自发形成的**自愿交换**系统。但他在《道德情操论》中解释了自利如何与美德重叠，即“同理心”。我认为那些推行**社会主义**意识形态的人在人性观上存在严重矛盾：他们认为人在私有经济中是贪婪的，但在政府经济中却是天使。我认为权力越集中，人性的恶习就越容易显现。我的意识形态是权力下放，建立一个从下而上的系统，让人们拥有最大的自主权。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Pierre Poilievre**: Adam Smith in 1776 wrote this book which described what we now call the free market system. The economy is guided by the invisible hand of the free market. But the other half was in this book called the theory of moral sentiments in which he explains how self-interest overlaps with virtue, what he called fellow feeling. I have found that those who push a socialist ideology have a gross contradiction in their view of human nature. They say that human beings are wretched when they're in the private voluntary economy, but they're angels when they're in the governmental economy. My ideology is that we should disperse power that it should be a bottom-up system.

</details>

### 加拿大住房危机

**Steven Bartlett**: 社会主义者可能会反驳说，资本主义导致了现在的**不平等**，年轻人买不起房。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: I guess if there's socialists listening now, they might think, well, we tried this sort of capitalistic approach and it's resulted in inequality, people can't buy homes anymore.

</details>

**皮埃尔·波利耶夫**: 我们现在实行的是“权贵的社会主义”。政府通过沉重的监管阻碍房屋建设，限制了供应，导致有豪宅的人更富有，而工人阶级却买不起房。在加拿大，新房成本的大大部分不是土地、人工或木材，而是**政府税费**和官僚审批。我们的建筑许可发放速度在 **OECD** 国家中排名倒数第二。我的目标是消除这些官僚障碍，通过快速许可和减税来“解锁”房屋建设。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Pierre Poilievre**: Well, what we have now is socialism for the very rich. Government is actively intervening in the economy to forcefully redistribute wealth up the chain. In Canada, the vast majority of the cost that goes into building a new home is not land, labor, or lumber. It's government. We have the second slowest building permits of any country in the OECD. My goal is to remove all of that bureaucracy and make it tax-free to build homes.

</details>

### 货币通胀与愤怒的工人阶级

**Steven Bartlett**: 加拿大的**幸福感排名**一直在下降，为什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: Canada have consistently dropped down the sort of happiness league table. Why?

</details>

**皮埃尔·波利耶夫**: 部分原因是食物价格通胀。更广泛地说，是**货币通胀**。过去十年，加拿大的货币供应量从1.4万亿增加到了2.8万亿。这不是东西变贵了，而是购买它们的钱贬值了。这就是所谓的**坎蒂隆效应**（Cantillon Effect）：在货币扩张中，最先接触到钱的是富有阶层，等钱流向工人阶级时，它已经贬值了，工资遭到了破坏。这就是为什么整个西方世界的工人阶级都如此愤怒。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Pierre Poilievre**: Part of it is food. But more broadly, it's the monetary inflation. We've doubled our money supply in Canada in 10 years. It is not actually that these things cost more. It's that the money with which we buy them is worth less. There's something called the Catalon effect, which is that the first people to touch the money in a monetary expansion are those who are already wealthy. By the time it trickles down to the working-class people, their wages have been destroyed.

</details>

### AI时代的挑战

**Steven Bartlett**: **Anthropic** 发布的一份报告显示，**AI** 正在颠覆初级就业市场。你认为这次技术革命与以往有什么不同吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: Anthropic released a report showing where job disruption will take place. Do you think this time is fundamentally different than those prior technological revolutions?

</details>

**皮埃尔·波利耶夫**: 关键在于颠覆的速度。这次技术是建立在具有全球分发能力的互联网之上的。我担心的近期的失业问题。如果 AI 能够让我们以更低的成本生产更多东西，那么这种红利应该传递给普通人，而不是被通货膨胀抵消掉。政府的公共政策目标应该是确保 AI 成为人类的使能者（Enabler），而不是替代者。我们需要规则来赋予人们更多的代理权和对生活的控制权。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Pierre Poilievre**: I would say yes, because of the speed of disruption. This technology is built on the internet which has global distribution. My concern is the near-term displacement. As these technologies bring down costs, those savings should be passed on to people. The public policy objective is to ensure that it becomes an enabler of humanity, not a replacement for it. The rules around technology should always be geared towards giving people more agency.

</details>

### 个人成长与斯多葛哲学

**Steven Bartlett**: 你的个人经历非常特别，你是一个被领养的孩子，后来还遇到了生母。这段经历如何影响了你？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: Your upbringing, your biological mother adopted you at 16 years old. How has it changed you as a man?

</details>

**皮埃尔·波利耶夫**: 我被两位教师领养。我的生母在16岁时做出了无私的决定，让我们有更好的机会。我的养母非常宽宏大量，她鼓励我去见生母。我的养父后来公开了**同性恋**身份，他教导我要诚实面对自己。这些经历教给我：不要去评判别人，要爱他们本来的样子。我也深受**马可·奥勒留**《**沉思录**》和**斯多葛主义**的影响，专注于自己能控制的事情。当你专注于可控的事物时，你就是生活的主宰，而不是被动受挫的对象。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Pierre Poilievre**: I was adopted by two school teachers. My biological mother made a selfless decision when she was 16. My adoptive father would eventually come out as gay. My parents taught me an important lesson: to thine own self be true. I'm a believer in a stoic approach, like Marcus Aurelius's Meditations. Put all of your emphasis on the things that are within your control. When you focus on what you can control, you are the actor rather than the acted upon.

</details>

### 对加拿大的愿景

**Steven Bartlett**: 你对世界和加拿大最大的恐惧是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: What are you most afraid of for Canada and for the world?

</details>

**皮埃尔·波利耶夫**: 我对加拿大最大的恐惧是我们一直阻碍自己的潜力，逐渐衰落，人们失去对未来的希望，就像温水里的青蛙。对于世界，我担心西方世界不再坚持其基础原则，即源自《**大宪章**》的自由。但我对未来是乐观的，加拿大拥有丰富的资源和受过良好教育的人口。如果我们做正确的事，未来属于加拿大。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Pierre Poilievre**: The biggest fear I have for Canada is that we just keep blocking our own potential and decline and opportunity vanishes. My biggest fear for the world is that the western world does not stay true to its foundational principles, the principles that grew out of the Magna Carta. But I'm actually optimistic about the future. The future belongs to Canada if we do the right things.

</details>
