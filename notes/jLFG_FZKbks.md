### 访谈开篇：买房还是租房？

**Steven Bartlett**: 租房还是买房？这是大多数人一生中做出的**最大的财务决定**。今天，我们要讨论拥有住房的所有**不可回收成本**（unrecoverable costs），包括**房产税**、**维护费用**——我认为这是人们最容易低估的一项。此外还有**应急支出**。我准备了一大堆案例，还有一个**“5%原则”**（5% rule），用来判断租房是否是更好的财务选择。我们还会探讨人们常犯的**十大财务错误**。例如，**税务筹划**的机会。**Ben Felix** 的公司管理着超过 3000 人的资产，他的核心观点是基于**学术研究**为人们提供理财建议。我们的**大脑和心理因素**往往会阻碍我们做出正确的长期财务决策。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: Renting versus owning a home, the biggest financial decision most people make in their life. So, we're going to talk about all of the unrecoverable costs of owning a home, including property taxes, maintenance costs, which is the one that I think people underestimate the most. And then there's also emergency costs. I've got a whole stack of them, as well as a 5% rule to figure out if renting is a better financial decision. We'll go through that. What else have we got? So, this is something that people just don't think enough about, which is the top 10 financial mistakes that I think people make. For example, tax planning opportunities. Ben Felix's firm manages the money of more than 3,000 people. His whole thesis is giving people money advice that is based on academic research. Our brains, our psychology, absolutely gets in the way of making good long-term financial decisions.

**Ben Felix**: 没错。很多人认为在开始投资之前需要掌握大量背景信息。但我认为，那些只了解一点点、知道**指数基金**（index funds）是明智之选的人，长期来看反而会是更出色的投资者。有大量证据表明，这种策略会跑赢大多数其他投资策略。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: Right. A lot of people believe they need to have a lot of background information before they can start investing, but I would argue that people who know just a little bit, that just know that index funds are sensible, they will be better long-term investors. There's a ton of evidence supporting that this will outperform most other investment strategies.

</details>

### 理财的底层逻辑：工程学与心理学

**Steven Bartlett**: Ben，世界上有很多谈论个人理财和投资的人。你采取的方法与 YouTube 上其他理财专家有什么不同？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: Ben, there are lots of people out in the world talking about personal finance and investing. What is the approach you take that you think is different to lots of the other sort of finance experts that are on YouTube that are giving people advice?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 我一直试图采取的方法是：我们能从**学术文献**中汲取什么？那些花大量时间思考这些问题的聪明人，他们的研究成果如何应用在普通人的财务决策中？关键问题包括**租房与买房**的对比、**资产配置**（asset allocation），以及为什么不应该去追求那些看似诱人但风险巨大的投资策略。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: What I think, and the approach that I've always tried to take, is what can we take from academic literature, very smart people who spent a lot of time thinking about these things, what can we take from them and apply to making good financial decisions for a typical person? And what are the key questions that you've sought to answer? Is renting versus owning a home. Asset allocation is another big one. Another important question people wonder about is, why should I not do this other investment strategy that seems very attractive?

</details>

**Steven Bartlett**: 投资和赚钱这门“游戏”中，心理学占了多大比重？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: And how much of this game of investing, making money, is comes back to psychology?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 我常说，投资问题已经解决了——用指数基金就行了。难点在于**知行合一**。因为我们的**心理**绝对会干扰长期的正确决策。人类的大脑是为了**生存**进化的，而不是为了思考长期、抽象的概念，比如今天把钱投进股市，忽略中间的所有波动，留到以后退休用。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: So, I like to say investing's been solved. We're going to use index funds. That's it. The hard part is actually doing that. Because our brains, our psychology, absolutely gets in the way of making good long-term financial decisions. Our brains are designed for survival. They're not designed to thinking about long-term abstract concepts.

</details>

**Steven Bartlett**: 关于理财和投资，学术研究中有没有所谓的“最佳心理状态”？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: And is there academic research about the best sort of mental approach to take towards money and finance and investing?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 其中一个最好的方法，虽然有点反直觉，就是**不要看你的投资情况**。有学术论文显示，人们看投资频率越高，承担的风险就越小，收益也越低。因为如果你每天盯着波动，会觉得股市极其危险；而事实上，对于能长期持有的人来说，股票比人们想象的要安全得多。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: So, one of the best approaches, and it's a little bit counterintuitive, is to not look at your investments. There is a an academic paper showing that the more people look at your investments, the less risk they take, and the lower returns they earn. Because the stock market goes up and down. In reality, for long-term investors who can buy and hold for a very long period of time, they're a lot safer than people think.

</details>

### 设定高质量目标：PERMA 模型

**Steven Bartlett**: 你在桌上放了一些模型。能解释一下这些框架吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: We've got some props here. Could you just give explain to me the high-level of what these things are on the table and the different frameworks we're going to go through?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 这是一个我非常喜欢的模型，叫 **PERMA 模型**，源自**积极心理学**（Positive Psychology）。心理学不仅对投资很重要，对弄清楚你长期的投资策略目标也至关重要。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: Sure. This is the PERMA model, which comes from positive psychology. Psychology is important for investing well, but it's also important for figuring out what your long-term investing strategy should be.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: PERMA 模型包含五个要素：
1.  **正面情绪**（Positive emotion）：享受你正在做的事。
2.  **投入感**（Engagement）：进入“**心流**”（flow）状态。
3.  **人际关系**（Relationships）：与亲近的人建立强有力的联系。
4.  **意义**（Meaning）：成为比自己更伟大的事物的一部分。
5.  **成就感**（Accomplishment）：设定并实现困难的目标。

你可以对照这些类别来思考你的财务目标。比如你想买法拉利，你可以问问自己：它能提供持久的正面情绪吗？还是只能维持几天？它能帮你建立人际关系吗？（比如加入车友会）。很多时候，你会发现有些开支并不符合这些要素。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: So, the items of the PERMA model: positive emotion, engagement, the idea of getting into flow. Relationships, having good, strong relationships with people. Meaning is being part of something that is bigger than yourself. And accomplishment is achieving hard things. Setting goals and achieving them. You're going to look at those as categories and think about what other goals you may have that fit into those categories. For example, a Ferrari. It would be wise to see if a Ferrari contributes to any of these things. It might—maybe you spend hours racing it, that's engagement. Or maybe a friend group, that's relationships.

</details>

### 人生最大的财务陷阱：十大理财错误

**Steven Bartlett**: 我们来谈谈人们常犯的十大金钱错误。哪些是特别值得深入探讨的？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: And you've got the the 10 money mistakes people make here. Can you run me through those ones?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 第一个是颇具争议的：**赚得不够多**。很多人觉得收入多少是注定的，无法改变。但我认为，投资你的**人力资本**（human capital）——无论是通过正规教育、技能提升还是创业——都能让你成为更具价值的资产。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: So, this one's controversial. It's not earning enough money. A lot of people feel like they don't have an option. I don't think that's necessarily true. Investing in your human capital, those are all ways to make your own self a more valuable asset and allow you to earn more money.

</details>

**Steven Bartlett**: 我一直认为职业生涯初期有五个“水桶”。前两个是**知识**和**技能**，这两者是最核心的，因为它们永远不会流失。后三个是资源、人脉和名声，这些可能会因为职业波动而枯竭。所以年轻人应该优先填满前两个水桶，获取市场上**稀缺且互补的技能组合**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: I've always thought of it across these sort of five buckets. The first two buckets are our knowledge and then our skills. These two first buckets are so imperative because they can almost never be unfilled. Whereas the other three buckets—resources, network, and reputation—can unfill. So optimize for filling your knowledge and skills as much as you possibly can. Acquiring a rare but complementary stack of knowledge and skills that the market values.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 绝对正确。历史数据表明，**正规教育**或**技能培训**与**终身收入**（lifetime earnings）之间存在机械性的正相关。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: That's absolutely true. There's data on this, too, where we know that there has been a mechanical relationship between formal education or trade education and lifetime earnings.

</details>

**Steven Bartlett**: 第二个错误是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: What's the second one you've got there?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: **储蓄不足**。财富是随时间**复利**（compounding）增长的。如果你储蓄不够，就会错过复利效应。等到了 50 或 60 岁才意识到储蓄不足，那就很难追赶了。这和健康很像：如果你长期饮食不当且不运动，晚年就会很艰难。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: Second one is not saving enough. The tricky thing about saving is that wealth compounds over time. And if you're not saving enough, you're missing out on compounding and it gets a lot harder to catch up. Some people will wake up when they're 50 or 60 and realize they haven't saved enough. By that time, there's very little that you can do about it.

</details>

### 投资风险与机会成本

**Ben Felix**: 第五个错误是**不承担投资风险**。股市提供了惊人的长期回报，不参与其中是巨大的错误。很多人完全不碰股票，或者投资得太保守。这背后有巨大的隐性成本——即**机会成本**（opportunity cost）。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: Number five might be one of the bigger ones, which is not taking investment risks. The stock market has delivered these incredible long-term returns. Not participating in that is a huge mistake. By not participating in the stock market when you could be, you're giving up a huge amount of economic gain.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 比如你手头有 1 万美元，如果以 7% 的年化回报率投资 40 年，它会变成 **15 万美元**。所以，当你今天花掉 1 万美元买车时，你实际上是花掉了未来的 15 万美元。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: So let's say it's 7% return over 40 years, that $10,000 would be $150,000. So when you spend $10,000 today, you're actually spending $150,000 in 40 years' time.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 第六个错误是**承担错误的风险**。很多人不买指数基金，而是去**选股**、交易期权或炒币。这些风险往往带有负的预期回报，或者因为交易频繁产生高昂的成本。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: Number six. Taking the wrong risks with your investments. A lot of people pick individual stocks, trade options, crypto tokens. A lot of those types of risks have negative expected returns, or they have high costs if you're doing a lot of trading.

</details>

### 买房 vs 租房：深度拆解 5% 原则

**Steven Bartlett**: 买房是一项好的投资吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: What about buying a house? Is that a good investment?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 我不认为自住房是投资。它更像是一个为你提供居住服务的资产。当你比较买房和租房时，必须考虑**不可回收成本**：
1.  **房贷利息**：付给银行的钱，拿不回来的。
2.  **机会成本**：原本可以投入股市的**首付款**。房产升值通常只能跑赢通胀一点点，而股票远超通胀。
3.  **房产税**：通常在房价的 0.5% 到 1% 之间。
4.  **维护成本**：人们低估最多的一项。以前我认为是 1%，但学术界认为可能超过 2%。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: I wouldn't consider buying a house to live in an investment. It's an asset that funds your housing consumption. You have to think about unrecoverable costs: Mortgage interest. Opportunity costs—whatever equity you have in a home is equity that you could have otherwise invested in the stock market. Property taxes. And maintenance costs—the one people underestimate the most. It could well be over 2% of the property value per year.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 于是我提出了 **“5% 原则”**：将房价乘以 5%，再除以 12。得到的数字就是与买房成本相当的月租金。比如 30 万美元的房子，乘以 5% 是 1.5 万美元，除以 12 是 **1250 美元**。如果你能以低于 1250 美元的价格租到类似的房子，从财务上讲，租房更好。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: I came up with this idea called the 5% rule. You multiply the price of a home by 5% and then divide by 12. For a $300,000 house, the result is 1,250. That is the equivalent rent where you're roughly break even. If you could rent for 1,250 or less, renting is a better financial decision.

</details>

**Steven Bartlett**: 那么年轻人应该买房吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: Do you think for the average young person, they should be buying a house?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 对年轻人来说很难。房价高，而且买房会限制**灵活性**（mobility）。如果你为了买房花光积蓄，结果在这个城市出现更好的工作机会，或者房价下跌（就像最近多伦多的公寓市场），你就会陷入困境。租房能让你在需求变化时（比如结婚生子）更轻松地搬家。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: For young people, it's really tough. It can limit your mobility. In Toronto, prices have plummeted. If you get a job offer somewhere else, what are you going to do with that condo at a loss? Plus, transaction costs are huge. Renting allowed my family to move four times as our needs changed. If we had been homeowners, the transaction costs would have been insane.

</details>

### 资产配置：100% 股票策略？

**Steven Bartlett**: 你曾做过一个关于“金融界最具争议的论文”的视频。那是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: You made a video called the most controversial paper in finance. What paper was that?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 那是关于**生命周期资产配置**的。传统观念认为，年轻时风险偏好高，年老时应增加债券比例。但这篇论文模拟了一百万个假设人生，发现最优策略是 **100% 持有股票**，其中 1/3 是国内股票，2/3 是国际股票。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: It was a paper on life cycle asset allocation. Conventional wisdom says you should start riskier in stocks and move towards safer bonds as you get older. They found that the optimal portfolio is a 100% equity portfolio, with a big chunk in international stocks—roughly 1/3 domestic, 2/3 international.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 因为从长远来看，股票比人们想象的要安全，而**债券**在**高通胀**时期会被彻底摧毁。当然，这在金融界引起了很大争议，因为它挑战了根深蒂固的行业标准。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: Stocks are a little bit safer for long-term investors than we thought, and bonds are actually riskier. During periods of high inflation, bonds get absolutely decimated.

</details>

### 避坑指南：金融机构的“陷阱”

**Steven Bartlett**: 有哪些理财产品是人们绝对不应该投资的？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: What are some financial products that you should not invest in?

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 一个是**备兑开仓**（covered calls）产品。它利用了投资者的“现金流偏好”，让你卖掉股票的上涨潜力来获取一点点利息。实际上，你放弃的长期增值空间远大于得到的收益。另一个是**主题基金**（thematic ETFs），比如 AI 基金、大麻基金。这些基金通常在某个行业最火、价格最高的时候推出，随后往往就是大跌。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: A big one is covered calls. It plays on the preference for income, but you're giving up so much upside. Another one is thematic ETFs—AI, cannabis, space. They get launched when asset prices are at the peak because of interest, and then the returns tend to be very poor.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 还有**高昂的费用**。费用的复利效应非常惊人。如果你每年付 1% 的管理费，长期来看，你会损失很大一部分潜在财富。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: Fees compound. Any rate of return that compounds over long periods of time can be very impactful.

</details>

### 面向未来：AI 与市场效率

**Steven Bartlett**: AI 会改变这一切吗？人们担心失业。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: And does AI change any of this equation? A lot of people are worried about losing their jobs.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 我不是劳动经济学家，但我喜欢看历史。回顾过去，历次技术革命都伴随着恐慌。比如 **ATM 机**出现时，人们以为银行柜员会失业，但结果是银行开设了更多分支机构，柜员职位反而增加了。这就是 **杰文斯悖论**（Jevons paradox）：效率的提升反而可能扩大市场。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: I like looking at history. People thought ATMs were going to wipe out bank tellers. What ended up happening was the cost of operating a branch decreased, banks opened more branches, and there were actually more bank teller jobs. It's the Jevons paradox—efficiency can expand the market instead of shrinking it.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 作为投资者，你必须接受**有效市场**（efficient market）的概念。这意味着今天的所有信息——包括大家对 AI 的期待——都已经反映在股价里了。如果你觉得特斯拉好，所以买特斯拉，那你已经晚了，因为全世界都知道它好。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: An efficient market is where prices always reflect all available information. If you think Tesla is a great car and buy the stock, that information is already included in the price.

</details>

### 结尾：守财奴 vs 败家子

**Steven Bartlett**: 最后一个问题，关于婚姻与金钱。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steven Bartlett**: Question number nine is about who you marry.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 研究将人们分为**守财奴**（tightwads）和**败家子**（spendthrifts）。有趣的是，这两种人更容易被对方吸引，但也因此在婚姻中产生更多矛盾。如果你想存钱过理想生活，而配偶只想今天挥霍，那会非常痛苦。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: Research identifies two spending profiles: tightwads (who don't like to spend) and spendthrifts (who do). They are more likely to end up marrying each other than someone with the same profile. They tend to be less satisfied in their marriages due to conflict around money.

</details>

**Ben Felix**: 总结来说，关注你**能控制的事项**：合理的财务计划、目标设定、资产配置、应急基金和税务筹划。不要试图击败市场，买入指数基金，然后忘掉密码，这就是最好的策略。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ben Felix**: Focus on the things you can control: an appropriate financial plan, right goals, asset allocation, emergency savings, and tax planning. Accept the market's return, and then don't do anything. Don't check the app. Lose the password.

</details>
