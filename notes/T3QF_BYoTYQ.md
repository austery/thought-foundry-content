### H-1B签证新规引发的混乱与澄清

上周，特朗普总统签署了一项**行政命令**（Executive Order: 美国总统发布的一项具有法律效力的指令，无需国会批准），规定每份新的**H-1B签证**（H-1B Visa: 美国用于雇佣高技能外国工人的主要非移民签证类别）申请将向公司收取10万美元。H-1B签证是美国雇佣高技能外国工人的主要签证类型。这项声明在外国工人中引发了一波恐慌，雇主感到困惑，白宫也随即发布了一系列澄清。网上流传着机场的恐慌景象，乘客们试图下飞机，因为他们担心如果离开美国，可能就无法再返回。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Last week, President Trump signed an executive order that would charge companies $100,000 dollars for each new H-1B visa application. The H1B is the main visa used for hiring skilled foreign workers in the United States. The announcement triggered a wave of panic among foreign workers, confusion amongst employers, and a flurry of clarifications from the White House. Images were shared online of panics at airports as passengers tried to disembark airplanes that were ready to take off, fearing that if they left the country, they might not be allowed to return.</p>
</details>

商务部长霍华德·卢特尼克（Howard Lutnick）在签署仪式上站在特朗普旁边，他告诉记者这是一项年度费用，称公司需要决定一名外国工人是否“足够有价值”，以证明除了工人薪水外，每年向政府支付10万美元是合理的，这进一步加剧了混乱。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick added to the confusion - standing next to Trump at the signing, he told reporters that it was an annual charge, saying companies would need to decide whether a foreign worker was “valuable enough” to justify paying a hundred thousand dollars a year to the government, in addition to the worker’s salary.</p>
</details>

在接下来的24小时内，白宫紧急纠正了记录。卡罗琳·利维特（Karoline Leavitt）发布了一份三点澄清，声明该费用不是年度性的，而是一次性支付；它仅适用于新申请，不适用于续签；并且现有的签证持有者再次入境美国不会被收费。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Over the next 24 hours, the White House scrambled to correct the record. Karoline Leavitt issued a three-point clarification, stating that the fee was not annual — it is a one-time payment, that it applies only to new applications, not renewals and that current visa holders would not be charged to re-enter the country.</p>
</details>

### H-1B政策的转变及其影响

特朗普总统的这项声明带有一定的讽刺意味。在他2020年的竞选期间，他曾称赞H-1B项目是“一件好事”和“我一直支持的事情”。不到一年前，他在一次竞选播客中表示，“如果你从大学毕业，你应该随文凭获得一张**绿卡**（Green Card: 允许外国人在美国永久居住和工作的证件）。”而现在，他却提出了一项费用，这将使大多数企业难以在美国雇佣外国工人，从而实际上终结了H-1B签证类别。尽管许多新闻都集中在科技行业，但H-1B签证也被美国医疗保健部门广泛使用，用于招聘国际医学毕业生或在国外受训的医生及其他专业人士。此外，大学也依赖H-1B签证来雇佣教授、研究人员和博士后。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This announcement from President Trump comes with a certain irony. During his campaign in 2020, he praised the H-1B program calling it “a great thing” and “something I’ve always supported.” Less than a year ago he told a podcast – while he was campaigning- that “if you graduate from a college, you should get a green card with your diploma.” Now, he’s proposing a fee which would effectively end the H 1B visa category by making it prohibitive for most businesses to hire foreign workers in the United States. While a lot of the news has been about the tech industry - the visas are widely used by the U.S. healthcare sector to recruit international medical graduates or foreign-trained doctors and other professionals trained abroad. Universities additionally rely on them to hire professors, researchers and postdocs.</p>
</details>

网上流传的估计称，这项签证费用每年可能带来140亿美元的收入，但这些预测假设需求保持不变，并且费用适用于年度续签，这两点都不符合事实。由于新规定鼓励公司在海外办事处雇佣高技能工人，而不是将他们带到美国，实际收入可能会显著降低。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Estimates circulated online that the visa fee could raise $14 billion dollars a year in revenue - but these projections assumed that demand would remain unchanged and that the fee would apply to annual renewals — neither of which is true. The actual revenue is likely to be significantly lower as the new rule incentivizes companies to hire highly skilled workers at foreign based offices instead of bringing them into the United States.</p>
</details>

H-1B签证于1990年设立，旨在帮助美国雇主在需要特定技术专长的“专业职业”中雇佣外国工人。它迅速成为公司填补软件工程、数据科学、生物技术和其他工人需求超过国内供应领域职位的首选签证。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The H-1B visa was created in 1990 as a way for U.S. employers to hire foreign workers in “specialty occupations” that require specific technical expertise. It quickly became the go-to visa for companies looking to fill roles in software engineering, data science, biotech, and other fields where the demand for workers exceeds the domestic supply.</p>
</details>

美国政府每年发放85,000份新的H-1B签证，其中65,000份分配给拥有学士学位的申请人，20,000份分配给拥有美国研究生学位的申请人。需求如此之高，以至于在申请窗口开放的24小时内，所有配额就被抢光，因此政府会通过抽签来决定谁能获得签证。抽签系统并非最佳方法，因为它意味着拥有非常稀有和重要技能的外国毕业生与拥有更普通资格的人获得签证的几率相同。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Each year, the U.S. government issues 85,000 new H-1B visas — 65,000 go to applicants with bachelor’s degrees and 20,000 go to those with U.S. graduate degrees. Demand is so high that the entire allotment gets claimed within 24 hours of the application window opening, so, the government then runs a lottery to decide who gets a visa. The lottery system is not the best approach as it means that a foreign graduate with really rare and important skills ends up having the same odds of getting a visa as someone with more common qualifications.</p>
</details>

H-1B签证有效期为三年，可延长至六年。此后，工人必须要么离开美国，要么转为绿卡身份。绿卡分配有国家配额限制，即任何国家获得的绿卡数量不能超过总数的7%。这意味着来自中国和印度等人口大国的人等待绿卡的时间非常长，而进入美国工作的唯一真正途径就是通过H-1B签证。一旦获得H-1B签证，这些工人就与他们的雇主绑定，如果他们失业，必须在60天内找到新工作，否则将面临遣返。由于绿卡配额的限制，许多中国和印度工人发现自己被困在临时签证上，他们试图在美国建立生活，但深知一旦失业可能很快就会被遣返。如果他们的子女在国外出生，这些子女一旦成年就会失去留在美国的权利。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The H-1B visa lasts for three years and can be extended to six. After that, workers must either leave the country or transition to a green card. There is a per-country cap on the number of green cards allocated – so, no country can get more than 7% of the available green cards – this means that people from countries with high populations like China and India have really long wait times for green cards and the only real route into working in the United States is through the H-1B visa. Once they get a H-1B, these workers are tied to their employer – and if they lose their job, they have 60 days to find another or face deportation. Because of the cap on Green Cards, many Chinese and Indian workers find themselves stuck on temporary visas – while they try to build lives in the US – aware that losing their job could quickly lead to deportation. If they have children who were born abroad – those children lose the right to stay as soon as they become adults.</p>
</details>

### H-1B签证的使用情况与争议

近年来，H-1B签证项目主要被科技公司使用。2022年，亚马逊（Amazon）是H-1B签证的最大担保方，其次是塔塔咨询服务公司（Tata Consultancy Services）、谷歌（Google）、微软（Microsoft）和印孚瑟斯（Infosys）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In recent years, the H-1B visa program has been most heavily used by tech firms. In 2022, Amazon was the top H-1B sponsor, followed by Tata Consultancy Services, Google, Microsoft, and Infosys.</p>
</details>

H-1B工人往往年轻、受过高等教育且愿意搬迁。如果可能的话，他们也倾向于留在美国。许多人最终成为永久居民，创办公司，或在美国公司担任领导职务。美国七家最有价值的上市公司中，有四家——谷歌、微软、特斯拉（Tesla）和英伟达（Nvidia）——由前H-1B签证持有者领导。四家最有价值的私营公司的首席执行官也是如此。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">H-1B workers tend to be young, highly educated, and willing to relocate. They also tend to stay in America if they can. Many go on to become permanent residents, start companies, or take leadership roles in U.S. firms. Four of the seven most valuable public companies in America — Google, Microsoft, Tesla, and Nvidia — are led by former H-1B holders. So are the CEOs of the four most valuable private companies.</p>
</details>

显然，随着《**天才法案**》（Genius Act: 一项可能旨在吸引或留住高技能人才的立法）的通过，美国更加关注稳定币、加密货币等技术，以及其他不需要像芯片设计那样高水平教育的业务。因此，随着这类业务的增长，吸引科学家和工程师可能变得不那么重要。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Obviously with the passing of the Genius Act – The United States is focusing more on technology like stablecoins, cryptocurrencies and other businesses which don’t really require the same level of education as was needed for things like chip design – so attracting scientists and engineers may be less important as the focus on this type of business grows. A background in Physics isn’t really needed to come up with something like Fartcoin.</p>
</details>

H-1B签证一直是美国高技能移民制度的基石。它并非完美无缺——批评者称其容易被滥用并可能压低工资——但它帮助美国在科学、技术和创新领域保持了优势。多年来，高技能移民一直是美国科技和科学领导地位的关键。超过20%的美国诺贝尔奖获得者是移民。这项新费用将使这种优势面临风险。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The H-1B visa has been a cornerstone of America’s skilled immigration system. It’s not perfect — critics say that it’s vulnerable to abuse and can suppresses wages — but it has helped the U.S. maintain its edge in science, technology, and innovation. Highly skilled immigration has been key to Americas tech and scientific leadership over the years. More than 20% of American Nobel prizewinners were immigrants. This new fee puts that edge at risk.</p>
</details>

H-1B项目的支持者经常引用一项研究，声称每雇佣一名H-1B签证的外国工人，就能为美国人创造五个额外的就业机会。其逻辑是，高技能工人能提高生产力，帮助公司成长，并允许企业扩大国内业务。值得注意的是，五份工作的数据来自一项单一研究，并未得到普遍接受。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Supporters of the H-1B program often cite a study claiming that each foreign worker hired on an H-1B visa leads to the creation of five additional jobs for Americans. The logic is that skilled workers boost productivity, help companies grow, and allow firms to expand their domestic operations. It’s worth noting that the five-jobs figure comes from a single study and isn’t universally accepted.</p>
</details>

批评者认为，该签证可能会压低工资。特别是外包公司被指控利用该项目以低于市场价的工资引进工人。2023年的一项分析发现，60%的H-1B职位支付的工资低于该职业的中位数工资。这可能可以通过H-1B签证持有者通常更年轻来解释。劳工部（Department of Labor）要求雇主支付现行工资或支付给类似美国工人的实际工资，以较高者为准，但执法不力，而且H-1B工人极难更换工作，这可能意味着雇主会利用他们。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Critics argue that the visa can suppress wages. Outsourcing firms, in particular, have been accused of using the program to bring in workers at below-market rates. A 2023 analysis found that 60% of H-1B jobs were paid below the median wage for the occupation. Now this could be explained by H-1B visas being held by typically younger workers. The Department of Labor requires employers to pay either the prevailing wage or the actual wage paid to similar U.S. workers — whichever is higher — but enforcement is patchy, and the fact that it is extremely difficult for H-1B workers to change jobs – possibly means that employers take advantage of them.</p>
</details>

该项目的性质决定了它既可以创造就业机会——当它引进高技能工人帮助美国公司创新和成长时——也可以压低工资，这取决于它的使用方式。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The nature of the program is that it can both create jobs – when it brings in highly skilled workers that help US firms to innovate and grow – or it could suppress wages, depending on how it’s used.</p>
</details>

关于美国是否有足够的**STEM**（Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics: 科学、技术、工程和数学领域的总称）毕业生来满足需求也存在争议。一些研究说是，另一些则否。明确的是，许多美国的STEM毕业生最终从事了不相关的工作。根据一份报告，只有大约四分之一拥有STEM学位的美国人实际从事STEM工作。这不一定意味着人才过剩，可能意味着这些工作不够吸引人，或者雇主更喜欢更具流动性且成本较低的外国工人。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There’s also disagreement about whether the U.S. has enough STEM graduates to meet demand. Some studies say yes. Others say no. What’s clear is that many STEM graduates in the U.S. end up working in unrelated fields. According to one report, only about a quarter of Americans with STEM degrees actually work in STEM jobs. That doesn’t necessarily mean there’s a surplus — it could mean that the jobs aren’t attractive enough, or that employers prefer foreign workers who are more mobile and less expensive.</p>
</details>

签证的捍卫者表示，如果没有全球人才，美国公司将难以竞争。批评者则认为，该项目已成为那些不愿投资培训或支付高工资的公司的补贴。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The visa’s defenders say that without access to global talent, U.S. companies will struggle to compete. Its critics say that the program has become a subsidy for firms that don’t want to invest in training or pay high wages.</p>
</details>

### 对国际学生和美国大学的影响

几十年来，美国大学一直吸引着来自世界各地的顶尖学生。吸引力不仅在于教育质量，还在于毕业后留在美国工作并最终定居的可能性。H-1B签证被视为学习与公民身份之间的桥梁。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For decades, U.S. universities have attracted top students from around the world. The draw wasn’t just the quality of education — it was the possibility of staying on after graduation, working in the U.S., and eventually settling down. The H-1B visa was seen as the bridge between study and citizenship.</p>
</details>

国际学生支付高昂的学费，通常是国内学生的两到三倍。许多人来自印度和中国等国家，在美国获得学位意味着巨大的经济投入。人们曾相信这是一项很好的投资，即美国大学的学位会带来在美国顶尖公司的工作。这项新费用使这一假设受到质疑。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">International students pay high tuition fees, often two or three times what domestic students pay. Many come from countries like India and China, where the cost of a U.S. degree represents a major financial commitment. The belief was that it was a good investment — that a degree from a U.S. university would lead to a job at a top US firm. The new fee throws that assumption into question.</p>
</details>

如果雇佣一名外国毕业生的成本上升到10万美元，再加上根据工人技能和资历支付的高薪，许多雇主将干脆停止这样做。这意味着国际学生的就业机会减少，以及最初来美国学习的动力降低。美国大学的申请人数可能会下降，尤其是在外国学生占研究生人口很大一部分的STEM领域。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If the cost of hiring a foreign graduate rises to $100,000 – plus a high salary that takes the workers skills and qualifications into account, many employers will simply stop doing it. That means fewer job offers for international students, and less incentive to study in the U.S. in the first place. American Universities could see a drop in applications, especially in STEM fields where foreign students make up a large share of the graduate population.</p>
</details>

此外，这项费用还存在将学生推向其他国家的风险。加拿大、英国、澳大利亚和新加坡都已努力吸引全球人才，提供毕业后工作签证、快速居留通道和较低的就业门槛。如果美国让留下来变得更困难，学生们就会去其他地方。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There’s also a risk that the fee will push students towards other countries. Canada, the UK, Australia, and Singapore have all made efforts to attract global talent — offering post-study work visas, fast-track residency, and lower barriers to employment. If the U.S. makes it harder to stay, students will go elsewhere.</p>
</details>

对于美国大学而言，影响可能是财务和声誉上的。国际学生通过支付更高的费用来补贴整个教育系统。他们带来了多样性、研究能力和全球联系。如果他们不再来，影响将波及各个院系，不仅是工程实验室，还包括经济学研讨会、商学院和医学研究中心。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For American universities, the impact could be financial as well as reputational. International students subsidize the system by paying higher fees. They bring diversity, research capacity, and global connections. If they stop coming, the effects will be felt across departments — not just in engineering labs, but in economics seminars, business schools, and medical research centers.</p>
</details>

H-1B签证从未被设计为学生通道，但它默认成为了一个。新费用可能会关闭这条道路，使外国大学对国际学生更具吸引力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The H-1B visa was never designed as a student pathway, but it became one by default. The new fee may close that path making foreign universities more attractive to international students.</p>
</details>

### 离岸外包加速与美国竞争力下降

H-1B签证旨在将高技能工人引入美国。但如果这样做的成本变得过高，公司可能就会停止尝试。另一种选择——特别是对于大型公司而言——就是直接在海外招聘，而且他们越来越多地这样做。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The H-1B visa was designed to bring skilled workers into the United States. But if the cost of doing that becomes too high, companies may simply stop trying. The alternative – especially for the biggest firms - is to just hire abroad — and increasingly, that’s what they’re doing.</p>
</details>

在特朗普的第一个任期内，签证处理速度减慢，拒签率上升，审查也更加严格。公司通过将工作转移到海外来应对。他们没有将工人带到美国，而是在加拿大、墨西哥、印度和东欧建立了团队。这项新费用可能会加速这种转变。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">During Trump’s first term, visa processing slowed down, denial rates rose, and scrutiny increased. Firms responded by moving jobs offshore. Instead of bringing workers into the U.S., they built teams in Canada, Mexico, India, and Eastern Europe. This new fee could accelerate that shift.</p>

远程工作技术使这一切变得更加容易。班加罗尔的软件工程师现在可以为一家美国公司工作，而无需踏足美国。华沙的数据分析师、新加坡的生物技术研究人员和多伦多的网络安全专家也是如此。疫情使分布式团队常态化。签证费用为公司提供了另一个维持这种模式的理由。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Remote work technology has made doing this so much easier. A software engineer in Bangalore can now work for a U.S. company without ever setting foot in the country. The visa fee gives companies another reason to keep them that way.</p>

还有一个竞争力的问题。如果美国公司无法雇佣所需的人才，它们就有可能落后。如果人才流向其他国家——那些拥有更宽松移民政策的国家——创新也会随之而去。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

### 法律挑战与政治背景

这项行政命令已经引发了法律辩论，移民专家指出签证费用受《**移民和国籍法**》（Immigration and Nationality Act: 规范美国移民、入籍和公民身份的主要联邦法律）的管辖，该法对如何评估费用有具体规定。法律允许成本回收，这意味着政府可以收取费用以弥补处理申请的开支，但它不授权惩罚性定价。这项新费用远远超出了审查H-1B申请的成本。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The executive order has already triggered legal debate- where immigration experts have pointed out that visa fees are governed by the Immigration and Nationality Act, which lays out specific rules for how fees can be assessed. The law allows for cost recovery — meaning that the government can charge a fee to cover the expense of processing applications — but it doesn’t authorize punitive pricing. The new fee goes far beyond what it costs to review an H-1B petition.</p>
</details>

卡托研究所（Cato Institute）警告称，尽管该费用可能缺乏明确的法定依据，但法院仍可能维持其有效性。移民法赋予行政部门广泛的自由裁量权，过去对总统公告的挑战结果好坏参半。2020年，特朗普曾试图在疫情期间暂停某些签证类别的入境，此举在法庭上被阻止。但其他限制措施则得以保留。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Cato Institute has warned that while the fee may lack a clear statutory basis, courts could still uphold it. Immigration law gives the executive branch wide latitude, and past challenges to presidential proclamations have had mixed results. In 2020, Trump tried to suspend entry for certain visa categories during the pandemic. That move was blocked in court. But other restrictions have survived.</p>
</details>

如果该费用受到挑战——大多数观察家预计会如此——案件可能取决于它被定性为国家安全措施、劳动力市场保护还是收入工具。政府在这点上一直含糊其辞。商务部长霍华德·卢特尼克将其描述为确保公司只雇佣“足够有价值”的工人的一种方式。这并非一个法律标准。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If the fee is challenged — and most observers expect it will be — the case could hinge on whether it’s framed as a national security measure, a labor market protection, or a revenue tool. The administration has been vague on that point. Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick described it as a way to ensure companies only hire “valuable enough” workers. That’s not a legal standard.</p>
</details>

在法院做出裁决之前，该费用仍然有效，公司将不得不决定是支付、规避还是对抗它。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Until the courts weigh in, the fee stands and companies will have to decide whether to pay it, avoid it, or fight it.</p>
</details>

### 移民政策与产业政策的脱节：现代案例

就在签证费用宣布前几天，国土安全部（Department of Homeland Security）进行了其历史上最大规模的工作场所移民突袭行动。超过400名特工突袭了佐治亚州的一家现代汽车（Hyundai）电动汽车工厂，逮捕了475名外国工人。大多数被拘留者是持错误类型签证入境的韩国公民。这次突袭是与州政府官员协调进行的，他们封锁了道路并提供了巴士运送工人。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Just days before the visa fee was announced, the Department of Homeland Security carried out the largest worksite immigration raid in its history. More than 400 agents descended on a Hyundai electric vehicle plant in Georgia, arresting 475 foreign workers. Most of those detained were South Korean nationals who had entered the country on the wrong type of visa. The raid was coordinated with state officials, who blocked roads and provided buses to transport the workers.</p>
</details>

在此之前，这家工厂一直被誉为一项重大成功案例。现代汽车已承诺向该项目投资126亿美元，预计将在一个经济停滞的地区创造8,500个美国就业岗位。地方政府已花费数亿美元升级基础设施、加深港口、建设学校和住房以支持该工厂。这次突袭使所有这些都面临风险。

这些工人并非传统意义上的无证移民。许多人是根据签证豁免计划或B-1商务访问签证合法进入美国的，这两种签证都允许短期商务停留，但不允许从事实际劳动。代表工人的移民律师表示，他们是工程师和设备专家，被带进来安装和校准高度专业化的机械。据他们的律师称，培训美国工人从事同样的工作需要三到五年时间。

According to their lawyers, it would take three to five years to train American workers to do the same work.</p>
</details>

韩国并非低工资国家，它是美国的亲密盟友，也是美国制造业最大的外国投资者之一。这次突袭引发了外交反弹。韩国外交部长飞往华盛顿。现代汽车高管措手不及。佐治亚州共和党州长曾大力支持该项目，但他拒绝置评，部分原因可能是他的州在这次突袭中扮演了角色。在移民及海关执法局（Immigration and Customs Enforcement: ICE）特工进入之前，佐治亚州警就封锁了道路，佐治亚州惩教署的巴士被用来运走韩国工人。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">South Korea is not a low-wage country. It’s a close U.S. ally and one of the largest foreign investors in American manufacturing. The raid triggered a diplomatic backlash. South Korea’s foreign minister flew to Washington. Hyundai executives were blindsided. Georgia’s Republican governor, who had championed the project, declined to comment, perhaps partially because his state had played a role in the raid. 这一事件暴露了美国移民政策与产业政策之间的不匹配。联邦政府希望外国公司在美国建厂，但其移民规定却使它们难以引进完成工作所需的人员。这引发了一个问题：美国是否仍在做生意——还是政府只是像一个第三世界国家一样，为了收费而敲诈公司？

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

The federal government wants foreign companies to build in America — but its immigration rules make it extremely difficult for them to bring in the people they need to get the job done. It raises the question: is the United States still in the business of doing business — or is the government just shaking down companies for fees, in the style of a third-world country?</p>
</details>

### 对全球人才流动和创新中心的影响

几十年来，美国为高技能移民提供了一条可预测的途径：获得学位，找到工作，并努力争取公民身份。H-1B签证是实现这一目标的法律工具。拟议的费用引入了一个财务障碍，可能会将一些来自中等收入国家的最佳申请人排除在外，因为这项费用超过了他们的年薪。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For decades, the U.S. offered a predictable route for skilled migrants: earn a degree, secure a job, and work towards citizenship. The H-1B visa was the legal instrument that made this possible. The proposed fee introduces a financial barrier that could exclude some of the best applicants from middle-income countries where the fee exceeds annual salaries.</p>
</details>

虽然这可能对美国不利，但对中国和印度等国家可能有利。曾经离开这些国家的人才现在可能会留在国内。

新的签证费用可能会加速一个已经开始的转变——那些曾经将人才输出作为经济战略的国家，现在正在投资于人才保留。

没有公开信，没有协调一致的反击，也没有最依赖外国人才的公司的首席执行官发表重大声明。那些曾经警告限制性移民政策将把创新推向海外的高管们，现在却无话可说。

### 结论：美国商业环境的不确定性与声誉损失

长期以来，美国一直是世界上最优秀、最聪明的人才前来学习、工作和创业的地方。H-1B签证远非完美，但它是这一承诺的一部分——高技能工人为经济做出贡献，雇主获得在国内找不到的人才的一种方式。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The United States has long been the place where the world’s best and brightest come to study, work, and build companies. The H-1B visa was far from perfect, but it was part of that promise — a way for skilled workers to contribute to the economy, and for employers to access the talent they couldn’t find at home.</p>
</details>

新的签证费用改变了这项协议的条款。它鼓励科技和工程公司在其他国家扩张——这在今天比以往任何时候都更容易做到。它使医疗专业人员和科学家更难移居美国，并降低了美国大学的吸引力，同时扼杀了它们的主要收入来源。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The new visa fee changes the terms of that deal. It incentivizes tech and engineering firms to expand in other countries — which is easier to do today than ever before. It makes it harder for medical professionals and scientists to move to the United States, and it reduces the attractiveness of American universities while killing a major revenue stream for them.</p>
</details>

更深层次的问题可能是整体模式。这项费用只是众多政策之一——它们被迅速制定、解释不一且经常修订——使得美国的商业环境越来越难以驾驭。我们看到关税似乎每周都在变化，对销往中国的AI芯片征收15%的税，推动改变可口可乐配方，要求解雇英特尔首席执行官，以及与哥伦比亚广播公司（CBS）和迪士尼（Disney）等媒体公司达成与监管批准同时进行的和解。甚至Cracker Barrel的标志也未能逃脱总统的审查。特朗普政府的做法与其说是“小政府”，不如说是“亲力亲为”——这种风格在十年前会让自由市场共和党人感到震惊。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The deeper problem may be the overall pattern. The fee is just one of many policies — quickly enacted, inconsistently explained, and often revised — that have made the U.S. business environment increasingly difficult to navigate. We have seen tariffs that seem to change on a week by week basis, a 15% tax on AI chip sales to China, a push to change the Coca Cola recipe, demands for the firing of Intel’s CEO and settlements with media companies like CBS and Disney that coincided with regulatory approvals. Even the Cracker Barrel logo has not avoided presidential scrutiny.
