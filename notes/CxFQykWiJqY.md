### AI 创业的误区与现实

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: 有一种创业者，这可能是一个贬义词，但他们认为自己可以轻易地、一键式地创办一家企业。他们觉得：“因为我买了这门课，我就有了每年赚取一百万美元的副业秘方。”
现在 AI 领域有一种反模式，就是“我有 50 个 AI 代理在运营我的公司，并且有一套 AI 告诉我每天该做什么。” 我认为这是错误的做法。这就像你在制造一个“垃圾”机器，而不是“老虎机”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: There's a category of entrepreneurs, which might be a negative term, but it's someone who thinks they can just build a business with a single click, effortlessly. Because I bought this course, I now have the recipe for making a million dollars a year as a side hustle. There's kind of an anti-pattern with AI right now where I have 50 AI agents running my company and I have a suite of AI that tells me what to do every day. I think that's the wrong approach. That's just like you're making a slop machine, slop, not slot.

</details>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: 每个人都在 Twitter 上说：“我自动化了所有事情，我每周只工作一小时，周末靠我的 SaaS 应用就能赚 1000 万美元。” 大部分都是营销辞令。他们对你撒谎。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: Everyone on Twitter is like, "I automated everything and I work one hour a week and I make $10 million on the weekend with my SAS app." Like, most of that is just marketing. They're lying to you.

</details>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: 在很大程度上，你可以轻易地售卖希望。但你售卖的是一种“跳过辛苦工作、直接触达价值”的愿景，而这永远不会发生，永远行不通。但对于向你推销课程的人来说，他们会赚一大笔钱。他们找到了印钞的方法。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: Uh, for the most part, you can sell hope really easily. But you're selling this vision of like skipping the hard work, shortcutting directly to the value, which will never happen. It'll never work. But for the person who's selling you that course, they're going to make a ton of money. They they found the way to print money.

</details>

<summary>Original English</summary>

### 验证想法：拥抱失败

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: 我每周都会尝试一个不同的想法，我正在实验的东西，然后我很快就会学到那是个坏主意。我做得越多，就越习惯于证明自己是错的。我学到了一个反直觉的事实：在初创公司中，你实际上是在追逐“证明自己是错的”。这是可能发生的最好的事情，因为你节省了数周或数月的时间。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: Over and over I tried that kind of like once a week. I had a different idea, something I was experimenting with, and then I'd learn pretty quickly that it was a bad idea. The more I did it, the more I got used to proving myself wrong, and I I kind of learned the counterintuitive fact that in startups, you're actually chasing proving yourself wrong. That's the best thing that can possibly happen cuz you're saving weeks or months of time.

</details>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: 最初，我花几个月时间构思想法，然后希望有人能证明我是对的，说这是值得构建的。但这恰恰是你不应该做的。我那样浪费了大约 3 个月。你实际上应该去寻找那些能告诉你“为什么这个行不通”的人。如果你找不到它行不通的理由，那么你确实有了一个可以追求的有形想法。如果我能重来一次，我会去寻找那个强有力的理由，说明为什么某个东西行不通，而不是寄希望于可能成功的理由。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: In the beginning, I was building ideas for months and then I would hope that someone would prove me right and say this was worth building, but that's the opposite of what you should be doing. I wasted maybe 3 months doing that. You should actually be hunting for someone to tell you why this won't work. If you can't find a reason it won't work, then you actually have some sort of tangible idea you should pursue. If I could do it all over again, I would be hunting for the strong reason why something won't work instead of hoping for the reason that might build.

</details>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: 尽可能多地构建，这能提供最多的信息。我想，如果我没有在那之前失败 10 次，我就不会最终创立 Gum Loop。与用户尽可能多地交流，这几乎是一种你必须赢得的特权，因为一开始你没有用户可以交流。你可能会恳求人们试用你的产品，这并不有趣，但你必须经历。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: as much as you can. Like that's what provides the most information. I don't think I would have ended up building Gum Loop if I didn't fail 10 times before that. Talking to users as much as you can, it's almost a a privilege you have to earn because in the beginning you won't have any users to talk to.

</details>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: 我认为将用户置于你所构建的一切的最前沿，即使他们说的是你不愿听的话——“你的产品很糟糕”，“你没在构建正确的东西”，“它没有解决我的问题”——这实际上是你获得的最有价值的反馈。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Max Brodeur-Urbas**: I think putting them at the forefront of everything you're building, even if uh they're saying things you don't want to hear, your product sucks or you're not building the right thing or it's not solving my problem, that's actually the most val valuable feedback you can get.

</details>

<details>

Like if you find that thing that will get you to work until midnight and not want to close your laptop, every day becomes easier.

### 用户群体与产品定位

<summary>Original English</summary>
