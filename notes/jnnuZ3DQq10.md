为什么一些对中国政府大加挞伐的批评者能得到宽容，而另一些人却遭到无情的惩罚？我们都知道，在中国网络上发表异见本质上是极其危险的，但我们今天看到的现实却充满了刺眼的矛盾。像**艾未未**这样著名的异见人士最近能够访问中国，并在他的媒体节目中赞美这趟旅行，尽管他曾屡次触碰该政权的政治红线。相比之下，许多跨越了完全相同红线的人，面临的却是限制出境、流亡，甚至是长达数年的监禁和百万美元的**跨国悬赏**（Transnational Bounties: 跨国追捕和通缉的赏金）。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Why are some loud critics of the Chinese government tolerated while others are ruthlessly punished? We all know that dissenting online in China is inherently risky, but the reality we see today is full of glaring contradictions. Prominent dissenters like Ai Weiwei were able to visit China recently and praise the trips on their media shows, despite repeatedly violating the regime's political red lines. In contrast, many people who cross those exact same red lines face exit bans, exile, or even multi-year prison sentences and million-dollar transnational bounties.

</details>

如果国家镇压仅仅是为了公正地执行规则，那么模式应该是连贯的，但事实并非如此。**Elon Musk**在**X**（原推特）上宣传武汉实验室理论后，遭到了中国政府的严厉批评。然而，他的**上海超级工厂**在政权的全力支持下继续扩张。与此同时，喜剧演员**罗杰叔叔**仅仅开了一个关于**华为**手机监视公民的讽刺玩笑，他在中国的所有商业存在一夜之间就灰飞烟灭了。那么，这里的治国原则到底是什么？中国网络监管背后那奇怪的逻辑又是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

If state repression was simply about enforcing the rules impartially, the pattern would be consistent, but it isn't. Elon Musk was sharply criticised by the Chinese government after promoting the Wuhan lab theory on X. Yet his Shanghai Gigafactory continues to expand with the full support of the regime. Meanwhile, the comedian Uncle Roger made a single satirical joke about Huawei phones surveilling citizens, and his entire business presence in China was vaporised overnight. So, what is the governing principle here? What is the strange logic behind China's online policing?

</details>

### 结果导向的系统

就我个人而言，作为一个在中国生活了近30年、在2021年移居英国前在上海建立事业的中国大陆音乐制作人，我不得不亲自在这个诡异的矛盾中寻找出路。自从我开始在这个频道表演倡导个人自由和香港自由的歌曲后，**北京国家安全部门**指示我在中国的家人要求我停止。如果我回去，我将面临牢狱之灾。然而，我的音乐署名依然在中国的大片和热门电视节目中清晰可见。直到今天，我与中国一线艺人的合作仍在悄然继续。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Speaking personally, as a mainland Chinese music producer who lived in China for nearly 30 years and built a career in Shanghai before moving to the UK in 2021, I've had to navigate this bizarre contradiction myself. After I began performing songs advocating for individual liberty and Hong Kong's freedoms on this channel, Beijing's national security department directed my family in China to demand that I stop. I face prison if I return. And yet my music credits remain perfectly visible in Chinese blockbuster films and TV shows. My collaborations with A-list artists in China quietly continue today.

</details>

这可能看起来仅仅是官僚机构的无能，但事实并非如此。这是一个高度蓄意、选择性的网络监管架构。要理解这个架构，我们必须超越“言论自由与审查制度”这种过于简单的二元对立。这种框架预设了一个基于规则运作的系统。然而，中国运行的是一个**结果导向的系统**（Outcome-based system: 完全根据战略优先级调整的管治体系），它完全根据国家的战略优先级进行校准，这是许多观察家误解的现实。这也正是我将这个频道的重点从音乐倡导转向**结构性分析**的确切原因。音乐可以保存自由，但仅仅保存情感是不够的。我们需要具体的框架，向一个日益将自身自由视为理所当然的现代西方世界解释这种现实。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

This might appear to be mere bureaucratic incompetence. But it is not. It is a highly deliberate, selective online policing architecture. To understand this architecture, we must move beyond the simplistic binary of free speech versus censorship. That framework assumes a rule-based system. China, however, operates on an outcome-based system calibrated entirely to strategic priorities, a reality that many observers misunderstand. This is exactly why I am shifting the focus of this channel from music advocacy to structural analysis. Music preserves liberties, but emotional preservation is not enough. We need concrete frameworks to explain this reality to a modern West that increasingly takes its own liberties for granted.

</details>

### 技术即国家生存

在这段视频中，我将剖析现代独裁体制实际上是如何运作的，并分析这一架构中经常被许多观察家忽略的两个双重标准。一旦你理解了这两个层面，矛盾就会烟消云散。要解开这种奇怪的逻辑，我们需要了解这种选择性的网络监管架构最初是如何以及为什么建立起来的。正如我们所知，中国实行一党制，不受有实质意义的宪法限制，这一事实白纸黑字地写在中共党章中。在这种条件下，统治逻辑与经典的法治根本不同。正如奥地利哲学家**Friedrich Hayek**（弗里德里希·哈耶克）所警告的那样，当一个国家在没有宪法限制的情况下追求压倒一切的集体目标时，它就会抛弃公正性。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

In this video, I will unpack how modern autocracy actually functions and analyse two recurring double standards in this architecture that many observers miss. Once you understand these two layers, the contradiction disappears. To unpack this strange logic, we need to understand how and why this selective online policing architecture was built in the first place. As we know, China operates under a one-party system unrestricted by a meaningful constitution, a fact literally codified in the CCP's party charter. Under such conditions, the governing logic differs fundamentally from the classical rule of law. As the Austrian philosopher Friedrich Hayek warned, when a state pursues overarching collective goals without constitutional limits, it abandons impartiality.

</details>

在审查在线帖子时，当局问的问题从来不是“你违反规定了吗？”唯一重要的问题是：“你与国家当前的战略优先级一致吗？”那么，北京政权到底有着怎样压倒一切的目标和优先事项，驱使它建立起这个令人费解的架构呢？要回答这个问题，我们必须倒回20世纪90年代初。1989年天安门镇压之后，西方的制裁严重挑战了中国政权的合法性。在巨大的压力下，**邓小平**被迫进行了一百八十度的大转弯重置，放弃了严格的意识形态纯洁性，并积极拥抱西方资本主义，这为**FDI**（外国直接投资）打开了大门。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

When policing online posts, the question authorities ask is never, "Did you break a rule?" The only question that matters is, "Are you aligned with the state's current strategic priorities?" So what exactly are the Beijing regime's overarching goals and priorities that drove it to build this perplexing architecture? To answer that, we must rewind to the early 1990s. Following the 1989 Tiananmen crackdown, Western sanctions severely challenged the legitimacy of the Chinese regime. Under immense pressure, Deng Xiaoping was forced to make a U-turn reset, giving up strict ideological purity and aggressively embracing Western capitalism, which opened the door to FDI, foreign direct investment.

</details>

这次重置的核心，是对**STEM**（科学、技术、工程和数学）和工业化的极度优先考量。这是为了实现政权“民族复兴”的终极目标，也是为了纠正人们眼中因缺乏技术而导致的所谓百年屈辱。那次掉头开启了工程师主导党内最高层的趋势，这一趋势一直延续至今。从那一刻起，该政权形成了一个简单的战略痴迷：**技术意味着国家的存亡**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

At the very centre of the reset was a hyper-prioritisation of STEM and industrialisation. This was designed to achieve the regime's ultimate goal of national rejuvenation, a corrective to a perceived century of humiliation brought about by a lack of technology. That U-turn started a trend of engineers dominating the highest echelons of the party that continues to this day. From that moment on, the regime developed a simple strategic obsession: Technology meant national survival.

</details>

### 打造数字局域网

现在考虑一下历史时机。万维网（World Wide Web）恰好在同一时间，即1989年被创造出来。当中国在90年代接入互联网时，政权对此感到十分矛盾。它一方面欢迎技术发展，另一方面又挪用了从**Cisco**（思科）等西方公司转移来的技术，构建了世界上最孤立的审查基础设施——**防火长城**（Great Firewall: 中国用以隔离和审查互联网的庞大基础设施）。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Now consider the historical timing. The World Wide Web was created at the exact same time in 1989. When China connected to the internet in the 1990s, the regime felt ambivalent about it. While it welcomed technological development, it simultaneously appropriated technology transferred from Western corporations like Cisco to construct the world's most isolated censorship infrastructure, the Great Firewall.

</details>

要了解它的规模，你可以访问一个叫做“中国防火墙测试”的网站，输入你每天使用的西方平台。**Google**、**YouTube**、**ChatGPT**、**Instagram**、**TikTok**、**Facebook**、X、**LinkedIn**和**WhatsApp**。它们中99%在中国大陆（不包括香港）被封锁。从1998年到2009年左右防火墙建成，几乎所有外国在线服务都因拒绝遵守国家审查而被系统性地清除。由此，一个庞大、孤立且高度工业化的网络空间——“中国局域网”诞生了。今天，超过10亿的中华人民共和国公民完全依赖西方平台的国内替代品，占全球互联网流量的20%以上。顺便说一句，我在这里使用“中华人民共和国公民”而不是“中国大陆人”，是为了避免任何民族上的暗示。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

To grasp the scale of this, you can visit a site called China Firewall Test and input the Western platforms you use daily. Google, YouTube, ChatGPT, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook X, LinkedIn, and WhatsApp. 99% of them are blocked in mainland China, excluding Hong Kong. From 1998 to the firewall's completion around 2009, almost all foreign online services were systematically purged for refusing to comply with state censorship. Consequently, a vast, isolated and heavily industrialised cyberspace called the 'Chinese Intranet' was born. Today, over 1 billion PRC citizens depend entirely on domestic equivalents of Western platforms, accounting for over 20% of global internet traffic. And by the way, I use 'PRC citizens' here instead of 'Chinese mainlanders' to avoid any ethnic implications.

</details>

### 互联网数字驯化

获得能可靠绕过防火长城的VPN一直极其困难。老实说，绝大多数中国公民从未觉得有必要使用它们。在成长过程中，我曾天真地以为**百度**、**微博**和**微信**就是整个互联网。直到2018年出国留学，我才知道YouTube是什么。但与普遍的看法相反，这个内部网络不仅仅是为了压制异见而建立的。它是为了心理学家所说的**数字驯化**（Digital Domestication: 通过环境设定让用户在潜移默化中内化自我审查的心理机制）而设计的。自全球信息革命曙光初现以来，这个局域网的存在就是为了训练大陆用户实时内化自我审查，以适应政权优先事项的转变。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

It has always been extremely difficult to obtain VPNs that could reliably bypass the Great Firewall. And to be honest, the vast majority of PRC citizens never feel the need to use them. Growing up, I genuinely thought Baidu, Weibo and WeChat were the entire internet. I didn't even know what YouTube was until I studied abroad in 2018. But contrary to popular belief, this intranet wasn't built merely to suppress dissent. It was engineered for what psychologists call digital domestication. Since the dawn of the global information revolution, this intranet has existed to train mainland users to internalise self-censorship in real time. adapting to the regime's shift in priorities.

</details>

我们在避免触碰终极红线方面拥有深刻的肌肉记忆，例如：永远不要直呼最高领导人的名字；永远不要挑战国家的建国神话或专制制度；永远不要与敏感的民间组织有牵连。一旦越界，你就会面临数字孤立，失去主要的通讯和银行应用，或者更糟——被扣上一个叫做**寻衅滋事罪**（picking quarrels and provoking trouble: 中国司法体系中常用于打压异见的口袋罪名）的万能罪名，这将带来3到5年的监禁。久而久之，中国公民学会了保持非政治化，偶尔发挥创造力，使用暗语来巧妙地避开红线。当地人把这叫做“开启困难模式玩游戏”。而那些公开发表异见的人，经常被局域网用户排斥，认为他们走的是只有流亡者才想走的“简单模式”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

We possess a profound muscle memory for avoiding ultimate red lines, such as: Never name the supreme leader; Never challenge the country's founding myth or the autocratic system; Never associate with uh sensitive civil organisations. Step out of line and you face digital isolation, the loss of your primary communication and banking apps, or worse, a catch-all charge of 'picking quarrels and provoking trouble', which carries a 3- to 5-year prison sentence. Over time, PRC citizens learn to remain apolitical, occasionally navigating creatively using coder language to artfully skirt red lines. Locals call this playing the game on hard mode. Explicit dissenters are often ostracised by intranet users for taking the easy mode that only exiles want to play.

</details>

这个系统完美地契合了中国最伟大的经济繁荣期。从政权的角度来看，这一战略是一个巨大的胜利。技术扩张带来了三十年的GDP大幅增长，同时通过数字驯化，造就了庞大且具备内化自我约束力的民众。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

This system perfectly coincided with China's greatest economic boom. From the regime's perspective, the strategy was a triumph. Three decades of massive GDP growth achieved by technological expansion and a massive populace with internalised self-regulation driven by digital domestication.

</details>

### 科技与人文的差异

这种对科技领域根深蒂固的偏袒，导致了这一架构中的第一个结构性双重标准。让我们回到那个鲜明的对比：Elon Musk对阵罗杰叔叔。2023年，Elon Musk在X上向数千万观众宣传了新冠病毒武汉实验室泄漏理论。中国政府立刻威胁了他在中国的商业利益。这个警告是由**《环球时报》**（中国最强大的官方媒体喉舌）发出的，登上了国际头条。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

This deep-seated bias toward the technological sector leads to the first structural double standard in this architecture. Let's go back to the glaring comparison: Elon Musk versus Uncle Roger. In 2023, Elon Musk promoted the COVID Wuhan lab-leak theory on X to tens of millions of viewers. Instantly, the Chinese government threatened his Chinese business interests. The warning was issued by the Global Times, China's most powerful state media mouthpiece, making international headlines.

</details>

但接下来实际发生了什么呢？Elon 从未撤回那条帖子。他访问了中国，继续经营目前依然是世界最大的**Tesla**（特斯拉）上海超级工厂，并在相当于中国版X的微博上保持着活跃账号。就在几天前，他跨越了另一条重大的红线，评论美国针对委内瑞拉和伊朗的举措是如何为遏制中国的扩张主义做准备的。然而，他在中国的业务安然无恙。形成鲜明对比的是，喜剧演员罗杰叔叔仅仅随口开了个关于华为手机窃听有关**习近平**主席对话的玩笑。他在中国所有平台上的账号瞬间灰飞烟灭，一夜之间摧毁了他在中国开展业务的能力。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

But what actually happened next? Elon never retracted the post. He visited China, continues to run Gigafactory Shanghai, still the world's largest Tesla factory, and maintains an active account on Weibo, the Chinese equivalent of X. And just days ago, he crossed another major red line by commenting on how US moves against Venezuela and Iran are setting the stage to deter China's expansionism. Yet, nothing happened to his business in China. In stark contrast, comedian Uncle Roger made a passing joke about Huawei phones surveilling conversations about President Xi. His accounts across all Chinese platforms were instantly vaporised, destroying his ability to conduct business in China overnight.

</details>

这完美地概括了国家的偏见：偏袒科技和资本，同时残酷地惩罚人文学科的批评者。Elon Musk极大助力了中国电动汽车供应链的发展。因为他的利用价值与国家利益一致，所以无论他跨过多少红线，政权都欢迎他。而在人文学科领域活动的罗杰叔叔最初被认为是无害的，但就在他触碰了一条微小红线的瞬间，立刻被重新归类为对社会稳定的潜在威胁。这种偏见如此根深蒂固，以至于政权会为了科技部门选择性地放弃自己的审查制度。虽然西方互联网被封锁，但像**GitHub**这样的关键开发者平台仍然可以访问。为什么？因为瘫痪GitHub将会瘫痪中国整个科技产业。精明的公民经常利用GitHub的去中心化结构来存档被审查的内容，比如被删除的武汉采访，从而躲过警察的追查，因为国家无法在不关闭整个网站的情况下封锁特定的代码库。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

This perfectly encapsulates the state's bias, favouring tech and capital whilst brutally punishing humanity's critics. Elon Musk significantly aids in the development of China's EV supply chain. Because his utility aligns with the state, he is welcomed by the regime no matter how many red lines he crosses. Uncle Roger, operating in the humanities, was initially deemed harmless but was instantly reclassified as a potential threat to social stability the moment a tiny red line was crossed. This bias is so deeply entrenched that the regime will selectively abandon its own censorship for the tech sector. While the western internet is blocked, crucial developer platforms like GitHub remain accessible. Why? Because crippling GitHub will cripple China's entire tech industry. Savvy citizens routinely exploit GitHub's decentralised structure to archive censored content like deleted interviews from Wuhan, dodging the police because the state cannot block a specific repository without shutting down the entire site.

</details>

### 切换赛道的代价

当个人转换他们的专业方向时，这种双重标准就变得极其黑暗。想一想两个杰出头脑截然不同的命运：**李录**和**阮晓寰**。李录曾是1989年天安门广场抗议活动中最受通缉的学生领袖之一。镇压后，他成功逃往美国，在哥伦比亚大学获得了三个学位，并成为了一名非常成功的对冲基金经理。最关键的是，他说服了**Warren Buffett**（沃伦·巴菲特）投资中国电动汽车公司**比亚迪**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

This double standard becomes incredibly dark when individuals switch their professional focus. Consider the divergent fates of two brilliant minds, Li Lu and Ruan Xiaohuan. Li Lu was one of the most wanted student leaders of the 1989 Tiananmen Square protests. He managed to flee to the US after the crackdown, obtained three degrees at Columbia University, and became a highly successful hedge fund manager. Crucially, he convinced Warren Buffett to invest in the Chinese EV company BYD.

</details>

通过这笔投资以及他坚定的持股（即使在股价暴跌80%的时候），他促成了该公司从电池制造商转型为全球占据主导地位的电动汽车制造商。因为他的丰功伟绩完美契合了国家主导前沿科技的目标，政权干脆无视了针对他依然有效的逮捕令。自2010年代以来，他已经成为中国的知识界超级巨星，在顶尖大学演讲，并保持着高调的微博账号。所以，这位拯救了电动汽车品牌并最终在销量上超越特斯拉的人，曾经是中国头号通缉犯。好好体会一下这件事。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

Through this investment and his steadfast shareholding, even when the stock plummeted by 80%, he enabled the company's transformation from a battery maker into the world's dominant electric vehicle manufacturer. Because his monumental contribution aligned perfectly with the state's goal of dominating frontier tech, the regime simply ignored his active arrest warrant. <summary>Original English</summary>
