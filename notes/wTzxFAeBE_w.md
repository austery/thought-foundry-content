<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

</details>

**Pentagon 记者 Dave Phillips**: 军队进入这项新政策时没有计划如何执行。因此，我的报道显示，各种高度熟练的人在军队中，比如医生、律师、飞行员，政府大量投资支付其非常昂贵的教育的人，已经被迅速解雇，没有明显的替代品。即使他们不再从事这些工作，他们仍在名单上。他们中的许多人，实际上，大多数人，在付费行政假期中度过了数月，领取薪水，但无法做他们的工作。我采访了一位名叫 Parker Moore 的海军中士，海军花费多年训练他成为航空母舰上的**核裂变技术员**。从本质上讲，他们的工作是监督整个团队，使航空母舰的心脏运转。但一旦宣布了规则，他们很快就被告知要休假。现在该航空母舰的司令官说"不，我们需要你"。但海军还是让他们休假。那艘航母**亚伯拉罕·林肯号**没有 Moore 中士就离开了，现在在中东没有一位真正关键的核反应堆监督员。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Pentagon Reporter Dave Phillips**: The military went into this new policy with no plan of how to carry it out. And so my reporting showed that all sorts of highly skilled people in the military, people like doctors, lawyers, pilots, people that the government invested heavily in paying for their very expensive education have been quickly dismissed without an obvious replacement. And even though they're not in their jobs anymore, they're still on the books. Many of them, actually, most of them, spent months and months on paid administrative leave, collecting a paycheck, but unable to do their jobs. I spoke to one Navy chief named Parker Moore, who the Navy had spent years training to be a nuclear fission technician on an aircraft carrier.
