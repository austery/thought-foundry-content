### 肥料与氦气：比石油更致命的短缺

**Steve Keen**: 霍尔木兹海峡只有 21 公里宽，是一个极其狭窄的关口。人们只想到油价变贵，但实际上，生产系统的关键要素正在被切断。你将无法再生产芯片，因为**氦气**短缺；你也无法生产粮食，因为**肥料**消失了。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: Well, it's the choke point in the Persian Gulf to get through. You've got like 21 km. Okay, that's an incredibly narrow gap for ships to pass through and that means that all the countries that pump not just oil but fertilizer, helium, all these critical elements for the production system all have to pass through this point. critical elements of the production system are being terminated by this conflict. You can't produce chips anymore. And you can't... Well, you got hang on. You can't produce these chips either because the fertilizer is disappearing.

</details>

**Steve Keen**: 全球 30% 的氦气来自横跨沙特和伊朗的气田。氦气是**半导体产业**绝对核心的元素，没有它，你的 iPhone、平板电脑、所有电子设备的 CPU 和内存芯片都无法制造。如果切断 30% 的氦气供应，就切断了全球 30% 的半导体产能。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: So about 30% of the world's helium comes from a gas field which spans both Saudi Arabia and Iran. And then helium is absolutely critical for the semiconductor industry. It didn't matter 100 years ago. Helium is an essential element to make them for our iPhones, our tablets, everything electronic. If you need semiconductors, you need helium. So if you cut off 30% of the world's helium supply, you cut off the capacity to produce 30% of the world's semiconductors.

</details>

### 能源与全球生产总值的同步崩溃

**Steve Keen**: 能源与生产之间的联系异常紧密。在过去的 40 年里，全球能源消耗的年百分比变化与全球生产总值（GWP）的变化几乎步调一致。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: The really important point and I can bring up one of my own charts here is the role of energy in production because if we don't have energy we can't produce goods and services and the link is incredibly tight. This is looking at change in energy and change in gross world product over the last 40 years.

</details>

**Steve Keen**: 当能源上升时，GDP 上升；当能源下降时，GDP 下降。现在我们正在失去全球 20% 的**液化天然气**和大量的石油。我们可以预见能源将下降 5% 到 10%，那么全球生产总值也必然会下降 5% 到 10%。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: So, when energy goes up, GDP goes up. And when energy goes down, GDP goes down. Now, we're losing 20% of the world's liquefied natural gas, a substantial proportion of its oil as well. We could see a 5 or 10% fall in energy. we will certainly see a 5 or 10% fall in global world gross world product.

</details>

### AI 的繁荣与毁灭周期

**主持人**: 你曾预测过 2008 年金融危机。你现在认为 AI 会导致另一场金融崩盘吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: I've heard you make a few predictions about the future of the economic markets. You know, you're famous for predicting 2008 and the financial crash that occurred then. I've heard you saying that you think because of AI there's going to be another financial crash around the corner within one or two years.

</details>

**Steve Keen**: 是的。AI 正在发生的是一个经典的经济“繁荣与萧条”周期。新技术的开发（如当年的铁路）会让所有人疯狂涌入，导致过度投资。每个投资 AI 的人都野心勃勃地想成为地球上唯一的 AI 供应商。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: Yeah. What's happening with AI is a classic economic boom and bust cycle. A common pattern in capitalism is that some new technology will be developed like railways for example. AI is a natural example of that and what you get is massive overinvestment in the first instance because everybody who invests in AI has the ambition of being the only AI provider on the planet.

</details>

**Steve Keen**: 2026 年，大科技公司（Meta、亚马逊、微软、谷歌、甲骨文）预计仅在 AI 基础设施上就将花费 7200 亿美元。投资与收益比达到了 5:1，这在历史上是不可持续的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: The big tech companies, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, Alphabet, own Google, Oracle is on track to spend 720 billion on AI infrastructure in 2026 alone. We are seeing a 5:1 ratio of money being spent versus money coming in. Which is historically unsustainable.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: But I don't I'm not wor as worried about that as long-term consequences of AI because this is the first technology which implies you can actually virtually eliminate labor as necessary for reducing output. I think 50% of jobs could be wiped out. And when when everybody starts losing all their money very very quickly, you see this contraction. And get frugal and pull back in again. Lay people off and so on and so forth.

</details>

**Steve Keen**: 我认为**全民基本收入（UBI）**是必要的，考虑到机器人和 AI 对就业的影响。如果人们有了 UBI，他们就不必打三份工来维持生存，而是可以考虑自己创业的可能性。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: So, I think universal basic income is a necessity given what robotics and AI can do to employment. If he got a UBI, he wouldn't have to work at those three jobs. He might work at one or he might actually consider his own business possibilities in that situation.

</details>

### 未来展望：饥饿游戏还是星际迷航？

**Steve Keen**: 我们面临两种可能性：一种是《星际迷航》式的未来，拥有制造商品的复制器，我们都过着能源充沛的生活；另一种是《饥饿游戏》，只有一小群精英拥有所有机器人并生活得极好，而绝大多数人则被压迫。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: We have two possibilities. We have a future where Star Trek's high future where you have replicators that make the goods and we consume and and we all live a energy abundant life. or the hunger games where there's one little elite that gets has all the robots and lives extremely well and we tolerate and oppress the vast majority.

</details>

**Steve Keen**: 最基本的一点是，我们的系统比我们自认为的要脆弱得多。如果我们不尊重地球的物理约束，我们就会掉下所谓的“塞内卡悬崖（Seneca Cliff）”，从富足的未来坠入崩溃。停止选举那些傻瓜，我们需要一个以人为本、尊重物理现实的经济管理方案。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Keen**: Basic thing is our system is far more fragile than we've convinced oursel that it is. And if that robustness is destroyed... then we can fall off what's called the Seneca cliff. We can go from an abundant future to a collapse. Stop electing fools. We need to reverse back to having a humanoriented and physically realistic view of how the economy managed should be managed.

</details>
