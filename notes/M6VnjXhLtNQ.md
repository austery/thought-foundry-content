### 鸟类行为研究：香烟头的新用途

**记者6**: 最后，世界各地，许多鸟类都养成了一种奇怪的习惯。新西兰的歌鸫、墨西哥的家雀、加拉帕戈斯群岛的达尔文雀，它们都在巢里放香烟头。英国的一些鸣禽甚至直接在室外的烟灰缸里筑巢。现在，一项新研究为解释这种现象提供了证据。研究发现，对于遍布欧洲的色彩鲜艳的小蓝冠山雀来说，烟草中的毒素可能有助于阻止其巢穴中的寄生虫。**波兰一所大学**的研究人员研究了公园和森林中的鸟类，并监测了它们在几种不同类型巢穴中的健康状况。简单来说，一些鸟巢是普通的鸟巢箱，另一些则混有香烟头。大约两周孵化后，来自混有香烟头的巢穴中雏鸟的血液检查表明它们更健康。理论是，香烟头含有约 4,000 种化学物质，包括尼古丁、砷和重金属，可能有助于抵御可能影响鸟类及其脆弱雏鸟的害虫。墨西哥城的一项独立实验也提出了类似的建议。当研究人员在雀巢中添加害虫时，这些鸟类则会添加更多的香烟头。一位帮助领导波兰这项研究的进化生态学家希望，这项工作能有一个明确的启示，那就是“鸟类很聪明”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Reporter 6**: And finally, around the world, a lot of birds have picked up a curious habit. Song thrushes in New Zealand, house finches in Mexico, Darwin's finches in the Galapagos, they're all putting cigarette butts in their nests. Some song birds in Britain are even straight up nesting inside outdoor ashtrays. Now, a new study adds evidence to why this may be happening. It found that for the colorful little blue tits, which are found across Europe, the toxins in tobacco may keep parasites out of their nests. Researchers at a university in Poland studied the birds both in parks and the forest and monitored the health of them split across a couple different kinds of nests. Boiling it down a bit, some were in normal nest boxes, others had cigarette butts mixed in. And about 2 weeks after hatching, blood work on babies from the cigarette butt nests indicated they were healthier. The theory is that the cigarette butts, which have about 4,000 chemical compounds in them, including nicotine, arsenic, and heavy metals, may have helped ward off pests that could affect the birds and their helpless hatchlings. A separate experiment out of Mexico City suggested something similar. When researchers added pests to finch nests, the birds responded by adding more cigarette butts. An evolutionary ecologist who helped lead the study out of Poland hopes there's one clear takeaway from all of this work and it's that quote birds are smart.
