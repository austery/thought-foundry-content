### 我们是演化的产物：直觉与理性之争

我们先从第一小节开始，标题叫做“我们是演化的产物”。大家想想，我们平时做决策的时候，更多是根据理性，还是根据直觉？虽然我们多数人都以**理性人**（Rational Economic Man: 经济学中假设的能完全理性决策的个体）自居，大部分人在学校里面都学过一点基本的逻辑学，但是，只有在做数学题或者参加逻辑学考试的时候，才会用到逻辑推理，平时在生活中是用不到的。毕竟，逻辑推理是一件让人头疼的事情。想想这些名词，比如什么**三段论**（Syllogism: 一种由大前提、小前提和结论组成的演绎推理形式）、**演绎法**（Deduction: 从一般原理推导出特殊结论的推理方法）、**肯定前件**（Modus Ponens: 一种逻辑推理规则，如果P则Q，P成立，则Q成立）、**否定后件**（Modus Tollens: 一种逻辑推理规则，如果P则Q，Q不成立，则P不成立）等等，是不是又让你回想起当初学习逻辑学的痛苦经历？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Let's start with the first section, titled "We Are Products of Evolution." Think about it: when we make decisions in our daily lives, do we rely more on reason or intuition? Although most of us consider ourselves "rational economic actors," and most people have learned some basic logic in school, we only use logical reasoning when solving math problems or taking logic exams; it's not typically used in everyday life. After all, logical reasoning can be a headache. Think of terms like syllogism, deduction, modus ponens, modus tollens, and so on. Don't they remind you of the painful experience of studying logic back then?</p>
</details>

我们为何讨厌做逻辑推理呢？这是因为我们人类的心智系统并不是为了做逻辑推演或者做数学题而演化出来的。除了极少数的数学家之外，没有人是“喜欢”做数学题的。做逻辑推理十分耗费脑力，从演化的角度来说，通过理性推理来做决策是不划算的。那划算的方式是什么呢？那就是**自然选择**（Natural Selection: 生物演化中，适应环境的个体更有可能生存和繁殖的机制）给我们塑造了一套**直觉系统**（Intuitive System: 大脑快速、无意识地做出判断和决策的机制）。这套直觉系统让我们能够凭借直觉直接做出反应。我们在日常生活中做判断的时候往往是不动脑子的，大脑会自动运行，凭直觉就做出了决定。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Why do we dislike logical reasoning? This is because our human cognitive system did not evolve for logical deduction or solving math problems. Except for a very few mathematicians, no one "enjoys" doing math. Logical reasoning is very mentally demanding, and from an evolutionary perspective, making decisions through rational inference is not cost-effective. So, what is the cost-effective way? It is that natural selection has shaped an intuitive system for us. This intuitive system allows us to react directly based on intuition. In our daily lives, we often make judgments without conscious thought; the brain operates automatically, and decisions are made intuitively.</p>
</details>

比如说，今天晚上到底是吃肯德基还是吃麦当劳？吃麻辣烫还是炸鸡排？这些决策难道是经过逻辑推理以后才做出的吗？不是的。真实的情况是，你打开外卖软件，看到这家就点它了！不仅是“今晚吃什么”这种小事，对于很多人而言，就连“跟谁谈恋爱”、“上大学选什么专业”、“到底买不买房”等等人生大事，也都是凭借直觉做出的决策。我们就是一上头、一拍脑袋，就决定跟对方在一起了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For example, tonight, should I eat KFC or McDonald's? Malatang or fried chicken? Were these decisions made after logical reasoning? No. The reality is, you open a food delivery app, see a place, and just order from it! It's not just small matters like "what to eat tonight"; for many people, even major life decisions like "who to date," "what major to choose in college," or "whether to buy a house" are made based on intuition. We just get a sudden impulse, make a snap decision, and decide to be with someone.</p>
</details>

在这里我并不是要呼吁大家不要凭借直觉做决策，而要凭借逻辑推理做判断，并不是的。事实上，凭借直觉做判断恰恰体现了自然选择对我们人类心智的一种“雕琢”。这就是说，直觉往往对我们的生存与繁衍是很有用的。要知道，我们大部分的直觉都是对的。比如说，碰到烫的东西就要本能地躲开；看到悬崖这种高处的地方也本能地望而却步；闻到美味的食物就忍不住想要把它吃掉；遇到美丽的异性就本能地想要获取对方欢心。这些直觉都没过脑子，都是自动反应。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Here, I am not advocating that you should not make decisions based on intuition, but rather based on logical reasoning; that is not the case. In fact, making judgments based on intuition precisely reflects a "sculpting" of our human mind by natural selection. This means that intuition is often very useful for our survival and reproduction. You should know that most of our intuitions are correct. For example, we instinctively pull away from hot objects; we instinctively hesitate when seeing high places like cliffs; we can't help but want to eat delicious food when we smell it; and we instinctively want to win the affection of attractive people. These intuitions are all automatic reactions, without conscious thought.</p>
</details>

大家要知道，没有人会通过逻辑推理做出如下理性的论证：

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You should know that no one would make the following rational argument through logical reasoning:</p>
</details>

**进食论证**

前提1：进食是人类生存和繁衍所必需的。
前提2：我是个人类。
推论1：我需要进食才能生存与繁衍。
前提3：我的眼前有一份美食。
结论2：我需要吃掉眼前这份美食。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">**Argument for Eating**

Premise 1: Eating is necessary for human survival and reproduction.
Premise 2: I am a human.
Inference 1: I need to eat to survive and reproduce.
Premise 3: There is a delicious meal in front of me.
Conclusion 2: I need to eat this delicious meal in front of me.</p>
</details>

论证完毕！如果人类祖先是这么一步一步地靠逻辑推理做决策的话，那我们的祖先早就饿死了。所以说，直觉对我们的生存与繁衍是非常有效的工具，它的好处就是快、省脑力、省时间。想想看，我们的早期智人祖先如果遭遇到了一只大老虎，是没时间进行理性分析的，而是凭直觉撒腿就跑。有演化论学者把与人类生存与繁衍直接相关的直觉归纳了起来，分为了四类，统称为F4，也就是Feed（进食）、Fight（干仗）、Flee（逃跑），还有最后一个和繁殖相关的F开头的单词。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Argument complete! If our human ancestors had made decisions step-by-step through logical reasoning like this, they would have starved long ago. Therefore, intuition is a very effective tool for our survival and reproduction; its advantages are speed, saving mental effort, and saving time. Think about it, if our early hominid ancestors encountered a large tiger, they wouldn't have time for rational analysis; they would instinctively turn and run. Evolutionary scholars have summarized the intuitions directly related to human survival and reproduction into four categories, collectively known as the F4: Feed, Fight, Flee, and the last F-word related to reproduction.</p>
</details>

其实，除了和生存与繁衍直接相关的这些F4本能之外，我们对一些相对复杂的情境做出思维上的判断和决策，其实也是凭借直觉进行的。这样做依然是为了能够把复杂的情况简化并高效处理。学者们由此建构了heuristic这个概念，中文世界里面有的译作“**启发式**”（Heuristic: 简化复杂问题、快速找到满意解的认知捷径或经验法则），有的译作是“**捷思法**”（Heuristic: 同“启发式”，指快速、简便的思维策略），有的译作是“**探索法**”（Heuristic: 同“启发式”，指在信息不完全时寻找解决方案的方法）。这些翻译听起来可能有点让人不明所以。其实，所谓的heuristic就是“**认知套路**”（Cognitive Routine: 大脑处理信息和决策时形成的固定模式或快捷方式）的意思。如果用书面一点的方式来表述的话，可以译作“**认知定式**”（Cognitive Set: 同“认知套路”，指大脑在特定情境下倾向于采用的思维模式），或者我们可以打比方说，heuristic其实就是指我们大脑里面的思维快捷方式。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In fact, beyond these F4 instincts directly related to survival and reproduction, our mental judgments and decisions in relatively complex situations are also made intuitively. This is still done to simplify complex situations and process them efficiently. Scholars have thus constructed the concept of "heuristic." In the Chinese world, some translate it as "启发式" (heuristic: a cognitive shortcut or rule of thumb that simplifies complex problems and quickly finds satisfactory solutions), some as "捷思法" (heuristic: similar to "启发式," referring to quick and simple thinking strategies), and others as "探索法" (heuristic: similar to "启发式," referring to methods for finding solutions when information is incomplete). These translations might sound a bit confusing. In reality, what "heuristic" means is a "cognitive routine." If expressed in a more formal way, it can be translated as "cognitive set," or we can say metaphorically that heuristic actually refers to the mental shortcuts in our brains.</p>
</details>

当我们人类碰到一些复杂的情况的时候，依然不是通过理性来推理，而是凭借直觉做判断，这就叫认知定式。这样做的原因依然是做逻辑推理太费脑力、成本太高，而且速度很慢。于是自然选择为我们打造了一些思维的套路，可供我们快速反应，直接给出问题的答案。比如说，我今天到底是去这座山上打猎还是去那座山上打猎呢？这个问题好复杂，要真正搞清楚的话，我得为此去做调研，通过实地考察获得数据，比如说什么猎物的数量、猎物的种类、危险程度等等，然后还要根据这些数据做分析和推理。哎呀，太麻烦了！那比起用这样理性的方式做出决策，我还不如看看部落里的其他人，尤其是部落里面那个打猎能手，他去哪座山，我就跟着去就行了。再者说，大家都去同一个地方的话，人多力量大，这样还能打到更多的猎物。从众就是一个这样的“方便法门”。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When humans encounter complex situations, they still don't reason through rationality but make judgments based on intuition; this is called a cognitive set. The reason for this is still that logical reasoning is too mentally demanding, too costly, and too slow. Thus, natural selection has created certain thinking patterns for us, allowing us to react quickly and directly provide answers to problems. For example, should I hunt on this mountain today or that mountain? This question is so complex; to truly figure it out, I would have to conduct research, gather data through field observations—such as the number of prey, types of prey, danger levels, and so on—and then analyze and infer based on this data. Oh, it's too much trouble! Compared to making decisions in such a rational way, I might as well observe other people in the tribe, especially the skilled hunter in the tribe. Whichever mountain he goes to, I'll just follow. Furthermore, if everyone goes to the same place, there's strength in numbers, and we can catch more prey. Conformity is such a "convenient method."</p>
</details>

德国心理学家格尔德·吉仁泽（Gerd Gigerenzer）在他的《简捷启发式》（Simple Heuristics That Make Us Smart）一书中指出，认知定式它不仅仅是一种因脑力有限退而求其次的决策工具，而且在许多现实情境中，它比复杂的逻辑推理更有效。通过简化决策过程，认知定式可以减少我们在不确定情境下可能会犯的错误，并在缺乏信息或资源的情况下提供较为可靠的解决方案。吉仁泽认为，认知定式并非粗心大意的象征，而恰恰是大脑高效运作的体现。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">German psychologist Gerd Gigerenzer, in his book "Simple Heuristics That Make Us Smart," points out that cognitive heuristics are not merely second-best decision-making tools due to limited brainpower, but are often more effective than complex logical reasoning in many real-world situations. By simplifying the decision-making process, cognitive heuristics can reduce the errors we might make in uncertain situations and provide relatively reliable solutions when information or resources are lacking. Gigerenzer believes that cognitive heuristics are not a sign of carelessness, but rather a manifestation of the brain's efficient operation.</p>
</details>

诺贝尔经济学奖得主、心理学家丹尼尔·卡尼曼（Daniel Kahneman）在他的《思考，快与慢》（Thinking, Fast and Slow）一书中将人类的心智系统分为两层系统：**系统一**（System 1: 丹尼尔·卡尼曼提出的快速、自动化、无意识的思维系统）和**系统二**（System 2: 丹尼尔·卡尼曼提出的慢速、有意识、需要努力的思维系统）。系统一是指快速、自动化且无意识的思维过程，也就是一种直觉式的反应，它主要涉及的就是直觉和认知定式。而系统二则是一种慢速的、耗费资源的、有意识地通过理性和逻辑进行推理的心智系统。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Nobel laureate in economics and psychologist Daniel Kahneman, in his book "Thinking, Fast and Slow," divides the human mind into two systems: System 1 and System 2. System 1 refers to fast, automatic, and unconscious thought processes, which are intuitive reactions, primarily involving intuition and cognitive heuristics. System 2, on the other hand, is a slow, resource-intensive, conscious cognitive system that reasons through rationality and logic.</p>
</details>

系统一的特点是快反应，系统二的特点是慢思考。你运用系统二的时候，需要条分缕析地做逻辑推理，这是特别耗费脑力的，所以大多数人才不喜欢做数学题。我们更喜欢用系统一，直接、快速反应。系统一在我们的日常生活中占据主导地位，因为它能快速响应环境中的变化。相比之下，系统二虽然可以提供更加精确和深入的分析，但是耗时耗力，通常只有在做重要决策或者复杂问题时候才会被调用。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">System 1 is characterized by quick reactions, while System 2 is characterized by slow thinking. When you use System 2, you need to perform logical reasoning meticulously, which is very mentally demanding, which is why most people dislike doing math problems. We prefer to use System 1 for direct, quick reactions. System 1 dominates our daily lives because it can quickly respond to changes in the environment. In contrast, while System 2 can provide more precise and in-depth analysis, it is time-consuming and effort-intensive, and is usually only invoked when making important decisions or solving complex problems.</p>
</details>

现在人工智能很火热，为了方便你能理解认知定式，我们可以用机器思维来类比人的思维。用逻辑推理来做判断和决策是上一代人工智能的范式，也就是人类专家事先给机器输入各种各样的规则，然后根据这个**专家系统**（Expert System: 一种基于人类专家知识和推理规则的人工智能系统）让机器去做逻辑推理。比如，打败国际象棋大师加里·卡斯帕罗夫（Gary Kasparov）的人工智能“深蓝”（Deep Blue）就是基于这种专家系统而建构的。大量硬编码知识是由人类国际象棋大师提供的。“深蓝”在博弈时动用的心智模型就相当于卡尼曼所说的系统二。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Artificial intelligence is very popular now, and to help you understand cognitive heuristics, we can use machine thinking to analogize human thinking. Making judgments and decisions using logical reasoning was the paradigm of the previous generation of artificial intelligence, meaning human experts would pre-program various rules into the machine, and then based on this expert system, the machine would perform logical reasoning. For example, the AI "Deep Blue" that defeated chess grandmaster Gary Kasparov was constructed based on such an expert system. A large amount of hardcoded knowledge was provided by human chess grandmasters. The mental model "Deep Blue" employed during gameplay was equivalent to what Kahneman calls System 2.</p>
</details>

但是，这种基于专家系统而建构的人工智能，一旦转换到围棋领域，就无法在围棋比赛中战胜人类棋手了。这是因为围棋相比于国际象棋它要复杂得多。国际象棋大约有10^40种可能的局面，而围棋则大约有10^170种可能的局面。这个天文数字远远超过了宇宙中原子的总量。任何人类专家都不可能完备地思考这么多可能的局面。专家系统擅长的是在有限状态的空间里面进行推理，但在围棋这种“复杂游戏”中，就显得算力不够了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">However, this type of AI, built on expert systems, could not defeat human players in Go once it transitioned to the domain of Go. This is because Go is much more complex than chess. Chess has approximately 10^40 possible positions, while Go has about 10^170 possible positions. This astronomical number far exceeds the total number of atoms in the universe. No human expert could possibly fully contemplate so many possible positions. Expert systems excel at reasoning within a finite state space, but in a "complex game" like Go, their computational power proves insufficient.</p>
</details>

大家都知道，打败人类围棋棋手的AlphaGo是新一代的人工智能，它是基于**神经网络**（Neural Network: 受生物大脑结构启发，用于机器学习和模式识别的计算模型）的范式而建构的。人类并不需要为这种人工智能事先编写围棋规则，而是基于大量的棋谱让机器进行自主学习。它还可以通过与自身博弈来不断改进策略。这样训练出来的神经网络在下围棋的时候，动用的心智模型就相当于卡尼曼所说的系统一，也就和人类基于直觉和认知定式而做的决策更为类似。比如，AlphaGo为何这一步要这般落子，编写AlphaGo的程序员也无法解释，它的运作过程是一个“黑箱”。而人类围棋大师在下围棋的时候，同样是凭借一种直觉式的感觉就落子了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As everyone knows, AlphaGo, which defeated human Go players, is a new generation of artificial intelligence, built on the neural network paradigm. Humans do not need to pre-program Go rules for this type of AI; instead, the machine learns autonomously based on a vast number of game records. It can also continuously improve its strategies by playing against itself. The mental model employed by such a trained neural network when playing Go is equivalent to what Kahneman calls System 1, and is thus more akin to human decisions made based on intuition and cognitive heuristics. For example, why AlphaGo makes a particular move cannot be explained even by the programmers who wrote it; its operation is a "black box." Similarly, human Go masters make their moves based on an intuitive feeling.</p>
</details>

### 演化时滞：现代生活中的认知偏误

好，说了半天认知定式这么好用，那是不是说我们做决策的时候只需要凭借直觉，拍脑袋给方案就行了？答案是：并非如此。这认知定式的好处是快速给出反应，但是这成也萧何是败也萧何。我们在生活中做出错误的决策也是认知定式造成的。自然选择赋予我们的认知定式，在过去大部分情况下都比较管用，但是在面对现代相对复杂的生活情境时，往往会失效，甚至带来负面作用。而这就是缘于我们人类现有这一套心智系统是**不合时宜**的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Alright, after talking so much about how useful cognitive heuristics are, does that mean we only need to rely on intuition and make snap decisions? The answer is: not quite. The advantage of cognitive heuristics is providing quick reactions, but what makes them successful can also lead to failure. The wrong decisions we make in life are also caused by cognitive heuristics. The cognitive heuristics endowed by natural selection were mostly effective in the past, but in the face of relatively complex modern life situations, they often fail or even bring negative effects. This is because our current human cognitive system is "out of sync" with the times.</p>
</details>

我们心智系统里的各种认知定式是千百万年的自然选择的产物，大部分情况下，这种思维快捷方式给的直觉反应都是对的，有利于我们的生存与繁衍。但是，人类这个物种有一个和其他动物都不一样的地方，那就是我们今天的生存环境，相比于我们远古祖先的生存环境，那是发生了翻天覆地的变化。从几百万年前的东非稀树大草原，到今天高楼大厦组成的混凝土森林，这对其他许多物种来说，都是可以造成毁灭性打击的环境变化。当然，这一方面说明了我们人类的适应能力之强，但另一方面也说明我们现代人活在一种特别“拧巴”的处境之中。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The various cognitive heuristics in our mental system are products of millions of years of natural selection. Of course, this demonstrates the strong adaptability of humans, but it also shows that modern humans live in a particularly "awkward" situation.</p>
</details>

所谓“拧巴”是指我们现代人身上的本能、直觉、认知定式，都是为了适应远古时代的生存环境而被自然选择筛选出来的。然而环境变化太快了，人类的农业革命距今不到8000年，工业革命距今200多年，信息革命也就最近几十年的事情。别看这8000年、200年好像挺久远的，但放在生物演化的视角来看，根本不值一提。生物本能的演化，至少是以十万年乃至百万年为单位来衡量的。而这就造成了所谓的“**演化时滞**”（Evolutionary Lag: 生物演化速度未能跟上环境变化速度，导致生物特征与当前环境不适应的现象）。也就是说，人类本能的演化没跟上环境变化的节奏，存在时间上的滞后。

While 8,000 years or 200 years might seem like a long time, from the perspective of biological evolution, it's negligible. The evolution of biological instincts is measured in units of at least hundreds of thousands or even millions of years. This has led to what is called "evolutionary lag." 好，我们说完了进食这种F4之类的本能出现了演化的时滞，我们再来说我们现代人的思维方式。其实，现代人的思维方式也出现了时代错位。有个典型的认知定式叫作“**易得性认知定式**”（Availability Heuristic: 人们根据信息在记忆中被提取的容易程度来判断事件发生概率的认知偏差）。这就是指人在判断一件事情到底会不会发生的时候，是根据相关信息在我们的记忆中是否容易被调取来决定的。简单来说就是，如果一件事给我留下的印象特别深，我就会判断它更容易发生。这其实是很有用的思维快捷方式，正所谓“吃一堑长一智”或者“一朝被蛇咬十年怕井绳”，这是有道理的。假如一个人被蛇咬过，那么他始终会记得被蛇咬了之后疼得死去活来，差点把命给丢掉了的体验。那下次看到跟蛇相似的东西，那可得躲着点。这虽然会造成一些统计上的假阳性，也就是假警报，但这种假阳性造成的损失比起真的被蛇咬，依然是可接受的误报。所以这种认知定式也就成了更值得采取的保守策略。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Alright, having discussed how instincts like the F4, such as eating, exhibit evolutionary lag, let's now talk about the thinking patterns of modern humans. <p class="english-text">But today, we have entered an era where media information is highly developed, even overflowing. ### 成为“调参侠”：抵抗偏误，重塑思维

那么我们应当如何抵抗演化时滞，减少认知偏误呢？要知道，认知定式是千百万年的自然选择为我们训练出来的神经网络。而今天，我们自己要做自己的“**调参侠**”（Parameter Tuner: 互联网上对调节人工智能参数的工作人员的戏称）。是的，人工智能的训练是需要人类进行监督和调节的，毕竟机器本身缺乏对任务本质、社会规范和动态环境的主观理解能力。而我们的大脑又何尝不是这样呢？
