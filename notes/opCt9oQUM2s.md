### HSDD：被误解的女性健康危机

**Nam Kiwanuka**: 您正在收听的是《Mistreated》。我是 Nam Kiwanuka。你知道吗，对于经历低性欲或低性冲动的女性，其实有一个医学术语。它被称为**性欲低下障碍**，简称 **HSDD**。根据美国国家卫生研究院（NIH）的数据，HSDD 是女性中最常见的性问题。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: You're listening to Mistreated. I'm Nam Kiwanuka. Did you know that there is a medical term for women who are experiencing low sexual desire or libido? It's called hypoactive sexual desire disorder or HSDD. According to the National Institutes of Health, HSDD is the most commonly identified sexual problem among women.

</details>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: HSDD 的特征是缺乏性幻想、性感受或对性刺激的接受能力，这种情况持续至少六个月，引起个人痛苦，且并非由另一种医疗状况引起。NIH 强调，医学界一些人对女性 HSDD 的看法是“不准确的”，认为它类似于男性的勃起功能障碍（ED）。**性欲低下障碍并非女性版的勃起功能障碍**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: HSDD is characterized by a deficiency of sexual thoughts, feelings, or receptiveness to sexual stimulation that has been present for at least six months, causes personal distress, and is not due to another medical condition. The NIH stressed that HSDD in women has been viewed quote inaccurately by some in the medical community as being similar to erectile dysfunction ED in men. Hypoactive sexual desire disorder is not female erectile dysfunction.

</details>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: 一项研究显示，低性欲的患病率从绝经前女性的 26.7% 到自然绝经女性的 52.4% 不等。用于治疗 HSDD 的药物之一叫做 **Addyi**。2025 年 12 月，历史被改写了，美国食品药品监督管理局（**FDA**）批准该药物用于绝经前和绝经后的女性。它成为了首个治疗 65 岁以下女性低性欲的药物。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: Ajamama study showed that the prevalence of low sexual desire ranged from 26.7% among premenopausal women to 52.4% among naturally menopausal women. One of the medications used to treat HSDD is called Addyi. And in December 2025, history was made when the US Food and Drug Administration, the FDA, approved it for both pre and post-menopausal women. It became the first medication to treat low sexual desire for women under the age of 65.

</details>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: 但你知道吗？包括**伟哥**（Viagra）在内，FDA 已经批准了 **26 种**针对男性性健康的药物，但针对女性性健康的药物只有 **2 种**。Addyi 最初在 2015 年获批。开发该药物的制药公司负责人 **Cindy Eckert** 曾说：“HSDD 在 1977 年就被界定了。医学界了解它已经很久了，但社会偏见开始质疑女性的快感是否重要。Addyi 的临床试验规模是伟哥的三倍，FDA 只花了 6 个月就批准了伟哥，但我们花了 **6 年时间**才让 Addyi 获批。”

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: But did you know that including Viagra, there are already 26 drugs that have been approved by the FDA for male sexual health, but only two FDA approved drugs that target women's sexual health. Addyi was first approved in 2015. Cindy Eckhard, whose pharmaceutical company developed Addyi, said this. HSDD was characterized in 1977. Medicine has understood it for some time now. But what crept in was social commentary, a societal bias that questions whether pleasure matters for women. In the end, Addyi was approved on the basis of science with clinical trials involving 13,000 women, which was three times the size of the trials conducted on Viagra. Granted, erectile dysfunction is different from HSDD in women, but the FDA took 6 months to approve Viagra, and by contrast, it took us 6 years to earn approval for Addyi.

</details>

### 医学盲区与生物学本质

**Nam Kiwanuka**: 制作过程中最让你惊讶的是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: Was there anything that surprised you along the way? Like, what's the biggest thing that surprised you?

</details>

**Ashling Chini**: 医生在女性身体培训方面的匮乏。这既令人震惊又令人担忧，医疗机构几代人以来从未优先考虑过女性身体的研究。另一个惊讶点是 HSDD 本身。它不是因为对伴侣不感兴趣或压力大，它感觉像是发生在身体内部的**生物学原因**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ashling Chini**: How little do physicians get trained on women's bodies. It's both stark and alarming. Medical establishments have never prioritized women's bodies in research for generations. Another thing was this condition HSDD. It disappears not necessarily because they aren't interested in their partner anymore, it's biology.

</details>

**Ashling Chini**: 研究人员对患有和不患有 HSDD 的女性进行了脑部扫描，在核磁共振（MRI）中，你可以清晰看到她们**大脑活动对性图像反应的差异**。虽然早在 1977 年就有记载，但我们对此依然知之甚少。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ashling Chini**: They did brain scans and in the MRIs you could see the difference on how their brain activity was reacting to sexual images. It's been in the literature since 1977 and yet we don't really know very much about it.

</details>

### 从抗抑郁药到 FDA 的层层重担

**Nam Kiwanuka**: Addyi 是如何诞生的？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: How did this drug come to be?

</details>

**Ashling Chini**: 它最初是德国公司开发的一种**抗抑郁药**。就像伟哥最初是血压药一样，参与试验的女性报告说这让她们变得“更饥渴”（friskier）。它在神经系统层面起作用。2010 年首次提交 FDA 被拒。后来 Cindy Eckert 介入，她本以为遵循男药的路线图会一帆风顺，结果却困难重重。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ashling Chini**: It started off as a anti-depressant developed by Boehringer Ingelheim in Germany. Kind of like how Viagra was a blood pressure drug, women were reporting that it was making them friskier. They took it to the FDA in 2010 and it was rejected. Then Cindy Eckert thought it was going to be easy sailing following the roadmap for men, but it wasn't.

</details>

**Ashling Chini**: 甚至当 Cindy 面对 FDA 评审小组时，发现成员清一色全是**泌尿科医生**。当她质疑为什么不用神经专家时，对方很不高兴。FDA 甚至质疑“一个月只增加一次性生活”是否有意义。但对于完全没有性生活的关系来说，**从零到一年 12 次**是巨大的改善。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ashling Chini**: When she went in front of the FDA, the panel were all urologists. They didn't like her talking back to them. They were like, "Oh, improving by one more event a month is not very much." But if you're not having any sex, once a month is actually a lot. It's potentially 12 times a year, a huge improvement.

</details>

### 双重标准：被禁绝的一杯红酒

**Nam Kiwanuka**: 为什么伟哥被视为革命性突破，而粉色药丸却被视为成问题的？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: Why do you think it was seen as problematic whereas the blue pill was seen as a revolutionary thing?

</details>

**Ashling Chini**: 因为文化层面：我们从未真正尊重**女性对身体的掌控权**和对性愉悦的权利。医学界常把性功能简化为“裤腰带到膝盖”之间的机械运动，而忽视了神经递质。此外还有离谱的双重标准：男人吃伟哥可以喝五杯酒，而女性吃 Addyi 却被要求“滴酒不沾”，甚至要签署类似“家长同意书”的表格。这完全是打着保护女性的旗号在剥夺她们的信任。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ashling Chini**: The cultural layer is that we've never been comfortable with women having agency over their own bodies. We don't respect women's right to enjoy pleasurable sex. With Viagra, labels say guys can take five glasses of wine, but women cannot take any. They had to sign a form like a permission slip. It's this guise of protecting women because they can't be trusted.

</details>

### 人性视角：被摧毁的家庭与最后的选择权

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nam Kiwanuka**: In the documentary, you also hear from women who HSDD was destroying their relationships. You really humanized it.

</details>

**Ashling Chini**: 是的，很多女性为了维持连接而进行“**义务性行为**”（duty sex），这让双方都不好受。我们采访了一位叫 Barbara 的女性，她的丈夫因此与她离婚。听证会上人们对她女儿非常刻薄，竟然说“那就再找个新丈夫”。这完全无视了人的**人性**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ashling Chini**: A lot of women would just grin and bear it and have duty sex, and that doesn't make anyone feel great. We interviewed Barbara, whose husband divorced her because of this. People were so mean, saying "just get a new husband." That's not seeing someone's humanity.

**Ashling Chini**: 归根结底，性愉悦应该是**基本人权**。这应该是你的**选择**，你应该拥有像男性一样的身体自主权。我们不应该剥夺女性决定是否使用安全药物来改善生活的权利。
