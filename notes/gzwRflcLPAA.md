### 对华策略与能源筹码

**Friedberg**: 我认为总统和政府在 4 月份将迎来本届任期内最重要的会议，那就是与**中国**的会面。在特朗普上任前后的言论中，达成一份“**大交易**”（Grand Bargain）显然是他的首要任务。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Friedberg**: I think the president and the administration have probably the biggest meetings of the term coming up in China in April. My estimation based on the conversations is that finding a grand bargain or a deal with China is probably one of his top priorities.

</details>

**Friedberg**: 在这个背景下，对马杜罗和伊朗采取的行动，实际上是在谈判前创造**最大化的筹码**。原因很简单：伊朗 90% 的石油出口都流向了中国。通过对这些**供应链**施加重大影响或破坏，美国在谈判中获得了更好的立足点。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Friedberg**: That being the case, I think you could look at the actions in Iran as creating maximal leverage going into those negotiations. The reason for that: 90% of the oil that comes out of Iran goes to China. Creating leverage by having significant influence or damage or destruction to those supply chains for China gives the United States footing to be able to negotiate a better deal for America.

</details>

**Chamath**: 我想补充一点。这不仅关乎地区冲突，更关乎全球局势。昨晚发生了一件重要的事情，中国官方公布了 **GDP** 增长目标，仅为 4.5% 到 5%，这是三十年来的最低水平。当一个国家增速减半，人口却翻倍，社会就会变得更加动荡。为了转移国内矛盾，入侵**台湾**就成了一个可能的选项。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Chamath**: I'll build on what Friedberg said. I think all of this centers around that geopolitical discussion. Last night, something important happened: the official Chinese bureaucracy posted what their GDP targets were, a range of 4.5 to 5%, the lowest it has ever been in about 30 years. If you have that as a starting point, what is it in China's best interest to do? It becomes obvious that the right thing to do would be to invade Taiwan.

</details>

**Chamath**: 特朗普总统看到了这一点。如果你想避免与中国发生灾难性的全球战争，该怎么做？中国经济极度依赖进口石油，其中约五分之一来自伊朗和委内瑞拉。现在这些油源被切断了。再加上我们在俄罗斯方面尝试达成的交易，如果俄罗斯的石油也被纳入考量，那将覆盖中国 40% 的石油需求。这不仅实现了**去美元化**的反击，还切断了这些非法资金在全球制造混乱的渠道，在 4 月份的大谈判前彻底围困了中国。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Chamath**: The president saw that. If your goal is to prevent war with China, how would you do it? The most interesting takeaway is that they are enormously dependent on imported oil. About a fifth of it comes exclusively from Iran and Venezuela. And now all of that is off the table. If you add Russia into that mix, it's about 40% of China's oil. You hem in China going into a massive moment at the end of March where there is the potential for a grand bargain.

</details>

### 战争范式的转变：科技与特种作战

**Jason**: Emil，请向我们解释一下，这次行动为什么这么顺利？委内瑞拉的行动也是如此。显然有一些我们不知道的新技术在起作用。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: Explain to us what you know that makes these two operations go so smoothly. There's obviously some new technology here.

</details>

**Emil**: 撇开所谓的“**大乱斗发射器**”（Discombobulator）不谈，我们拥有一支训练极其有素的军队。反恐战争在很多方面是个灾难，但当年的战士现在已经是将军了，他们吸取了教训。相比之下，中国军队缺乏实战经验，他们最近唯一有实战背景的将领还被清洗了。经验在预判风险时至关重要。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Emil**: Besides the discombobulator, what we've got is a very well-trained military. The people now who are fighting that are generals now. And so they've learned a lot of lessons. And you compare that to the Chinese military. They don't have a lot of experience. Conflict experience matters because you understand going in what are the things that could go wrong.

</details>

**Emil**: 其次，我们拥有惊人的技术，涵盖空间、空中、陆地、海洋和网络。想象一下，100 个人进入委内瑞拉防守最严密的基地，安全地带走总统及其夫人，而且**零伤亡**。这些“兵棋推演”已经在架子上放了很多年，每一个方案都经过了反复更新。我们在伊朗的“**午夜之锤**”（Midnight Hammer）行动也是提前多年规划好的。我们正在以极其精密的方式最小化生命损失并最大化成功率。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Emil**: And then you have incredible technology. You imagine a hundred guys goes into the most fortified compound in Venezuela, take him and his wife out safely and are out with no KAS. These war games have been on the shelf for a long time. Midnight hammer in Iran was planned years ahead of time. We have a very sophisticated way of doing these things to minimize loss of life and maximize success.

</details>

**Friedberg**: Emil，这种任务在 5 年或 10 年前能达成吗？是因为技术进步太快了吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Friedberg**: Emil, do you think we would have been able to pull off that mission as successfully 5 or 10 years ago? Has the technology improved that quickly?

</details>

**Emil**: 不仅仅是技术成熟，更重要的是**交战规则**（Rules of Engagement）。过去在阿富汗，有些规则简直疯了：如果对方拿小枪，你就得拿小枪。为了所谓的“对等”，我们的士兵时刻处于危险之中。现在我们放松了这些惩罚性的限制，推行责任制。这更像是**鲍威尔主义**（Colin Powell approach）：全力以赴，目标明确，使用压倒性的力量。在过去四年里，我们一直没这么做。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Emil**: I'd say no. It's the rules of engagement. In Afghanistan, if the guy had a small gun you had to have a small gun. The rules of engagement were so punishing that we were at risk all the time. We've gotten back to pushing responsibility to the field. It's more of a Colin Powell approach: go all in, have a clear objective, come out, use overwhelming force.

</details>

### 无人机机群与 AI 战场的未来

**Jason**: 我听说过去一周美国部署的无人机比历史上任何时候都多。这是真的吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: My understanding is that there have been more drones deployed by the United States this past week than we've done in the history of military activity. Is that right?

</details>

**Emil**: 变化非常大。从奥巴马时期用“**捕食者**”袭击阳台上的恐怖分子，到现在的**俄乌战争**，70% 的伤亡是由无人机造成的。这就是为什么像 **Anduril** 这样的公司如此重要。我目前在运行一个名为 **LUCAS** 的项目（廉价无人攻击机系统）。这些单程攻击无人机，航程可达 500 到 700 英里，时速堪比飞机，携带巨大弹头，而成本仅在 5 万到 8 万美元之间。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Emil**: It changes it big. Russia Ukraine war happened where 70% of the casualties are because of drones. That's why companies like Anduril are making unmanned systems. I'm running the Lucas program, low-cost unmanned combat attack systems. These one-way attack drones can go 500-700 miles at the speed of an airplane, carry a big warhead. Those are like $50,000 to $80,000.

</details>

**Jason**: 这其中有多少是 AI 控制的？我们该多担心 AI 犯错？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: How much of this should basically be AI? How confident are you that this isn't going to make a mistake?

</details>

**Emil**: 未来的复杂战争将是 AI 控制的**无人机机群**。AI 可以学习**自动目标识别**，区分目标。虽然还没达到“全自动驾驶”（FSD）的成熟时刻，但在特定场景下，比如拦截**高超音速导弹**，AI 是唯一的选择。人类没有那么快的反应时间。中国已经在开发“异构自主”（Heterogeneous Autonomy），让不同种类的无人机相互通信，确保不攻击同一个目标。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Emil**: A sophisticated drone war is going to be drone swarms controlled by AI. AI could do automatic target recognition. For Chinese hypersonic missiles, you've got 90 seconds. A human doesn't have the reaction time. China has drone swarms called heterogeneous autonomy, where they take different kinds of drones and how they communicate with one another.

</details>

### 深度解析：Anthropic 合同终止内幕

**Jason**: 这是一个爆炸性新闻：五角大楼正式通知 **Anthropic**，其被视为**供应链风险**。此前战争部在上周五取消了与 Anthropic 价值 2 亿美金的合同。Emil，到底发生了什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: Some breaking news: The Pentagon has formally notified Anthropic that it's been deemed a supply chain risk. The department of war cancelled Anthropic $200 million contract on Friday. Emil, explain to us what happened here.

</details>

**Emil**: 这得从拜登的 AI 行政令说起，那份命令限制了算力，实际上内定了少数几家赢家，Anthropic 就是其中之一。他们通过 **Palantir** 深度嵌入到了美国政府最敏感的部分。但我 8 月份上任后查看合同时，发现内容令人震惊。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Emil**: If you remember the Biden executive order on AI, Anthropic was one of those winners. They got very sticky to the workflows. I came in in August and I looked at the contracts. I was like, holy cow.

</details>

**Emil**: 合同规定你不能用他们的模型策划**动能打击**（kinetic strike），不能用来移动卫星，甚至不能策划可能导致伤害人类的作战方案。我当时就问：你觉得战争部是干什么的？于是我们开始了长达三个月的谈判。他们想搞“例外制”，但我告诉他们：我无法预测未来 20 年我们要怎么用 AI，“**所有合法用途**”必须是基本标准。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Emil**: They say you can't use them to plan a kinetic strike. You can't use their AI model to move a satellite. I said: What do you think these folks do? We use the Department of War. So we started these negotiations. Exceptions doesn't work. All lawful use seems like a good thing.

</details>

**Emil**: 冲突的爆发点是在马杜罗突袭行动之后。Anthropic 的一名高管询问 Palantir：他们的软件是否被用于那次行动？这可是**机密信息**！这意味着他们不仅想监控我们的使用情况，还可能随时根据自己的“道德偏好”切断服务。这让我们意识到，美国领导层对单一软件供应商的依赖是多么危险。如果某个“流氓开发人员”给模型下毒，或者在关键时刻拒绝执行指令怎么办？这最终导致了周二与 **Dario**（Anthropic CEO）极其戏剧化的会面。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Emil**: The trigger point was after the Maduro raid. One of their execs called Palantir and asked: was our software used in that raid? Which is classified information! And implying that if it was used, that might violate their terms of service. It raised enough alarm that we're potentially so dependent on a software provider that has the ability to not only shut it off, maybe it's a rogue developer who could poison the model. So then that culminated in the Tuesday dramatic meeting with Dario.

</details>

**Chamath**: 这太令人难以置信了。这意味着对于政府、公司或任何人来说，你不能只使用一种模型。因为随时可能有一个公司的内部人员陷入某种“道德狂热”，然后以此危害你的业务。这就是“**去平台化**”（deplatforming）的千倍增强版。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Chamath**: The reality is that what Anthropic showed is that they are going to have a political perspective. If you're not figuring out how to be multi-model and agnostic, you're taking on enormous business risk. This is deplatforming all over again, but this is that times a thousand.

</details>

**Jason**: 其他公司呢？比如 **OpenAI** 或 **Grok**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jason**: Take us through OpenAI, Gemini and Grok. Have any of those given you any push back?

</details>

**Emil**: **Grok** 全力支持所有合法用途，因为马斯克追求的是真理，而不希望意识形态干扰作战决策。我们也正在和 **Google** 合作。至于 **OpenAI**，**Sam Altman** 表现得很像个爱国者。他找我提供解决方案，甚至还在为 Anthropic 说话，说不要把他们列为供应链风险，那对行业不利。但我需要冗余，我需要至少两到三个模型来互相竞争价格和性能。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Emil**: Grok's all in for all lawful use cases because we don't want ideology. We have Google for all lawful use cases on unclassified networks. Sam Altman was being a real patriot. He was trying to protect Anthropic to his credit. He was like, don't call him a supply chain risk. But I need redundancy.

</details>
