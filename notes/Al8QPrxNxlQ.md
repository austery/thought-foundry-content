### 公开登记的有效性与再犯率

**主持人**: 好的。你研究了关于公开性罪犯登记制度及其有效性的数据。你发现了什么？
<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: All right. So you looked at data on public sex offender registries and their effectiveness. What did you find?

</details>

**Abigail Nubla**: 是的。基本上，我想从头开始讲，在深入探讨之前，我想再次重申，性犯罪是一种非常严重的罪行。我绝不是想淡化或否定这一点。目标，我认为，是降低**再犯率**和提高**公众安全**。所以，为了理解全局，了解性犯罪的再犯率已经很低是有帮助的。有一项来自2000年代初的研究，一项元分析，它基本上回顾了许多不同的研究，发现大约75%的性罪犯在获释20年后没有再次犯罪。所以大约25%的人再次犯罪了。然后有更近的2022年的数据表明，这个数字已经下降到7%。所以我们面对的不是一群不可避免会再次犯罪的人。但我也不能忽视那7%，因为这是一种非常严重的罪行，意味着它确实会发生。那么问题应该是，公开登记制度能否将这7%进一步降低？目前没有数据显示是这样的。在美国，有几个司法管辖区实施了公共登记制度，并且有研究考察了它们是否降低了再犯率。结果是没有统计学上的显著差异。所以没有数据表明它能降低性罪犯的再犯率。
<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Abigail Nubla**: Yeah. So so basically, I mean, I think to start in the beginning and, and before I get into it, I want to reiterate again that sexual offences are a very serious crime. So I think to understand kind of the whole picture, it's helpful to know that sexual offences already have a pretty low recidivism rate. But I also don't want to minimize that 7% because it's a very serious crime and it means that it does happen. So then I think the question should be, would a public registry reduce that 7% even further? And there's no data to show that that's the case. So in the United States, there's a few jurisdictions which have implemented public registries, and there have been studies to look at whether they did reduce recidivism rates. And there was no statistically significant difference. So there's no data kind of showing that it does, or demonstrating that it does reduce recidivism rates for sexual offenders.

</details>

### 有效的再犯率降低措施

**主持人**: 那么，我们来看看另一方面。有哪些措施能有效降低再犯率？
<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: Then with that, let's look on the other side. What measures are effective at lowering re offending rates.

</details>

**Abigail Nubla**: 是的。有几件事。从2000年代初到现在，我们知道7%的数字已经显著下降。但这并非由单一因素造成。许多专家认为，这是多种因素共同作用的结果。总的来说，我们对犯罪学的了解在过去二十年里有了很大进步。我们理解得更好了。我们也更了解**治疗**和**专业化的治疗项目**。所以我们开发了这些可以帮助人们的、个性化的方法。安大略省的私人登记制度也可以以专业化、个性化的方式使用。安大略省目前不这样做，但马尼托巴省有一个系统，有一个委员会。如果一名性罪犯即将获释，警方认为他们有潜在的高再犯风险，他们可以将其提交给该委员会。委员会将审查一系列因素，例如罪犯受害者的年龄？他们的心理健康史？他们是否参加过任何治疗项目？他们会审视所有这些，然后决定他们是否认为此人是高风险再犯者。如果是，那么他们可以采取一系列不同的个性化措施，例如，对该人进行持续监视，以及通知要求。所以，如果他们认为确实有必要通知社区，他们可以这样做，最极端的情况是通知整个马尼托巴省。但这将是他们会采取的最极端的通知措施。在此之前，还有很多不同级别的较低措施。
<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Abigail Nubla**: Yeah. So there's a couple of things. And I mean we know from the early 2000 to now, there has been a pretty significant decrease to the 7%. But there's not one thing that's caused that. A lot of the experts say it's really just a bunch of different factors working together. So in general, our criminology knowledge is grown a lot in the past two decades. So we just understand things better. We also understand therapies and specialized therapies and treatment programs better. So we've developed those things which can help people in an individualized kind of approach. And also, there are ways to use Ontario's private registry in in specialized ways as well, and individualized ways. So Ontario doesn't currently do this, but Manitoba has a system where there's a committee and and if a sex offender is being released and the police believe that they are potentially high risk to re offend, they can refer them to that committee. The committee will then look at a bunch of factors like what were the age of that offender's victims? What is their psychological history? Did they partake in any sort of treatment programs? And so they'll look at all that and they'll decide whether they believe the person is a high risk to re offend. And if they do, then there's a bunch of different individualized options they can take, like, for example, ongoing surveillance of the person and also notification requirements as well. So if they think that it's truly necessary to notify a community, they can do that all the way up to notifying all of Manitoba. But that is like the most drastic measure of notification that they would take. And there's a bunch of different lower levels before that.

</details>

**Abigail Nubla**: Yeah. So so this ruling is is quite recent. Basically, the judge looked at two parts of Christopher's law and found that. So the first part is that if you commit a sex offence, you are automatically on the registry. And the second part is that if you get convicted of multiple sex offences, you are on that registry for life. So he found both of those parts to be over broad and too general, basically painting everyone with a broad brush, not taking into consideration the individual circumstances of particular people, and treating low and high risk offenders the same way.

</details>
