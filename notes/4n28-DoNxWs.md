### 创业最艰难的时刻：裁员与韧性

作为一名创业者，最困难的事情莫过于解雇员工。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I think the most difficult thing for me as entrepreneur is letting people go.</p>
</details>

在公司成立的第一年，我们获得了资金并迅速扩张到70名员工，但随后不得不分三轮裁员，将人数削减至20人。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The first year funding we scaled to 70 people. Then we had to cut down to 20 and that was over three rounds of layoffs.</p>
</details>

这些时刻总是我作为创业者最糟糕的日子，甚至只是不得不解雇一个人，第一次解雇员工，都让我记忆犹新。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And I think those are always my worst days as an entrepreneur. Probably the thing I remember most is even just having to let one person go. First time I had to fire someone.</p>
</details>

这可能让我作为一名创业者变得更加谨慎，但看着员工的眼睛，告诉他们即将失业，仍然感觉非常艰难和糟糕。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And it's probably made me more prudent as an entrepreneur. But it still felt so hard, you know, to like let someone go, look them in the eye, let them know they're not going to have a job anymore. I just it felt horrible.</p>
</details>

那是一段非常痛苦的转变期，但事后看来，那也是我们学到最多的时候，因为我们当时决定只专注于客户，赢得下一笔交易，让下一个客户满意。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And so that was a very painful shift. But then in hindsight, that's also when we learned the most cuz then we just said I just said, "Hey, I just got to focus on the customer, just win the next deal, make the next customer happy.</p>
</details>

我们相信，如果足够快地做到这一点并产生足够的收入，公司就能实现盈利。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And if we do that fast enough and we generate enough revenue, we'll be profitable."</p>
</details>

我们确实做到了，在一年内实现了盈利，但直到2007年，也就是公司成立七年后，我们才真正找到了**产品市场契合点**（Product-Market Fit: 指产品在市场中找到了足够多的目标客户，并能有效满足他们的需求）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And it's still hard and challenging, but I think now I feel like I have more perspective. Like one, I'm really choosing to do it. I love building companies. I've realized that. And I love the challenge.</p>
</details>

即使我们经历低谷（而且我们仍然会经历），比如失去客户、失去交易、失去员工，感觉依然很糟糕。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And even when we have the lows, and we still have them, you know, you lose a customer, you lose a deal, you lose an employee, it still feels really terrible.</p>
</details>

但我知道，每一个低谷之后都会有高潮，比如赢得新交易、招募到优秀人才、实现**流动性事件**（Liquidity Event: 指公司股权转化为现金的过程，如被收购或上市）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And and I've kind of realized that the highs don't come without the lows.</p>
</details>

<details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I think when you can persevere through those moments, those are the entrepreneurs that that have success.</p>
</details>

### 关于“转向”（pivot）的思考

我认为大多数SaaS创业者，通常至少需要2到3年才能真正找到产品市场契合点。所以你必须有这种心态：“嘿，这会花很长时间”，而你的调整（pivots）可能只是微调——在产品内、在愿景内进行优化，而不是完全放弃你的愿景。

说实话，我创办的大多数公司都坚持了创始愿景，并且大部分优化工作都是为了实现这个愿景而做的数千个细节。比如在G2，我们做了成千上万次优化来提升评论转化率，但我们从未回避“评论将对软件买家和卖家建立更好连接至关重要”这一核心理念。

所以对我来说——如果你真的相信你的愿景，我永远不会转向（pivot）。
