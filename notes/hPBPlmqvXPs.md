### 领导职位中的普遍焦虑

工作中，人们常感觉自己从一个充满焦虑、或许是绩效焦虑的时刻走向下一个时刻。我一直在强调“焦虑”这个词，对吗？因为我们有必须完成的任务，所以工作中并没有一条贯穿始终的快乐主线。我们如何才能摆脱这种特定的认知陷阱呢？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There's a feeling that the task you do at work, you're going from one anxiety-filled, maybe performance anxiety-filled moment to the next. Mostly I keep saying the word anxiety, right? There's not a through line of joy exactly because we have these tasks we have to deliver. How do we get out of that particular perception trap?</p>
</details>

事实上，大多数人在工作中并没有感受到太多焦虑。他们确实没有。这取决于工作表现以及你所从事的职业类型。因此，担任领导职务的人会承受很大的焦虑。你承担的领导责任越多，通常就会有越多的焦虑。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You know, the truth of the matter is that most people don't feel a lot of anxiety in their jobs. They don't. What people, it depends on the performance, the kind of job that you find yourself. And so people in leadership jobs, have a lot of anxiety. The more leadership responsibility that you have, the more the more anxiety in general you're going to have.</p>
</details>

而这实际上是一种选择。这对许多首席执行官（**CEO**：Chief Executive Officer，公司最高行政管理人员）来说是一个巨大的惊喜。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And that's something that's actually chosen. And that's a big surprise to a lot of CEOs.</p>
</details>

### CEO的隐性挑战：孤独与愤怒

在一位普通CEO任期的前24个月里，排名第一和第二的情绪是孤独和愤怒。是孤独和愤怒，而不是喜悦和满足，也不是喜悦和美好的惊喜，绝非如此。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The number one and two emotions in the first 24 months of an average CEO's tenure, the number one and two emotions are loneliness and anger. loneliness and anger, not joy and contentment, not joy and lovely surprise, no.</p>
</details>

事情并非如此。作为CEO，你唯一会遇到的惊喜是，比如总法律顾问打来电话（这通常不是好事），或者其他任何可能发生的事情。许多CEO对此感到非常惊讶，因为你的古老的**边缘系统**（Limbic System: 大脑中负责情绪、记忆和动机的区域）会告诉你：“往上爬吧，伙计，**最高荣誉**（Brass Ring: 成功的象征，指代高层职位）就在那里，那会非常棒！”但当他们真正到达那个位置时，却并不喜欢。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That's not how it works. The only surprises you get as CEO is like, the general counsel called, that's bad, or whatever it happens to be, and a lot of them are really caught by surprise, because once again, your ancient limbic system says, climb man, the brass ring, that's where it's at, it's gonna be so great, and they get there and they don't like it,</p>
</details>

顺便说一句，预测CEO离职或失败的首要因素，就是他们不喜欢CEO这份工作。在这个国家乃至全世界，有许多CEO和管理者渴望成为CEO，但却不想真正承担CEO的职责。因为负面情绪的强度和程度都非常高。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The number one predictor of CEO demise, by the way, is not liking the job of CEO. And there are CEOs all over this country, there are managers all over this country and all over the world who want to be CEO but don't want to do CEO. Because the negative emotionality is so intense and so high.</p>
</details>

### 理解焦虑本质与应对策略

我建议领导者们多做的一件事，就是真正地投入到更具创造性、更具沉思性以及更多支持性的角色中，这样他们才能真正感到快乐。你必须非常非常适合承受巨大的焦虑。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And one of the things that I recommend leaders do a lot is actually step into more creative roles, step into more contemplative roles, step into more supporting roles, and they can actually be happy. You have to be really, really suited to bear up under a lot of anxiety.</p>
</details>

焦虑不过是“无焦点恐惧”而已。这就是焦虑的定义：无焦点恐惧，它通常是**杏仁核**（Amygdala: 大脑边缘系统中处理情绪，特别是恐惧和焦虑的关键区域）在**边缘系统**（Limbic System: 大脑中负责情绪、记忆和动机的区域）中的一种适应不良。你需要了解它是如何运作的，并具备极强的自我管理能力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Anxiety is nothing more than unfocused fear. That's the definition of anxiety. Unfocused fear, which is. usually a maladaptation of the amygdala in the limbic system of the brain. You need to know how it works and be incredibly self-managing.</p>
</details>
