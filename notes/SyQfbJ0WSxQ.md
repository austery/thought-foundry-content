### 欧洲的能源三难困境

在这种新环境下，许多国家在三个能源目标之间面临着日益艰难的权衡，我称之为我的**能源三难困境**（Energy Trilmna: 指能源政策在安全性、可持续性和可负担性之间面临的权衡取舍）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In this new environment, many countries face an increasingly difficult tradeoff between three energy goals that I will call my energy trilmna.</p>
</details>

我的团队认为我不应该使用“三难困境”这个词，因为在三难困境中你无法真正逃脱，但我将尝试向你们展示我们可以摆脱这个困境。那么，这个三难困境的三个方面是什么呢？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">My team argues that I should not really use trilmna because in a trilmna you can't really escape, and I'm going to try to show you that we can escape the trilmna. So what are the three angles of the trilmna?</p>
</details>

我们需要安全的能源，因为地缘政治现实已发生巨大变化；我们需要可持续的能源，因为气候现实并未改变，或者有人会说变得更糟了；我们还需要可负担的能源，因为家庭和企业都需要它。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We need energy that is secure because the geopolitical reality has changed dramatically. We need energy that is sustainable because the climate reality has not changed, or some would argue that it has changed for the worse. And we need energy that is affordable because households and corporates need it.</p>
</details>

<details>

### 忽视能源安全的代价

在俄罗斯入侵乌克兰之前，欧洲实际上优先考虑了能源三难困境中的两个要素：可持续性和可负担性。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So, before Russia's invasion of Ukraine, Europe de facto prioritized two elements of the trilmna: sustainability and affordability.</p>
</details>

我们并没有像现在这样非常关注安全性。因此，作为其**Fit for 55**（Fit for 55: 欧盟旨在到2030年将净温室气体排放量减少至少55%的一揽子政策）目标的一部分，欧盟旨在到2030年将其能源消费中可再生能源的份额提高到45%。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We didn't really think very much about security, not in the terms that we're thinking about it now. So, as part of its Fit for 55 targets, the EU aimed to raise the share of renewables in its energy consumption to as much as 45% in 2030.</p>
</details>

与此同时，近一半的天然气进口来自俄罗斯。这种组合使能源成本得到控制，从而促进了欧洲的竞争力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At the same time, almost half of the gas imports came from Russia. The combination kept energy costs contained, facilitating, as a result, Europe's competitiveness.</p>
</details>

对于能源密集型产业来说，能源仍然相对便宜。那些可再生能源产出较低的**间歇性**（Intermittency: 指可再生能源（如太阳能和风能）发电量因天气条件变化而波动不定的特性）时期，天然气作为备用，并未导致显著的价格波动。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Energy remained relatively cheap for energy-intensive industries, and those periods of intermittency, you know, when renewable output was low and gas served as a backup, did not lead to significant price volatility.</p>
</details>

但我们最终为忽视第三个目标——能源安全——付出了代价。当地缘政治环境改变时，对我们经济的影响是严重的，这些后果是我们作为中央银行家必须面对和应对的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But we ultimately paid the price for neglecting the third goal, which is energy security. When the geopolitical context changed, the consequences for our economy were severe—consequences that we as central bankers had to face and combat.</p>
</details>

首先，我们看到了通货膨胀飙升。欧元区能源通胀在2022年飙升至37%，将当年总体通胀推高至创纪录的8.4%。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">First, we saw a surge of inflation. Energy inflation in the Euro area surged to 37% in 2022, pushing headline inflation to a record of 8.4% that year.</p>
</details>

因此，所有中央银行都不得不通过实施**欧洲央行**（ECB: European Central Bank: 负责欧元区货币政策的中央银行）历史上最快的**货币紧缩**（Monetary Tightening: 中央银行通过提高利率或减少货币供应来抑制通货膨胀的政策）来做出显著反应，以控制通货膨胀。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So, in response, what all central banks had to do was to significantly respond by implementing the fastest monetary tightening in the history of the ECB in order to bring inflation back under control.</p>
</details>

我很高兴地说，我们已经成功做到了这一点，通胀目前已在我们的中期目标范围内得到控制，并将保持在该水平。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And I'm pleased to say that we have managed to do that, and it is now under control at our medium-term target, and it will stay there.</p>
</details>

现在，能源价格飙升导致了第二个后果：它们削弱了欧洲的竞争力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now, surging energy prices led to a second consequence: they have reduced Europe's competitiveness.</p>
</details>

尽管能源冲击的急性阶段已经过去，但新的地缘政治现实对欧洲的能源成本留下了持久的印记，削弱了其相对于世界其他地区的地位。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Although the acute phase of the energy shock has passed, the new geopolitical reality has left a lasting imprint on Europe's energy costs, weakening its position relative to other regions of the world.</p>
</details>

欧盟的电价仍然是美国的约2.5倍，天然气价格几乎是美国的4倍。这种差距正在影响企业如何投资、如何创新，甚至它们选择在哪里设立运营。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Electricity prices in the EU remain about two and a half times higher than in the United States, and gas prices almost four times higher than in the United States. This gap is shaping how firms invest, how firms innovate, and even where they choose to locate their operations.</p>
</details>

我们在欧洲央行的研究表明，欧洲企业在应对能源冲击时，会显著减少其资本和研发支出，这与美国同行不同。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Our research at the ECB shows that European firms significantly reduce their capital and research and development expenditure in response to energy shocks, unlike their US counterparts.</p>
</details>

人们越来越担心，更高的能源价格也可能导致**去工业化**（Disindustrialization: 工业生产和就业在经济中占比下降的现象），因为欧洲企业在与外国竞争对手的较量中举步维艰。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There are growing concerns that higher energy prices may also be contributing to the disindustrialization as European firms struggle against foreign competition.</p>
</details>

更高的能源价格也使得欧洲更难充分拥抱将推动未来生产力的新技术，我指的是人工智能。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And higher energy prices make it harder for Europe to fully embrace the new technologies that will drive future productivity, and by that, I mean artificial intelligence.</p>
</details>

因此，如果我们想保障欧洲的工业竞争力、高水平的研发并利用人工智能，降低能源成本至关重要。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So it is essential to bring energy costs down if we are to safeguard Europe's industrial competitiveness, its high level of research and development, and leverage artificial intelligence.</p>
</details>

我坚信我们能做到，但我们不能以牺牲其他目标为代价。我们必须确保能源来源安全、能源可持续且可负担，这就是我告诉你们的那个三难困境。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And I'm confident that we can, but we cannot do so by trading off other goals. We must ensure that our energy sources are secure, that our energy is sustainable, and that it is affordable—the trilmna that I told you about.</p>
</details>

### 清洁能源：唯一的出路

欧洲实现这些目标的唯一可行途径是更多地依赖本地清洁能源。这条道路可以帮助实现三难困境的三个目标。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The only viable path for Europe to achieve these objectives is to rely more on locally clean energy. This path could help meet the three goals of the trilmna.</p>
</details>

首先，可再生能源以及其他清洁电力生产技术，对于任何可信的减少气候变化影响的计划都至关重要。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">First, renewables, alongside other clean technologies for producing electricity, are central to any credible plan to reduce the impact of climate change.</p>
</details>

其次，考虑到我们缺乏自然资源，可再生能源提供了欧洲通过进口化石燃料永远无法达到的能源独立水平。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Second, renewables offer a level of energy independence that, given our lack of natural resources, Europe would never reach with imported fossil fuels.</p>
</details>

第三，由于可再生电力几乎为零的边际成本，一个主要基于可再生能源的系统最终应能降低能源价格。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And third, as the marginal cost of renewable electricity is close to zero, a system based largely on renewables should ultimately bring down energy prices.</p>
</details>

但我们都知道，对于这一切存在一些怀疑，怀疑欧洲在短期到中期内是否真的可能实现这种价格下降。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But we all know that there is some skepticism about all that, and skepticism as to whether such price reductions are really possible in Europe over the short to medium term.</p>
</details>

关键问题在于，建设和运营一个基于可再生能源的系统涉及大量的额外开支，这些被称为系统成本，并最终转嫁到能源账单上。那么这些成本是什么呢？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And the key issue is that building and operating a renewables-based system involves significant additional expenses that are known as the system costs, which are passed onto the energy bills. So what are these costs?</p>
</details>

这些系统成本来自不同的来源。其中一些成本是不可避免的，但如果我们对转型真正抱有雄心，其他成本可以被最小化。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Those system costs, they come from different sources. Some of those costs are unavoidable, but others can be minimized if we are truly ambitious with the transition.</p>
</details>

不可避免的成本包括对新发电能力和现代化电网的大规模投资。欧盟委员会估计，这些每年将需要近1500亿欧元的投资。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The unavoidable costs include massive investment in new generation capacity and modernized grid. The European Commission estimates that these will require an annual investment of nearly 150 billion euros.</p>
</details>

如果对关键原材料的限制增加投入成本或延迟项目，这个数字甚至可能上升。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And that number may even rise if restrictions on critical raw materials increase input costs or delay projects.</p>
</details>

但这并非唯一的成本，还有其他可以最小化的成本。例如，与可再生能源固有的**间歇性**（Intermittency: 指可再生能源（如太阳能和风能）发电量因天气条件变化而波动不定的特性）相关的成本。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But that's not the only cost. And there are other costs that can be minimized. For instance, the cost related to the inherent intermittency of renewables.</p>
</details>

间歇性导致更高的价格波动，最终导致更高的平均账单，这又助长了通货膨胀，这是中央银行家关注的一个问题。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Intermittency leads to higher price volatility, which ultimately feeds into higher average bills, which contributes to inflation, which is a cause for concern for a central banker.</p>
</details>

我们去年11月就看到了这一点，当时欧洲大部分地区，特别是德国的风力发电量较低，导致燃气发电量激增以满足需求。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We saw this in November last year when low wind output across much of Europe, especially in Germany, prompted a surge in gas-fired generation to meet demand.</p>
</details>

几个国家的电价飙升，其影响波及整个欧洲，包括挪威的一些地区。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Electricity prices jumped in several countries, and the effects were felt across Europe, including in some parts of Norway.</p>
</details>

还有与**限电**（Curtailment: 指当可再生能源发电量过剩或电网容量不足时，故意减少其输出以保持系统平衡的做法）相关的成本，即为了保持系统平衡而故意减少可再生能源产出，这通常会导致向供应商支付费用。
