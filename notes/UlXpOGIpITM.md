### Kubernetes 的突破

**主持人**: 你认为到底是什么让 **Kubernetes** 取得了突破性的成功？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host**: What do you think really made Kubernetes breakthrough?

</details>

**Kelsey Hightower**: 头号成功标准就是 **Docker**。现在，你无需再争论 Java、Python 还是 Ruby 更好，你只需要讨论如何调度 Docker 容器。因为你可以直接复用相同的 Docker 容器，我们当时就已经赢在了起跑线上。我记得我收到了一封来自微软 CEO **Satya Nadella** 的邮件，我当时的反应是：“天哪，他写了这封非常友好的邮件”，然后我打开里面的 PDF 附件，发现薪酬方案里直接多加了一个零。你看着这个会想：“我甚至都不知道他们还能给出这种待遇。”我们知道这在行业里是有可能发生的，但是对于一个在 1999 年高中毕业、仅仅选择了考一个 A+ 认证的人来说，根本不知道还存在这样的机会。所以，我当时是真的非常认真地在考虑去微软。

我并不是那种纯粹的生成式 AI 反对者，我只是不喜欢对它进行那种天真的推广和应用。我认为它应该更具战略性。因为既然我把生成式 AI 当作一种工具，而不是那种要淘汰人类的巨大威胁，那么我就可以以更务实的方式去使用它。当涉及到 AI 时，在这场会议正式开始前，我唯一要做的就是告诉大家，不要提 AI 这个词。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Kelsey Hightower**: The number one success criteria was Docker. Now instead of talking about Java versus Python versus Ruby, you only have to talk about scheduling Docker containers. We were already off to a running start because you could just reuse the same Docker containers. And I remember I get this email from Satia, the CEO of Microsoft, and I'm like, man, he wrote this nice email and I open a PDF and there's a zero get added to the equation. And so you're looking at this like, I didn't even know that they do that. We know that it happens, but the person that graduated from high school in 1999 that chose the A+ certification didn't know that was available. So, I was serious about going to Microsoft. I'm not just like a Genai hater. I just don't like the naive promotion and adoption of it. I think it should be way strategic. And since I think about Genai as a tool versus the great human replacement, then I can use it in way more primatic ways. And when AI is involved, the one thing I just do before the thing kicks off in this meeting, do not say AI because...

### Kelsey Hightower 的非凡履历

**主持人**: **Kelsey Hightower** 被公认为 Kubernetes 社区最具影响力的声音之一，但你绝对猜不到他的职业生涯是如何开始的。在 19 岁时，他从大学辍学成为了一名 DSL 宽带网络安装工，随后成为一名自学成才的开发者，并最终晋升为 **Google** 的杰出工程师。在 43 岁时，他在整个行业的顶峰选择退休。

今天我们将回顾 Kelsey 步入科技界的这段非传统经历，以及他如何（往往在不知不觉中）不断为自己创造新的机会。我们将探讨容器大战背后的内部故事，以及 Puppet、Docker、**Terraform** 和 CoreOS 之间发生的一切，以及 Kubernetes 是如何最终胜出的。我们还会聊到他如何从一名 Google 的独立贡献者（IC）做到高管级别，以及他为什么拒绝了来自 **Satya Nadella** 亲自发出的微软 Offer 但薪酬却依然翻倍。他还为那些担心被 AI 商品化的软件工程师们提供了极其接地气且务实的建议。

如果你是一名正在思考自己长期职业轨迹的工程师，不管你是想晋升到 Staff 以上级别、成为独立开发者，还是正默默计划离开这个行业，这期节目都是为你准备的。坦白说，这期节目比平常的时长要长很多，因为我简直是被死死地钉在椅子上了，全程都在入神地听 Kelsey 讲故事和他的思考。

**Host**: Kelsey High Tower is known as one of the most influential voices in the Kubernetes community, but you wouldn't guess from how his career started. At 19, he dropped out of college to be a DSL model installer, became a self-taught developer, and still went on to become a distinguished engineer at Google. At age 43, he then retired at the very top of the industry. Today we cover Kelsey's unconventional path into tech and how he kept creating new opportunities for himself often unknowingly. The inside story of the container wars, puppet, Docker, Terraform, Coros and how Kubernetes eventually won. Going from a Google IC to executive level and how he rejected a Microsoft offer from Sachio Nadella himself and still doubled his compensation. His grounded on pragmatic advice for software engineers worried about being commoditized by AI and so much more. If you're an engineer thinking about your long-term career trajectory, whether that's getting into a staff plus level, going independent, or even quietly planning to leave the industry, this episode is for you.
