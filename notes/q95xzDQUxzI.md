### 中美稀土与芯片协议：一次短暂的缓和

唐纳德·特朗普和习近平在本周四，他们六年多来的首次会晤中，同意推迟对**稀土元素**（Rare Earth Elements: 一组具有独特磁性、催化和光学性质的17种金属元素）和计算机芯片的出口管制。他们达成的这项协议为期一年，此前数月两国之间的关税、报复性限制和停滞不前的谈判不断升级。美国和中国还就与芬太尼相关的美国关税，以及两国航运业的针锋相对的征税达成了协议。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Donald Trump and Xiinping agreed this Thursday at their first meeting in over six years to delay export controls on rare earths and on computer chips. The deal they struck lasts for one year and follows months of escalating tariffs, retaliatory restrictions, and stalled negotiations. The US and China additionally reached agreements on American tariffs related to fentinel and on tit fortat levies on each other's shipping industries.</p>
</details>

尽管唐纳德·特朗普在会后听起来很得意，但这项协议大多被视为中国的胜利，因为美国接受了将其对人工智能芯片等高科技出口的限制与中国对稀土出口的限制挂钩的谈判条件。随着20%的芬太尼关税减半，中国商品的平均关税率降至31%，远低于美国对巴西和印度征收的50%关税，也低于对许多加拿大商品征收的35%关税，这至少在目前使得中国的生产更具吸引力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While Donald Trump sounded triumphant after the meeting, the deal was mostly seen as a win for China where the US accepted a negotiating link between its restrictions on high-tech exports like AI chips and China's restrictions on rare earth exports. With the 20% fentinel tariff hald, the average tariff rate on Chinese goods falls to 31% which is well below the 50% US tariffs on Brazil and India and below the 35% tariffs on many Canadian goods making production in China more attractive at least for now.</p>
</details>

尽管市场似乎松了一口气，但潜在的紧张局势依然存在，经济学家们将这项协议描述为“粗略、临时且建立在沙滩之上”。稀土元素一直是近期僵局中的一个关键谈判点。它们是电动马达、智能手机、飞机、光纤网络、医疗成像设备等诸多产品中的关键组成部分。它们还在导弹制导系统和喷气发动机中发挥作用。中国已经花费数十年时间在稀土生产领域建立了主导地位，而这种主导地位现在正被用作**地缘政治杠杆**（Geopolitical Leverage: 指一国利用其在地理位置、资源或经济影响力等方面的优势，来影响或控制其他国家的政治或经济决策）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While markets seem to have breathed a sigh of relief, the underlying tensions are still in place and the economists described the agreement as sketchy, temporary, and built on sand. Rare earth elements have been a key negotiating point in the most recent standoff. These are crucial components in electric motors, smartphones, airplanes, fiber optic networks, medical imaging equipment, and much more. They also play a role in missile guidance systems and jet engines. China has spent decades building dominance in their production and that dominance is now being used as leverage.</p>
</details>

稀土是一组在元素周期表上突出显示的17种金属元素。它们并非像其名称所暗示的那样特别“稀有”，但它们通常以低浓度共存，这使得它们的开采和分离过程非常复杂且昂贵。精炼阶段过程混乱、能源密集，并且通常涉及强酸。这部分解释了中国为何主导该行业：它多年来建立了基础设施，承担了环境成本，并在其他国家不愿参与时扩大了生产规模。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Rare earths are a group of 17 metallic elements highlighted here on the periodic table. And if you learn how to spell all of their names, you'll have a real edge in playing Scrabble. They're not particularly rare, as their name would imply, but they're often found together in low concentrations, which makes mining them and separating them a very complex and expensive process. The refining stage is messy, energyintensive, and often involves strong assets. This is partially why China dominates the industry. It built the infrastructure over the years, accepted the environmental costs, and scaled up production when other countries were reluctant to get involved.</p>
</details>

虽然稀土产量在过去25年中大幅增长，但需求也同样迅速增长。这些元素在现代制造业中几乎不可或缺。例如，**钕磁铁**（Neodymium Magnets: 一种由钕、铁和硼组成的强力稀土永磁体）被用于电动马达、风力涡轮机和硬盘驱动器中。它们体积小、功能强大且高效，非常适合小型化组件和降低能耗。没有它们，建造无人机之类的设备将非常困难。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While rare earth production has grown massively over the last 25 years, demand has grown just as fast. These elements have become almost indispensable in modern manufacturing. Neodyinium magnets, for example, are used in electric motors, wind turbines, and hard drives. They're small, powerful, and efficient, ideal for miniaturizing components, and reducing energy consumption. It would be difficult to build something like a drone without them.</p>
</details>

稀土还用于抛光粉、电池合金和医疗成像设备等产品中。它们在光纤电缆的生产中发挥着关键作用，而光纤电缆构成了互联网和现代电信网络的骨干。不同的稀土被用于掺杂玻璃纤维，从而使光信号能够在长距离传输中不降级地放大。这对于维持跨大陆的高速数据传输至关重要，为从视频流媒体和云计算到金融交易和全球通信的一切提供动力。如果没有稀土，光纤网络的效率和覆盖范围将受到严重限制，从而破坏支持数字经济的基础设施。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Rare Earths are also used in things like polishing powders, battery alloys, and medical imaging equipment. They play a critical role in the production of fiber optic cables, which form the backbone of the internet and modern telecommunications networks. Different rare earths are used to dope the glass fibers, enabling the amplification of light signals over long distances without degradation. This is essential for maintaining high-speed data transmission across continents, powering everything from video streaming and cloud computing to financial transactions and global communications. Without rare earths, the efficiency and reach of fiber optic networks would be severely limited, undermining the infrastructure that supports the digital economy.</p>
</details>

军事应用也确实存在，每当稀土供应被切断时，这些应用通常会在文章中被大肆宣传，但它们并非需求的主要驱动力。哈佛大学指出，电动汽车所需的矿物投入是传统汽车的六倍，风力发电厂所需的矿物是燃气发电厂的九倍。因此，尽管稀土确实具有军事用途，例如在导弹制导系统、喷气发动机和雷达设备中，但军事应用所需的数量相对较小。主要需求来自电子产品、汽车、能源和电信等民用行业。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Military applications exist too, and these are usually hyped in articles whenever the supply of railroads is cut off, but these applications are not the primary driver of demand. Harvard points out that electric cars require six times the mineral inputs of conventional cars and wind plants require nine times more minerals than gas-fired plants. So while rare earths do have military uses like in missile guidance systems, jet engines and radar equipment, the quantities needed for military applications are relatively small. The main demand comes from civilian industries like electronics, cars, energy, and telecommunications.</p>
</details>

### 稀土产业的兴衰：美国与中国的角色变迁

从1960年代到1980年代，美国是全球稀土元素的主要生产国，这主要归功于加利福尼亚州的**山口矿**（Mountain Pass mine）。该矿于2002年因有毒废物泄漏（放射性废水渗入周围沙漠）而关闭。由于中国进口价格更低、环境合规成本高以及污染问题，该矿已不再具有经济可行性。该矿区于2012年复活，2015年破产，并于2017年被成立MP Materials的金融家接管。今年早些时候，美国政府以4亿美元收购了该公司15%的股份，作为旨在建立国内稀土磁铁供应链的更广泛公私合作的一部分。苹果公司已承诺投入5亿美元进行长期合作，以采购美国制造的稀土磁铁，并在山口矿建立稀土回收设施。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">From the 1960s through to the 1980s, the United States was the dominant global producer of rare earth elements, largely due to the Mountain Pass mine in California, which was shut down in 2002 after a toxic waste spill where radioactive waste water leaked into the surrounding desert. The mine was no longer viable due to cheaper Chinese imports, environmental compliance costs, and pollution issues. The site was revived in 2012, went bankrupt in 2015, and was taken over by financiers who founded MP Materials in 2017. Earlier this year, the US government acquired a 15% stake in the company for $400 million as part of a broader public private partnership aimed at building a domestic supply chain for rare earth magnets. Apple has committed $500 million in a long-term partnership to source Americanmade rare earth magnets and to build a rare earth recycling facility at Mountain Pass.</p>
</details>

从1990年代开始，环境法规、企业短期主义和外包的结合导致美国稀土产量下降。与此同时，中国战略性地投资该行业，提供更低的成本和更少的环境限制。到21世纪初，中国已超越美国，如今供应全球约80%的稀土，并处理超过90%的精炼。中国还生产几乎所有使用稀土材料的磁铁。在中国，从矿石到成品组件的整个供应链都是**垂直整合**（Vertically Integrated: 指公司拥有或控制其产品生产过程中的多个阶段）且地理上集中的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Starting in the 1990s, a combination of environmental regulations, corporate short- termism, and outsourcing led to a decline in US railroads production. China, meanwhile, strategically invested in the industry, offering lower costs and fewer environmental constraints. By the early 2000s, China had overtaken the US and today supplies around 80% of the world's rare earths and handles over 90% of the refining. China also manufactures nearly all of the magnets that use rare earth materials. In China, the entire supply chain from ore to finish components is vertically integrated and geographically concentrated.</p>
</details>

中国的支配地位在性质上可能更多是战略性的而非经济性的，因为销售稀土的利润并不高。美国每年在鳄梨进口上的花费是稀土进口的20多倍。当然，区别在于没有人曾威胁要切断鳄梨供应。由于稀土元素深入嵌入几乎所有先进工业的供应链中，控制其流动意味着对生产时间表、定价的影响，并在需要时提供巨大的地缘政治杠杆。中国在控制这个小行业方面的地缘政治优势来自于其造成巨大破坏的能力。当出口许可证被延迟或拒绝时，世界各地的供应链就会停滞。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">China's dominance is possibly more strategic than economic in nature as selling rare earths isn't hugely profitable. The US spends more than 20 times as much on avocado imports each year as it does on rare earths. The difference, of course, is that no one ever threatened to cut off the guacamole supply. Because railroads are so deeply embedded in the supply chains of nearly every advanced industry, control over their flow means influence over production timelines, pricing, and provides huge geopolitical leverage when needed. China's geopolitical edge in controlling this small industry comes from its ability to cause huge disruptions. When export licenses are delayed or denied, supply chains around the world come to a halt.</p>
</details>

稀土元素，尽管其名称如此，但并非特别稀有。然而，大规模精炼和生产它们的能力却是稀有的。中国通过数十年的工业投资、对环境成本的容忍以及技术工艺开发，建立了这种能力。这个过程混乱、昂贵，并且难以快速扩大规模，这使得在西方环境和监管限制下重建这个行业极其困难。在西方国家，环境审批可能需要数年时间。劳动力成本远高于中国，而且生产方面的专业知识极其有限，因为大多数在该领域有经验的工程师多年前就已退休。即使有政府支持和企业投资，进展也一直缓慢，因为重建产能需要的不仅仅是原材料。它还需要技术专长、监管改革和长期投资。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Rare earth elements, despite their name, are not particularly rare. But the ability to refine and produce them at scale is. China has built that capacity through decades of industrial investment, environmental tolerance, and technical process development. The process is messy, expensive, and difficult to scale up quickly, making it extremely difficult to rebuild this industry under western environmental and regulatory constraints. Environmental approvals can take years in Western countries. Labor costs are significantly higher than in China, and expertise in production is extremely limited as most of the engineers with experience in this space retired many years ago. Even with government backing and corporate investment, progress has been slow as rebuilding capacity requires more than just access to raw materials. It requires technical expertise, regulatory changes, and long-term investment.</p>
</details>

### 中国的稀土出口管制策略及其全球影响

中国的出口许可制度已成为其贸易战略的核心工具。自2023年以来，中国政府已将这些许可证的范围扩大到涵盖700多种不同产品，包括稀土、制造设备和其他关键投入。许可证通常签发6个月，并要求详细披露买方、预期用途以及将要制造的最终产品。在某些情况下，出口商被要求从客户那里收集技术文档或专有信息，作为许可证申请过程的一部分。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">China's export licensing system has become a central tool in its trade strategy. Since 2023, the Chinese government has expanded the scope of these licenses to cover more than 700 different products, including rare earths, manufacturing equipment, and other critical inputs. Licenses are typically issued for 6 months and require detailed disclosures about the buyer, the intended use, and the final product that will be built. In some cases, exporters are asked to collect technical documentation or proprietary information from their customers as part of the license application process.</p>
</details>

许可流程为北京提供了了解外国供应链的窗口。通过收集稀土如何以及在哪里使用的数据，中国当局可以跟踪需求模式、识别潜在的囤积行为，并监控可能转售材料的买家。许可制度还允许对每个客户进行选择性执法。许可证可以被延迟或悄悄地扣留，从而在无需正式禁令的情况下为买家制造不确定性。最近几个月，德国公司获得的批准多于其美国同行，而印度买家则面临更长的延迟。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The licensing process gives Beijing a window into foreign supply chains. By collecting data on how and where rare arts are used, Chinese authorities can track demand patterns, identify potential stockpiling, and monitor for buyers who might be reselling the materials. The licensing system also allows for selective enforcement on a customerbyc customer basis. Licenses can be delayed or quietly withheld, creating uncertainty for buyers without the need for formal bans. In recent months, German firms have received more approvals than their American counterparts, and Indian buyers have faced even longer delays.</p>
</details>

许可制度的结构反映了中国贸易方式的更广泛转变。它模仿了美国通过**外国直接产品规则**（Foreign Direct Product Rule: 一项美国出口管制法规，将美国限制的范围扩大到在国外制造但直接使用美国技术、软件或设备的物品）所设定的例子。北京采纳了这一逻辑，对依赖中国投入或技术诀窍的非中国生产商品施加限制。如果美国被中国切断供应，其他国家可能会犹豫是否向美国买家转口，这不仅是为了避免危及自身获取稀土的途径，还因为近期美国的贸易侵略已经使与世界其他国家的外交和商业关系紧张。在稀缺时刻，华盛顿可能会发现愿意为其承担巨大经济风险的历史盟友越来越少。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The structure of the licensing regime reflects a broader shift in China's approach to trade. It mimics the example set by the United States with the foreign direct product rule, a US export control regulation that extends the reach of US restrictions to items made abroad if they are the direct product of US technology, software or equipment. Beijing has adopted this logic, applying restrictions to goods produced outside of China if they rely on Chinese inputs or knowhow. If the United States was cut off by China, other nations might hesitate to reexport to American buyers, not just to avoid jeopardizing their own access, but also because recent US trade aggression has strained diplomatic and commercial relationships with the rest of the world. In a moment of scarcity, Washington may find that fewer of its historical allies are willing to take a big economic risks on its behalf.</p>
</details>

在最近的贸易争端中，**重稀土**（Heavy Rare Earths: 稀土元素中的一个亚组，原子序数较高，通常更稀有且难以提取）受到了中国政府的特别关注。这些元素因其独特的性质而特别有价值且极难替代。这些元素的一个用途是作为强力磁铁的添加剂，它们可以增强热稳定性，使磁铁在高温下保持强度，这在某些国防应用、空间应用和能源技术中是必不可少的特性。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Heavy rare arts have received particular attention from the Chinese government in the recent trade disputes. These elements are particularly valuable and extremely difficult to substitute due to their unique properties. One of the uses of these elements is as an additive in powerful magnets where they enhance thermal stability, allowing magnets to maintain their strength at high temperatures, an essential property in certain defense applications, space applications, and in energy technologies.</p>
</details>

中国政府在限制技术专长传播方面也付出了巨大努力。2023年，他们禁止了稀土加工技术的出口。根据《华尔街日报》和路透社的报道，中国商务部开始要求稀土企业登记技术人员并收缴他们的护照，有效地限制了他们出国旅行的能力，即使是出于度假等个人原因。这项政策针对参与稀土加工和磁铁制造的工人。目标是防止敏感技术知识的泄露，并保持中国在稀土技术方面的战略优势。许可制度在设计上是不透明的，这使得中国能够在不承担正式禁运的外交成本的情况下施加压力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Chinese government has gone to great lengths to restrict the spread of technical expertise too. In 2023, they banned the export of rare earth processing technology. And according to reports from the Wall Street Journal and Reuters, China's Ministry of Commerce began requiring rare earth firms to register technical staff and collect their passports, effectively limiting their ability to travel abroad, even for personal reasons like holidays. The policy targets workers involved in rare earth processing and in magnet manufacturing. The goal is to prevent leaks of sensitive technical knowledge and maintain China's strategic advantage in rare earth technologies. The licensing regime is opaque by design, giving China the ability to exert pressure without the diplomatic cost of a formal embargo.</p>
</details>

中国将稀土用作贸易武器并非新鲜事。2010年，在中日海洋争端期间，北京悄悄切断了对日本制造商的稀土出口。此举立即造成了混乱。措手不及的日本公司被迫停产。日本政府最终屈服，释放了因争端被捕的中国渔民，中国随后恢复了出货。这一事件给此前一直依赖**准时制生产**（Just-in-Time Manufacturing: 一种旨在通过仅在需要时生产所需数量的物品来减少库存和相关成本的生产策略）技术的日本公司留下了深刻印象。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">China's use of railroads as a trade weapon is nothing new. In 2010, during a maritime dispute with Japan, Beijing quietly cut off rare earth exports to Japanese manufacturers. The move caused immediate disruption. Japanese firms caught off guard were forced to halt production lines. The Japanese government caved in and released the Chinese fishermen that had been arrested as part of the dispute, and China resumed shipment shortly after. The episode left a lasting impression on Japanese firms who had up until that point relied on just in time manufacturing techniques.</p>
</details>
