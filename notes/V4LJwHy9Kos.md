### 美国军事葬礼上的《熄灯号》

最后，一个多世纪以来，美国**退伍军人**（military veterans: 曾在美国军队服役的人）葬礼上庄严而标志性的一部分，就是被称为**《熄灯号》**（Taps: 美国军事葬礼上演奏的24音符号角曲）的24音符**号角曲**（bugle call: 用号角演奏的短曲）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And finally, for more than a century, a solemn iconic part of funerals for American military veterans has been the 24 note bugle call known as taps.</p>
</details>

《泰晤士报》一直在报道，近年来这首曲子不仅由官方军事乐手在全国各地演奏，还有数千名志愿者参与演奏。回到2000年，大多数退伍军人都有资格获得包括演奏《熄灯号》在内的葬礼荣誉，但需求很快就超过了供应。一度每天约有1800名退伍军人去世，而军事号手只有500人。作为回应，五角大楼批准了数字号角。这基本上是一个号角形状的扬声器，播放**阿灵顿国家公墓**（Arlington National Cemetery: 美国著名的军事公墓）的《熄灯号》旧录音。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Times has been covering how in recent years that song has been played across the country, not just by official military musicians, but also by thousands of volunteers. At one point, there were about 1,800 veteran deaths a day and only 500 military bugers. In response, the Pentagon green lit a digital bugle. Basically, a bugle-shaped speaker that plays an old recording of taps from Arlington National Cemetery.</p>
</details>

然而，这些经过改装的号角受到了那些演奏真号角的人的广泛批评。他们抱怨这些数字号角具有欺骗性，或者有时会被错误地设置为播放错误的曲子。因此，全国各地的志愿者纷纷介入，确保尽可能多的葬礼能够由真人演奏传统的《熄灯号》。例如，去年“全美号手”（Bugles Across America）组织的成员在5000多场军事葬礼或活动中进行了演奏。一位志愿者告诉《泰晤士报》：“当我意识到他们在使用罐头音乐时，我的心都碎了。”一位19岁的学生也一直在葬礼上演奏，他说在演奏《熄灯号》时，他会思考每一个音符，他说：“这是一个故事，就像你在讲述这位士兵的故事。”

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But those modified bugles got a wide range of criticism from people who play the real thing. They've complained that they're deceptive or that they can sometimes be cued to play the wrong song altogether. So, across the country, volunteers have stepped in to make sure that as many funerals as possible get traditional taps played by a human. For example, members of the group Bugles Across America played at more than 5,000 military funerals or events last year. One volunteer told the Times, "It just broke my heart when I realized that they were using canned music."
