### 巨型项目的幻象：Xlinks电力项目的夭折

想象一下：你身处英国，天气一如既往地阴沉多雨，有人向你提出一个想法——从你家铺设一条4000公里长的延长线到摩洛哥沙漠，这样你就可以用太阳能为你的水壶供电……

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">OK, imagine this: you're in the UK, it's grey and raining—as usual—and someone pitches you the idea to run a 4,000-kilometre extension cord from your house to the deserts of Morocco – so that you can power your kettle with solar energy...</p>
</details>

这基本上就是现已取消的绿色能源项目**Xlinks**（Xlinks摩洛哥-英国电力项目：旨在将摩洛哥的太阳能和风能输送到英国的巨型能源项目）的设想。这个本应是工程和气候雄心的胜利的项目，计划在摩洛哥建设一个77平方英里的太阳能阵列、一个580平方英里的风力发电场和一个50平方英里的电池阵列，并通过世界上最长的海底电缆连接到英国。该项目本应以240亿英镑的成本供应英国8%的电力——这还是在相信不会出现成本超支的情况下，而成本超支总是会发生。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That’s basically what XLinks – the now cancelled green energy power project was. In what was supposed to be a triumph of engineering and climate ambition, the Xlinks Morocco-UK Power Project was designed to be a 77 square mile solar array, a 580 square mile wind farm and a 50 square mile battery array all based in Morocco and connected to the UK with the world's longest undersea power cable. The project would have supplied 8% of the UK's electricity at a cost of twenty-four billion pounds – and that’s if you believe that there wouldn’t be cost overruns – which there always are…</p>
</details>

该项目的批评者称其为“**绿色殖民主义**”（Green Colonialism: 指发达国家以环保之名，利用发展中国家的资源和劳动力来满足自身能源需求的行为），绿色和平组织表示，这只是全球北方剥削全球南方能源的又一个案例。尽管非洲强大的太阳能和风能潜力使其成为生产绿色能源的诱人之地，但许多当地居民质疑这些能源是否应该出口而非本地使用。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Critics of the project called it 'green colonialism' - with Greenpeace saying that it was just another case of the Global North exploiting the Global South for energy. While Africa’s strong solar and wind potential make it an attractive place to produce green energy, many residents question that the energy should be exported rather than used locally.</p>
</details>

不幸的是，Xlinks项目加入了越来越多宏大愿景、成本膨胀、最终因自身复杂性而失败的巨型项目“墓地”。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Unfortunately, the XLinks project has joined a growing graveyard of planned megaprojects that are grand in vision, bloated in cost, and ultimately undone by their own complexity.</p>
</details>

从从未到达目的地的**高速铁路**（High-speed rail: 一种以高速运行为特征的铁路运输系统）到带有水下声学系统（被媒体戏称为“**鱼类迪斯科**”（The fish disco: 一种旨在通过声波驱赶海洋生物远离核电站冷却水入口的系统））的核电站，西方国家在建设核心基础设施方面变得异常糟糕。问题不再是这些项目为何失败，而是我们是否已经忘记了如何成功。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">From high-speed rail lines that never reach their destination to nuclear plants with underwater acoustic systems—dubbed 'the fish disco' by the press—which is supposed to deter marine life, western countries have become astonishingly bad at building core infrastructure. The question is no longer why these projects fail—but whether we’ve forgotten how to succeed.</p>
</details>

### Xlinks项目的财务与技术挑战

Xlinks项目本应由私人资助，但开发商依赖英国政府提供为期25年的“**差价合约**”（Contract for Difference, CfD: 一种政府与能源项目签订的协议，保证项目以固定价格出售电力，以降低投资风险）。这是一种委婉的说法，意味着：“我们会建造它，但政府必须在未来25年内保证我们以固定价格出售电力。”因此，虽然并非官方补贴，但这是一种补贴形式，没有它，项目就无法进行。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">XLinks was supposed to be privately funded but the developers were depending on a 25-year Contract for Difference—or CfD—from the UK government – which is a fancy way of saying: 'We’ll build it, but the government has to guarantee us a fixed price for the electricity for the next twenty-five years – so while not officially subsidized – this was a type of subsidy and the project won’t be going ahead without it.</p>
</details>

这个价格是多少？每兆瓦时70到80英镑，这对于英国来说其实不算差。它比核电便宜，与海上风电相比也具有竞争力。但这绝不是一个便宜的价格。它大约是美国批发电价的三倍，因此如果制造业在英国生产，能源密集型产业仍将处于显著劣势。这份差价合约意味着英国政府在未来25年内将提供数十亿英镑的价格担保。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That price? £70 to £80 pounds per megawatt hour – which is actually not bad for the UK. It’s cheaper than nuclear and competitive with offshore wind. But it’s by no means a bargain. It is about three times the wholesale cost of electricity in the United States – so energy hungry industry would still be at a significant disadvantage if manufacturing in the UK. That Contract for Difference would’ve meant billions of pounds in government price guarantees over the next twenty-five years.</p>
</details>

接下来我们谈谈传输损耗。**高压直流电缆**（High Voltage Direct Current, HVDC: 一种用于远距离输电的技术，损耗相对较低）每千公里会损失大约3.5%的电力。对于一条4000公里的电缆来说，这意味着14%的损耗。除此之外，电池还会额外损失10%到15%。所以，在电力到达英国电网之前，你已经损失了超过四分之一的能量。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So, let’s talk about transmission losses next. High Voltage Direct Current or HVDC cables lose about 3.5% of their power per thousand kilometers. With a 4000-kilometer cable that’s 14% in losses. Then you’ve got battery losses on top of that of another 10 to 15%. So, you’re losing more than a quarter of your energy before it even hits the UK grid.</p>
</details>

想象一下你点了一份披萨，送货员在路上吃掉了四分之一。这就是Xlinks项目的情况。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Imagine ordering a pizza and the delivery guy eats a quarter of it on the way. That’s Xlinks.</p>
</details>

在安装第一块太阳能电池板之前，该项目的估计成本就从200亿英镑跃升至240亿英镑。计划中的电池阵列将比世界上最大的已建成电池系统大16倍，占据摩洛哥沙漠50平方英里的面积。海底电缆将是世界上最长的，横跨深海环境和多个司法管辖区。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The project’s estimated cost jumped from £20 billion to £24 billion pounds before a single solar panel was installed. The planned battery array would have been 16 times larger than the world’s largest completed battery system—occupying fifty square miles in the Moroccan desert. The subsea cable would have been the longest in the world, traversing deep-sea environments and multiple jurisdictions.</p>
</details>

像**Commonsense Sceptic**（Commonsense Sceptic: 一个YouTube频道，以批判性分析大型项目和政策闻名）这样的频道曾批评过美国提出的类似想法，原因在于它们对未经证实规模的依赖、对传输损耗的脆弱性、电池系统巨大的材料密集度、以及对恐怖袭击、风暴或极端天气事件造成的损害的单一脆弱性。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Channels like the Commonsense Sceptic have criticized similar ideas that have been pitched in the United States due to their reliance on unproven scale, the vulnerability to transmission losses, and the sheer material intensity of the battery systems, the singular vulnerability to terrorist attacks and damage from storms or extreme weather events.</p>
</details>

### 政治决策与本土偏好

几周前，英国能源大臣埃德·米利班德（Ed Miliband）否决了这项提案。他表示，该项目“漏洞太多，不具成本效益”。他列举了安全问题、交付风险以及缺乏国内经济效益等原因。他表达了对“本土可再生能源”的偏好——翻译过来就是：“我们宁愿在约克郡建造风力发电场，也不愿铺设4000公里长的延长线到非洲。”

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Ed Miliband, the UK’s Energy Secretary, rejected the proposal a few weeks ago. The project, he said, had “too many holes and didn’t provide value for money. He cited security concerns, delivery risk, and a lack of domestic economic benefit. He expressed a preference for 'homegrown renewables’ - Translation: 'We’d rather build wind farms in Yorkshire than run a 4,000 km extension cord to Africa.'</p>
</details>

不幸的是，Xlinks并非个例，它是一个更大模式的一部分。英国的基础设施雄心充满了以大张旗鼓开始却以闹剧收场的项目。旨在连接伦敦与北部的**HS2**（High Speed 2: 英国规划中的高速铁路项目）在成本飙升至1000亿英镑以上后，其北部路段被取消。在此过程中，它花费了1.19亿英镑用于建设一条特殊隧道，以保护一个受保护的蝙蝠群落——尽管没有证据表明高速列车会干扰蝙蝠。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Xlinks is unfortunately not an outlier—it’s part of a bigger pattern. The UK’s infrastructure ambitions are littered with projects that start with fanfare and end in farce. HS2, the high-speed rail line meant to connect London to the North, saw its northern leg scrapped after costs ballooned past £100 billion pounds. Along the way, it spent £119 million pounds on a special tunnel to preserve a protected bat colony—despite there being no evidence that high-speed trains interfere with bats.</p>
</details>

还有萨默塞特的核电站**欣克利角C**（Hinkley Point C: 英国萨默塞特郡在建的核电站项目），现在预计成本将达到460亿英镑。其中一个更令人惊讶的特点是：一个提议中的“鱼类迪斯科”——一个旨在将海洋生物驱离冷却水入口的声学威慑系统。该电站的开发商**EDF**（Électricité de France: 法国电力公司）曾试图放弃这部分计划，理由是潜水员安全和效果存疑，但环保活动家们强烈反对。这个“鱼类迪斯科”可能不得不继续进行。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Then there’s Hinkley Point C, the nuclear plant in Somerset, now expected to cost £46 billion pounds. Among its more surprising features: a proposed “fish disco”—an acoustic deterrent system designed to keep marine life away from the cooling water intake. EDF, the plant’s developer, tried to abandon this part of the plan, citing diver safety and questionable effectiveness, but environmental campaigners pushed back. The fish disco may have to go ahead.</p>
</details>

### 巨型项目的“铁律”与“四重崇高”

英国失败的基础设施项目反映了一种更深层次的弊病——不仅在英国，而且在大部分发达国家：一种过度承诺、交付不足、并陷入监管、诉讼和政治风险规避泥潭的发展体系。正如丹麦经济学家本特·弗莱维伯格（Bent Flyvbjerg）所指出的，由于心理偏见、政治激励和结构复杂性的混合作用，巨型项目“几乎总是超预算、超期、效益不足”。他提出了一个现象，称之为“**巨型项目铁律**”（Iron Law of Megaprojects: 指绝大多数大型项目都会出现成本超支、延期或未能达到预期效益的情况），即十个项目中有九个会发生成本超支、延误或未能提供预期效益。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Britain’s failed infrastructure projects reflect a deeper malaise – not just in the UK, but in much of the developed world: of a system of development that overpromises, underdelivers, and gets bogged down in a tangle of regulation, litigation, and political risk aversion. As the Danish economist Bent Flyvbjerg has noted, megaprojects are “almost always over budget, over time, and under benefit” due to a mix of psychological bias, political incentives, and structural complexity. He identifies a phenomenon he calls the “iron law of megaprojects,” where nine out of ten projects incur cost overruns, delays, or fail to deliver expected benefits.</p>
</details>

这种糟糕的表现是由他所称的“**四重崇高**”（Four sublimes: 指技术、政治、经济和美学上的兴奋感，驱动规划者追求宏大项目，往往牺牲现实性）所驱动的：技术、政治、经济和美学上的刺激感，促使规划者追求宏大项目，往往以牺牲现实性为代价。当这与有缺陷的预测、战略性误报以及忽视历史教训的倾向相结合时，这些力量导致了他所称的“**不适者生存**”（Survival of the unfittest: 指那些最具误导性乐观的项目获得批准，但随后最有可能失败的现象）——即那些最具误导性乐观的项目获得批准，但随后最有可能失败。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This poor performance is driven by what he terms the “four sublimes”: the technological, political, economic, and aesthetic thrills that motivate planners to pursue grand projects, often at the expense of realism. When this is combined with flawed forecasting, strategic misrepresentation, and a tendency to ignore lessons of the past, these forces lead to what he calls the “survival of the unfittest”—where the most misleadingly optimistic projects are approved but are then - the most likely to fail.</p>
</details>

巨型项目是实实在在的，能吸引注意力，并给政治领导人带来积极主动的形象。最重要的是，它们往往是媒体的焦点——它们所吸引的注意力吸引着那些除了宣布新巨型项目所获得的知名度之外，几乎没有其他更喜欢的事物的推动者。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Megaprojects are tangible, garner attention, and lend an air of proactiveness to political leaders. On top of that - they tend to be media magnets – and the attention they draw appeals to promoters who enjoy few things more than the visibility they get from announcing a new megaproject.</p>
</details>

### 历史的对比：爱尔兰的成功经验

几个月前，我听了爱尔兰经济学家大卫·麦克威廉姆斯（David McWilliams）的播客，名为《好人为何会做出坏决定？》（Why do good People Make Bad decisions?）。在播客中，他指出巨型项目并非总是像今天这样停滞不前。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A few months ago I was listening to a podcast from the Irish economist David McWilliams called “Why do good People Make Bad decisions? In the podcast he pointed out that megaprojects didn’t always stall out like they seem to today.</p>
</details>

他以1920年代的爱尔兰为例，当时爱尔兰刚刚经历了一场独立战争和一场内战，在脆弱的民主制度下，却在短短三年内建成了**阿德纳克鲁沙水电站**（Ardnacrusha hydroelectric plant: 爱尔兰在1920年代建造的标志性水电项目）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">He used the example of Ireland in the 1920s, which had just come through a war of independence, a civil war, and with a fragile democracy in place, managed to build the Ardnacrusha hydroelectric plant in just three years.</p>
</details>

当时的爱尔兰是一个后殖民国家，没有资金，没有工业，也没有能源。它被描述为一个“啤酒和饼干经济”，全国唯一的真正工业是健力士（Guinness）和雅各布斯（Jacobs）。两者都依赖农业，因此根本没有真正的工业基础。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Ireland at the time was a post-colonial state with no money, no industry, and no energy. It had what is described as a beer and biscuits economy, where the only real industry in the country was Guinnesses and Jacobs. Both of which drew on agriculture, so there was no real industrial base at all.</p>
</details>

爱尔兰没有像其他欧洲国家那样拥有大量的化石燃料储量，因此没有发生真正的工业革命——廉价能源可以转化为生产。爱尔兰人如果想在工业中找到工作，就只能移居国外。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There were no large deposits of fossil fuels in Ireland like there were in other European countries – so there had been no real industrial revolution – where cheap energy could be converted into production. Irish people instead moved abroad if they wanted to find jobs in industry.</p>
</details>

### “过度民主化”与“不适者生存”

麦克威廉姆斯在他的播客中认为，西方社会今天已成为“**过度民主化**”（Hyperdemocracy: 一种政治系统，其中每个人都有否决权，但没有人承担责任，导致决策和执行效率低下）的受害者——一个每个人都有否决权，但没有人承担责任的系统。他认为，善意的监管、环保主义、**邻避主义**（NIMBYISM: Not In My Backyard，指居民反对在自己居住地附近建设他们认为不受欢迎的设施）和地方主义已经创造了一个如此不堪重负的系统，以至于即使是基本的基础设施也无法交付。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">McWilliams argues in his podcast that Western societies have today become victims of “hyperdemocracy”—a system where everyone has a veto, but no one has responsibility. He argues that well-intentioned regulation, environmentalism, NIMBYISM and localism have created a system so overburdened that even basic infrastructure becomes impossible to deliver.</p>
</details>

举个例子，我们来谈谈欣克利角C——英国的旗舰核电项目——它也是有史以来建造的最昂贵的核电站。欣克利角C每兆瓦的成本是韩国同类电站的四倍，甚至比法国由同一公司建造的几乎相同的电站还要贵25%。

</details>
