### AI自主工作能力的持续飞跃

我们看到，在过去的许多年里，人工智能持续展现出非常稳定的进步，大约每三到四个月，它就能够完全自主地完成时长是之前两倍的任务。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We are seeing very consistent improvement over many years, where every three to four months, AI is able to do a task that is twice as long as before, completely on its own.</p>
</details>

我们可以对此进行推断：从现在起一年，或者可能两年后，顶级的**模型**（AI Models: 指人工智能系统，尤其是指大型语言模型等）将能够完全自主地工作一整天甚至更长时间。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We can extrapolate this: in a year from now, or maybe two years from now, the top models will be able to work completely on their own for a whole day or more.</p>
</details>

### 任务时长对AI委派工作的重要性

我认为任务时长之所以特别有趣，原因在于它能让你将越来越多的工作委派给**语言模型**（Language Models: 一种人工智能程序，通过学习大量文本数据来理解和生成人类语言）和**智能代理**（AI Agents: 能够自主感知环境、做出决策并执行任务的人工智能系统）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The reason I think why task length, specifically, is interesting is because that's what allows you to delegate more and more work to language models and agents.</p>
</details>

即使你拥有一个非常聪明的模型，但如果它需要频繁的反馈或与你互动，那么它能让你委派的工作范围就会受到很大限制。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Even if you have a very clever model, if it needs feedback or frequent interaction with you, it severely limits what you can delegate to it.</p>
</details>

比如，如果你每隔10分钟就需要与它交流。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If you need to talk to it every 10 minutes, for example.</p>
</details>

反之，如果你拥有一个可以连续工作数小时的系统，你就可以拥有一整个团队，将任务委派给它们并进行管理。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Conversely, if you have something that can work for hours at a time, you can have a whole team to which you delegate tasks and manage them.</p>
</details>

这些智能代理足够聪明，能够自主工作，纠正自身错误，并进行迭代改进。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The agents are smart enough to work on their own, correct their own errors, and iterate.</p>
</details>
