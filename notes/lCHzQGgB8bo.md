### 保护区改革现状与洪水灾情

**主持人 Jay**: 从36个缩减到9个——这是安大略省在《保护安大略法案》（**Bill 97**）成为法律后，保护区管理机构的数量变化。80多年来，保护区一直在管理洪水风险、规划干旱应对、发放许可证、监测水资源等工作。那么为什么要将36个机构缩减到9个呢？政府表示，这项改革将减少重复、加快许可证审批、改善洪水管理等。但这项计划面临了激烈反对。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host Jay**: From 36 down to nine. That's how many **conservation authorities** the province will have now that **Bill 97, the Plan to Protect Ontario Act**, has become law. For over 80 years, conservation authorities have been managing flood risks, planning for droughts, issuing permits, monitoring water, and more. So why shrink 36 agencies to nine? The government says consolidation will cut overlap, speed up permit applications, and improve flood management, among other benefits. But the plan has faced fierce opposition.

</details>

**主持人 Jay**: 评论者指出，关键的本地知识将被排除在决策之外，让社区更容易遭受洪灾和其他环境伤害。那么，这项改革是否有助于还是阻碍安大略应对洪灾等威胁的能力？洪灾预计在未来只会恶化。目前，这种威胁在东北安大略充分显现，洪水已关闭高速公路，多个社区宣布进入紧急状态。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Host Jay**: Critics say, quote, "critical local knowledge will be excluded from key decisions, leaving communities more exposed to flooding and other environmental harms." So will this help or hurt Ontario's ability to deal with threats like flooding, which is only expected to get worse in years to come? Right now, that threat is on full display in northeastern Ontario, where flooding has shut down highways and multiple communities have declared states of emergency.

</details>
