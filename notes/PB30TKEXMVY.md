### 毕业生最低收入测试与专业筛选

**雷切尔·阿布拉姆斯 (Rachel Abrams)**: 明白。你刚才提到这次变革有两个大动作，那么第二个动作是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: Okay. So, you told us that two major buckets of changes were coming. What is the second?

</details>

**罗恩·利伯 (Ron Lieber)**: 第二个重大变革是，政府宣布不再为那些毕业生无法通过**最低收入测试**（Minimum Earnings Test）的项目提供学生贷款。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ron Lieber**: So the second big change, the government has said that it does not want to provide student loans anymore to programs whose alumni don't meet a minimum earnings test.

</details>

**雷切尔·阿布拉姆斯 (Rachel Abrams)**: 收入测试？请解释一下这具体是什么，又是如何运作的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: Okay. An earnings test. Explain what that is and how it works.

</details>

**罗恩·利伯 (Ron Lieber)**: 基本的规则是这样的：对于完成本科学业的人，政府将在他们毕业四年后进行一次评估。某个特定项目的毕业生，其平均收入必须高于本州内仅有高中学历、且年龄在25至34岁之间的群体的平均收入。核心逻辑很直白：如果你读了大学，挣得却还没有一个高中毕业生多，那这所学校到底给你带来了什么价值？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ron Lieber**: So here's the basic deal. For people who finish their undergraduate program, there's going to be a measurement four years later. And those folks from a particular program must on average earn more than the people in their state who only finished high school and are between the ages of 25 and 34. So the basic bottom line is if you're not earning more than a high school graduate does on average, what has the school done for you?

</details>

**雷切尔·阿布拉姆斯 (Rachel Abrams)**: 更确切地说，如果一个学位不能让你处于比高中毕业生更好的财务状况，联邦政府为什么还要出资补贴它呢？是这个逻辑吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: And more to the point, why is the federal government subsidizing a degree that doesn't put you in any better financial position than a high school student? Is that the idea?

</details>

**罗恩·利伯 (Ron Lieber)**: 没错。而且这个收入测试同样适用于高级学位（硕士/博士）和专业院校。针对研究生的测试逻辑类似，只是具体数据有所不同。它会考察研究生毕业四年后的收入情况，看他们的收入是否高于全美拥有学士学位的25至34岁在职成年人的收入中位数。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ron Lieber**: Exactly. So this earnings test, it will also be applied to advanced degrees and professional schools. And in that case, the test is similar but with different numbers. It will look at the earnings of alumni four years after graduation to see whether they earn more than the median salary for working adults age 25 to 34 who have a bachelor's degree.

</details>

**雷切尔·阿布拉姆斯 (Rachel Abrams)**: 你能举几个可能会通不过这项测试的专业项目的例子吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: Can you give some examples of programs that might fail this test?

</details>

**罗恩·利伯 (Ron Lieber)**: 好的。我一直在研究一个非常详尽的数据集，其中包含了全美各类学校中超过30,000个本科专业的数据。分析显示，许多学校的本科宗教学专业最终可能无法通过测试。此外，大批艺术类院校的特定学位以及戏剧项目也往往垫底。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ron Lieber**: Well, I've been looking at a pretty detailed data set of like over 30,000 undergraduate majors at all sorts of different schools. So, undergraduate religion degrees at a lot of schools may end up flunking the test. A whole bunch of fine arts schools with particular degrees and theater programs often end up at the bottom.

</details>

**雷切尔·阿布拉姆斯 (Rachel Abrams)**: 那么我们需要明确一点：这项关于收入测试的规定在未来三年内还不会产生实质性的处罚后果。因为根据测试的机制设定，只有当某所特定高校的特定项目在连续三年中有两年未通过测试时，后果才会降临。而所谓的“后果”，就是该项目将彻底失去获得联邦学生贷款的资格。这意味着这些项目在那个时间点上或许并不会立刻消失，但如果这些项目中有大量学生依赖借贷来维持学业，他们就必须寻找其他的途径来筹集学费，而学校届时也可能不得不自掏腰包为他们提供资助。

<details>
