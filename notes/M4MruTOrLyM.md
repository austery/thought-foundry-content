### AI颠覆信息获取与新闻业经济模式

人工智能工具正在改变人们在互联网上寻找信息的方式，这种变化的速度之快，让出版商难以适应。当用户向聊天机器人提问而非使用搜索引擎时，他们得到的是答案，而不是可供点击的链接，这正在改变互联网的经济模式，尤其是新闻提供商的经济模式，他们投入资金收集信息，却无法再从中获利。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Artificial intelligence tools are transforming the way people find information on the internet and this change is happening faster than publishers can adapt. When users question chatbots rather than using search engines, they are given answers, rather than links to follow and this is changing the economics of the internet – and in particular the economics of news providers – who spend money gathering information that they can no longer monetize.</p>
</details>

在过去几年里，数百万用户已从搜索引擎转向AI聊天工具进行研究、获取推荐和实时答案。像**ChatGPT**、**Claude**和**Perplexity**这样的工具现在直接回答问题，而这些问题过去常常将读者引向他们认为可信的原始在线资源。随着用户逐渐远离可信的新闻来源，他们对那些旨在模仿权威的AI系统越来越信任，这些工具从网络上抓取答案，却不提供任何问责机制。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Over the last few years, millions of users have shifted from search engines to AI chat tools for research, recommendations, and real-time answers. Tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity are now directly answering questions that once sent readers to primary online sources that they felt they could trust. As users drift away from trusted news sources, they place growing faith in AI systems trained to mimic authority—tools that scrape the web for answers but offer no accountability.</p>
</details>

大约一年前，谷歌推出了**AI Overviews**（AI概览）功能，最近又推出了**AI Mode**（AI模式），这些功能直接在谷歌搜索页面上回答问题，通常不注明原始来源。对用户而言，这感觉无缝衔接，他们不必不断调整搜索词来寻找所需信息，也不必阅读十篇不同的文章来找出问题的答案；但对出版商来说，这却是一场灾难。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">About a year ago Google rolled out AI Overviews and then more recently AI Mode - features that answer questions directly on the google search page, often without crediting the original sources. For users, this feels seamless – they don’t have to constantly refine their search terms to find the information they were looking for and they don’t have to read ten different articles to work out an answer to their question - but for publishers, this has been disastrous.</p>
</details>

几周前，**Enders Analysis**基于**Sistrix**数据发布的一份报告显示，新闻在搜索结果中的可见度已大幅下降。自2019年以来，**《镜报》**在谷歌上的曝光率下降了80%，**《每日邮报》**损失了一半以上。即使是拥有忠实订阅群体的专业刊物**《金融时报》**，今年春季的流量也下降了21%。该报告将这种下降主要归因于谷歌系统的变化，特别是AI概览和AI模式的推出，而非出版商的策略。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A report by Enders Analysis, based on Sistrix data was released a few weeks ago showing that news visibility in search results has collapsed. The Mirror’s presence on Google is down 80% since 2019. The Mail has lost more than half. Even the Financial Times – a specialist publication with a loyal subscriber base, saw a 21% drop in traffic this spring. The report attributes this decline primarily to changes in Google’s systems, in particular - the rollout of AI Overviews and AI Mode, rather than publisher strategy.</p>
</details>

这种转变是结构性的。AI工具在受众接触到他们寻求的信息来源之前就将其拦截。谷歌的AI概览甚至对那些并非寻求AI答案的用户也这样做。出版商无法在不完全从搜索中消失的情况下选择退出，这意味着维持新闻业的经济模式——点击量、订阅和广告——正被那些从新闻提供商那里提取价值却不给予任何回报的系统迅速侵蚀。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This shift is structural. AI tools are intercepting the audience before they reach the source of the information they are seeking. Google’s AI overviews is doing this even for users who weren’t looking for an AI answer. Publishers can’t opt out from this without disappearing from search entirely – meaning that the economic model that sustained journalism—clicks, subscriptions, advertising—is being rapidly eroded by systems that extract value from news providers without returning any.</p>
</details>

**《经济学人》**的这张图表显示，新闻和媒体绝不是受此变化影响最大的行业。科学、教育和参考网站（如**维基百科**）的表现更差，其中健康信息受影响最为严重。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This chart from the Economist shows that news and media are by no means the most affected by this change. Science and education sites and reference sites (like Wikipedia) are doing even worse – with health information being the most impacted.</p>
</details>

搜索引擎过去是——在某种程度上现在也仍然是——互联网的门户，但那扇门正在迅速关闭。用户行为的转变已经可以衡量。今年4月，**苹果**公司报告了**Safari**搜索量首次下降，他们将这一变化归因于用户转向AI聊天工具而非传统搜索引擎。**TechRadar**在12月进行的一项调查发现，27%的美国用户和13%的英国用户现在使用像ChatGPT这样的AI工具来开始他们的信息收集，而不是搜索引擎。他们列举的原因包括速度、特异性和易用性。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Search engines were – and still are – to a certain extent - the gateway to the internet – but that gate is rapidly closing. The shift in user behavior is already measurable. In April, Apple reported the first-ever decline in Safari search volume, a change they attributed to users turning to AI chat tools instead of traditional search engines. A TechRadar survey conducted in December found that 27% of U.S. users and 13% of UK users now use AI tools like ChatGPT instead of search engines to begin their information gathering. The reasons cited include speed, specificity, and ease of use.</p>
</details>

谷歌向新闻网站的引荐流量已从2019年的约65%降至今天的30%，但这种下降早在AI概览推出之前就开始了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Google referrals to news sites have dropped from around 65% in 2019 to 30% today, but this decline began long before AI Overviews.</p>
</details>

几年前，人们常说“互联网上什么都不会消失”——这通常是警告人们不要发布任何日后可能感到羞耻的内容——但我们后来了解到，有些东西仍然会消失，即使它们从未被删除，因为它们变得根本无法找到。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A few years ago, people used to say that “nothing ever disappears from the internet” – usually as a warning against posting anything that you might later be ashamed of – but we have since learned that things can still disappear – even if they are never erased because they just become impossible to find.</p>
</details>

在过去几年里，谷歌在寻找用户所需信息方面的效率越来越低，这既因为网络被**SEO**（Search Engine Optimization: 搜索引擎优化）内容饱和，也因为谷歌开始返回赞助搜索结果。用户发现他们必须筛选大量杂乱信息才能找到可信来源。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Over the last few years, Google became less and less efficient at finding the information you were searching for – both because the web became saturated with SEO-optimized content, and because google started returning sponsored search results. Users found that they had to sift through clutter to find credible sources.</p>
</details>

正当互联网搜索功能开始衰退时，AI工具出现了。据**《大西洋月刊》**报道，AI聊天机器人已经取代了超过25%美国人的搜索习惯。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">AI tools turned up on the scene right as internet search was faltering and according to The Atlantic - AI-chatbots have already replaced search for more than 25 percent of Americans.</p>
</details>

谷歌自己的AI功能让这种转变更加极端，因为它们为那些原本打算使用传统搜索的用户提供了AI答案。问题在于，如果事情继续朝这个方向发展，AI将“自食其果”。如果新闻机构和记者无法通过努力研究重要故事来谋生，那么这项工作就不会有人去做，聊天机器人将不得不诉诸于编造答案，依赖新闻稿或故意误导读者的宣传内容。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Google’s own AI features have made this switch even more extreme, As they provide AI answers to people who were planning on using traditional search. The problem is that if things continue in this direction AI will eat itself. If news organizations and reporters can no longer earn a living by doing the hard work of researching an important story – that work just won’t be done – and chatbots will just have to resort to making up answers, relying on press releases or on propaganda – posted deliberately to mislead readers.</p>
</details>

内容生产者显然正在努力寻找一种新的结构，让AI公司为他们有价值的信息付费，但如果无法实现，开放网络可能会演变成截然不同的东西。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Content producers are obviously working to find a new structure where AI companies pay them for valuable information, but if that can’t, the open web may evolve into something very different.</p>
</details>

### 在线评论生态系统的瓦解与信任危机

新闻业并非唯一受到威胁的事物。整个在线评论生态系统——曾经是消费者决策的重要组成部分——也正在瓦解。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Journalism isn’t the only thing under threat. The entire ecosystem of online reviews—once a vital part of consumer decision-making—is breaking down too.</p>
</details>

在互联网出现之前，评论发表在杂志上。但那些评论并不总是值得信赖。大广告商可能会影响编辑决策，读者核实声明的方式也有限。互联网提供了一种替代方案：独立的评论员通过透明度和一致性建立信任。科技评论员、汽车评论员和利基专家通过诚实赢得了忠实受众，并通过观看量和订阅将这种信任变现。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Before the internet, reviews were published in magazines. But those weren’t always trustworthy. Big advertisers could influence editorial decisions, and readers had limited ways to verify claims. The internet offered an alternative: independent reviewers who built trust through transparency and consistency. Tech reviewers, car reviewers, and niche experts earned loyal audiences by being honest—and monetized that trust through views and subscriptions.</p>
</details>

在线评论是早期互联网最棒的特点之一。像**亚马逊**这样的平台提供了一种新的透明度——来自真实用户的真实反馈。对购物者来说，这是一个突破。您不必相信广告或推销。您可以阅读其他消费者的想法。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Online reviews were one of the best things about the early internet. Platforms like Amazon offered a new kind of transparency—real feedback from real users. ### 开放网络的未来与信息生态系统的挑战

开放网络建立在一个简单的交换之上：出版商创建内容，用户访问网站，他们的注意力随后被货币化——为下一轮报道提供资金。AI工具正在打破这个循环。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The open web was built on a simple exchange: publishers create content, users visit websites, and their attention is then monetized—funding the next round of reporting. AI tools are already breaking that loop.</p>

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
