### 特朗普的50年抵押贷款提议及其潜在风险

上周末，特朗普总统在推特上提出了设立**50年抵押贷款**（50-year mortgage: 一种将还款期限延长至50年的住房贷款）的想法，以此帮助那些希望购房的美国人降低月供。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Last weekend, President Trump tweeted the idea of creating a 50-year mortgage as a way to help lower payments for Americans who want to buy a home.</p>
</details>

**联邦住房金融局**（Federal Housing Finance Agency: 负责监管房利美、房地美等政府支持企业的独立机构）局长比尔·普尔特称这项提议是“彻底的颠覆性变革”。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Federal Housing Finance Agency Director Bill Pulte called the proposal “a complete game changer.”</p>
</details>

这听起来可能很巧妙，但实际上并非如此。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It might sound clever – but it just isn’t.</p>
</details>

让我们来探讨一下，为什么将债务期限拉长半个世纪，对购房者来说可能带来灾难性的后果，以及历史对于这类想法有何启示。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Let’s look at why stretching debt across half a century would likely be disastrous for home buyers—and what history tells us about ideas like this.</p>
</details>

尽管这一想法（到目前为止）细节甚少，但政府官员表示，50年抵押贷款将降低每月还款额，并帮助那些目前因房价过高而无法进入住房市场的美国人。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While the idea is (so far) quite light on detail - administration officials are saying that a 50-year mortgage would lower monthly payments and help Americans who are currently priced out of the housing market.</p>
</details>

其逻辑是，只要延长贷款期限，就能减少每月还款额，但这种简单性掩盖了一个更为复杂的现实。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The logic is that if you just lengthen the term of a loan you can reduce the monthly payment – but that simplicity hides a much more complicated reality.</p>
</details>

将抵押贷款期限延长至半个世纪，将改变贷款机构对风险的定价方式、投资者对**抵押贷款支持证券**（Mortgage-backed securities: 以抵押贷款为基础资产发行的债券）的估值方式，并且需要修改联邦法律。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Extending mortgages to half a century would alter how lenders price risk, how investors value mortgage-backed securities, and would require changes to federal law.</p>
</details>

自2020年以来，美国房价上涨了约45%，其中大部分涨幅发生在利率接近于零的疫情期间。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">US Home prices have risen about 45% since 2020, with most of that increase having occurred during the pandemic years when interest rates fell near zero.</p>
</details>

如今，抵押贷款利率已达到20年来的最高水平，通常情况下，人们会预期这会压低房价，但房价在利率上升后却几乎没有变动。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Today - mortgage rates are at their highest level in twenty years, and you would normally expect that to depress home prices – but instead home prices have barely budged since rates went up.</p>
</details>

有限的住房供应使房价居高不下，但销售量已降至几十年来的最低水平。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Limited housing supply has kept values elevated – but - sales volumes have dropped to their lowest level in decades.</p>
</details>

除此之外，首次购房者的平均年龄已达到40岁。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">On top of that - the average age of a first-time buyer has now reached 40.</p>
</details>

在这种背景下，更长的贷款期限可能看起来像是一线生机——直到你仔细阅读细则。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Against that backdrop, a longer loan term might look like a lifeline—until you check the fine print.</p>
</details>

不幸的是，经济和政治远比新闻标题所暗示的要复杂得多。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The economics—and politics are unfortunately way more complicated than the headlines suggest.</p>
</details>

然而，从好的方面看，这项提案将使许多美国人有机会将一些东西传给他们的孩子——不幸的是，那东西将是一笔抵押贷款……
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">On the bright side however this proposal would allow many Americans the opportunity to pass something down to their kids – unfortunately that thing would be a mortgage…</p>
</details>

### 30年期固定利率抵押贷款的历史演变

30年期固定利率抵押贷款似乎是美国生活中一个永久的特征，但它诞生于危机之中。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The 30-year fixed-rate mortgage feels like a permanent feature of American life, but it was born out of crisis.</p>
</details>

本·卡斯尔曼（Ben Castleman）几年前在《纽约时报》上撰写了一篇关于美国抵押贷款市场的精彩历史文章。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Ben Castleman wrote an excellent history of the US mortgage market a few years ago in The New York Times.</p>
</details>

他解释说，在大萧条之前，房屋贷款由银行承保，而不是由美国政府担保。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">He explains that before the Great Depression - home loans were underwritten by banks – and not backed by the US government.</p>
</details>

它们通常期限为10年或更短（因为贷款机构不喜欢提供超长期贷款），而且与今天的抵押贷款不同，它们不是“**自我摊销**”（self-amortizing: 指贷款本金和利息在贷款期限内通过定期还款逐步偿清）的，这意味着借款人只需支付利息，并在贷款结束时支付一笔**一次性还清款**（balloon payment: 贷款期末一次性支付的较大金额款项）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They usually had terms of 10 years or less (as lenders don’t like making really long term loans) and, unlike mortgages today, they were not “self-amortizing” meaning that borrowers just made interest payments and the loan carried a balloon payment at the end.</p>
</details>

这意味着，如果借款人没有存够钱，他们通常不得不办理新的抵押贷款来偿还旧的贷款。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This meant that borrowers would often have to take out a new mortgage to pay off the old one if they hadn’t saved up.</p>
</details>

当金融系统在大萧条期间崩溃时，再融资枯竭，止赎（foreclosure）飙升，以至于在1930年代初期，近10%的美国房屋——我是说房屋，而不是抵押贷款——处于止赎状态。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When the financial system collapsed during the Great Depression, refinancing dried up and foreclosures soared – such that in the early 1930s, nearly 10 percent of U.S. homes – and I mean homes – not mortgages - were in foreclosure.</p>
</details>

为了稳定住房市场，联邦政府发行了政府担保债券，以便他们可以购买违约抵押贷款，并将其重新发行成具有更长到期日和更低月供的固定利率贷款。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">To stabilize the housing market, the federal government issued government-backed bonds so that they could buy up defaulted mortgages and reissue them as fixed-rate loans that had longer maturities and lower monthly payments.</p>
</details>

政府随后将这些抵押贷款出售给私人投资者，由**联邦住房管理局**（Federal Housing Administration: 提供抵押贷款保险以保护贷款机构免受损失的政府机构）提供抵押贷款保险，这样投资者就知道他们购买的贷款将全额偿还。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The government then sold off those mortgages to private investors, with the Federal Housing Administration providing mortgage insurance so those investors knew the loans they were buying would be paid off in full.</p>
</details>

**房利美**（Fannie Mae: 美国最大的住房抵押贷款二级市场机构）以及后来的**房地美**（Freddie Mac: 美国第二大住房抵押贷款二级市场机构）通过创建二级市场改变了该系统的规模。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Fannie Mae and, later, Freddie Mac changed the scale of the system by creating a secondary market.</p>
</details>

它们从贷款机构购买抵押贷款，将其打包成证券，然后出售给投资者。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They bought mortgages from lenders, bundled them into securities, and sold them to investors.</p>
</details>

这种流动性使得银行能够循环利用资本并提供更多贷款，将30年期抵押贷款从政府实验转变为大众市场产品。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That liquidity allowed banks to recycle capital and offer even more loans, turning the 30-year mortgage from a government experiment into a mass-market product.</p>
</details>

到1960年代，30年期抵押贷款已成为主导结构。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">By the 1960s, the 30-year mortgage had become the dominant structure.</p>
</details>

它的吸引力显而易见：可预测的还款、无提前还款罚金以及在利率下降时能够再融资。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Its appeal was obvious: predictable payments, no prepayment penalties, and the ability to refinance when rates fell.</p>
</details>

卡斯尔曼指出，对于美国房主来说，30年期固定利率贷款是一种单边押注。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Castleman points out that for US homeowners the 30 year fixed rate loan is a one-sided bet.</p>
</details>

如果通货膨胀大幅上升，贷款人会亏损，而借款人则会获利。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If inflation goes way up, the lenders lose and the borrowers win.</p>
</details>

而如果利率下降，借款人可以以更低的利率进行再融资。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While if interest rates fall, the borrower can just refinance at a lower rate.</p>
</details>

### 国际抵押贷款制度对比与立法障碍

其他国家则采取了不同的路径。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Other countries took different paths.</p>
</details>

在英国和加拿大，固定利率通常只持续几年，直到最近，加拿大的抵押贷款期限还是25年。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In Britain and Canada, fixed rates typically last just a few years – and until recently Canadian Mortgages were 25 year loans.</p>
</details>

德国提供长期固定利率贷款，但限制再融资。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Germany offers long-term fixed rate loans but restricts refinancing.</p>
</details>

丹麦的系统与美国相似，但要求更高的首付和更严格的承保。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Denmark’s system resembles America’s but demands higher down payments and stricter underwriting.</p>
</details>

美国因其长期限、固定利率和便捷的再融资组合而独树一帜——这种组合保护了现有房主，但在利率上升时（正如我们刚刚看到的），可能会冻结市场流动性。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The U.S. stands out for combining long duration, fixed rates, and easy refinancing—a mix that shields existing homeowners but can freeze mobility when interest rates rise – which is what we have just seen.</p>
</details>

从理论上讲，这听起来很简单——延长贷款期限并减少还款额。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">On paper, it sounds easy - stretch the term and shrink the payment.</p>
</details>

但在实践中，这将是一场立法和金融上的“扭曲表演”——它需要重写联邦法律。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In practice, it would be a legislative and financial contortion act - it would require rewriting federal law.</p>
</details>

根据**多德-弗兰克法案**（Dodd-Frank Act: 美国2010年通过的金融监管改革法案），**合格抵押贷款**（Qualified Mortgages: 符合特定标准，为贷款机构提供法律保护的抵押贷款）的期限不能超过30年，因此，50年期的产品需要新的立法，贷款机构才能大规模提供。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Under the Dodd-Frank Act, qualified mortgages can’t have terms longer than 30 years, so a 50-year product would need new legislation before lenders could offer it at scale.</p>
</details>

### 50年抵押贷款的经济学与政治考量

<details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">According to Politico and CBS, Bill Pulte pitched the idea directly to Trump, who posted it online without any vetting.</p>
</details>

一些助手和保守派盟友批评此举，称其为糟糕的政策和糟糕的政治。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Some aides and conservative allies criticized the move, calling it bad policy and bad politics.</p>
</details>

特朗普后来似乎与该提案保持了距离。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Trump later appeared to distance himself from the proposal.</p>
</details>

当在福克斯新闻（Fox News）被问及此事时，他告诉记者这“可能有点帮助”，但总体上他似乎对此不以为然。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When asked about it on Fox News, he told the reporter it “might help a little bit” but overall he seemed to brush it off.</p>
</details>

乍一看，50年期抵押贷款听起来像是削减每月贷款支付的好方法——有点像大萧条时期抵押贷款的重组方式——从10年期带一次性还清款变为30年期自我摊销。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At first glance - the 50-year mortgage sounds like a great way of cutting monthly loan payments – kinda like the way mortgages were restructured during the great depression – from ten years with a balloon payment to thirty years and self-amortizing.</p>
</details>

如果50年期抵押贷款的利率与30年期抵押贷款相同，那么美国平均抵押贷款的月供将每月减少约250美元。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If a fifty-year mortgage had the same interest rate as a thirty-year mortgage has, the monthly payment on the average American mortgage would fall by around $250 dollars a month.</p>
</details>

问题在于，50年期抵押贷款的利率不会与30年期抵押贷款相同。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The problem is - that rates on a fifty-year mortgage wouldn’t be the same as they are on a thirty-year mortgage.</p>
</details>

更长的期限意味着更大的风险，如果得不到补偿，贷款机构就不会承担这种风险。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
