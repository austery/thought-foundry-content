### 动态定价的隐忧：消费者面临的价格差异

今年秋季的一个早晨，几十名志愿者通过视频通话聚集在一起，进行了一项实验。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">One morning earlier this fall, a group of a few dozen volunteers gathered on a video call for an experiment.</p>
</details>

他们都登录了杂货购物应用**Instacart**（在线杂货配送平台），并同时选择在华盛顿特区一家特定的**Safeway**（美国连锁超市）购买相同的商品——鸡蛋。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They all logged on to the grocery shopping app Instacart and simultaneously selected the same item for pickup from the same store, eggs from a particular Safeway in DC.</p>
</details>

他们发现，系统向他们提供了显著不同的价格。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">What they found was that they were being offered significantly different prices.</p>
</details>

对一些购物者来说售价为3.99美元的鸡蛋，对另一些人来说却卖到4.79美元，同一件商品价格高出近20%。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The eggs that cost $3.99 for some shoppers were being sold to others for $4.79, nearly 20% more for the exact same product.</p>
</details>

还有几十名志愿者在全国各地的其他商店对其他商品进行了同样的实验，比如在圣保罗一家**Target**（美国大型零售连锁店）购买一盒**Cheerios**（麦片），或在西雅图购买**Wheat Thins**（薄脆饼干）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Dozens more volunteers did the same thing for other products at other stores scattered around the country, like a box of Cheerios at a Target in St. Paul or wheat thins in Seattle.</p>
</details>

他们也发现自己被提供了完全不同的价格。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They also discovered they were being offered completely different prices.</p>
</details>

**Consumer Reports**（消费者报告：美国消费者权益组织）和进步政策团体**Groundwork Collective**（进步政策团体）于今天上午公布的这些发现，凸显了所谓的**动态定价**（dynamic pricing: 实时调整商品价格的策略）如何在经济中蔓延，公司正利用复杂的算法实时调整价格。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The findings, which were published this morning by Consumer Reports and the Groundwork Collective, a progressive policy group, highlight how so-called dynamic pricing is spreading across the economy as companies use sophisticated algorithms to adjust prices in real time.</p>
</details>

这种策略多年来一直用于**Uber**（优步：网约车服务公司）或机票等领域，但《纽约时报》首席经济记者**Ben Castleman**表示，现在动态定价正日益成为从餐馆到零售等所有购物体验的一部分。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The strategy has been used for years for things like Uber or airline tickets, but Times chief economics correspondent Ben Castleman says now dynamic pricing is increasingly becoming a part of the shopping experience for everything from restaurants to retail.</p>
</details>

经济学家指出，公司一直以来都会为不同的客户调整价格，对吗？你可以剪优惠券获得折扣，可以去欢乐时光或早鸟特价。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Economists point out that companies have always adjusted prices for different customers, right? You can clip coupons to get a discount. You can go to a happy hour or an early bird special.</p>
</details>

这些方法大多是透明的，对吗？消费者知道他们在特定时期会得到更高的价格或更低的价格，他们可以据此进行计划。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Most of those methods are transparent, right? consumers know that they are getting a higher price or a lower price during certain periods and they can plan around that.</p>
</details>

我从消费者权益倡导者那里听到的担忧是，公司现在采用的这些方法在很多情况下透明度要低得多，消费者更难知道他们是否获得了最优惠的价格，甚至是否与坐在他们旁边的人获得了相同的价格。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The concern that I hear from consumer advocates is that these sorts of methods that are being adopted now by companies are in many cases much less transparent and it's more difficult for consumers to know whether they're getting the best price or even if they're getting the same price as somebody else who's sitting next to them.</p>
</details>

这引发了一个担忧，即我们正走向一个世界，在这个世界里，你的购物习惯、收入或居住地会导致你获得与他人不同的价格。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And it raises the concern that we're heading towards a world in which your shopping habits or your income or where you live leads you to get a different price from somebody else.</p>
</details>

也有一些证据表明，这实际上会导致整体价格更加波动，甚至更高。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There's also some evidence that this actually leads to more volatile and maybe even higher prices overall.</p>
</details>

如果公司都只是通过算法设定价格，那么最终这实际上可能导致价格上涨，而此时消费者已经对可负担性感到担忧，特别是对杂货等商品。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">that if companies are all just setting prices on algorithms that eventually that can actually lead to higher prices at a time when consumers are already worried about affordability, particularly for things like groceries.</p>
</details>

针对《纽约时报》的提问，Target发言人表示，他们不对Instacart上的价格负责，而Safeway拒绝置评。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In response to questions from the Times, a Target spokesman said it's not responsible for prices on Instacart and Safeway declined to comment.</p>
</details>

Instacart的一位女发言人表示，其部分合作商店确实会进行短期定价测试，以“了解对消费者最重要的是什么，以及如何保持必需品的价格可负担”。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A spokeswoman for Instacart said that some of its partner stores do short-term pricing tests to quote, "Learn what matters most to consumers and how to keep essential items affordable."</p>
</details>

包括**Amazon**（亚马逊）和**Home Depot**（家得宝）在内的公司此前曾因使用动态定价而受到批评，但最终在消费者强烈反对后撤回了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Companies, including Amazon and Home Depot, have previously been called out for using dynamic pricing, only to retreat after consumer backlash.</p>
</details>
