### 谷歌AI搜索的准确性挑战

**播报员**: 当你在谷歌上搜索某样东西时，顶部的那个由AI生成的回答已经成为常态。搜索引擎在2024年推出了这项功能。但问题持续存在：“它的准确性有多高？”最近，《纽约时报》让一家分析AI幻觉的初创公司对谷歌的回复进行了测试。它发现，今年秋天，结果的准确率为85%。在谷歌进行一次升级后，2月份再次进行测试，准确率上升至91%。大约十次中有九次正确，这可以得A。但考虑到谷歌每年处理超过5万亿次搜索，这意味着它每小时会给出数千万个错误答案。谷歌本身也承认，AI概览可能包含错误。它在页面上用细则写着：“请仔细核对回复。”但分析发现的另一件事是，谷歌的许多AI答案，即使是正确的，也是“无根据的”，意味着它们链接到的网站并不能完全支持该信息。这使用户难以自行核实准确性，如果他们无法追溯信息来源的话。现在，这里有一个关键点需要注意，高效检查大量AI概览答案（此次分析超过4000个）的最佳方法是也使用AI来完成，这正是该初创公司在此案例中所做的。这可能会引入它自身的错误，而谷歌的一位发言人称此次分析“存在缺陷”。欲了解报告的完整详情，包括AI概览如何被操纵，请访问 nytimes.com。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Announcer**: When you Google something, that little AI generated answer right at the top has become the norm. The search engine rolled out the feature in 2024. But the question continues to be, "How accurate is it?" Recently, The Times asked a startup that analyzes AI hallucinations to put Google's responses to the test. It found this fall the results were accurate 85% of the time. It tested again in February after Google did an upgrade and it had risen to 91%. Being right roughly nine out of 10 times can get you an A. But considering that Google processes more than 5 trillion searches a year, that means it provides tens of millions of wrong answers every hour. Google itself acknowledges that AI overviews can include errors. It says so right on the page in fine print. Doublech checkck responses. But another thing the analysis found is that many of Google's AI answers, even correct ones, were ungrounded, meaning they linked to websites that didn't completely support that information. That makes it challenging for users to check the accuracy themselves if they can't trace where the information came from. Now, a key thing to note here, the best way to efficiently check a large number of AI overview answers, more than 4,000 for this analysis, is to use AI for that, too, which the startup did in this case. That can introduce its own errors, and a spokesman for Google called the analysis flawed. For a full look at the report, including how AI overviews can be manipulated, go to ny times.com.
