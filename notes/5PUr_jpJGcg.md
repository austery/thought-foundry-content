### 1929年黑色星期二：大萧条的序幕

您知道吗，1929年股市在一天之内惊人地损失了12%的价值？欢迎来到**黑色星期二**（Black Tuesday: 1929年10月29日，美国股市崩盘日），这是1929年**股市崩盘**（Stock Market Crash: 股票市场价格急剧下跌的事件）的严峻高潮。这一天，经济受到了巨大冲击，并在美国民众心中留下了持久的创伤。

### 1987年黑色星期一：自动化交易的冲击

想象一下，一觉醒来发现股市刚刚暴跌了22%以上。这不是噩梦，这是1987年10月19日的**黑色星期一**（Black Monday: 1987年10月19日，全球股市暴跌日），是**道琼斯工业平均指数**（Dow Jones Industrial Average: 美国股市的重要指数）历史上最大单日百分比跌幅。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Imagine waking up to find that the stock market has just no dived by over 22%. That's not a nightmare. It's Black Monday, October 19th, 1987. the largest single day percentage drop in the history of the Dow Jones Industrial Average.</p>
</details>

这一事件就像一场慢动作的金融末日。但话说回来，谁不喜欢一点点混乱来为周一增添刺激呢？崩盘的规模令人瞠目结舌。想象一下，**程序化交易**（Program Trading: 基于计算机算法自动执行交易的策略）系统，这些本应是现代金融的奇迹，却集体崩溃了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This event was like a financial apocalypse in slow motion. But hey, who doesn't love a little chaos to spice up a Monday? The sheer scale of the crash was jaw-dropping. Picture this. Automated trading systems, the supposed marvels of modern finance, decided to collectively have a meltdown. Program trading, which was meant to be the future of trading, instead triggered a selling frenzy.</p>
</details>

市场就像一场多米诺骨牌游戏，每一块倒下的牌都加速了恐慌。投资者无助地看着他们的投资组合蒸发，只留下一串零和许多疑问。如果您想知道我们是如何防止这种情况再次发生的，那就引入了**熔断机制**（Circuit Breakers: 股市中当市场波动达到预设水平时暂停交易的机制）。

不，不是您吹风机烧保险丝时需要重置的那种，而是旨在暂时停止交易的机制，就像市场变得过于混乱时的一个金融“暂停”。这些机制是在1987年之后引入的，目的是在恐慌来临时冷却市场。可以把它们想象成在情况变得过于真实时的暂停按钮。

These were introduced post 1987 to cool down the market when panic sets in. Think of them as a pause button for when things get too real.</p>
</details>

黑色星期一的影响并未局限于美国，它就像一场金融疫情，在全球市场蔓延开来。从伦敦到东京，证券交易所都在崩盘的重压下关闭。这清楚地提醒我们，世界已经变得多么紧密相连。坏消息传播得很快，在1987年，它以数字信号的速度传播。

From London to Tokyo, stock exchanges shuttered under the weight of the crash. It was a stark reminder of how interconnected the world had become. Bad news travels fast. 就在您认为情况不会变得更糟的时候，请记住这只是即将发生的事情的预演。金融世界即将吸取一些惨痛的教训，但在此之前，另一场风暴——几乎冻结了整个全球金融体系的崩溃——即将到来。敬请关注，因为如果黑色星期一是开场戏，那么接下来发生的就是一场全面爆发的戏剧。

### 2008年雷曼兄弟破产：全球金融危机的震中

如果我告诉您，美国历史上最大的破产案几乎让全球金融体系崩溃，您会相信吗？这不是虚构，这是**雷曼兄弟**（Lehman Brothers: 一家美国投资银行，其在2008年的破产引发了全球金融危机）的故事，这家华尔街巨头在2008年9月15日轰然倒塌。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">What if I told you that the largest bankruptcy in US history nearly brought the global financial system to its knees? It's not fiction. It's the story of Lehman Brothers, a titan of Wall Street that crumbled spectacularly on September 15th, 2008.</p>
</details>

雷曼兄弟一夜之间6000亿美元的资产化为乌有，其倒闭是撼动金融世界核心的地震，点燃了2008年**金融危机**（Financial Crisis: 广泛的金融市场动荡和经济衰退）最严重的阶段。突然之间，世界看到了全球经济变得多么相互关联和脆弱。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">With $600 billion in assets wiped out overnight, Lehman's collapse was the earthquake that shook the financial world to its core, igniting the worst phase of the 2008 financial crisis. Suddenly, the world saw just how interconnected and vulnerable the global economy had become.</p>
</details>

随着雷曼兄弟的倒下，全球信贷市场的复杂网络陷入停滞，银行和企业像离开水的鱼一样，急切地需要现金。世界目瞪口呆地看着信任几乎在一夜之间蒸发，贷款停止，随之而来的是商业的命脉也停止了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As Lehman Brothers fell, the intricate web of global credit markets seized up, leaving banks and businesses gasping for cash like fish out of water. The world watched in disbelief as trust evaporated almost overnight, lending ground to a halt, and with it, the lifeblood of commerce.</p>
</details>

连锁反应迅速而残酷。大大小小的公司裁员数千人。曾经是安全和成功象征的房屋，随着止赎率飙升，成为绝望的中心。各地的家庭都面临着财务破产的严酷现实。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The ripple effects were swift and brutal. Companies large and small, slashed jobs by the thousands. Homes, once symbols of security and success, became the epicenters of despair as foreclosures skyrocketed. Families everywhere faced the harsh reality of financial ruin.</p>
</details>

后果的规模令人震惊。想想第二天，即2008年9月16日，**AIG**（American International Group: 美国国际集团，一家大型保险公司，在2008年金融危机中获得政府救助）的救助。美国政府不得不拿出850亿美元来拯救这家保险巨头，使其免于在**有毒资产**（Toxic Assets: 价值不确定且难以出售的资产，通常指次级抵押贷款相关证券）的重压下崩溃。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The sheer scale of the fallout was staggering. Think of the AIG bailout the very next day, September 16th, 2008. The US government had to fork over $85 billion to save the insurance giant from collapsing under the weight of toxic assets.</p>
</details>

这清楚地提醒我们，隐藏在阴影中的**系统性风险**（Systemic Risk: 某个金融机构或市场崩溃可能引发整个金融系统崩溃的风险）。这是对“**大而不倒**”（Too Big to Fail: 指某些大型金融机构规模过大，其倒闭会对整个经济造成灾难性影响，因此政府不得不进行救助）理念的冰冷证明。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It was a glaring reminder of the systemic risks lurking in the shadows. A chilling testament to the idea of too big to fail.</p>
</details>

雷曼兄弟的倒闭不仅仅是一场金融灾难，更是一场人类悲剧。数百万人失去了工作、房屋和储蓄。经济格局伤痕累累，留下了深刻而痛苦的印记，需要数年才能愈合。失业率在2009年10月飙升至10%的峰值，而正式始于2007年12月的经济衰退持续拖延，留下了破坏的痕迹。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Lehman's downfall was not just a financial debacle. It was a human tragedy. Millions lost jobs, homes, and savings. The economic landscape was scarred, leaving deep, painful grooves that would take years to heal. Unemployment soared to a peak of 10% by October 2009, and the recession, officially beginning in December 2007, dragged on, leaving a trail of devastation in its wake.</p>
</details>

但关键在于，雷曼兄弟的倒闭只是开场戏。这场金融地震为进一步的戏剧奠定了基础，导致美国政府陷入背水一战的境地，需要果断干预以防止全面的系统性崩溃。接下来发生的事情将挑战经济政策的根基，并重塑金融监管的格局。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But here's the kicker. Leman's collapse was just the opening act. This financial earthquake set the stage for further drama, leading to a moment when the US government found itself in a back against the wall situation, needing to intervene decisively to prevent a total systemic collapse. What unfolded next would challenge the very foundations of economic policy and reshape the landscape of financial regulation.</p>
</details>

### AIG救助：大而不倒的困境

美国政府曾斥资850亿美元拯救一家公司。您能猜到是哪一家吗？不，它不是科技巨头，也不是大型零售商，它是**AIG**（American International Group: 美国国际集团），这家保险巨头突然被认定为“大而不倒”。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The US government once shelled out $85 billion to save a single company. Can you guess which one? No, it wasn't a tech giant or a mega retailer. It was AIG, the insurance behemoth that was suddenly deemed too big to fail.</p>
</details>

2008年9月16日，就在雷曼兄弟倒闭的第二天，AIG摇摇欲坠，濒临崩溃，威胁要将全球金融体系一同拖垮。但政府为什么要介入，又是什么让AIG如此关键呢？AIG不仅仅是另一家保险公司，它是复杂金融合同网络中的关键环节，涉及**信用违约互换**（Credit Default Swaps: 一种金融衍生品，为债券违约提供保险）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">On September 16th, 2008, just a day after Lehman Brothers went belly up, AIG teetered on the brink of collapse, threatening to take the global financial system down with it. But why did the government step in and what made AIG so critical? AIG was not just another insurance company. It was the lynch pin in a complex web of financial contracts involving credit default swaps which essentially act like insurance policies for investments.</p>
</details>

这些互换与**次级抵押贷款**（Subprime Mortgages: 信用记录较差的借款人获得的抵押贷款，风险较高）等有毒资产挂钩，而这些资产已经变质。随着这些资产的崩溃，AIG的担保变成了负债，威胁到全球金融的根基。恐慌情绪显而易见，“大而不倒”一词被贴在每一个金融头条上。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These swaps were tied to toxic assets, subprime mortgages that had turned sour. As these assets crumbled, AIG's guarantees became liabilities, threatening the very fabric of global finance. The panic was palpable and the term too big to fail was plastered across every financial headline.</p>
</details>

这清楚地提醒我们，相互关联的金融巨头所带来的系统性风险。这次救助对许多人来说是一剂苦药，在纳税人中激起了愤怒和不信任，他们本质上是在为企业的鲁莽行为买单。这感觉就像是在奖励不良行为，公众的看法也因此恶化。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A stark reminder of the systemic risk posed by interconnected financial giants. The bailout was a bitter pill for many, stirring up anger and disbelief among taxpayers who were essentially footing the bill for corporate recklessness. It felt like rewarding bad behavior and public perception soured.</p>
</details>

骚动不仅仅是因为巨额资金，尽管850亿美元不容小觑。它关乎对本应稳定可靠的机构的信任，或者说缺乏信任。这次干预引发了一系列旨在防止未来灾难的政策变化。例如，**多德-弗兰克法案**（Dodd-Frank Act: 美国在2010年通过的金融改革法案，旨在提高金融透明度并减少系统性风险）旨在提高透明度并降低系统性风险。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The uproar wasn't just about the staggering amount of money, though $85 billion is nothing to sneeze at. It was about trust or the lack thereof in institutions that were supposed to be stable and reliable. This intervention sparked a wave of policy changes aimed at preventing future fiascos. The DoddFrank Act, for instance, sought to increase transparency and reduce systemic risks.</p>
</details>

但这次救助也留下了一个悬而未决的问题：如果AIG“大而不倒”，那么其他金融巨头呢？**道德风险**（Moral Hazard: 指当一方在交易中受到保护，从而可能采取更冒险行为的倾向）是真实存在的，全世界都在密切关注，想知道这个安全网是否会鼓励未来更冒险的行为。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But the bailout also left a lingering question. If AIG was too big to fail, what about other financial giants? The moral hazard was real, and the world watched closely, wondering whether this safety net would encourage even riskier behavior down the line.</p>
</details>

当我们剖析这些事件时，很难不感到沮丧和敬畏，因为所做出的决策是如此重大。AIG救助是一个分水岭时刻，它迫使政策制定者和公众都必须正视金融体系中风险与回报之间错综复杂的博弈。然而，当紧急支票上的墨迹未干时，另一场风暴正在酝酿。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As we dissect these events, it's hard not to feel a mix of frustration and awe at the magnitude of the decisions made. The AIG bailout was a watershed moment, one that forced policymakers and the public alike to reckon with the intricate dance of risk and reward in our financial system. And yet, as the ink dried on those emergency checks, another storm was brewing.</p>
