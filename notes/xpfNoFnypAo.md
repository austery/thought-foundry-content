### AI对SaaS商业模式的冲击

微软历来都是最伟大的软件公司。过去，你们经历了从销售**Windows**（微软公司开发的操作系统）许可证和Windows或微软光盘，到现在销售**Microsoft 365**（微软提供的订阅服务，包含Office应用、云存储等）订阅服务的转型。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Microsoft historically has been the greatest software company. You've gone through a transition in the past where you used to sell Windows licenses and disks of Windows or Microsoft, and now you sell subscriptions to 365.</p>
</details>

**软件即服务**（Software as a Service, SaaS: 一种通过网络提供软件的模式），虽然其每用户的增量成本极低，但SaaS公司在市场上表现却大幅不佳，因为**AI**的**销货成本**（COGS: Cost of Goods Sold，指生产或提供服务直接相关的成本）非常高，这彻底打破了这些商业模式的运作方式。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Software as a service, with incredibly low incremental cost per user, has seen its companies underperform massively in the markets because the COGS of AI is just so high, and that completely breaks how these business models work.</p>
</details>

作为可能最伟大的软件公司，你们如何转型以适应这个销货成本变得至关重要的新时代？
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">How do you, as perhaps the greatest software company, transition to this new age where COGS matters a lot?</p>
</details>

### 微软的转型策略：消费型订阅

我认为这是一个很好的问题，因为从某种意义上说，商业模式本身的杠杆将保持相似。人们喜欢订阅，因为他们可以为此做预算；它们本质上是对封装在订阅中的某些消费权利的授权。从某种意义上说，这变成了一个定价决策：你被授权消费多少？如果你看看所有的编码订阅服务，它们大致就是这样，对吧？它们通常有专业版、标准版等等。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I think that this is a great question because, in some sense, the levers of the business models themselves are going to remain similar. People like subscriptions because they can budget for them; they are essentially entitlements to some consumption rights that come encapsulated in a subscription. In some sense, it becomes a pricing decision: how much consumption are you entitled to? If you look at all the coding subscriptions, that's kind of what they are, right? And they kind of have the pro tier, the standard tier, and what have you.</p>
</details>

我认为这就是定价和利润结构将如何分层。有趣的是，对于微软而言，好消息是我们几乎在所有这些衡量标准上都涉足了这项业务。事实上，在产品组合层面，我们几乎对所有其他消费杠杆都提供了消费型订阅。我认为，时间会证明这些模式中哪些在哪些类别中是合理的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I think that's how the pricing and the margin structures will get tiered. The interesting thing is, at Microsoft, the good news for us is we are in that business across all those meters. And in fact, at a portfolio level, we pretty much have consumption subscriptions to all of the other consumer levers as well. Time will tell which of these models make sense in what categories.</p>
</details>
