### 中国“山寨”模因股的崛起

2019年，CNBC对全球一些大型公司进行了一项调查，结果发现，仅在过去一年中，就有五分之一的北美公司声称其**知识产权**（Intellectual Property: 智力劳动成果的专有权利）被中国竞争对手窃取。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In 2019, CNBC surveyed some of the largest companies in the world and found that one in five North American companies were claiming that Chinese competitors had stolen intellectual property from them within the last year alone.</p>
</details>

这是一个相当令人震惊的统计数据。但今年夏天我们得知，被窃取的不仅仅是企业知识产权。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That's a fairly shocking statistic. But we learned this summer that it's not just corporate IP that's being stolen.</p>
</details>

今年夏天，投资者在投资了一批在美国上市的中国股票后损失了数十亿美元，这些股票在社交媒体上被推广后不久就大幅贬值。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Investors lost billions of dollars this summer after investing in a group of US-listed Chinese stocks that collapsed in value shortly after being promoted on social media.</p>
</details>

看来，中国人正在“山寨”美国最伟大的创新之一：**模因股**（Meme Stock: 因社交媒体热潮而非基本面被推高的股票）。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Chinese, it seems, are ripping off one of America's greatest innovations, the meme stock.</p>
</details>

没有什么比一个好的模因股故事更让我喜欢的了，我想我们都知道模因股是由美国人发明的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now, there's nothing I love more than a good meme stock story. And I think we all know that meme stocks were invented in America by Americans.</p>
</details>

虽然模因股热潮在2021年随着GameStop和AMC的兴起而爆发，但彭博社早在2020年就已宣布该年为模因股之年，并重点提及了Herz、Kodak和电动汽车公司。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While the memetock craze exploded in 2021 with GameStop and AMC, Bloomberg had already declared 2020 the year of the meme stock, highlighting Herz, Kodak, and EV companies.</p>
</details>

不幸的是，2022年对于模因股投资者来说是艰难的一年。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

模因股**ETF**（Exchange Traded Fund: 交易所交易基金），一个真实存在的投资产品，在2022年暴跌，从每股70美元跌至25美元。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The memeto ETF, which is a real thing that you can meme invest in, collapsed in 2022, falling from $70 a share to 25.</p>
</details>

2024年，随着GameStop和其他几只模因股卷土重来，市场出现了一点复苏。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There was a bit of a resurgence in 2024 as GameStop and a few other meme stocks made a comeback.</p>

但今年夏天，又一批老式的美国模因股兴起，被《投资者商业日报》戏称为**“呆子股”**（Dork Stocks: 投资者商业日报对某些美国模因股的戏称）。

### 监管机构的警告与应对

美国联邦调查局（**FBI**）上个月表示，他们收到的关于**“拉高出货”**（Pump and Dump: 一种通过虚假宣传推高股价后抛售获利的欺诈行为）股票欺诈的受害者投诉同比增长了300%。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The FBI said last month that it had seen a 300% year-on-year increase in victim complaints, referencing ramp and dump stock fraud.</p>
</details>

该机构补充说，投资者在社交媒体上成为目标，欺诈者冒充合法的经纪公司或知名股票分析师。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It added that investors were being targeted on social media by people impersonating legitimate brokerage firms or well-known stock analysts.</p>
</details>

投诉数量如此大幅增加，你可能会希望FBI能逮捕一些罪犯。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now, this is a huge increase in complaints. So, you would hope that the FBI is arresting some criminals, but both the FBI and the Justice Department have been overworked and under a lot of stress this summer with over a thousand agents working 24-hour shifts removing politicians names from the Epstein files, meaning that there's no time left to investigate investor fraud.</p>
</details>

然而，今年夏天FBI和司法部都因处理爱泼斯坦（Epstein）文件中的政客姓名而工作过度、压力巨大，超过一千名特工24小时轮班工作，这意味着他们没有时间调查投资者欺诈。

</details>

据《华尔街日报》报道，这些中国股票上市的交易所——纳斯达克——本周宣布，正寻求收紧对小型中国股票的上市规则，因为它表示这些上市已成为欺诈和操纵的温床。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">According to the Wall Street Journal, NASDAQ, the exchange on which these Chinese stocks were all listed, announced this week that it's seeking to tighten its rules for small Chinese stocks, as it says that these listings have become a hotbed for fraud and manipulation, which you would have hoped would have all moved to crypto, where it's legal.</p>
</details>

纳斯达克提出了一项新规则，要求主要在中国运营的公司现在必须在**IPO**（Initial Public Offering: 首次公开募股）中至少筹集2500万美元才能在纳斯达克上市。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">NASDAQ proposed a new rule requiring that companies principally operating in China will now have to raise at least $25 million in an IPO in order to go public on NASDAQ.</p>
</details>

他们还寻求将未来上市的最低流通股数量增加到1500万美元。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They're also seeking to increase the minimum float for future listings to $15 million.</p>
</details>

他们表示，对于不再符合上市标准的公司，应该有更快的退市机制。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And they're saying that there should be faster D-listings for companies that no longer meet listing standards.</p>
</details>

这些修改在实施前需要获得**SEC**（Securities and Exchange Commission: 美国证券交易委员会）的批准。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The changes will require approval from the SEC before they can be implemented.</p>
</details>

考虑到欺诈投诉增加了300%，我想SEC会非常忙碌，特别是因为路透社报道称，该机构的关键部门在今年早些时候流失了15%到19%的全职员工。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">With this 300% increase in fraud complaints, I imagine that the SEC are quite busy, especially because Reuters reports that key divisions of the agency lost between 15 and 19% of their full-time headcount earlier this year.</p>
</details>

据纳斯达克称，今年和去年申请在美国交易所上市的中国公司数量创历史新高，其中中国和香港的公司主导了美国**微盘股**（Micro Cap: 市值非常小的公司股票）的IPO市场。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">According to NASDAQ, a record number of Chinese companies applied to list on US exchanges both this year and last, with China and Hong Kong-based companies dominating the US micro cap IPO market.</p>
</details>

美国审计监管机构**PCAOB**（Public Company Accounting Oversight Board: 美国公众公司会计监督委员会）在今年早些时候曾面临被关闭的威胁，过去在检查许多审计美国上市公司的中国会计师事务所时遇到了困难。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The PCAOB, the US audit regulator, which was threatened with being shuttered earlier this year, has faced difficulties in the past inspecting many of the Chinese accounting firms that audit USlisted companies.</p>
</details>

在这种情况下，如果公司未能提供符合要求的审计师出具的审计报告，PCAOB可以启动导致公司从美国证券交易所退市的行动。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In such cases, the PCAOB can initiate actions that lead to a company's delisting from US stock exchanges if it doesn't provide an audit from a compliant auditor.</p>
</details>
