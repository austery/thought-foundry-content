### 孕育之美：从无到有的生命力

对 Jermaine Dupri 而言，**音乐制作**（Music Production: 统筹音频录制、编曲与创作的核心过程）绝非枯燥的工作，而更像是一场“孕育”。在录音室里，大多数人可能还在面面相觑、寻找灵感，但当一个念头闪现，让它从零变成具体形态的过程便是最有魅力的时刻。这个过程可能耗时五分钟，也可能磨人地持续两天。这种看着创意“从底层生长（growing from the bottom up）”并最终交付给听众去消化的过程，是他热爱制作的根本动力。

<details>
<summary>Original English Source</summary>
What do I love most about producing? It's almost like having a baby for me. Like, you go in the studio, and it's not an idea that, you know, most people will be looking around the studio trying to figure out what we're going to do. And then we get an idea, and then we start making that idea come to life. And depending on how long it takes, you know, sometimes it takes five minutes, sometimes it takes two days to get this, but it’s still like making a baby. Yeah, so, that's what it feels like, though. It's like, that's how I feel when I make music. I watch it go from, you know, nothing to something, and then I give it to you guys, and then y'all have to digest it. So it's a trying process. But that's the one thing that I love about watching, you know, watching it grow from the bottom up.
</details>

### 亚特兰大之子：城市崛起与个人成就的共振

作为来自亚特兰大南区（College Park）的音乐人，Dupri 的成功与这座城市的崛起紧密相连。在 90 年代初期，亚特兰大的音乐场景尚处于“婴儿期”，虽然已有 S.O.S. Band 和 Peabo Bryson 等前辈奠基，但整体仍在建设中。他之所以自诩为亚特兰大的“市长”，是因为他与城市共同成长。他创作著名的 **《欢迎来到亚特兰大》**（Welcome to Atlanta），初衷是出于一种不甘——他发现当时的时尚文化中，衣服上印的是伦敦、东京或洛杉矶，却唯独没有亚特兰大。这种对家乡身份认同的渴望，驱动他不断推出艺术家和音乐，向世界展示这座城市的魅力。

<details>
<summary>Original English Source</summary>
How has Atlanta played a role in my success? Well, I'm from College Park... I feel like Atlanta, when I first started making music with Kris Kross and even earlier in '91, '92, the whole city was building at this time. The music scene prior to me was here, we had like, S.O.S. Band, Brick, Peabo Bryson... So we had a cool music scene here, but it still was, you know, in the birth. And I just feel like my success came with the growth of this city. That's why I feel like I'm the mayor. I am the mayor, but, yeah... And that's why I created "Welcome to Atlanta," because I felt like, I saw people taking other cities more serious than this city. They used to have this shirt... they would have Detroit, LA, Tokyo and London... I’m like, damn, why they don’t put Atlanta on these shirts? And it felt like the city wasn't doing what it needed to do. So I just kept pushing, working, trying to create artists and create music and bring more and more people to the city and show how beautiful this city is.
</details>

### 爆款公式：如“烘焙蛋糕”般的复利逻辑

关于创作爆款单曲，Dupri 有一套独特的**追溯法**。他认为，当你获得一首热门曲目后，必须复盘并锁定那些让听众产生强烈共鸣的“核心元素”。他将制作比作“烘焙蛋糕”：无论你想加什么糖霜、做成柠檬味还是红丝绒味，底层的烘焙逻辑是不变的。他以去年的热单 **《Made for Me》** 为例：当歌手 Muni Long 提出需要更多“弹性（bounce）”时，他从 TikTok 上的一个混音视频（将 Mariah Carey 的《We Belong Together》节奏与新生代歌手 Yeat 的人声结合）中获得了启发。他意识到年轻人依然热爱他多年前为 Mariah 创作的那个节奏基因（Hi-hats 的排列方式），于是将同样的“节奏架构”植入新歌，使其成功登顶。

<details>
<summary>Original English Source</summary>
What's my formula for making songs? ...Once you get a hit, you have to follow whatever it was that you did to get to that hit. So, like, the first time that I did "Jump," ...I started tracing back what I did to get to that song... It's almost like baking a cake. Every time you bake the cake, you do basically the same things. Whether you put different icing on the cake, whether it's a lemon cake or it's a red velvet cake. Whatever it is, you go into baking that cake the same... I'm going to give you an example. I had a little song that came out last year called "Made for Me" by Muni Long... I was watching TikTok... and I saw that they did a mash-up of Mariah Carey and this group, this artist named Yeat, and they put Yeat over top of "We Belong Together." And these kids was dancing to the beat... I said, OK, I'm going to take that same thing (the hi-hats) and I'm going to put it in "Made for Me." And sure enough, it became a number-one record. It's just like making a cake. If you see it happen one time, it can happen again.
</details>

### “酷”的定义：尝试他人未竟之事

Dupri 坦言自己其实并不知道什么是“酷”，他只是敢于尝试。在 Kris Kross 时期，他突发奇想让成员把 **Girbaud 连体裤**反过来穿。尽管最初遭到了质疑，但当他们穿着反穿的衣服走过 Lenox Square 商场时，全场震惊的目光证明了这种视觉冲击力的成功。他的逻辑很简单：**尝试那些别人没有在做的事情**，这往往就会成为引领潮流的“最酷的事”。

<details>
<summary>Original English Source</summary>
How do I know what's cool? ...I don't. I'm going to say that I don't. I don't know what's cool, I just try things. So if you go back to, like, Kris Kross, one day... I said, "You should put it (the jumper) on backwards." And, you know, he looked at me like, "What's wrong with you?" ...And I was like, "Just do it. Why don't you do it, why not?" ...And when we walked through Lenox Square, I kid you not, the whole mall seemed like they saw something that they'd never seen before... what I learned in that process was that it was the coolest thing that they had ever seen in their life. So to answer your question, I don't know, but I'll try something that I don't believe somebody else is doing. And it normally turns out to be the coolest thing, you know?
</details>

### 精神灯塔：远距离的非正式师承

在职业生涯的塑造上，Dupri 的父亲是他现实中的引路人，但在商业逻辑和艺术造诣上，他更多是通过“远距离观察”自学成才。他将 Teddy Riley、Quincy Jones、Berry Gordy 和 Russell Simmons 等传奇音乐大亨视为自己的**精神导师**（Mentors: 虽然未曾直接对话，但在远方通过其作品和决策进行学习的对象）。最后，他强调了**愿景预测**（Forecasting）的力量——正如他曾在 YouTube 上看到 TED 演讲并告诉自己“我也要站在那里”一样，这种对未来的预见并付诸行动的特质，贯穿了他的整个音乐生涯。

<details>
<summary>Original English Source</summary>
Who mentored me? Now I was mentored, I'm sure, by my father... not really mentored for the music business, just more or less showing me around and showing me how to be. And then I started watching other people from afar, like Teddy Riley, Quincy Jones, Berry Gordy, Andre Harrell, Russell Simmons... they basically are the ones that mentored me without me actually talking to them. I am a person that usually forecasts things that I want to see happen in my life. And I saw this on YouTube one day, people up on stage, talking at TED talk, and I was like, "I'm going to do that." And I'm here.
</details>
