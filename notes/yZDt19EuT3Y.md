### 英国主权AI基金：阻止人才流失与重塑国家战略

英国科技大臣**利兹·肯德尔**（Liz Kendall）在伦敦一家自动驾驶汽车初创公司宣布启动了一项前所未有的政府AI计划——**主权AI部门**（Sovereign AI Unit）。这项由国家支持的风险投资基金规模达5亿英镑（约合6.75亿美元），其核心目标是阻止英国最优秀的AI公司重蹈覆辙：在英国起步、发展壮大后，最终被**谷歌**（Google）收购或迁往**加州**（California）。这一举措的时机并非偶然，就在同一周，**OpenAI**刚刚暂停了其在英国的数据中心项目。英国曾眼睁睁看着其AI领域的瑰宝**DeepMind**在2014年以5亿美元的价格被谷歌收购，此后人才持续流失至**硅谷**（Silicon Valley）。这项基金正是英国政府“不再重蹈覆辙”的宣言。

与传统的科研拨款或学术资助不同，主权AI部门的运作模式更像一家真正的**风险投资**（Venture Capital: 对初创企业进行股权投资以换取高回报的投资形式）公司。它直接持有股权，决策迅速，并能为每家初创公司提供高达2000万英镑的投资。更重要的是，它还提供了传统风投无法比拟的独特优势：在英国国家超级计算机网络上提供高达100万**GPU小时**（GPU Hours: 图形处理器计算时间的计量单位）的计算资源、一个工作日内完成签证审批，以及政府在数据获取、采购和监管快速通道方面的直接支持。该基金由**Balderton Capital**（一家管理着50亿英镑资产，投资过**Revolut**和**Wavy**的风投基金）的合伙人**詹姆斯·怀斯**（James Wise）担任主席，日常运营则由曾在谷歌工作八年、后在硅谷**Y Combinator**任职的**约瑟芬·坎特**（Josephine Kant）负责。这表明基金的领导团队并非传统公务员，而是深谙初创公司运作之道的人士。

UK technology secretary, Liz Kendall, stood at a self-driving car startup in London and launched something that doesn't quite look like any government AI program that's come before. It's called a sovereign AI unit, a 500 million pound, which is about 675 million US dollars, state-backed venture fund designed specifically to stop the UK's best AI companies from doing what they always do, start in Britain, get very good, then sell to Google or relocate to California. The timing wasn't subtle, either. OpenAI had just paused plans for its UK data center project the same week. Britain watched DeepMind, its crown jewel AI lab, get acquired by Google back in 2014 for 500 million dollars. It's watched talent bleed to Silicon Valley ever since. This fund is the government essentially saying, "Not again." I'm L, I have a PhD in computer science, and I analyze AI developments to understand what's actually happening beneath all of the hype. Here's what makes this different. This is not a research grant program. It is not academic funding. The sovereign AI unit operates like a proper venture capital firm. It takes equity stakes, moves fast, and writes checks up to 20 million pounds per startup. But it comes with something venture capital cannot offer, up to 1 million GPU hours on the UK's national supercomputer network, visa decisions within one working day, and direct government help with data access, procurement, and regulatory fast-tracking. The fund is chaired by James Wise, a partner at Balderton Capital, a 5 billion pound venture capital fund behind Revolut and Wavy, and run day-to-day by Josephine Kant, who spent 8 years at Google and then worked at Y Combinator in Silicon Valley before taking this role. So, they're not civil servants, they're people who know how startups actually work.

### 利基战略：聚焦AI基础设施与专业应用

该基金的战略并非试图打造一个“英国版**ChatGPT**”，因为英国此前与硅谷正面竞争的尝试以失败告终。相反，该基金正瞄准坎特所称的“镐和铲”**利基市场**（Niche Market: 指市场中具有特殊需求或偏好的细分市场），即英国拥有真正研究深度的基础设施、工具和专业应用领域。这包括在**受监管行业**（regulated industries）中工作的AI智能体、基于英国生物医学数据训练的**药物发现模型**（drug discovery models），以及针对**异构计算**（Heterogeneous Computing: 结合不同类型处理器（如CPU和GPU）以优化性能的计算架构）的硬件优化。

首家获得股权投资的公司是伦敦初创企业**Colossus**，该公司正在开发系统级软件，旨在使不同的芯片架构协同工作，实时优化模型和芯片。随着AI推理成为主导成本，且计算格局不再局限于**英伟达GPU**（Nvidia GPUs），这种**编排层**（orchestration layer）软件正成为关键基础设施。此外，还有六家公司获得了超级计算机网络的使用权：**Premamentum**（用于阿尔茨海默症和帕金森病研究的生物基础模型）、**Cosine**（用于国防和国家安全的世界模拟和编码智能体）、**Cursive**（由前DeepMind研究人员资助的智能体AI基础设施）、**Double Word**（主权推理基础设施）、**Twig Bio**（用于生物制造的AI驱动菌株设计），以及**Odyssey**（由在英国和加州均有业务的英国AI研究人员创立的自主系统世界模型）。目前，约30多家公司正在与该基金进行洽谈，仅计算资源一项就价值数千万英镑。

The strategy isn't quite to build a British ChatGPT. Britain tried competing head-on with Silicon Valley and lost. Instead, the fund is targeting what Kah calls "pick and shovel" niches, the infrastructure, tooling, and specialized applications where the UK has genuine research depth. Think AI agents that work in regulated industries, basically. Drug discovery models trained on UK biomedical data, hardware optimization for heterogeneous computing. The first company to get equity funding was Colossus, a London startup building systems-level software that makes different chip architectures work together, optimizing models and silicon in real time. As AI inference becomes the dominant cost and the compute landscape fragments beyond just Nvidia GPUs, this kind of orchestration layer becomes critical infrastructure. Six more companies got access to the supercomputer network, Premamentum, biological foundation models for Alzheimer's and Parkinson's research, Cosine, world simulation and coding agents for defense and national security, Cursive, agentic AI infrastructure funded by ex-DeepMind researchers, Double Word, sovereign inference infrastructure, Twig Bio, AI-driven strain design for biomanufacturing, and Odyssey, world models for autonomous systems founded by British AI researchers with operations in both the UK and California. Around 30 more companies are in discussions as well. The compute access alone is worth tens of millions of pounds to these startups.

### 主权考量：地缘政治背景下的战略防御

然而，我们必须正视规模问题。法国承诺投入1090亿欧元（约1120亿美元）用于AI投资，美国通过《芯片法案》部署了520亿美元，**微软**（Microsoft）仅在与OpenAI的合作上就花费了超过130亿美元。**亚马逊**（Amazon）、**Meta**、谷歌和微软预计仅今年就将合计投资6500亿美元用于AI基础设施。相比之下，英国的5亿英镑显得微不足道。英国并非试图在资金上超越硅谷，也无法做到。它试图打破的是英国人才创造出卓越成果后，因遭遇计算资源或资金瓶**颈**（Bottleneck: 指系统或流程中限制整体性能或吞吐量的环节）而被迫被收购或迁离的循环。该基金本质上是对这种“噩梦”的保险，它不是通过匹配美国的资本，而是通过消除迫使英国公司离开的特定瓶颈。

围绕这一举措的措辞明确强调了**主权**（Sovereignty: 国家独立自主处理内外事务的最高权力）。肯德尔在启动基金时表示，其目的是确保“专业知识、决策权和经济价值留在英国，而不是流向少数外国科技巨头。”这实际上是外交辞令，意指“当地缘政治紧张局势发生变化时，我们不希望完全依赖美国的AI基础设施。”欧洲目睹了俄罗斯入侵乌克兰，暴露了对外国能源和技术的危险依赖。AI无疑是新的战略脆弱点。如果OpenAI或谷歌明天决定限制英国用户对**API**（Application Programming Interface: 应用程序编程接口，允许不同软件系统相互通信）的访问，无论是出于监管、地缘政治还是商业原因，关键基础设施都可能停摆。主权AI基金正是对这种情景的对冲。

这种模式并非没有先例。**以色列**（Israel）通过有针对性的政府支持和军事研发溢出效应，建立了一个全球领先的**网络安全**（Cybersecurity: 保护计算机系统和网络免受信息泄露、盗窃或损坏的技术和过程）产业，尽管其经济规模远小于英国。问题在于，AI凭借其庞大的计算需求和“赢者通吃”的动态，能否遵循类似的路径，或者说，规模是否仅仅是保持相关性的先决条件。

Now, let's be honest about the scale problem. France just committed 109 billion euros to AI investment. That's about 112 billion US dollars. The US deployed 52 billion dollars through the CHIPS Act. Microsoft spent over 13 billion dollars on its OpenAI partnership alone. Amazon, Meta, Google, and Microsoft are expected to invest a combined 650 billion dollars in AI infrastructure just this year. Against that, 500 million pounds looks like a rounding error. The UK isn't trying to outspend Silicon Valley, it can't. What it's trying to do is interrupt the cycle where British talent builds something brilliant, hits a computer capital wall, and immediately gets acquired or relocates. The fund is essentially insurance against that nightmare, not by matching American capital, but by removing the specific bottlenecks that force UK companies to leave. The language around this is explicitly about sovereignty. When Kendall launched the fund, she framed it as making sure, I'm quoting, "Expertise, decision-making, and economic value stay in the UK rather than flowing to a small number of foreign tech giants." That is essentially diplomatic code for, "We don't want to be entirely dependent on American AI infrastructure when geopolitical tensions shift." Europe watched Russia's invasion of Ukraine expose dangerous dependencies on foreign energy and technology. AI is definitely the new strategic vulnerability. If OpenAI or Google decided tomorrow to restrict API access to UK users, whether for regulatory, geopolitical, or commercial reasons, critical infrastructure could grind to a halt. The sovereign AI fund is a hedge against that scenario. There's precedent for this working, too. Israel built a globally significantly cybersecurity industry through targeted government support and military R&D spillovers, despite having a far smaller economy than the UK. The question is whether AI, with its massive computer requirements and winner-take-all dynamics, can follow a similar path, or whether scale is simply a prerequisite for relevance.

### 挑战与未来展望：工业政策的边界

**坎特韦尔**（Cantwell）告诉记者，英国是一个小国，需要专注于少数真正擅长的领域并做到极致。这正是其**利基战略**（Niche Strategy: 专注于特定细分市场或专业领域以获得竞争优势的策略）。不要试图面面俱到，而是构建英国研究深度能带来真正优势的特定部分。该基金的模式本身也非同寻常。政府的AI资金通常流向大学和国家实验室，产生论文，但很少产生商业产品。通过瞄准**风险投资支持**（venture-backable）的公司并以风投的速度运作，英国正试图在极具价值的学术研究和能产生经济效益的市场就绪解决方案之间找到平衡点。基金启动时，他们甚至开玩笑说，从首次会面到宣布交易仅用了4天，比歌手**克雷格·大卫**（Craig David）的速度还快。这对于政府采购来说通常是闻所未闻的，但它必须如此。官僚延误的每一天，都意味着对**Anthropic API**和**谷歌云AI服务**的依赖进一步加深。

然而，令人不安的是，这可能还远远不够。首批七家获得支持的初创公司中，有六家位于**伦敦**（London），这引发了人们对该基金究竟是在建设国家整体能力，还是仅仅在补贴一个城市的科技生态系统的疑问。英国企业利润超过25万英镑的公司，其企业税率为25%，而加州的州企业所得税为8.84%。研究显示，自2023年末以来，已有6000名公司董事改变了居住国，在工党2024年10月预算增加**资本利得税**（Capital Gains Tax: 对出售资产（如股票、房地产）所获利润征收的税）并减少商业资产减免后，人才外流速度加快。仅仅向初创公司投入资金，并不能消除促使创始人出国的结构性激励。

那么，接下来会发生什么？如果该基金能够证明英国的AI公司可以在不迁离的情况下在国内扩张，并且**主权计算**（Sovereign Compute: 国家自主拥有和控制的计算基础设施）、快速签证和定向资本能够弥补税收和资本劣势，那么整个模式将得到验证。其他欧洲国家无疑正在密切关注。如果它成功了，法国、德国和其他国家很可能会效仿。如果失败了，如果最优秀的公司在达到规模后仍然出售给谷歌或迁往**旧金山**（San Francisco），那么5亿英镑将成为一个非常昂贵的教训，证明**产业政策**（Industrial Policy: 政府为促进特定产业发展而采取的干预措施）无法超越经济引力和惯性。更深层次的问题是，AI主权在当前规模下是否可行。训练前沿模型无疑需要数十亿美元。美国和中国正在计算基础设施上投入数千亿美元。像英国这样的中等强国，能否在这种环境中开辟出有意义的能力，还是AI会像核武器和半导体一样，成为只有超级大国才能竞争的技术？英国的赌注是，AI并非单一事物，它包括基础设施、应用、工具、专业模型和边缘部署，一个国家可以在这个技术栈的特定部分占据主导地位，而无需试图拥有整个庞大的体系。这是一种**利基理论**（Niche Thesis: 认为通过专注于特定细分市场或专业领域可以取得成功的理论）。它的正确与否，将决定该基金是被铭记为英国确保其AI未来的时刻，还是证明6.75亿美元无法在万亿美元竞赛中买到主权的时刻。

Cantwell told reporters that Britain is a small country and needs to pick up a few things that we're really excellent at and execute on those really well. That is the niche strategy. Don't try to build everything. Build the specific pieces where UK research depth gives a genuine edge. The model itself is very unusual. Government AI money typically flows to university and national labs, generating papers, but rarely commercial products. By targeting venture-backable companies and operating at VC speed, Britain is trying to thread the needle between academic research, which is super valuable, and market-ready solutions which generate money. Why set at the launch? They closed one deal from first meeting to announcement in 4 days, faster than Craig David, who joked. That's generally unusual for government procurement, but it has to be. Every day of bureaucratic delay is another day of deepening dependence on Anthropic APIs and Google Cloud AI services. The uncomfortable part is that this might not be enough. Six of the first seven backed startups are based in London, which raises questions about whether this is generally building national capability or just subsidizing one city's tech scene. The UK's corporate tax rate is 25% for companies with profits over 250,000 pounds, compared to California's 8.84% state corporate income tax. Research shows 6,000 company directors changed their country of residence since late 2023, with departures accelerating after Labour's October 2024 budget increased capital gains tax and reduced business asset relief. Throwing money at startups doesn't undo the structural incentives driving founders abroad. So, what's going to happen next? If the fund can prove the UK-based AI companies can scale domestically without relocating, that the combination of sovereign compute, fast visas, and target capital compensates for the tax and capital disadvantages, it validates the entire model. Other European countries are of course watching. If it works, I would expect France, Germany, and others to replicate it. If it doesn't, if the best companies still end up selling to Google or moving to San Francisco once they hit scale, then 500 million pounds becomes a very expensive lesson that industrial policy cannot override economic gravity and inertia. The deeper question is whether AI sovereignty is even viable at this scale. Training frontier models cost billions for sure. The US and China are investing hundreds of billions in compute infrastructure. Can a middle power like the UK carve out meaningful capability in that environment or does AI follow nuclear weapons and semiconductors as a technology where only superpowers can compete? The UK's bet is that AI isn't one thing. It is infrastructure, applications, tooling, specialized models, and edge deployment, and that a country can dominate specific segments of that stack without trying to own the whole big thing. That is a niche thesis. Whether it's correct determines whether this fund is remembered as the moment Britain secured its AI future or the moment it proved that $675 million cannot buy sovereignty in a trillion-dollar race.
