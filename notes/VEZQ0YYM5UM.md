### 艺术与生活的交融

**德比·米尔曼**: 罗斯，让我们聊聊你的成长故事。你在**圣克拉拉普韦布洛**（Santa Clara Pueblo）长大，身边环绕着几代艺术家和思想家。你的母亲**罗克珊·斯温策尔**（Roxanne Swentzell）和你的外祖母都开拓了将制作与意义相结合的道路。你是什么时候开始意识到，艺术也可以成为一种生存语言的？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: Rose, let's talk a little bit about your origin story. You grew up in Santa Clara Pueblo, surrounded by generations of artists and thinkers. Your mother, Roxanne Swentzell, and your grandmother both forged paths that united making and meaning. When did you first sense that art could also be a kind of language for survival?

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: 嗯。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: Hm.

</details>

**德比·米尔曼**: 我这一上来就问得挺深的，而且速度很快。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: I'm going real deep, real fast.

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: 这非常好，我非常喜欢。首先我想说的是，在我们的生活中，**艺术和生活之间其实没有区别**。一切都是一个创造性的过程，一切都是学以致用的，一切都带有意图和意义。因此，我们所做的事情，以及我们在世界上穿行的方式，都寄托着一种去创造美学现实的深切期许。我认为我的母亲是一位令人内难以置信的雕塑家。她利用自己被赋予的制作陶器的能力——这种能力来自于我们之前世世代代的传承。她用这种能力去进行她所需要的沟通，而这成为了我们的生计。她用自己的雕塑支撑起了我们的家庭。但对我们来说，所谓的“艺术界”是一件非常奇怪的事情，因为她同时也在制作我们日常吃饭用的陶器。她运用自己塑造土地的手艺来建造我们的房屋，种植我们的食物。因此，这种实用性以及与土地的关系，贯穿于我们生活的方方面面，是与生俱来的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: That's really good, I love that. I would like to say first that there wasn't a difference between art and life. That everything was a creative process and everything was applied and everything had intention and meaning. So what we did and how we moved through the world had an invested interest in creating a reality that was aesthetic. So I think that my mother's an incredible sculptor. And she took the ability that she had been given to work in ceramics. That came from generations and generations before us. And she used it to communicate in a way that she was needing to, and it became our livelihood. She supported our family with her sculpture. But it was like the art world was something very strange because she was also making the pottery that we ate out of. She used her ability to craft earth to build our home, to grow our food. And so that utility and relationship with it was innate in all walks of our life.

</details>

### 实验性的成长经历

**德比·米尔曼**: 你曾把童年的家描述为“实验性的”。我了解到，你的母亲有一次曾经切断了家里的电源，以此来看看全家人会如何去适应。那次尝试进展得怎么样？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: You've described your childhood home as experimental. And I understand that your mother once turned off the electricity to see how the family could adapt. How'd that go?

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: 哦，当我在早上六点半必须去赶公交车的时候，那真的让人非常抓狂和沮丧。但我依然充满感激。我的母亲直到今天，依然在不断寻找扎根的方法，去琢磨如何将与地球和生命相处的内在价值应用到生活的每一个角落。所以，这其中的一部分就是种植我们自己所有的食物，并摸索我们如何才能实现可持续地生活。我们有幸生活在祖先留下的家园里，拥有种子，并与能促进耕作的精神信仰保持着紧密的联系。因此，那些种子已经在那个环境中适应了非常漫长的时间。我们拥有流传了很久的传统，使我们能够在新墨西哥州北部的干旱高地上维持生计。而切断电源，正是为了重新找回不依赖于某种系统进行生存的感觉。现在，正是因为她当时切断了电源、在很长一段时间里对我们进行家庭教育、让我们自己种植食物，我如今甚至能够听见电流的声音。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: Oh, it was really frustrating when I had to catch the bus at 6:30 in the morning. But I am so grateful. My mom is always, still to this day, searching for ways to root and to figure out how to apply the innate values of relationship to Earth and being, in all walks of life. And so part of that was growing all our own food and figuring out how we could live sustainably. And we have the privilege of living in our ancestral home, having seed and relationship to spirituality that can foster the farming. So those seeds have been adapted to that environment for a very long time. We have tradition that's passed down for a really long time to be able to support ourselves in the high desert of northern New Mexico. And turning off the electricity was one step towards remembering what it's like to not be dependent on a system. And now because of the fact that she turned off the electricity, she homeschooled us for a long time, we grew our own food, I can actually hear electricity.

</details>

**德比·米尔曼**: 真的吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: Really?

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: 是的，我对这个非常敏感——是的，这一切都是——我们适应了所有我们添加到生活中的事物。而当我们把它们拿走时，我们才开始意识到我们受其影响有多深。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: Yeah, I'm really sensitive to like -- yeah, it's all -- we adapt to all the things that we add to our lives. And when we take it away, then we start realizing how much we're affected by it.

</details>

### 自给自足与选择权

**德比·米尔曼**: 那些早期的经历教会了你关于自给自足，或者说自给自足与想象力之间的关联的什么道理？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: What did those early experiences teach you about self-reliance or the connection between self-reliance and imagination?

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: 我觉得，理解真正的**可持续性**意味着我们始终拥有选择的权利。因为我妈妈没有把我们送进学校系统，她从很早就刻意对我学和我的哥哥进行家庭教育，而我们是直到后来才自己选择去学校上学的。而现在我们依然在继续学习。我认为那是她培养我们做出选择的能力的一种方式。我们永远都有选择的权利。我们不是我们所生存的这个世界的受害者。如果我们被教会了如何实现可持续，如何去创新，以及如何弄清在任何情况下如何生存，那么当我们应对周围的世界时，我们就是处于自主掌控的能动状态中，对吧？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: I feel like understanding true sustainability means that we always have a choice. Because my mom didn't put us into the school system, she, you know, intentionally homeschooled me and my brother from early on, and we chose to go to school later. And now we keep going to school. And I think that was a way of her building a capacity in us to choose. There's always a choice. We're not victims to the world that we live in. We can always, if we're taught how to be sustainable and how to innovate and figure out how to survive in any situation, then we are in our agency when we navigate the world around us, right?

</details>

**德比·米尔曼**: 那需要一种我们作为孩子时并不总能学会去培养的自给自足能力。你是如何维持这种状态的？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: That requires self-reliance that is not always something that we're learned to cultivate as children. How do you sustain that?

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: 我是如何维持它的？我喜欢去记住这一点：我并不需要那些我以为我需要的东西，对吧？比如，我们被一次又一次地告知我们需要某样特定的东西。而每天我都会意识到，这是我做出的选择。然后我就在这种关系中掌握了主动权。只要我不需要它，它就统治不了我，也占有不了我。因此，如果我保持与自然世界、我的食物来源等事物的联系，那么我就是在积极地参与到我所建立的这些关系中去。这包括了艺术界，包括了汽车文化，包括了教育，包括了所有的决定，比如今天来到这里。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: How do I sustain that? I love to remember that I don't need the things I think I need, right? Like we’re told and told and told that we need X, Y and Z. And every single day, I realize it's a choice I'm making. And then I'm in my power in relationship to it. As long as I don't need it, it doesn't rule me, doesn't own me. And so if I maintain my relationship with the natural world, my food sources, etc., then I am actively engaging in the relationships that I'm making. And that includes the art world, that includes car culture, that includes education, that includes all the decisions, you know, being here today.

</details>

And I'd be looking at all the nice cars. And in my head I was like, when I grow up, I'm going to have a nice car. That was just like, you know, the goal, right?

And growing up in Espanola, where the cruise line on Sunday is everybody get in their nice car and you put on some good tunes, and you lean back, and you are present, you're enjoying your community, you're having a sense of self-worth, you're enjoying the sunset. You have a good drink from Sonic, right? That feeling is actually presence, and that feeling is when aesthetic gets reapplied to our life and when we make those aesthetic decisions that we are in a state of agency, right? We're a state of empowerment in ourselves, right? And so I was building myself to have that moment to create that aesthetic experience for myself that I felt was reminiscent of what I knew of applied Indigenous aesthetics. Where it's not in a white cube on a white box, in some other building somewhere where you don't necessarily have access to. It's for everyone. Everyone has access to that experience.

</details>

### 打破艺术的边界

**德比·米尔曼**: 但你的工作确实跨越了不同的学科，陶艺、金属、汽车修复、表演，现在还包括了所有的这些。你是一微都想象着这些界限会消融，还是说随着你在实践中所做的工作，这是更自然而然发生的？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: But your work does move between disciplines, ceramics, metal, automotive restoration, performance, and now includes all of them. Now, did you always imagine those boundaries dissolving, or did that happen more organically over the work that you've done in your practice?

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: 我一直在想，如果我不再像世俗定义的那样去做一名艺术家，我还会继续做事情吗？我肯定依然会继续。我仍然会从一个地方去到另一个地方，不断地制作各种东西。我觉得我总是对如何去实现感兴趣——我是一个梦想家。我经常在脑海中勾勒出下一个最好的东西，我躺在那里，像这样：那扇窗户应该安在那个位置，我该如何实现它？就像我看一辆车，我看到的不仅仅是这辆车本身，我看到的是它可以成为的样子，对吧？就像我看一个花园，我看到的不仅仅是当前的花园，我看到的是它可以成为的样子，然后我就动手去做了。我觉得无论如何，我都将继续做这些事情。我总是会去寻找，无论是在我周围的世界里，去思考如何改善它，如何倾听它，询问我能如何为它提供服务，然后去工作。而当我退后一步，看着某些事物被转换了模样时所获得的那种满足感，就是我应用于周围所有事物的美学。而且我觉得这也是内在的。所以，这也是对我们心理和精神空间的一种内在探索。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: I always wonder if I stop being an artist in the way that the world is "arting." I would still be doing stuff. I would still be going from one place to the next and making things constantly. And I feel like I'm always interested in how -- I'm a dreamer. I constantly am imagining the next best thing, I'm laying there, like, that window should be over there and how can I do that? And like, I look at a car and I don't see the car, I see what it could be, right? Like, I look at a garden and I don't see the garden, I see what it could be, and then I begin. And I feel like I will always be doing that no matter what. I'm always going to be searching and whether that's in the world around me and how to better that and how to listen to it, ask how I can be of service of it, and then do work. And the satisfaction of stepping back and seeing how something transformed, that is that applied aesthetic to the world around me in all things. And I feel like that is also internal. So it's also that internal investigation of our psychological and spiritual spaces.

</details>

### 承载意识的器物

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: You've called cars vessels. And many of your figures, many of the sculptures you make, you've called vessels for transformation. What did these vessels hold?

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: **意识**（Consciousness）。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: Consciousness.

</details>

**德比·米尔曼**: 给我们详细讲讲这一点。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: Tell us about that.

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: 我本身就是一个器物，我拥有觉知，我在移动，我在这世界上有意识地做出决定。我也制作那些内部是中空的陶瓷器物，它们在注视着，它们在开展工作，它们是独立的，它们做出自己的决定，并在世界上穿行，承载着它们需要去完成的工作。汽车也是如此。我们居住的房屋、我们占据的空间也是如此，它们都在注视着，都在倾听着，也都在做出决定。如果我们能有所觉察的话。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: I am a vessel and I am aware and I am moving and I am making decisions intentionally in this world. And I make ceramic vessels that are hollow inside, and they are watching, and they are doing work, and they are independent, and they make their own decisions, and they move through the world with a job to do. And so do cars. And so do the houses we live in, the spaces we inhabit, they’re all watching, they’re all listening, and they're all making decisions. If we are aware.

</details>

**德比·米尔曼**: 告诉我你如何变得有所觉察。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Debbie Millman**: Tell me how you become aware.

</details>

**罗斯·B·辛普森**: 我喜欢去进行询问，我认为我们能做的第一件事就是开始询问。然后等待一个答案。我认为，我们已经在自己体内形成了一种肌肉记忆，即过度优先考虑人与人之间的互动，以至于我们已经停止去理解和相信：我们可以与人类之外的万物进行沟通。所以我制作了拟人化的陶瓷器物，将它作为一个桥梁，因为人类真的很喜欢与其他人类对话，并与人产生互动。如果我们能够看到一张拟人化的面孔，并开始去感受和倾听，那么我们就能开始建立起一种与那些我们一直认为是无生命的事物进行沟通的肌肉力量。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rose B. Simpson**: I like to ask, and I think the first thing that we can do is begin to ask. And then wait for an answer. And I think we have so consistently built a muscle inside of ourselves of prioritizing a human interaction that we've stopped understanding and believing that we can be in communication with that which is beyond human, right? And so I make anthropomorphized vessels in ceramics so that it's a bridge, because humans really like to talk to other humans and have interactions with those.
