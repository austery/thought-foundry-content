### 1837年恐慌：信用泡沫的破裂

在安德鲁·杰克逊（Andrew Jackson）总统于1832年废除了美国第二银行之后，各州掀起了一股银行狂潮，印发的货币几乎只靠乐观情绪支撑。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">After President Andrew Jackson killed the second bank of the United States in 1832, states unleashed a banking frenzy, printing money backed by little more than optimism.</p>
</details>

土地价格翻了两番，棉花出口也蓬勃发展，直到1837年5月10日，纽约银行暂停了**硬币支付**（specie payments: 将纸币兑换成金银硬币），不可避免的崩溃终于来临。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</p>
<p class="english-text">Land prices quadrupled and cotton exports boomed until the inevitable crash came on May 10th, 1837 when New York banks suspended specie payments, refusing to exchange paper money for gold or silver.</p>
</details>

金融纸牌屋轰然倒塌，场面惊人。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The financial house of cards collapsed spectacularly.</p>
</details>

一些城市的失业率飙升至25%，而土地价值暴跌了75%。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Unemployment hit 25% in some cities, while land values plummeted 75%.</p>
</details>

农民失去了世代相传的家族财产，城市工人挤满了施粥所。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Farmers lost generations of family property. Urban workers crowded into soup kitchens.</p>
</details>

全国850家银行以惊人的速度倒闭。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And the nation's 850 banks failed at an alarming rate.</p>
</details>

在这场长达五年的经济噩梦中，近40%的美国企业倒闭，国会最终于1841年通过了《破产法案》，这是美国首次承认有时债务根本无法偿还。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">After nearly 40% of American businesses failed during this 5-year economic nightmare, Congress finally passed the Bankruptcy Act of 1841, America's first acknowledgement that sometimes debts simply couldn't be paid.</p>
</details>

当国家蹒跚着走向复苏时，很少有人能想到，仅仅三十年后，一场更具破坏性的崩溃将不仅席卷美国，还会波及整个工业化世界。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As the nation limped towards recovery, few could imagine that just three decades later, an even more devastating crash would sweep across not just America, but the entire industrialized world.</p>
</details>

### 1873年恐慌：铁路扩张与长期萧条

1873年恐慌。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The panic of 1873.</p>
</details>

当美国仍在消化1837年恐慌带来的惨痛教训时，一场更具破坏性的经济飓风正在酝酿。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While America was still absorbing the devastating lessons of 1837, an even more destructive economic typhoon was brewing.</p>
</details>

1873年9月18日，随着美国激进的铁路扩张（耗费了近10亿美元的投资资本）以及主要银行**杰伊·库克公司**（Jay Cooke & Company）的倒闭，1873年恐慌爆发。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The panic of 1873 erupted when America's aggressive railroad expansion, consuming nearly $1 billion in investment capital, collapsed with the failure of major banking house Jay Cooke & Company on September 18th, 1873.</p>
</details>

纽约证券交易所史无前例地暂停交易10天，89家铁路公司债券违约，两年内超过18,000家企业倒闭。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The New York Stock Exchange suspended trading for an unprecedented 10 days while 89 railroads defaulted on their bonds and over 18,000 businesses closed their doors within 2 years.</p>
</details>

这不仅仅是美国的一场灾难，它引发了经济学家后来称之为“大萧条”的时期，持续了五年多。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This wasn't merely an American disaster. It unleashed what economists would later call the long depression lasting over 5 years.</p>
</details>

农产品价格暴跌45%，导致数千家庭陷入贫困。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Farm prices plummeted 45% driving thousands of families into destitution.</p>
</details>

1877年大铁路罢工期间，劳工骚乱爆发，联邦军队杀害了100多名抗议者。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Labor unrest exploded with the Great Railroad strike of 1877 where federal troops killed over 100 protesters.</p>
</details>

格兰特（Grant）总统，尽管其经济复杂性堪比一块砖头，仍否决了一项可能提供救济的通胀法案，坚持维持**金本位**（gold standard: 货币价值与黄金挂钩的制度），不顾普遍的苦难。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">President Grant demonstrating the economic sophistication of a brick vetoed an inflation bill that might have provided relief, insisting on maintaining the gold standard despite widespread suffering.</p>
</details>

尽管破坏性巨大，但这场恐慌与56年后将震撼全球资本主义根基的灾难——大萧条——相比，仍显得微不足道。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While devastating, this panic would pale in comparison to the cataclysm that would shake the foundations of global capitalism just 56 years later, the Great Depression.</p>
</details>

### 大萧条：全球资本主义的震撼

咆哮的20年代并非以一声呜咽结束，而是以震耳欲聋的崩溃告终，1929年10月股市惊人地崩盘，抹去了300亿美元的市值，几乎是整个联邦预算的两倍。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The roaring 20s ended not with a whimper, but a deafening crash when the stock market collapsed spectacularly in October 1929, erasing $30 billion in value, nearly twice the entire federal budget.</p>
</details>

随之而来的不仅仅是一场衰退，而是大萧条，一场在美国历史上无与伦比的经济浩劫。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">What followed wasn't just a recession, but the Great Depression, an economic apocalypse unmatched in American history.</p>
</details>

到1933年，工业生产减半，房屋建设下降80%，失业率飙升至惊人的25%。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">By 1933, industrial production had been cut in half. Home building dropped by 80%. And unemployment soared to a staggering 25%.</p>
</details>

银行系统崩溃，9000家银行倒闭，卷走了70亿美元的存款，那是数百万没有政府保险保护的美国人的毕生积蓄。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The banking system imploded as 9,000 banks failed, taking with them 7 billion in deposits, the life savings of millions of Americans who had no government insurance protection.</p>
</details>

被称为**胡佛村**（Hoovervilles: 大萧条时期无家可归者的棚户区）的流浪者营地遍布美国，嘲讽着那位坚称繁荣即将到来的总统。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Homeless encampments called Hoovervilles sprouted across America, mocking the president who insisted prosperity was just around the corner.</p>
</details>

**沙尘暴**（Dust Bowl: 1930年代美国中西部严重的干旱和沙尘暴灾害）将美国腹地变成了荒地，迫使绝望的**俄克佬**（Okies: 指沙尘暴时期从俄克拉荷马州及周边地区迁徙到西部的人）西迁，只为求生。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Dust Bowl transformed America's heartland into a wasteland, sending desperate Okies west in search of mere survival.</p>
</details>

农场止赎达到了流行病的程度，有时农民甚至武装起来阻止拍卖。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Farm foreclosures reached pandemic levels, sometimes met with armed farmers preventing auctions.</p>
</details>

只有当富兰克林·罗斯福（Franklin Roosevelt）推出他激进的“新政”时，美国才开始缓慢地恢复经济稳定。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Only when Franklin Roosevelt introduced his radical New Deal would America begin the slow climb back toward economic stability.</p>
</details>

但即使是他大胆的计划也未能阻止1937年的另一次急剧下滑。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But even his bold programs couldn't prevent another sharp downturn in 1937.</p>
</details>

### 1973-1975年石油危机衰退：滞胀的出现

1973年至1975年的石油危机衰退。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The oil crisis recession 1973 to 1975.</p>
</details>

当**石油输出国组织**（OPEC: Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries，由主要产油国组成的国际组织）成员国因美国在**赎罪日战争**（Yom Kippur War: 1973年埃及和叙利亚对以色列发动的战争）中支持以色列而愤怒，策划了一场石油禁运，导致石油价格几乎在一夜之间翻了两番时，美国的战后经济盛宴戛然而止。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">America's postwar economic party screeched to a halt when OPEC nations furious over US support for Israel in the Yom Kippur War orchestrated an oil embargo that quadrupled petroleum prices virtually overnight.</p>
</details>

加油站挂出“抱歉，无汽油”的牌子，美国人排队数小时加油，而油价从每加仑30美分飙升至1美元以上。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Gas stations displayed sorry no gas signs as Americans endured hours long lines for fuel that had jumped from 30 cents to over a dollar per gallon.</p>
</details>

这不仅仅是不便，而是一场经济战，暴露了美国对外国石油的危险依赖。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This wasn't just an inconvenience. It was economic warfare that exposed America's dangerous dependence on foreign oil.</p>
</details>

股市损失了45%的价值，失业率翻倍至9%。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The stock market lost 45% of its value while unemployment doubled to 9%.</p>
</details>

或许最令人担忧的是，一个新的经济怪物出现了：**滞胀**（Stagflation: 经济停滞与高通货膨胀并存的现象）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Perhaps most alarmingly, a new economic monster emerged. Stagflation.</p>
</details>

这是此前被认为不可能出现的高通胀（达到12%）与经济停滞增长的结合。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The previously impossible combination of high inflation reaching 12% and stagnant economic growth.</p>
</details>

尼克松（Nixon）总统的价格管制政策适得其反，导致从卫生纸到糖等各种商品短缺。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">President Nixon's price controls backfired spectacularly, creating shortages of everything from toilet paper to sugar.</p>
</details>

这场危机永久性地结束了美国战后的经济黄金时代，打破了繁荣将持续不中断的假设。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The crisis permanently ended America's post-war economic golden age, shattering the assumption that prosperity would continue uninterrupted.</p>
</details>

然而，即使是这场痛苦的衰退，也会被**美联储**（Federal Reserve: 美国中央银行系统）仅仅六年之后施行的蓄意经济“休克疗法”所掩盖。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Yet, even this painful recession would be overshadowed by the deliberate economic shock therapy administered by the Federal Reserve just 6 years later.</p>
</details>

### 1981-1982年衰退：沃尔克冲击疗法

1980年代初的衰退，1981年至1982年。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The early 1980s recession 1981-1982.</p>
</details>

美国的通胀噩梦在1980年达到了14.8%的恐怖峰值，促使新上任的**美联储主席保罗·沃尔克**（Paul Volcker: 1979-1987年担任美联储主席）实施经济“化疗”，蓄意引发衰退以遏制通胀。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">America's inflation nightmare reached its terrifying peak at 14.8% in 1980 prompting newly appointed Federal Reserve Chairman Paul Volcker to administer economic chemotherapy deliberately inducing a recession to kill inflation.</p>
</details>

他的武器是将利率提高到几乎难以想象的20%，使得抵押贷款变得难以负担，并摧毁了依赖信贷的企业。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">His weapon raising interest rates to an almost unimaginable 20% making mortgages unaffordable and crushing businesses dependent on credit.</p>
</details>

治疗奏效了，但副作用是残酷的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The treatment worked, but the side effects were brutal.</p>
</details>

失业率飙升至10.8%，高于1970年代石油危机时期。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Unemployment soared to 10.8%. Higher than during the 1970s oil crisis.</p>
</details>

建筑业和制造业崩溃，密歇根州30%的汽车工人失业。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Construction and manufacturing sectors collapsed with 30% of Michigan's auto workers losing their jobs.</p>
</details>

已经陷入困境的农民面临全面危机，因为设备贷款无法偿还，土地价值暴跌。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Farmers already struggling faced a full-blown crisis as equipment loans became unpayable and land values plummeted.</p>
</details>

贫困率跃升至15%，是1960年代以来的最高水平。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The poverty rate jumped to 15%, the highest since the 1960s.</p>
</details>

里根（Reagan）总统尽管以繁荣为竞选口号，但坚定地支持沃尔克，承受了巨大的政治压力，抗议者将他们无力负担的房屋钥匙寄到白宫。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">President Reagan, despite campaigning on prosperity, stood firmly behind Volcker, absorbing intense political heat as protesters mailed keys to homes they could no longer afford to the White House.</p>
</details>

当衰退最终于1982年末结束时，通胀确实得到了控制，但付出了巨大的社会代价，这将重塑未来几十年的美国经济政策。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When the recession finally ended in late 1982, inflation had indeed been tamed, but at a tremendous social cost that would reshape American economic policy for decades to come.</p>
</details>

然而，即使是沃尔克的严厉药方也无法阻止25年后即将到来的一切。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Yet, even Volcker's harsh medicine couldn't prevent what would arrive 25 years later.</p>
</details>

一场如此严重的金融灾难，以至于它赢得了不祥的名称——大衰退。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A financial catastrophe so severe it would earn the ominous name the Great Recession.</p>
</details>

### 2007-2009年大衰退：次贷危机与全球蔓延

2007年至2009年的大衰退。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Great Recession 2007 to 2009.</p>
</details>

一场金融海啸始于看似局部的房地产小插曲，2007年**次级抵押贷款**（subprime mortgage: 贷给信用较差借款人的高风险抵押贷款）违约率惊人地上升。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A financial tsunami began as a seemingly contained housing hiccup when subprime mortgage defaults rose alarmingly in 2007.</p>
</details>

华尔街通过一种被称为**抵押债务凭证**（collateralized debt obligations, CDOs: 将各种债务打包后出售给投资者的金融产品）的金融炼金术，将这些高风险贷款转化为所谓的安全证券。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Wall Street had transformed these risky loans into supposedly safe securities through financial alchemy called collateralized debt obligations.</p>
</details>

当房价停止其永无止境的上涨时，整个系统轰然崩溃，场面惊人。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When housing prices stopped their perpetual rise, the entire system imploded spectacularly.</p>
</details>

投资银行巨头**雷曼兄弟**（Lehman Brothers）于2008年9月倒闭，导致全球市场自由落体。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Investment banking giant Lehman Brothers collapsed in September 2008, sending global markets into freefall.</p>
</details>

**道琼斯指数**（Dow Jones: 美国股市主要指数之一）从峰值暴跌54%，抹去了16万亿美元的家庭财富。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The Dow Jones plunged 54% from its peak, erasing $16 trillion in household wealth.</p>
</details>

失业率翻倍至10%，870万个工作岗位消失。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Unemployment doubled to 10% as 8.7 million jobs vanished.</p>
</details>

止赎像瘟疫一样席卷美国，超过600万家庭失去了家园。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Foreclosure swept America like a plague. Over 6 million families lost their homes.</p>
</details>

国会不情愿地批准了7000亿美元的银行救助计划，这些银行被认为“大到不能倒”，引发了民粹主义的愤怒。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Congress reluctantly approved a $700 billion bailout for banks considered too big to fail, sparking populist rage.</p>
</details>

美联储主席本·伯南克（Ben Bernanke）部署了前所未有的紧急措施，这可能阻止了一场彻底的金融崩溃。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Federal Reserve Chairman Ben Bernanke deployed unprecedented emergency measures that likely prevented a complete financial meltdown.</p>
</details>

复苏过程异常缓慢，重塑了美国人对经济机会的态度。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The recovery proved painfully slow, reshaping American attitudes toward economic opportunity.</p>
</details>

然而，一场更为不寻常的经济灾难正潜伏在前方，它并非由金融过度行为引发，而是由一种微小的病毒触发。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Yet, an even more unusual economic catastrophe lurked just over the horizon. one triggered not by financial excess but by a microscopic virus.</p>
</details>

### 2008-2009年全球金融危机：美国危机的全球化

2008年至2009年的全球金融危机。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The global financial crisis 2008 to 2009.</p>
