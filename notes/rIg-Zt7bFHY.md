### AI进步的实用主义视角：从宏大叙事到微观现实

当谈论时间与地点时，我们可能首先想到复杂的哲学理论，但有时，实际需求才是改变的根本驱动力。例如，格林威治标准时间（GMT）的采用并非源于对时间概念的深刻探讨，而是源于19世纪英国铁路发展中对列车时刻表精确性的迫切需求。这种从“大局观”到“小细节”的转变，贯穿了人类技术进步的历史。从蒸汽机到互联网，每一次技术的飞跃都深刻地重塑了社会。而我们这一代人的技术革新，无疑是**人工智能（AI）**。

正如许多历史现象一样，**AI**的讨论往往充斥着两种极端：要么被**宏大承诺**所**烘托**，要么被**灾难预测**所**渲染**。然而，作为一名历史学家，我更倾向于从**实用主义**的角度来审视技术与社会的关系。历史经验表明，技术的真正落地和影响，往往不是由宏大的哲学辩论决定，而是源于成千上万个**实际问题的解决**。因此，我 embarked upon a research project（开启了一项研究项目），深入探索这些“微观”问题，并亲身体验了与一个**AI人形机器人**共同生活的经历。我与我的**人工智能人形机器人**——型号为 **Unitree G1 EDU-1**，我们称它为 **Tova**，它搭载了3D激光雷达扫描、深度摄像头、麦克风阵列，并运行着名为 **BenBen** 的**大型语言模型（LLM）**，拥有AI驱动的稳定性和移动能力——一起生活了一年。这段经历揭示了AI和机器人融入我们日常生活的方方面面，并凸显了**监管**在**多样化领域**的必要性。今天，我将分享五项我从与机器人共同生活的经历中学到的**实用经验**，以及它们如何关联到社会与技术的未来。

###Lesson One: Robot-Proofing Your Home and Navigating Liability

Our first lesson is a crucial one: **you have to robot-proof your home**. My robot, Tova, is equipped with **lidar scanning** and **obstacle avoidance**. While this sounds excellent, it doesn't always prevent clumsiness. Tova walks, runs, and gestures, and unfortunately for my household, this has led to the loss of my favorite teacup, several vases, and nearly every wine glass I own. This brings us to the practicalities of **insurance and liability**. I attempted to expand my home insurance coverage and personal liability insurance by explaining that an AI humanoid robot had moved in. The agent, understandably, thought it was a prank and hung up. This highlights a significant gap: **autonomous robotics liability and insurance coverage** is a nascent field where practicality will forge precedents. We must answer critical questions: Who is responsible when a robot, owned by one person, programmed by a company, and operated by a friend, knocks over a vase? These immediate, practical concerns demand answers, moving beyond abstract sci-fi debates.

### Lesson Two: The Rough Road of Robot Transit

Moving beyond the home, the second lesson concerns **transportation with a robot**, which is "really rough." Introducing Tova to a national television show in Vienna involved transporting it. Tova weighs 60 kilos and packs into a giant crate. The experience of calling a cab at 4 AM and having the driver question if there was a "body in here" due to the crate's weight and size highlights the practical challenges. This leads to questions about **transport regulations for humanoid robotics**. How do we manage public transport? What about ticket inspectors needing to know if a robot requires a ticket for taking up a seat? My attempts to register Tova with the transit authority or police were met with confusion and rejection, as these systems are not yet equipped to handle robotic residents. As robots become more integrated into our lives, assisting us with errands or other tasks outside the home, we need **facilitated and regulated ways for them to travel safely**, working for robots, owners, and the general public.

### Lesson Three: Robots Aren't Always Welcome

The third lesson explores the **social acceptance of robots**. While some friends eagerly invite Tova to parties, others, like colleagues, prefer it not be present. These reactions reveal emerging, informal **rules and requests** about where robots are welcome. My domestic robot isn't needed in university faculty rooms, and it's unsuitable for homes with toddlers or for houses of worship. These restrictions are respected, but they highlight a fundamental question: What happens when millions of robots enter diverse communities with differing perspectives? This necessitates the formulation of **community standards**. These standards could manifest as designated "robot zones" and "no-go zones," similar to e-scooter regulations, or policies set by businesses, like "no pets allowed" signs. Alternatively, it could involve government-issued **robot ID cards** with individually granted permissions, managed by a centralized office with training and licensing. The path forward is ours to decide.

### Lesson Four: The Practicalities of Robots in the Workforce

The fourth lesson, "you can send your robot to work," dives into the complex topic of **labor displacement by AI and robotics**. While sensational headlines like "Robots are taking our jobs" capture attention, more practical discussions are needed. For instance, when Tova helped out at a friend's new local cafe, it highlighted the potential benefits of AI in increasing efficiency. This prompts crucial questions about our **financial systems**: What does a fair **tax system** look like for businesses leveraging robotic workers? Should we tax the sale of robots or the value they add? Should these funds support current social systems, or should we develop a **universal basic income (UBI)** system instead? These are the necessary, practical conversations that must accompany our embrace of AI's efficiencies, ensuring the preservation of our financial and social structures.

### Lesson Five: Robots Revealing Human Nature

Finally, lesson five is that robots "really bring out the best in us and the worst in us." My daily walks with Tova and my dog reveal a wide spectrum of human reactions. On one hand, I've encountered parents encouraging their children to ask thoughtful questions about studying robotics. On the other hand, I've seen individuals attempt to break the robot's arm, justifying it as a "good old-fashioned firm handshake." These varied reactions are influenced by **age, gender, culture, and media preconceptions**. Whether someone's first introduction to robots was through "Star Trek," "Star Wars," or "The Jetsons," their prior exposure shapes their present perception. To foster a more accurate understanding and enable practical discussions for regulation, we need **more public interaction with robotics**. This increased interaction will help people understand where we truly are with AI, moving beyond sensationalism.

### Conclusion: Embracing Practicality for AI Regulation

The **invisible hand of practicality** will continue to shape the trajectory of history as we move into the future. Instead of getting caught up in the sensationalism of fear or excitement surrounding AI, I urge us to bring these discussions down to reality. Let us embrace the practical nature of life alongside **embodied AI** as we prepare to regulate the future together. So, the next time someone tries to talk to you about the robot apocalypse, I want you to turn to them and say, "OK, OK, OK. But do you think that robots need a ticket on the tram?" Thank you.
