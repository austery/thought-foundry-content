### 历史的类比：20 世纪 20 年代与当下

**尼古拉·坦根**: 大家好，我是挪威主权财富基金的 CEO 尼古拉·坦根。今天我们有幸邀请到一位重量级嘉宾，我们现在身处法兰克福，与**欧洲央行 (ECB)** 行长**克里斯蒂娜·拉加德**在一起。

我们现在也看到了一点当下的影子：一方面是技术突破，我会将其与 **AI (人工智能)** 的发展和普及联系起来；另一方面是地缘政治挑战，挑战着我们熟悉了几十年的**国际秩序**。这就是那个类比。我认为我们需要从历史中汲取经验，努力避免 20 世纪 20 年代之后的结局——首先是**金融危机**、欧洲银行的破产，最终，由于当时处理不当，演变成了摧毁许多发达经济体的全球冲突，让当时的所有人都变得更贫穷、更稀少。

<details>

And we are seeing a bit of that at the moment technological breakthrough which I would associate with the development of AI and the diffusion of AI on the one hand and fragmentation challenges to the international world order as we have known him for decades. So that's the analogy and I think that we have to be informed by history and try to avoid what came after those developments in the 20s that ended up with number one financial crisis, bankruptcies of banks in Europe and eventually because the matter was not handled very well at the time a global conflict that destroyed many of the advanced economies and made all of us at the time poorer and fewer.

</details>

### 碎片化对欧洲的冲击

**尼古拉·坦根**: 那你认为这对金融市场有什么启示？因为我们知道 20 年代末发生了什么。

<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nicolai Tangen**: What do you think are the implications for financial markets then? Because we know what happened at the end of the 20s.

</details>

**克里斯蒂娜·拉加德**: 我认为这对各类政策制定者都有启示。这意味着要把重点放在**外交**而非战争上，放在公共预算的良好财务管理上，并关注真实的变革而非过度的炒作。这就是我所期待的有利发展。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Christine Lagarde**: I think there are implications for policy makers of all stripes. So it means putting the onus on diplomacy rather than war, the onus on good financial management of public budgets and being attentive to the real transformation and not so much hype. That's what I would see as favorable developments.

</details>

**尼古拉·坦根**: 我们现在看到的这种全球碎片化，对欧洲有什么影响？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nicolai Tangen**: The fragmentation we are seeing in the world just now, how does it impact Europe?

</details>

**克里斯蒂娜·拉加德**: 在所有发达经济体中，**欧洲**可能是最开放的。这对你熟知的一些北欧国家非常适用，对整个欧洲也是如此。因此，任何对贸易的冲击对开放经济体的影响，都会超过那些并非自给自足但对进出口依赖度不高的经济体。除此之外，欧洲自身的资源和能源非常有限，除了我们正在推动的**可再生能源**和我们作为一个大陆试图资助的**绿色转型**。除此之外，能源来源很稀缺。这使我们更加脆弱。一方面是开放性，另一方面是稀缺的化石能源，两者结合使得我们在当前局势下处于明显的劣势。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Christine Lagarde**: Europe is of all advanced economy is probably the most open. This is very true for some of the Nordic countries that you know well, but it's true for the whole of Europe. So any shock to trade for instance is going to impact an open economy more than an economy that you know is not self-sufficient but not particularly vulnerable to trade whether export or import. Added to that, Europe has very limited own resources and own energy sources with the exception of the renewable energies that we are pushing and the green transition that we are also trying to finance as a continent. But other than that, sources of energy are rare and that exposes us even more. So it's openness on the one hand, rare fossile energy sources on the other hand and the two combined exposes us significantly in the current situation.

</details>

It's actually fascinating to see how only a few weeks ago this was almost put on the back burner as if it was not a priority. Clearly what has happened in the Middle East is bringing this priority back to the front of the mind of leaders and this green transition that would increase the volume of renewable energies versus the dependency on fossil energy is a long-term objective that has to be pursued and at an accelerated pace. In the short term you know obviously every effort should be made in order to reopen the straight of Hormuz and facilitate the passing of this 20-25% oil traffic that goes through that particular straight. I think there is, you know, it's neither nor, but the focus on nuclear energy is also one that we will see developing faster in Europe as well.

### 价值观、社会民主与央行使命

**尼古拉·坦根**: 美国政策最近发生了一些变化。我们一起参加了达沃斯的开幕晚宴，你就坐在我旁边的桌子，当时你看起来并不开心。你当时在想什么？

**克里斯蒂娜·拉加德**: 你看出来了。我不擅长掩饰，没有所谓的“扑克脸”。我认为当时不能接受的是，一位美国政府代表在没有辩论和对话的情况下，就在晚宴结束时对着包括许多欧洲领导人和皇室在内的听众，大肆抨击欧洲。我认为所有的观点都是受欢迎的，只要有辩论，但他表达的方式让我无法接受，所以我离开了房间。

But the principles of democracy, the independence, the freedom of speech, the individual rights, the economic development at entrepreneurial level that it facilitates. I think those are precious precious principles that we have to secure and if it requires reduced benefits in some areas I think we should be prepared to consider that if we have to referee and trade off between military expenses because we want to secure that precious treasure that has been acquired over the course of time and generations and if we have to have less of those social benefits that of which cost increases as the demographic changes take place in Europe, aging societies, less working population. I think we should face it and decide what we want collectively.

</details>

We have to make sure that prices remain stable and that people can decide to invest, to consume, to be employed in a landscape where prices are stable. That's the mission. And to deliver on that mission, we have to take into account all the developments that affect European economies and that has internal elements and external elements. The geopolitical shocks is an external elements that has ramification in our economies. the internal element such as demographics for instance, such as the impact of artificial intelligence, such as the rise in productivity, we have to take more and more factors into account in order to determine how we deliver on the mission. That is true and it makes our life more complicated and it requires a very strong focus and strong analytical skills in order to deliver.

</details>

### AI、生产力与数字欧元

**尼古拉·坦根**: 欧洲在**生产力**数据上表现疲软。我们是怎么走到这一步的？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nicolai Tangen**: So we've had some quite weak numbers in terms of productivity in Europe including recently in Germany. How just how did we get to this point?

</details>

**克里斯蒂娜·拉加德**: 这不是最近的现象。我认为欧洲错过了**互联网革命**。当你追溯欧洲和美国生产力的演变时，可以明显看到分歧就是从那时开始的。美国继续保持生产力增长，而我们则停滞不前且处于低位，从未赶上。现在我们面临的问题是：**人工智能**的使用能否帮助我们提高生产力？我们正开始看到一些迹象。

<summary>Original English</summary>

It goes back to, you know, I would say that we in Europe have missed the internet revolution and when you trace back the evolution of productivity between Europe and the United States it's pretty obvious that this is where it started diverging but the US continued to increase productivity not at a very fast pace but increased whereas we remained stable and low and we never caught up with that. The real question we need to ask ourselves now is is the use of artificial intelligence not in a pioneer way as the US does... but the real question for us is will the diffusion and use of that help us move our productivity up? We're beginning to see some of that.

Europe is actually a little bit ahead of the US in terms of penetration of AI in the manufacturing process in the services elaboration.

You know when you compare electricity and AI, AI it new iterations and changes come about in 3 weeks, well maybe I'm exaggerating let's say 3 months... electricity the diffusion of electricity 30 years. So when do we see the productivity impact how do we measure it probably a lot faster than what we think. Will AI be deflationary? I wouldn't say at this point in time it will to the extent that It improves significantly productivity. It should. But you know what other factors do we need to take into account? Energy. Well, you know, to teach an LLM at the pioneering edge where they are now cost an absolute fortune, right? And it consumes masses of energy as well.

</details>

**尼古拉·坦根**: 为什么**数字欧元 (Digital Euro)** 对维持欧元的地位如此重要？

<details>

So, do I think that central bank money is critical in order to structure, organize, and hold the financial system? Yes. So my conclusion from those two elements is we should have a central bank currency which is available in digital form. Added to which I think that the the rail guards on which digital currencies and digital assets travel is a public good and I think that we should be building it. It will be in pilot phase in 2027 and then complete roll out in 29.

</details>

### 领导力、多样性与个人驱动力

**尼古拉·坦根**: 作为一个领导者，你是什么样的？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nicolai Tangen**: How are you as a leader?

</details>

**克里斯蒂娜·拉加德**: 这个问题你得问别人。但我相信倾听、学习、工作，并最终通过这些来实现领导。我更倾向于做一个**包容型领导者 (Inclusive Leader)**，而不是那种“我在山顶，跟我冲”的类型。我不从发号施令或行使权力中获得快感，那既不让我满足也无效率。

</details>

**尼古拉·坦根**: 现在的你和执掌律师事务所时的你有什么不同？

<details>
