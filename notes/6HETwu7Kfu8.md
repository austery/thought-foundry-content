### 序幕：住房安全的力量

**巴菲·威克斯 (Buffy Wicks)**: 欢迎来到加州州长候选人住房论坛。我叫**巴菲·威克斯**，这是我的真名，我很高兴能来到这里。我要感谢住房行动联盟 (Housing Action Coalition)、UC 伯克利的**特纳中心**、旧金山基金会以及 **Ezra Klein** 秀。

当我 2018 年当选时，我就知道我想致力于住房问题。那时我 40 岁出头，我和丈夫带着一个蹒跚学步的孩子，刚刚在湾区买了第一套房子，那是一次**令人心碎的经历**，我相信你们很多人都能感同身受。

我并非出身富贵。我是在加州农村的一个**移动房屋 (Mobile Home)** 里长大的。小时候我很为此感到尴尬，不敢邀请朋友来家里过夜，我曾梦想拥有一座木头做的房子。直到长大后我才意识到，我父母在 1978 年以 12,000 美元购买的那座移动房屋，为我提供了**住房安全感**，让我能够专注于学习，去社区学院，从大学毕业，去奥巴马总统的白宫工作，自己竞选公职并获胜进入立法机构，成为住房委员会主席，现在成为加州众议院的拨款委员会主席。

那座移动房屋让我成为了我。它是我成为最好的自己所需的住房安全。而对于我们州太多的家庭来说，这种机会太遥远了。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Buffy Wicks**: Hello Oakland. Hello housers. I am so excited to be here today. Welcome to the California Gubernatorial Candidate Housing Forum. My name is Buffy Wicks and it's my real name and I'm very happy to be here. I want to thank the Housing Action Coalition, UC Berkeley's Turner Center, the San Francisco Foundation, and the Ezra Klein Show.

So when I got elected in 2018, I knew I wanted to work on housing. I was in my early 40s. My husband and I, with a toddler, had just purchased our first home in the Bay Area, and it was a soul crushing experience, as I'm sure many of you can attest to.

And you know, I don't come from a lot. I grew up in a mobile home in rural California and I was embarrassed about this when I was a kid. I didn't want to invite people over for slumber parties. I dreamt of a house made of wood. But it wasn't until I was older I realized that that mobile home that my parents purchased for $12,000 in 1978 provided for me the housing security that allowed me to focus on my studies, to go to community college, to graduate from university, to go work for President Barack Obama's White House, to run for office myself, to win to get into the legislature, to become the housing chair, and now to be the appropriations chair in the California State Assembly. That mobile home allowed me to become me. It was the housing security I needed to become my best self.

</details>

**巴菲·威克斯 (Buffy Wicks)**: 为什么在地球上最富有的一片土地上，我们会有成千上万的人流落街头？为什么我们拥有全美最高的流浪率和最低的住房拥有率？为什么我们还缺少 **250 万套**住房？我很快意识到两件事：第一，加州通往**住房地狱**的道路是由良好的意图铺就的——那些旨在减缓建设的法律已经达到了它们明确的目的。第二，萨克拉门托存在非常强大的**既得利益集团**，他们对现状非常满意，因为现状符合他们狭隘的私人利益。

过去八年我们在政策上取得了巨大进步，但这种改变并不容易。作为前线见证者，政治斗争非常艰巨。这有时意味着要反对比你的朋友，甚至反对比地方政府——他们通常不想遵守州法律要求的住房规划。我们成功推进了分区上调 (Upzoning)、精简审批以及改革我们那**拜占庭式**的**环境影响评估 (CEQA)** 流程。我们必须专注于结果，而不是特殊利益集团的需求。

我们需要一位愿意在这一议题上投入政治资本、敢于打破常规的州长。我们需要的是更多的**剪彩仪式**，而不是签署法案的仪式。现在，我很荣幸地介绍本次论坛的主持人 **Ezra Klein** 先生，以及参与本次活动的候选人。

**Buffy Wicks**: Why do we have tens of thousands of people on our streets experiencing homelessness in one of the richest places on Earth? Why do we have some of the highest homelessness rates and lowest home ownership rates in the country? Why are we 2.5 million homes shy of where we need to be. And I quickly realized two things. One, California's road to housing hell was paved with very good intentions. Laws designed to slow construction had achieved their express purpose. And the second thing I realized was that there were very powerful entrenched interests in Sacramento that were perfectly content with the status quo. It was serving their narrow self-interest.

So, you know, we've made huge strides on policy in the last eight years, but that change hasn't been easy. And as someone who's not just bore witness to this, but who's been on the front lines of it, I can also tell you the politics of this is really difficult. It means sometimes disagreeing with your friends. We've achieved success in upzoning, in streamlining permitting, and reforming our Byzantine environmental review process.

This person will not be perfect. They will make mistakes. But what we, the voters, want to know is: are they willing to break some glass with decisive and aggressive action to advance a pro-housing agenda? We need more ribbon cuttings and less bill signing ceremonies. So, let's spend the next 90 minutes getting to know who's the biggest pro-housing gubernatorial candidate we have. It is my total honor and privilege to introduce to you the moderator of this forum, Mr. Ezra Klein, and the candidates who are participating in this event.

### 第一章：建筑成本的硬骨头

**艾兹拉·克莱恩 (Ezra Klein)**: 欢迎来到奥克兰。今晚台上的候选人根据民调是本次竞选中排名前五的民主党候选人：**汤姆·斯泰尔 (Tom Steyer)**、**哈维尔·贝塞拉 (Xavier Becerra)**、**凯蒂·波特 (Katie Porter)**、**马特·马汉 (Matt Mahan)** 和**安东尼奥·维拉莱戈萨 (Antonio Villaraigosa)**。

加州的住房危机值得我们花 90 分钟去关注。州长纽森在 2019 年上任时承诺建设数百万套住宅，尽管通过了数十项法案，但加州每年的新屋开工量基本持平。问题在于，如何将良好的意图转化为人们可以居住的房子？

我们将从你们都认同的一个问题开始：加州极高的**建筑成本**。**斯泰尔先生**，从你开始。兰德公司的一项研究发现，加州建造公寓的每平方英尺成本是德克萨斯州的两倍以上。你认为原因何在？你会怎么做？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ezra Klein**: All right. Hello, Oakland. The candidates on the stage tonight are according to the polls the five top Democratic candidates in this race. Tom Steyer, Xavier Becerra, Katie Porter, Matt Mahan, and Antonio Villaraigosa. Give them a hand.

Governor Gavin Newsom came into office in 2019 with a promise to build millions more homes. And yet, the number of new homes being built in California is basically the same as when Newsom took office. So the question of tonight is what has to happen to convert these good intentions into homes that people can live in?

And Mr. Steyer, we're going to begin with you. A Rand study found that the cost per square foot of constructing an apartment in California is over twice that of constructing it in Texas. Why do you think that is? And what would you do about it?

</details>

**汤姆·斯泰尔 (Tom Steyer)**: 推高成本的是建筑方式、劳动力成本、材料成本和融资成本。我们需要在建筑方式上做出真正的改变。

首先是**异地制造 (Offsite Manufacturing)**。我们现在盖房子和盖公寓楼的方式和一百年前没什么两样。新技术允许我们像制造汽车一样在工厂制造房屋部件，然后在现场组装。据估计，这可以降低 20% 的成本。州政府可以改变**建筑规范**并提供订单来支持这些公司。

第二是**融资**。我和妻子创办的社区银行已经资助了 17,000 套低收入住房。我们需要更积极地利用融资来降低成本。

第三，加州的许多市县并不想要住房。有人曾对我说，他们宁愿要一个二手车场也不想要公寓楼，因为二手车场不用上学，也不产生医疗成本。地方政府收取的费用极高，有时占房屋成本的 20%。我承诺在第一天就举行特别选举，堵住价值 200 亿美元的**企业房地产税漏洞**。我们要让住房不再是地方的“无资助强制令”，而是一项“有资助的指令”，这样地方政府就不会再拖后腿。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tom Steyer**: I know that in fact what's driving that up is the way that we construct, the cost of labor, the cost of materials, and the cost of financing. So let's talk about two of them.

One is about the construction offsite. We are building apartment buildings the way we have been doing it for a hundred years and there is new technology to do this where you manufacture the parts of the house offsite the way you'd manufacture a car and then you assemble it onsite. The estimates start at 20% cost reduction.

The second thing is about finance. The nonprofit community bank that my wife Kat Taylor and I started has financed 17,000 low-income housing units. The third thing I'll say is this: The cities and counties in California do not want to have housing in general. They'd rather have a used car lot than they would a new apartment building. I've said that I will on day one call a special election to close a corporate real estate tax loophole worth over $20 billion. So that it's not an unfunded mandate. It's a funded mandate.

</details>

### 第二章：劳工、工资与权衡

**艾兹拉·克莱恩 (Ezra Klein)**: **贝塞拉先生**，你昨天的计划提到加州的建筑成本太高，但你也希望在建房中使用更多的**工会劳动力**并提高工资标准。特纳中心的研究发现，支付**普遍工资 (Prevailing Wage)** 会使每套住房的成本增加约 9.4 万美元。你如何既削减成本又提高工资？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ezra Klein**: Mr. Becerra, yesterday you released a comprehensive housing plan. You say in it that it costs too much to build a home in California. You also say that you want more union labor and higher wage standards. An analysis from the Turner Center found that paying prevailing wage increase a cost per unit of housing by about $94,000. How do you both cut the cost of housing and increase the wages behind it at the same time?

</details>

**哈维尔·贝塞拉 (Xavier Becerra)**: 我们必须确保是由熟练的男女工人来建设，且价格合理。如果你建造一定高度以下（通常是 8 层）的**填充式住房 (Infill Housing)**，开发者有权协商合适的劳动力价格；如果是大型项目，普遍工资应是标准。我们不应该相信必须通过让建筑工人买不起房来盖房子。解决成本问题不仅仅是劳动力，还包括繁文缛节。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Xavier Becerra**: I think we need to make sure that we are building with men and women who are skilled and we're doing it at a price that we can afford. If you do infill housing up to a certain height, usually about eight stories, then you have the right as a developer to try to get the labor that you need and try to negotiate a good price. If you go beyond that, you're talking about major construction. Prevailing wage will be the standard. We should not believe that we have to build homes by making it so it's impossible for the carpenter who builds a home to never be able to afford to buy it.

</details>

**艾兹拉·克莱恩 (Ezra Klein)**: 我明白你的观点，但你必须在某处削减成本。融资、劳动力、材料，如果你增加了一个成本驱动因素，你减少了哪个？

**哈维尔·贝塞拉 (Xavier Becerra)**: 我们可以取消那些不合法的关税来降低建筑材料成本。此外，州政府必须精简州一级的**红利性程序 (Red Tape)**，并解决地方一级的高额收费。大多数住房目前保留给单户住宅，很少建造公寓。如果我们继续只建单户住宅，永远达不到目标。我们需要填充式建设，提供稳定的资金来源。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Xavier Becerra**: Well, if we could get rid of the Trump taxes, the tariffs, that would help us reduce the cost of building materials. We have to tack it at the local level because of the high fees that are imposed. Very little construction is done with apartments and condominiums. We're never going to reach the number we need if we continue to only build single family homes. That's why the legislation that allows us to really build out, do the infill where we know we have transportation will give us an opportunity.

</details>

### 第三章：速度、繁文缛节与“一站式”审批

**艾兹拉·克莱恩 (Ezra Klein)**: **波特女士**，你常说“时间就是金钱”。兰德公司的研究发现，德克萨斯州完成一个多户住宅项目需要 27 个月，而加州需要 **49 个月**。为什么这里这么慢？你会怎么做？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Ezra Klein**: Ms. Porter, you've often said that time is money. The Rand study found that it takes about 27 months to complete a multi-family housing project in Texas and 49 months in California. Why does it take so long here and what would you do about it?

</details>

**凯蒂·波特 (Katie Porter)**: 速度就是核心驱动因素。如果我们可以像科罗拉多州那样快 22 个月，房价就能降低 10% 到 20%。我强烈支持创建**统一的州级许可证**，让州政府更容易监控地方的拒绝。此外，应该限制市县在最后关头增加额外费用的权力。目前的系统允许不断增加延迟和成本，直到项目无法负担。

还有一个讽刺的事实：**保障性住房 (Affordable Housing)** 的单位建设成本比市场价住房还高。因为它们面临更多的社区阻力、更严格的分区限制。开发者不得不从七个不同的资金池里拼凑资金，可能拿到第七笔时第一笔就过期了。我们需要一个**整合的大型资金池**。此外，州政府应该为保障性住房提供土地。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Katie Porter**: Speed is the driver. If we could be 22 months faster, like Colorado, we could take 10 or even 20% off the price. I strongly support the pending legislation that would create one uniform statewide permit. I also think it's a really good idea to limit how many last second add-ons can come. 

Affordable housing projects face more delays and community resistance. Developers are piecing together financing from seven different pools of money. One consolidated bigger pot of money would help. The other thing is the state should be putting land up for affordable housing.

</details>

### 第四章：地方与州的博弈

**艾兹拉·克莱恩 (Ezra Klein)**: **马汉市长**，圣何塞已经批准了 2 万多套新房，但因为经济账算不过来，大部分都没动工。州政府能做些什么？

**马特·马汉 (Matt Mahan)**: 这种经历对我来说非常深刻。在圣何塞，过去两年我们将市中心和交通走廊沿线的多户住宅审批改为**行政审批 (Ministerial Approval)**，这意味着它们是“法定权利 (By Right)”，不需要经过规划委员会或市议会，甚至豁免 **CEQA**。

州政府可以对市县施加标准和限制。如果城市没能在最后期限内完成审批，或者试图用高额费用阻挠，州政府应该**接管审批权 (Override)**，通过“建筑商救济 (Builder's Remedy)”机制让开发者直接开工。起诉（如亨廷顿比奇案）往往拖延数年，并不能产生真正的房子。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Matt Mahan**: We've approved 22,000 homes and they're not getting built. In the last two years, we have moved our multifamily housing approvals to what's called a ministerial approval. It's essentially by right. Doesn't go to the planning commission. It actually exempts CEQA. 

When cities fail to meet permitting deadlines, when they try to use fees and local building codes to block housing, I think the state should override and create mechanisms for developers to move ahead whether or not the city likes it. Lawsuits drag on for years. I feel very strongly that our next governor cannot bring a lawyer's mindset to this problem.

</details>

**艾兹拉·克莱恩 (Ezra Klein)**: **维拉莱戈萨先生**，你的竞选网站捍卫 **Prop 13**（加州房产税限制令），但 Prop 13 迫使城市提高新房建设费用，因为它们无法收集足够的房产税。你常说愿意做不受欢迎的事，为什么不改革 Prop 13？

**安东尼奥·维拉莱戈萨 (Antonio Villaraigosa)**: 首先，我从 1978 年起就反对 Prop 13。斯泰尔说得对，这导致了“土地利用的财政化”，城市更偏向带状商场而不是住房。我主张修复整个破碎的税收系统。

1978 年时，商业地产承担 60% 的税负，房主承担 40%；现在反过来了。我们必须改变这一点。目前的系统对那些在 1978 年前买房的人和今天买房的年轻人不公平。我曾担任加州众议院议长，两次在赤字中实现预算盈余。我们需要扩大税基，不仅依赖高收入者（他们会离开），还要激励城市建造住房而非商场。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Antonio Villaraigosa**: First of all, I'm on record opposing Prop 13 since 1978. What Steyer was talking about, it's called the fiscalization of land use. Because of Prop 13, we reward a strip mall more than we would housing. 

When we passed Prop 13, commercial properties were paying 60% of the freight. Now it's the other way around. We've got to change that. We got to fix the whole broken tax system. We over-rely on the people at the top. I'm the only person on this stage that's actually been the speaker of the Assembly. We need to incentivize cities to build housing, not strip malls.

</details>

### 第五章：治理无家可归者与精神健康

**艾兹拉·克莱恩 (Ezra Klein)**: **贝塞拉先生**，加州在 2018 到 2023 年间投入了 240 亿美元治理无家可归问题，但露宿人口仍在增加。哪里出错了？

**哈维尔·贝塞拉 (Xavier Becerra)**: 我们没有专注于**结果**，缺乏问责制。对我来说，无家可归既是住房问题，也是**精神健康危机**。我们必须加强**预防**——投资于那些处于失业或医疗紧急状态边缘、即将失去住所的人，这比从街上拉人要便宜得多。

关于精神健康，我们不能让人们在明明无法为自己做出正确决定时，还任由他们流浪。作为文明人，我们有责任提供协助。我们需要胡萝卜加大棒。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Xavier Becerra**: We didn't focus on outcomes. The accountability wasn't there. To me, the homelessness crisis is much a mental health crisis as it is someone needing a shelter. We have to do far more to prevent people from ever becoming homeless. It will cost us far less to invest in someone maintaining their housing than trying to pull them off the street. 

We will not let you languish in the streets. If it's clear that you need help, then it's really our responsibility to provide some assistance. We don't let people make that decision when it's clear they're not making the right decisions for themselves.

</details>

**汤姆·斯泰尔 (Tom Steyer)**: 我不同意当前的“收容所加永久支持性住房”策略。人们讨厌收容所，因为它们危险、没有隐私且不能带宠物。而永久支持性住房每间房的造价高达 75 万到 100 万美元。

我主张**紧急过渡住房 (Emergency Interim Housing)**。你有带锁的钥匙、有隐私、能带宠物、有配套服务。这不仅更快、更便宜，而且 70% 的人愿意接受。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Tom Steyer**: People hate going into shelters because they think they're dangerous. They have no privacy. The strategy we've had has failed. Permanent assisted housing costs somewhere between $750,000 and a million dollars a key. What I'm talking about is something that is much cheaper, much faster, with privacy and a key.

</details>

### 终章：书单推荐

**艾兹拉·克莱恩 (Ezra Klein)**: 最后一个问题，给加州人推荐一本书。

*   **斯泰尔**: 《捕食者的时刻》(The Hour of the Predator)，关于社会权力变革与守护民主。
*   **贝塞拉**: 《黄金雨》(Rain of Gold)，关于努力奋斗实现美梦。
*   **波特**: 《去年》(Yesteryear)，一本关于女性面临的挫折与愤怒的小说。
