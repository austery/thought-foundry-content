### 交通信号优先 (TSP) 技术的应用

**[主持人]**: 我们来谈谈北边的 **Finch LRT**。他们宣布了**交通信号优先（Transit Signal Priority）**，对于不熟悉这个词的人来说，那是什么？目前推行得怎么样？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Host]**: Let's talk. Let's go north to the Finch LRT. They announced transit signal priority for people who aren't familiar with that. What is that and how has it been rolled out so far?

</details>

**Andy Takagi**: 简单来说，交通信号优先的工作原理是针对与汽车流量处于同一平面的列车。如果你停在红灯前，你就得像汽车一样等待一个完整的红灯周期才能通过，对吧？交通信号优先的方式是列车能够提前向红灯发出信号，说：“嘿，我载着很多乘客，让我通过吧。”然后它会为公交延长绿灯时间，或者在左转车辆之前提前给出绿灯信号。

这一直是 **Finch** 线路上的大问题，甚至从个人经验来看也是如此。当它刚开通我去乘坐时，我们很多时间都卡在红灯后面。如果你乘坐市中心的有轨电车，你会知道这是一种常见的挫折，即使是在 **Spadina** 或者像 **Finch** 这样有专属路权、不与其他交通工具竞争的线路上，你仍然要在红灯后面等待。

所以，交通信号优先是一种加速列车运行的方法，特别是像 **Finch** 这样许多站点都在信号灯远端的线路。你等了一个红灯，过了红灯后又马上停在车站，作为乘客这可能会非常令人沮丧，因为你停了两次。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Andy Takagi]**: Yeah. So basically the way transit signal priority works is for trains that run on the same level as car traffic. If you pull up to a red light, you're waiting there for a full red light cycle like a car would before you're able to go through right? The way transit signal priority works is the train is able to signal ahead to the red light and say, hey, I'm you know, I'm carrying a lot of passengers. Let me through and it'll extend a green light for transit, right. Or it'll have an early green light signal ahead of left turning vehicles. And that was a big problem on Finch, even just from personal experience. When I took it after it first opened, you know, we were we were stuck behind red lights a lot of the time. And, you know, if you ride a downtown streetcar, you know that that's a common frustration even on Spadina or, you know, lines like Finch where there's a right of way for the streetcar, you're not contending with other traffic, but still you have to wait behind a red light. And so transit signal priority is a way to to speed up the trains, especially when you have, like on Finch, where a lot of the stations are on the far side of the signal. So you're waiting at a red light, and then you pass the red light, and then you're stopping again at the station. That can be really frustrating as a rider, right? Because you're stopping twice.

</details>
