### 什么是多肽？乐高比喻

**[Steven Bartlett]**: Alex 医生，到底什么是多肽？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Steven Bartlett]**: Dr. Alex. What the hell is a peptide?

</details>

**[Alex Tatem]**: 多肽是一种结构性药物。最好的理解方式是，相对于通过药片服用的、在全身产生广泛影响的“小分子药物”，多肽是由一小段**氨基酸**组成的。你可以把氨基酸想象成构成人体的**乐高积木**。多肽就是蛋白质的碎片，设计用来精准瞄准某些受体，以非常针对性的方式影响细胞。或者你可以把它想象成一把**非常特定的钥匙**，去开启一把非常特定的锁。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Alex Tatem]**: Peptides are a structural class of medications. The best way to think about peptides is that just like we have small molecules which are drugs that are very small taken in a pill and have a wide ranging effect throughout the body. Peptides are derived from little pieces of amino acids which think of them as the Legos that make up the human body. The Legos that make up proteins. These are fragments of proteins that are designed to specifically target certain receptors and affect cells in a very targeted fashion. Or a best way to think about it is a very specific targeted key to unlock a very specific lock. So instead of a small molecule that may have a wide ranging effect throughout the body, peptides are much much more focused.

</details>

**[Alex Tatem]**: 我儿子非常喜欢乐高，他可以用同一套积木几分钟内搭出一枚火箭，然后再搭出一艘海盗船或一辆赛车。多肽也是如此，同样的氨基酸积木可以组合出功能迥异的物质。医疗中第一个被分离并使用的多肽是 1921 年的**胰岛素**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Alex Tatem]**: The best way to think about it is my son loves Legos, which is why I'm glad that we have these here. But he can take the same set of Legos and he can build a rocket ship and then just a few minutes later he can build a pirate ship and then he builds a race car. And he's using the same Legos, but he's creating very, very different things that all do very, very different things. And so peptides have become incredibly popular because yes, we have some really fascinating peptides that can help with anti-aging, with healing, and with tissue repair. The first peptide that was actually isolated and used in medicine, was insulin back in 1921.

</details>

### 制药行业的博弈与专利困局

**[Steven Bartlett]**: 既然这些多肽这么有效，为什么现在才突然在社会意识中爆发？背后的深层原因是什么？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Steven Bartlett]**: But there's a bigger question here which is why now why have the subject of peptides suddenly exploded into society's consciousness? What's going on? What's the big picture?

</details>

**[Alex Tatem]**: 这非常有趣。2013 年美国有一个“Myriad Genetics”法院案例。这家公司发现了导致乳腺癌的基因并申请了专利。但最高法院裁定，如果是**自然界存在**的东西，你就不能申请专利。这本是件好事，但其无意中的副作用是：制药公司突然失去了开发这些极具前景化合物的动力，因为他们无法将其货币化。药物研发需要投入数亿甚至数亿美元，如果你不能保护它，你就没有动力去做慈善。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**[Alex Tatem]**: So this is really interesting. In 2013 there was actually a court case in the United States. It was the Myriad Genetics case. I can't patent you know your muscle cells.
