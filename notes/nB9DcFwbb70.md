### 与权力者的对话

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Paikin**: President Biden called you to talk about the future of NATO. You spoke to Donald Trump during his first term. You sat down with Barack Obama to talk geopolitics. So, let's start with this. What do you try to accomplish when you get that kind of an audience?

</details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: 好问题，史蒂夫。对每个人、每位总统来说情况都不同。但我认为你真正想寻找的是：他们的深层思考是什么？真正驱动他们做决策（不仅仅是这一项决策，而是所有事情）的深层动机是什么？

这种短暂的会面很少是为了挖掘什么秘密，也很少能直接搞到突发新闻。更多是为了理解这个人的思维方式，这将塑造你未来的报道。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Thomas Friedman**: Good question, Steve. Of course, it's different from each person, each president, but I think what you're really looking for is, you know, what is the deep thinking? What is the deep thing that's really motivating them, not just about this decision, but about everything they're doing? It's rarely a secret, it's rarely a news story that you're going to break. It's more understanding how that person thinks. That will then shape your reporting going forward.

</details>

**史蒂夫·派金**: 你向他们提问，他们也会反过来问你吗？**特朗普**问过你问题吗？

<details>

我通常认为，高层领导人就像“装了子弹的手枪”，你永远不知道他们什么时候会在你面前走火。所以我并不刻意去结交他们。相比之下，我更愿意和现场的观众交流，通过和普通人交谈，我能更清楚地了解政治的边界。

In general, my sort of general view of reporting is that these senior leaders, they're loaded pistols, and you never know when they're going to go off in your face. So it's not something I cultivate or go out to do. I'd actually much rather talk to you, I'd much rather talk to the audience. I'll actually know what are the limits of politics much more by talking to them than the senior leaders.

### 特朗普风格：交易与退缩

**史蒂夫·派金**: 观众可能认为奥巴马充满求知欲，会问各种问题；而特朗普则不然，他只想告诉你他的想法。我的直觉准吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Paikin**: Because my hunch is this audience probably thinks Barack Obama, intellectually curious, he'd be asking all kinds of questions, and I suspect they also think Donald Trump, not particularly intellectually curious. He probably didn't ask any questions and just wanted to tell Tom what he thought. Am I close?

</details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: 有点那个意思。对特朗普来说，一切都是交易。你写了让他满意的东西，他就想奖赏你；反之，他就在推特上抨击你。

我从记者做起，走过75万英里的报道之路。我不是一个意识形态化的人。读者知道，我主张“既是又是”，而非“非此即彼”。我支持高墙配大门，支持做大蛋糕再分配。我是一个靠报道驱动的专栏作家，而不是坐在图书馆里抽烟斗、从意识形态角落向世界投射雷电的人。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Thomas Friedman**: There's a little of that. With Trump, everything is transactional. Did you write something he liked? Then he wants to reward you. He's also tweeted against me. So, if you write something he doesn't like, it goes the other way.

I came to be a columnist after being a reporter. I'm actually a reporter who has strong views on things, but every one of my opinions actually emerges from my reporting. I'm not an ideological person. I'm a very much a "both and" person, not an "either or" person. I'm for a high wall with a big gate. I'm for growing the pie and re-dividing the pie. I don't sit in my library and smoke a pipe and throw thunderbolts down on the world from some ideological quadrant.

</details>

**史蒂夫·派金**: 你在文章中说美国正成为一个由“不受法律和宪法价值观约束的冲动强人领导的流氓国家”。你从特朗普那儿收到反馈了吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Paikin**: Well, I wonder if you got in trouble for writing this. When you wrote that America is becoming, quote, "A rogue state led by an impulsive strongman disconnected from the rule of law and other constitutional American principles and values." Did you hear from Donald on that one?

</details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: 自本届政府宣誓就职以来，我没和其中的任何一个人说过话，他们也没找过我。这是我的选择，因为我更关心世界正在发生什么。

拥有这届政府的消息源其实很危险，因为一切都取决于特朗普刚发了什么推特。如果你问国务卿：“特朗普打算怎么对待普京和俄罗斯？”他根本不知道。华尔街现在流传着一个关于特朗普的梗，叫“**TACO 交易**”（TACO trade），意思是“特朗普总是临阵退缩”（**Trump Always Chickens Out**）。

如果你上周因为他说要对欧盟征收50%关税而抛售股票，你就亏大了，因为到周一他又说“哦，我晚点再弄那个”。这种做法注入了巨大的不确定性，投资者会被冻结，盟友也不知道他说的“是”到底还有没有价值。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Thomas Friedman**: No, I've not spoken to him. I haven't spoken to a single person in this administration since they were sworn in. My choice because I'm much more interested in what's going on in the world. Having sources in this administration is one of the most dangerous things you can have, because everything depends on what Donald Trump just tweeted.

There's a new meme going on around at Wall Street now about Trump called the TACO trade. Trump Always Chickens Out. If you sold your stocks last week when he said 50% tariff on EU, you got creamed, because by Monday it was back to, "Oh, I'll do that later." He doesn't realize how much uncertainty he has injected everywhere. Your allies literally don't know the value of when you said yes.

</details>

### 特朗普 1.0 vs 2.0：缓冲器与放大器

**史蒂夫·派金**: 在特朗普第一任期内，他身边还有人能阻止他的不良直觉。这些制衡机制现在还存在吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Paikin**: But I think we have assumed on this side of the border that America had checks and balances in place so that when the president wanted to indulge in his worst instincts, there were people around him who could say, "Not so fast Mr. President." Are those checks and balances still in place today?

</details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: 这是一个核心问题。特朗普 1.0 时期，他身边全是“**缓冲器**”（buffers）；而特朗普 2.0 时期，他身边全是“**放大器**”（amplifiers）。

在 1.0 时期，如果他在酒吧里听人建议说要把加沙变成地中海俱乐部，他回办公室告诉蓬佩奥或马蒂斯，他们会说：“好主意！让我们研究一下。”等他再想起来时，这事早就翻篇了。而这一次，他身边没有缓冲器。

这种局面非常危险。没人知道会发生什么，谁会被推特开除。他甚至根据一位阴谋论巫医——**劳拉·卢默**（Laura Loomer）的建议，开除了美国最高网络指挥官。这太疯狂了，但在现下的节奏中，这甚至掀不起什么水花，因为下一个逾矩行为只需 30 秒就会出现。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Thomas Friedman**: The central truth of this moment is that Trump one was surrounded by buffers, and Trump two is surrounded by amplifiers. In Trump one, if he said "let's turn Gaza into a Club Med," they would say, "What an amazing idea. Let's study that," and by the time he got back to it, he's already gone. This time, no buffers.

It's a very dangerous situation. No one really knows what's happening. He fired the head of the American NSA on the advice of a witch doctor named Laura Loomer, a conspiracy buff. That guy spent his whole career preparing for this job, and a nut, a witch doctor, told our president to fire him, and he fired him. But we can't obsess on that, because the next transgression is only 30 seconds away.

</details>

### 被误读的“世界是平的”

**史蒂夫·派金**: 你曾著名地将世界描述为日益相互依存、正在“变平”，而且似乎在变得更公平。现在特朗普是不是正在逆转这一切？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Paikin**: You famously described in one of your books that the world was becoming increasingly interdependent, "flattening" was the way you described it, getting fairer. Is Donald trying to unwind that?

</details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: 你完全误读了我的意思。我说的“变平”不是指“公平”。

几年前我在中国，一个大学生问我：“弗里德曼先生，世界还是平的吗？”我说：“在我所定义的意义上，世界比以往任何时候都更平。”这意味着更多的人可以在更多的地方，以更少的钱，进行连接、协作和竞争。这代表**机会平等**，但不一定结果公平。

当那个学生告诉我她在用 VPN 访问 **ChatGPT** 时，我问她：“你都在用 VPN 用 ChatGPT 了，居然还问我世界是不是平的？”所以，技术正以前所未有的方式赋能协作。虽然特朗普抨击全球主义者，但他知道贸易的重要性。如果改变了美国的本色，就会改变世界的本色。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Thomas Friedman**: No, I totally misread that. Not "fair." I said the world was flat in the way I meant it, which is that more people in more places can now compete, connect, and collaborate on more things than ever before. It means more equal opportunity.

That girl said "Mr. Friedman, I just used ChatGPT last weekend." I said, "You used ChatGPT on a VPN from China and you're asking me if the world is flat?" So, yes, it's flatter than ever in the way that technology is enabling more of us to compete, connect, and collaborate. If you change the way America is, you will change the way the world is, and you will miss that period when it's gone.

</details>

### 生态系统经济：关税无法触达的真实

**史蒂夫·派金**: 特朗普现在威胁要让 **iPhone** 回美国制造，对此你怎么看？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Paikin**: We see that President Trump is now threatening Apple. He wants iPhones manufactured in the United States. Tell us more about what you mean by that.

</details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: iPhone 象征着今天产品的制造方式：它们产自复杂的**自适应联盟**和生态系统。伟大的产品如 mRNA 疫苗或 iPhone 之所以强大，是因为它们解决了复杂问题。

世界已经转向了生态系统经济。比如福特 **F-150** 皮卡，只有 32% 的零部件是美国制造的。如果你中断了这个生态系统，你就无法以普通人负担得起的价格解决复杂问题。如果 iPhone 完全在美国制造，估计售价要 3500 美元。

特朗普对关税和贸易的看法不是一种信念，而是一种“迷信”，因为它完全脱离了现实。他身边的人只是为了忠诚而被录用的，不是为了洞察力。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Thomas Friedman**: The iPhone symbolizes how a lot of products are made today, in complex adaptive coalitions, ecosystems. The great thing about complex products is they solve complex problems. The world has actually moved much more to an ecosystem economy.

Ford F-150 pickup truck, today 32% of its parts are made in America. If you interrupt that ecosystem, you are unable to solve those complex problems at a price point that people can enjoy. The iPhone would cost $3,500 if we tried to make it all in America. Trump's belief in tariffs is not a conviction, it's a superstition, because it has nothing to do with reality.

</details>

### 信任战争：AI 时代的脆弱

**史蒂夫·派金**: 我们该如何与这位“最好的朋友”重建信任？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Paikin**: How exactly we are supposed to regather trust with somebody who was supposed to be our best friend?

</details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: 我们现在不是在打关税战，而是在打一场“**信任战争**”。

相互依存不是一种选择，而是我们的生存状态。我的朋友多夫·赛德曼说：“**信任**是唯一的合法性能增强药物。”有了信任，就像在硬地板上，我这小个子也能扣篮；没有信任，就像在叙利亚沙漠，你一毫米都跳不起来。

更深层的问题在于人工智能。AI 就像蒸汽，正在进入一切事物——手表、汽车、烤面包机，甚至你的髋关节置换配件。如果 AI 无处不在，一切都会变成“**TikTok**”问题：谁拥有数据？谁控制算法？如果美中之间、盟友之间没有信任机制，未来我们能交易的就只有大豆和酱油了。特朗普在 AI 辩论的门槛前发起关税战，简直是在玩火。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Thomas Friedman**: We're not in a tariff war, we are in a trust war right now. Trust is the only legal performance-enhancing drug. Where trust is in the room, it's like a hard floor. Without trust it's like the Syrian desert.

AI is like a vapor. It's going into everything—your watch, car, toaster, even your hip replacement. When AI is everything, everything becomes TikTok: Who owns the data? Who controls the algorithm? If we can't trust each other, all we're going to sell China is soybeans and all they're going to sell us is soy sauce. Donald Trump is in a tariff war on the cusp of that huge debate.

</details>

<details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: 别理会这些，这全是愚蠢且荒谬的。这只是他抛出来的诱饵，用来分散媒体对他在其他麻烦事上的注意力。

作为一名美国人，我为此感到羞愧。如果你问我这会不会发生，我的回答是：绝不会发生。我的建议是：**熬走他**（wait this guy out）。

<details>

It's an embarrassment to this American. If you're asking me, is it gonna happen? It's not gonna happen. My short answer is just wait this guy out.

</details>

### 中东僵局与犹太身份

**史蒂夫·派金**: 你今天的专栏非常悲观，甚至让人感觉“我们完蛋了”。你为什么会写出情绪如此强烈的文章？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Paikin**: Your column today, I suspect, is gonna go in the Pantheon of Friedman columns, because it really gave me the impression that... we're cooked! It was a very depressing column.

</details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: 在我的文字里总有一线希望。我仍然坚持“两国方案”。虽然成功的几率只有 5%，但我会把 100% 的精力投入其中，因为替代方案不是一国或三国方案，而是两族人民都失去国家，陷入无休止的内战。

我在专栏中警告以色列人：无论你认为报复的合理比例是多少，你都已经越界了。如果不找到与巴勒斯坦权力机构合作的途径，不仅战争会永无止境，而且我的孙辈们将会生活在“犹太国家沦为贱民国家”的世界里。我不希望看到那一天。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Thomas Friedman**: There is always a spark of hope in everything I write. I'm still for two states for two people. There's about a five percent chance this can work, but I'm devoting 100% of my energy to that, because the alternative will be no states for two people, a grinding civil war.

What I was warning Israelis is that whatever you think was the right proportion of retribution, you've gone past it. If you don't partner with a Palestinian authority, you're going to create a world where my grandson and granddaughter are gonna know what it is to be Jewish in a world where the Jewish state is a pariah state.

</details>

### 结语：数字公民与责任

**观众提问**: 在充斥着虚假信息的环境下，我们该如何保持乐观？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Audience Member**: How do we stay optimistic given that there's the Trump third term and all this?

</details>

**托马斯·弗里德曼**: 现在的互联网就像一条未经分拣的河流，里面有钻石，也有生锈的罐头和碎玻璃。每个公民都应该上一门“**数字公民**”课程。如果你无法建立内在的过滤器来辨别真伪，那我们就有大麻烦了。

我曾经被问到一个最好的问题：“**上帝在网络空间吗？**”我当时的回答是：如果我们希望上帝在那里，我们就必须通过我们在那里的行为把“上帝”带去。世界越复杂，那些古老而缓慢的准则就越重要——比如“己所不欲，勿施于人”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Thomas Friedman**: Every Canadian and every American should have to take a course in digital civics. The internet is an unfiltered stream full of diamonds and rusty cans and broken glass. If you don't build the internal filter to understand the difference, then we have a real problem.

Someone once asked me: "Is God in cyberspace?" If we want God to be in cyberspace, we have to bring him there by how we behave there. The more complex the world gets, the more everything old and slow matters more than ever—the 10 Commandments, do unto others as you wish them to do unto you.

</details>

**史蒂夫·派金**: 感谢**托马斯·弗里德曼**今晚的分享。谢谢大家，晚安。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Steve Paikin**: Thank you join me in thanking the guy who answered the questions. Tom Friedman. I'm Steve Paikin. Thank you and good night.

</details>
