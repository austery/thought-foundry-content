### 生物年龄与时间年龄：衰老的衡量标准

在过去的十年里，科学家们提出了各种衡量生物年龄的方法，这与时间年龄不同。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In the last decade,
scientists have come up
with various measures of
what's called biological age,
as distinct from chronological age.</p>
</details>

所以你的时间年龄只是你的生日蛋糕上有多少根蜡烛，显然我们大多数人都很熟悉这一点。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So your chronological age is just
how many candles there
are on your birthday cake,
and obviously most of us
are familiar with that.</p>
</details>

但是生物年龄的概念是观察你的细胞内部，观察你的身体内部，并从生物学层面上计算出你的年龄。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But the idea of biological age
is to look inside your cells,
look inside your body,
and work out how old you
are on a biological level.</p>
</details>

现在，我们在这方面还不够完美，但我们确实有各种不同的衡量标准。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now, we aren't perfect at doing this yet

of different measures.</p>
</details>

我们可以使用血液测试，我们可以使用所谓的表观遗传测试，或者我们可以做一些更基本和功能性的事情，比如你的握力会随着年龄的增长而下降。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We can use blood tests,
we can use what are
called epigenetic tests,
or we can do things that are
far more sort of basic and functional,

</details>

通过将你的握力值与给定年龄的普通人进行比较，我们可以为你分配一个生物年龄值。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And by comparing the value

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

所以基因组是你的DNA，它是生命的指令手册。

</details>

而表观基因组是位于你的基因组之上的一层化学物质。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And the epigenome is a layer of chemistry
that sits on top of your genome.</p>
</details>

如果你把你的DNA看作是那本指令手册，那么表观基因组就是页边空白处的注释，是贴在侧面的小便利贴，它们告诉细胞在特定时间使用哪个DNA。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If you think of your DNA
as that instruction manual,

the notes in the margin,

at which particular time.</p>
</details>

我们知道，随着年龄的增长，这种表观基因组会发生变化，因此通过测量表观基因组的变化，你可以为某人分配一个生物年龄。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We don't know what

</details>

我们知道它们与年龄相关，但我们不知道它们是否具有因果关系。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

### 衰老的十二个标志与潜在的干预手段

在过去的10到15年中，科学家们真正开始了解衰老过程的基本潜在生物学。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Over the last 10 or 15 years,
scientists have really

the fundamental underlying

</details>

他们将此分解为衰老的12个标志。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And they broke this down

</details>

其中一个标志是衰老细胞的积累。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">One of those hallmarks is the accumulation

</details>

现在，衰老只是一个生物学技术术语，指的是“老”。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now, senescent is just a
biological technical term for old.</p>
</details>

这些细胞随着时间的推移在我们所有体内积累，科学家们注意到这些细胞似乎会引发一系列不同的疾病，随着我们年龄的增长。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And scientists identified
drugs called senolytic drugs,

</details>

他们在小鼠身上进行了试验，它们确实有效地使小鼠在生物学上更年轻。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And they tried them out in mice,

make the mice biologically younger.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Firstly, it makes them live a bit longer,
and it's a good thing
if you're slowing down
the aging process, the
basic thing you want to see.</p>
</details>

但它并没有拖延生命尽头的虚弱期。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But it's not dragging out
that period of frailty at the end of life.</p>
</details>

它们患癌症的几率更小，患心脏病的几率更小，患白内障的几率更小。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They get less cancer, they
get less heart disease,
they get fewer cataracts.</p>
</details>

这向我们表明，通过针对衰老的基本过程，通过识别像衰老细胞这样的一系列与年龄相关的问题的驱动因素，我们可以解决许多甚至可能所有这些标志。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And what this shows us
is that by targeting
the fundamental processes of aging,
by identifying something
like senescent cells
that drives a whole range
of age-related problems,
we can hit many perhaps
even all of those hallmarks.</p>
</details>

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">One of the most exciting ideas

</details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I sometimes describe this as a treatment
that has fallen through a
wormhole from the future.</p>
</details>

这就是我们可以重置细胞内部生物钟的想法，这个想法最早出现在2000年代中期。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This is the idea that we can reset
the biological clock inside of our cells,

about in the mid-2000s.</p>
</details>

一位名叫**Shinya Yamanaka**（山中伸弥：日本科学家，因发现诱导多能干细胞（iPS细胞）而获得2012年诺贝尔生理学或医学奖）的科学家试图找出如何将常规的成人身体细胞完全恢复到其生物学存在的最初状态，即它们还是胚胎的时候。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There was a scientist
called Shinya Yamanaka
who was trying to find out how to turn
regular adult body cells all the way back
to the very beginning of
their biological existence,
the time when they were an embryo.</p>
</details>

现在，他对此感兴趣的出发点是创造干细胞，一种可以创造体内任何其他类型细胞的细胞，我们将来可能会用它来进行组织修复。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Now, he was interested in this
from the point of view
of creating stem cells,
a cell that can create any
other kind of cell in the body,
which we might be able to use
for tissue repair in future.</p>
</details>

但科学家们也注意到，除了逆转这些细胞的发育时钟外，它还会逆转衰老时钟。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But scientists also noticed,

it also turns back the aging clock.</p>
</details>

接受这四种**Yamanaka factors**（山中因子：一组转录因子，能够将分化的细胞重编程为诱导多能干细胞）的细胞实际上比未接受治疗的细胞在生物学上更年轻。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Cells that are given these
four Yamanaka factors
actually are biologically younger
than cells that haven't had the treatment.</p>
</details>

因此，科学家们决定将这些Yamanaka因子基因插入小鼠体内。
