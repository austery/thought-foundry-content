### AI产业的循环融资模式

处于**AI**（Artificial Intelligence: 人工智能）热潮中心的各大公司正忙于相互投资数十亿美元。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The companies at the center of the AI boom have been busy investing billions of dollars in each other.</p>
</details>

我相信你最近在媒体上看到过那些错综复杂的图表，展示了像OpenAI这样的公司如何投资其芯片供应商，或者像Nvidia这样的芯片制造商如何投资其客户，从而使他们能够购买更多的芯片。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I'm sure you've seen the spaghetti diagrams in the media recently showing how companies like OpenAI are investing in their chip suppliers or how chip manufacturers like Nvidia are investing in their customers, enabling them to buy more chips.</p>
</details>

我第一次注意到这些交易的奇怪之处是在三月份，当时Coreweave，一家从Nvidia购买芯片、将其放入数据中心并出租计算能力的公司，提交了**IPO**（Initial Public Offering: 首次公开募股）申请。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I first noticed how strange these deals were back in March when Coreweave, a company who buys chips from Nvidia, puts them in data centers, and rents out compute, filed to go public.</p>
</details>

其IPO招股说明书显示，Nvidia拥有该公司约5%的股份。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Its IPO perspectives revealed that Nvidia owned about 5% of the company.</p>
</details>

在经历了一段漫长的IPO低迷期后，当投资者兴趣似乎不温不火时，Nvidia提出以每股40美元的价格，通过2.5亿美元的订单来锚定这笔交易。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When investor interest seemed tepid after a long IPO drought, Nvidia offered to anchor the deal at $40 a share with a $250 million order.</p>
</details>

Bryce Elder当时在《金融时报》（FT）中将这笔交易描述为**衔尾蛇**（Ouroboros: 古代象征，一条蛇或龙吞食自己的尾巴）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Bryce Elder described the deal in the FT at the time as an oruraorus, an ancient symbol of a snake or a dragon eating its own tail.</p>
</details>

一个类似的比喻可能是一根插在自己身上的延长线。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A similar metaphor might be an extension cord plugged into itself.</p>
</details>

如果你对电力不太了解，这可能看起来像一台永动机，但相信我，我试过，无论你怎么配置，它都无法为你的家用电器供电。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If you don't know much about electricity, that might look like a perpetual energy machine, but trust me, I've tried it out and no matter how you configure it, it won't power your home appliances.</p>
</details>

你只需要外部能源才能启动一切。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You just need outside energy to get things going.</p>
</details>

粗略地说，这就是当前AI基础设施融资的现状。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That roughly speaking is the current state of AI infrastructure financing.</p>
</details>

虽然这些交易的数量和规模之大，让一些投资者相信AI价值链正在迅速发展，但另一些人则对这种循环性感到担忧。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While the sheer number and size of these deals have convinced some investors that the AI value chain is developing rapidly, others are concerned by the circularity.</p>
</details>

在几乎所有图表的中心，都有两家公司：OpenAI和Nvidia。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Two companies sit near the center of nearly every diagram. Open AAI and Nvidia.</p>
</details>

它们可能都在努力确保生态系统中的每个人，从供应商到客户再到云服务提供商，都对其成功抱有既得利益。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Each is likely trying to ensure that everyone in the ecosystem from suppliers to customers to cloud providers has a vested interest in their success.</p>
</details>

OpenAI最近宣布与Oracle达成一项3000亿美元的云基础设施协议，与Broadcom达成一项100亿美元的定制芯片合作，并与主要内存供应商建立了战略联盟。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Open AAI recently announced a $300 billion cloud infrastructure agreement with Oracle, a $10 billion custom chip partnership with Broadcom, and strategic alliances with major memory suppliers.</p>
</details>

据瑞银（UBS）分析师称，OpenAI仅在内存方面的承诺就占了全球当前容量的一半。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">According to UBS analysts, OpenAI's memory commitments alone account for half of the world's current capacity.</p>
</details>

与此同时，Nvidia承诺向OpenAI投资高达1000亿美元，而OpenAI反过来将购买数百万张Nvidia的AI图形卡。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Nvidia, meanwhile, pledged up to a hundred billion dollars in investment to Open AI, who will, in turn, buy millions of Nvidia's AI graphics cards.</p>
</details>

AMD也参与其中。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">AMD is also in on the game.</p>
</details>

OpenAI同意购买价值数百亿美元的AMD芯片。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Open AAI agreed to buy tens of billions of dollars worth of AMD chips.</p>
</details>

作为回报，AMD授予OpenAI以每股1美分的价格购买其10%股票的权利，前提是AMD达到某些股价目标并且OpenAI部署其芯片。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And in return, AMD gave Open AAI the right to buy 10% of its stock for 1 cent per share, contingent on AMD hitting certain share price targets and Open AAI deploying its chips.</p>
</details>

Matt Lavine当时解释这笔交易的方式是，如果OpenAI宣布与一家上市公司建立大型合作伙伴关系，那家公司的股价就会上涨。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The way Matt Lavine explained the deal at the time was that if OpenAI announces a big partnership with a public company, that company's stock price goes up.</p>
</details>

因此，OpenAI可以现金支付芯片费用，获得股票，当交易宣布时，股票就会飙升，从而有效地补偿OpenAI的购买。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So Open AAI could just pay for the chips in cash, receive stock, and when the deal is announced, the stock would rocket, effectively reimbursing Open AI for its purchase.</p>
</details>

每个人都赢了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Everyone wins.</p>
</details>

亚马逊（Amazon）也有自己的循环版本。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Amazon has its own version of the loop.</p>
</details>

它向聊天机器人Claude背后的公司Anthropic投资了超过80亿美元，作为回报，Anthropic承诺将亚马逊作为其主要的云服务提供商。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It invested more than $8 billion in Anthropic, the company behind the claw chatbot, and in return, Anthropic committed to using Amazon as its primary cloud provider.</p>
</details>

这意味着在亚马逊的定制AI芯片上训练和运行其模型，从**AWS**（Amazon Web Services: 亚马逊云计算服务）租用计算能力，并将Claude集成到亚马逊的企业AI平台Amazon Bedrock中。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That means training and running its models on Amazon's custom AI chips, renting compute from AWS, and integrating Claude into Amazon Bedrock, the company's enterprise AI platform.</p>
</details>

实际上，亚马逊正在资助一家将使用其芯片、在其云上运行并帮助销售其服务的公司。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In effect, Amazon is funding a company that will use its chips, run on its cloud, and help sell its services.</p>
</details>

现在，谷歌（Google）也加入了这个循环。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And now, Google is getting in in the loop, too.</p>
</details>

Anthropic刚刚宣布了一项协议，将获取多达100万个谷歌的**TPU**（Tensor Processing Units: 谷歌开发的专用人工智能芯片），到2026年将带来超过1吉瓦的计算能力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Anthropic just announced a deal to access up to 1 million of Google's TPUs, bringing over a gigawatt of compute capacity online by 2026.</p>
</details>

这项安排价值数百亿美元，使谷歌成为主要的投资者和基础设施提供商。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The arrangement is worth tens of billions of dollars and positions Google as both a major investor and infrastructure provider.</p>
</details>

Anthropic表示，它选择谷歌的芯片是因为它们的效率和性能，但这项交易也减少了它对Nvidia和亚马逊的依赖。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Anthropic says it chose Google's chips for their efficiency and performance, but the deal also reduces its reliance on Nvidia and Amazon.</p>
</details>

谷歌已经向Anthropic投资了30亿美元。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Google has already invested $3 billion in Anthropic.</p>
</details>

亚马逊已承诺80亿美元。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Amazon has pledged 8 billion.</p>
</details>

两家公司现在都提供云服务、定制芯片和战略资本。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Both companies now provide cloud services, custom chips, and strategic capital.</p>
</details>

Anthropic坚称它只是在推行多云战略。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Anthropic insists that it's just pursuing a multicloud strategy.</p>
</details>

但很难忽视它与美国三大云服务提供商之间如此深度的纠缠，而这三家公司现在都对其成功抱有经济利益。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But it's hard to ignore how deeply entangled it has become with all three of the largest US cloud providers, each of whom now has a financial interest in its success.</p>
</details>

还有埃隆·马斯克（Elon Musk），他似乎相信构建**AGI**（Artificial General Intelligence: 通用人工智能）的最佳方式是让他的公司相互“约会”。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Then there's Elon Musk who seems to believe that the best way to build artificial general intelligence is to have his companies date each other.</p>
</details>

他的AI初创公司XAI收购了Twitter（或称“万能应用”），后者为他的聊天机器人Grok提供实时数据，Grok有时被称为“麦加希特勒”。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">His AI startup XAI acquired Twitter or the everything app which supplies realtime data to Grog, his chatbot, sometimes referred to as Mecca Hitler.</p>
</details>

他的电动汽车公司特斯拉（Tesla）在汽车中使用了这个聊天机器人，可能明年还会用于其机器人。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Tesla, his electric car company, uses the chatbot in its cars and possibly its robots, which are coming next year.</p>
</details>

马斯克拥有XAI的多数股权，XAI最近从他手中收购了Twitter。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Musk owns a majority stake in XAI, which recently bought Twitter from him.</p>
</details>

他拥有特斯拉的少数股权，现在希望特斯拉的股东投资XAI。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">He owns a minority stake in Tesla and now wants Tesla shareholders to invest in XAI.</p>
</details>

这并非严格意义上的乱伦，但我们可能需要请埃罗尔·马斯克（Errol Musk）来解释为什么这没问题。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It's not incestuous exactly, but we'd have to get Errol Musk to explain why it's okay.</p>
</details>

整个事情开始看起来不像是一场科技繁荣，而更像是一条由风险资本和电力构成的莫比乌斯带。

<details>

### AI基础设施的巨大成本与盈利挑战

麦肯锡（McKinsey）预测，仅未来五年，芯片、数据中心和能源方面的**资本支出**（Capex: Capital Expenditure）将达到5.2万亿美元。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">McKenzie forecasts $5.2 trillion in capex for chips, data centers, and energy over the next 5 years alone.</p>
</details>

贝恩（Bain）表示，我们需要看到AI公司每年2万亿美元的收入才能证明这些支出的合理性。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Bane says that we'll need to see $2 trillion in annual revenue from AI companies just to justify that spending.</p>
</details>

OpenAI目前的收入约为130亿美元，本质上是一个烧钱的炉子。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Open AI has about $13 billion in revenues today and is essentially a money furnace.</p>
</details>

Anthropic是一个规模较小的烧钱炉。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Anthropic is a smaller money furnace.</p>
</details>

Nvidia利润丰厚，但并非1000亿美元的利润。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Nvidia is very profitable but not $100 billion profitable.</p>
</details>

所以问题变成了：谁来支付所有这些费用？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So the question becomes who's going to pay for all of this?</p>
</details>

这些交易相互关联的性质，以及我们需要用错综复杂的图表来理解它们的原因，引发了对循环融资的担忧。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The interconnected nature of these deals, the reason we need spaghetti diagrams to understand them has raised concerns about circular financing.</p>
</details>

公司之间相互投资，购买彼此的产品，并推高彼此的股价。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Companies are investing in each other, buying each other's products, and pushing up each other's stock prices.</p>
</details>

投资者现在正在质疑，如果AI需求或变现未能达到预期，这些相互依赖关系是否会带来风险。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Investors are now asking whether these interdependencies could pose risks if AI demand or monetization falls short of investor expectations.</p>
</details>

AI行业的投资结构开始类似于我们以前见过的一些模式，只是不在硅谷。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The AI industry's investment structure is starting to resemble something we've seen before, just not in Silicon Valley.</p>
</details>

在战后的日本，建立了大型工业集团，被称为**企业集团**（Keiretsu: 日本战后围绕银行和贸易公司建立的大型工业集团），通常围绕银行和贸易公司，公司之间相互持股并协调供应链。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In post-war Japan, large industrial groups known as kiritsu were built, usually around banks and trading houses with companies taking stakes in each other and coordinating supply chains.</p>
</details>

韩国的**财阀**（Chaebol: 韩国以家族控制为核心的大型综合性企业集团）体系也遵循了类似的模式，但由家族而非银行控制。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">South Korea's chaiball system followed a similar pattern, but with families in control rather than banks.</p>
</details>

这些模式并非为了竞争。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These models weren't about competition.</p>
</details>

它们至少最初是为了在资本受限的经济体中生存。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They were, at least initially, about survival in capital constrained economies.</p>
</details>

与你所依赖的企业建立紧密的财务关系似乎是合理的，这样你的供应链就能得到保障。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It seemed to make sense to have tight financial relationships with the businesses you relied upon so that your supply chains were secure.</p>
</details>

企业集团和财阀模式常因掩盖金融风险、资本错配和支撑缺乏竞争力的公司而受到批评。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The keratu and chibball models were often criticized for obscuring financial risk, misallocating capital, and propping up uncompetitive firms.</p>
</details>

当日本的资产泡沫在1990年代破裂时，相互持股的复杂网络使得不良投资几乎无法解开。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When Japan's asset bubble burst in the 1990s, the tangled web of crossholdings made it almost impossible to unwind bad bets.</p>
</details>

今天的AI巨头绝不缺乏资本，但它们正在构建这种相互依赖的网络。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Today's AI giants are by no means short on capital, but they are assembling these webs of mutual dependence.</p>
</details>

问题是，今天的AI巨头是否正在构建一个类似脆弱的结构，它从外部看起来稳定，但却依赖于持续的新资本流入才能维持运转。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The question is whether today's AI giants are building a similarly fragile structure which looks stable from the outside but depends on a constant influx of new capital to keep the lights on.</p>
</details>

### AI对能源的巨大需求

当我们看这些数字时，它们几乎像是编造出来的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When we look at the numbers, they almost seem made up.</p>
</details>

OpenAI的“星门”项目于今年一月在白宫活动上宣布，这是一项5000亿美元的计划，旨在全美建设10吉瓦的AI数据中心容量。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Open AI Stargate project announced this January at a White House event is a $500 billion plan to build 10 gigawatts of AI data center capacity across the US.</p>
</details>

首先，我们用吉瓦来谈论数据中心，这有点疯狂。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It's firstly kind of crazy that we're talking about data centers in terms of gigawatts.</p>
</details>

根据美国能源部网站的数据，一个典型的核电站平均产生1吉瓦的电力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">According to the US Department of Energy website, a typical nuclear power plant produces 1 gawatt of power on average.</p>
</details>
