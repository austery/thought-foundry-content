### 李飞飞：AI的下一个十年是空间智能

11月10日，李飞飞亲自撰文，认为**生成式AI**（Generative AI: 能够生成文本、图像、音频等新内容的AI模型）的下一个战场是**空间智能**（Spatial Intelligence: 理解、推理和操作三维空间信息的能力）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">On November 10th, Li Feifei personally authored an article, positing that the next frontier for generative AI is spatial intelligence.</p>
</details>

在文章中，她首次系统性地解释了什么是空间智能，它为什么如此重要，以及如何构建能够解锁空间智能的**世界模型**（World Models: 能够理解、推理、生成并与复杂世界交互的AI模型）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In the article, she systematically explained for the first time what spatial intelligence is, why it is so important, and how to build world models capable of unlocking spatial intelligence.</p>
</details>

李飞飞还指出了当前AI存在的致命缺陷：虽然AI掌握了海量的抽象知识，但是对于物理世界的常识和空间规律，它几乎一无所知。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Li Feifei also pointed out a critical flaw in current AI: although AI possesses vast amounts of abstract knowledge, it is almost entirely ignorant of common sense and spatial rules in the physical world.</p>
</details>

这种缺陷直接卡死了AI升级的大动脉。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This deficiency directly obstructs the main artery for AI's advancement.</p>
</details>

她敲响警钟，提出AI的下一个十年的真正突破不再是堆砌文字，而是要解锁空间智能，这才是连接感知、想象和行动的终极能力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">She sounded the alarm, proposing that the true breakthrough for AI in the next decade will no longer be about accumulating text, but about unlocking spatial intelligence, as this is the ultimate capability that connects perception, imagination, and action.</p>
</details>

文章发布之后，立即在社交平台引发了热议。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">After its publication, the article immediately sparked widespread discussion on social platforms.</p>
</details>

### 图灵的愿景与当前AI的局限

1950年，当计算机还只是用来做自动运算和简单的逻辑判断时，**艾伦·图灵**（Alan Turing: 英国数学家、逻辑学家，计算机科学和人工智能的先驱）在《计算机器与智能》一文中，就提出了一个影响深远的问题：机器能思考吗？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In 1950, when computers were only used for automatic calculations and simple logical judgments, Alan Turing, in his article "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," posed a profound question: Can machines think?</p>
</details>

在那个时代，这个问题显得有些天马行空，但是图灵凭借着非凡的想象力洞察到，智能或许不必天生，而是可以通过技术构建出来。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At that time, this question seemed somewhat fantastical, but Turing, with his extraordinary imagination, insightfully recognized that intelligence might not be innate but could be constructed through technology.</p>
</details>

正是这个想法，催生了后来被称为人工智能的科学领域。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It was this idea that gave rise to the scientific field later known as artificial intelligence.</p>
</details>

75年过去，AI确实取得了翻天覆地的进步，尤其是最近几年，以**大语言模型**（Large Language Models - LLM: 基于海量文本数据训练，能理解和生成人类语言的AI模型）为代表的生成式AI，已经从实验室走进了我们的日常生活。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Seventy-five years later, AI has indeed made revolutionary progress, especially in recent years, with generative AI, represented by large language models, moving from laboratories into our daily lives.</p>
</details>

这些AI展现出了曾经被认为不可能的能力，但是如果我们冷静下来审视，就会发现当前的AI依然存在巨大的局限。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These AIs have demonstrated capabilities once thought impossible, but if we calmly examine them, we will find that current AI still has significant limitations.</p>
</details>

李飞飞在文章中提到了几个关键问题：首先，自主机器人的愿景还远未实现，我们在科幻电影里看到的机器人场景，至今还停留在实验室或者高度受限的场景中。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Li Feifei mentioned several key issues in the article: firstly, the vision of autonomous robots is still far from being realized, and the robot scenarios we see in science fiction films are still confined to laboratories or highly restricted environments.</p>
</details>

其次，AI在加速科学研究方面的潜力还没有完全释放出来，很多研究的关键步骤依然需要人类手动完成。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Secondly, AI's potential in accelerating scientific research has not yet been fully unleashed, with many critical steps in research still requiring manual completion by humans.</p>
</details>

最后，AI还无法真正理解并且赋能人类的创作者，无论是在分子化学、建筑设计还是电影建模等领域，AI都没能成为真正的帮手，因为它缺乏对空间的理解。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Finally, AI is still unable to truly understand and empower human creators; whether in molecular chemistry, architectural design, or film modeling, AI has not become a true helper because it lacks an understanding of space.</p>
</details>

### 空间智能：人类认知的基石

为什么会出现这些问题？李飞飞认为，核心原因在于当前的AI没有掌握空间智能，而这种能力恰恰是人类认知的基础。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Why do these problems arise? Li Feifei believes the core reason is that current AI has not mastered spatial intelligence, a capability that is precisely the foundation of human cognition.</p>
</details>

要搞清楚这一点，我们得先回到智能是如何进化的这个根本问题上。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">To understand this, we must first return to the fundamental question of how intelligence evolves.</p>
</details>

提到智能，很多人会首先想到语言、逻辑、数学这些能力，但是实际上，空间智能才是更基础、更古老的智能形式。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When intelligence is mentioned, many people first think of abilities like language, logic, and mathematics, but in reality, spatial intelligence is a more fundamental and ancient form of intelligence.</p>
</details>

李飞飞在文章中把空间智能称为人类认知的脚手架，它就像建筑施工时的脚手架一样，支撑着我们对世界的感知、理解、推理和创造。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In the article, Li Feifei refers to spatial intelligence as the scaffolding of human cognition; it supports our perception, understanding, reasoning, and creation of the world, much like scaffolding during building construction.</p>
</details>

从进化的角度来看，动物们通过感官感知世界的简单行为，就已经开启了空间智能的进化之路。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">From an evolutionary perspective, the simple act of animals perceiving the world through their senses has already initiated the evolutionary path of spatial intelligence.</p>
</details>

随着进化，动物的神经系统越来越复杂，逐渐形成了从感知到行动的核心循环。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As evolution progressed, animal nervous systems became increasingly complex, gradually forming a core loop from perception to action.</p>
</details>

许多科学家推测，正是这种循环驱动了智能的进化，最终让人类成为了既能感知、又能学习、还能思考和创造的物种。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Many scientists speculate that this loop drove the evolution of intelligence, ultimately making humans a species capable of perceiving, learning, thinking, and creating.</p>
</details>

而空间智能，就是这个循环的核心。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And spatial intelligence is at the core of this loop.</p>
</details>

对于我们每个人来说，空间智能其实是一种我们每天都在用，但是却不自觉的能力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For each of us, spatial intelligence is actually a capability we use every day without conscious awareness.</p>
</details>

比如我们对空间布局的认知、对空间关系的实时判断、基于长期经验形成的空间直觉以及最基础的空间推理，它们背后是对复杂的空间感知、推理和预测，而这正是当前AI所最缺乏的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For example, our perception of spatial layout, real-time judgment of spatial relationships, spatial intuition formed from long-term experience, and the most basic spatial reasoning all rely on complex spatial perception, inference, and prediction, which is precisely what current AI most lacks.</p>
</details>

更重要的是，空间智能还是人类文明进步的关键驱动力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">More importantly, spatial intelligence is also a key driving force behind the progress of human civilization.</p>
</details>

### 空间智能驱动人类文明进步的三个历史案例

李飞飞在文章中举了三个极具代表性的例子。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Li Feifei cited three highly representative examples in the article.</p>
</details>

第一个例子是古希腊学者**埃拉托色尼**（Eratosthenes: 古希腊数学家、地理学家、天文学家，首次计算出地球周长）测量地球周长。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The first example is the ancient Greek scholar Eratosthenes measuring the Earth's circumference.</p>
</details>

公元前3世纪，埃拉托色尼发现了一个有趣的现象：在夏至这天，埃及的赛伊尼地区，太阳会直射到深井的底部，这时的物体是没有影子的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In the 3rd century BCE, Eratosthenes observed an interesting phenomenon: on the summer solstice, in the region of Syene, Egypt, the sun would shine directly to the bottom of deep wells, and objects would cast no shadows.</p>
</details>

而同一时间，在北方的亚历山大港，一根竖杆的影子会形成7度的夹角。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At the same time, in Alexandria to the north, a vertical pole would cast a shadow forming a 7-degree angle.</p>
</details>

凭借空间智能，埃拉托色尼把这两个看似无关的现象转化成了一个几何问题，他认为这7度的夹角，其实是因为地球是球形，两个地点在地球表面的纬度差异导致的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Utilizing spatial intelligence, Eratosthenes transformed these two seemingly unrelated phenomena into a geometric problem, reasoning that the 7-degree angle was due to the Earth being spherical and the latitudinal difference between the two locations on its surface.</p>
</details>

于是，他通过测量亚历山大港到赛伊尼的距离，再乘以50，最终计算出地球的周长约为4万公里。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Thus, by measuring the distance from Alexandria to Syene and multiplying it by 50, he ultimately calculated the Earth's circumference to be approximately 40,000 kilometers.</p>
</details>

这个结果和现代科技测量的实际值惊人地接近。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This result is astonishingly close to the actual value measured by modern technology.</p>
</details>

第二个例子是**詹姆斯·哈格里夫斯**（James Hargreaves: 英国发明家，珍妮纺纱机的发明者）发明**珍妮纺纱机**（Spinning Jenny: 18世纪中期英国发明的多锭纺纱机，显著提高了纺纱效率）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The second example is James Hargreaves inventing the Spinning Jenny.</p>
</details>

18世纪中期，英国的纺织业还是手工操作，一个工人用传统纺纱机一次只能纺一根线，效率很低。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In the mid-18th century, the British textile industry was still manual; a worker using a traditional spinning wheel could only spin one thread at a time, which was very inefficient.</p>
</details>

哈格里夫斯在观察妻子纺纱的时候，突然有了一个空间层面的灵感：如果把多个纺锤并排排列在同一个框架里，是不是就能让一个工人同时纺多根线呢？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">While observing his wife spinning, Hargreaves suddenly had a spatial insight: what if multiple spindles were arranged side by side in the same frame, allowing one worker to spin multiple threads simultaneously?</p>
</details>

基于这个想法，他发明了珍妮纺纱机，把纺纱效率提高了8倍。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Based on this idea, he invented the Spinning Jenny, increasing spinning efficiency eightfold.</p>
</details>

这个发明也直接推动了工业革命的爆发，让人类从手工生产进入了机器生产的时代。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This invention also directly spurred the Industrial Revolution, transitioning humanity from manual production to the era of machine production.</p>
</details>

第三个例子是**詹姆斯·沃森**（James Watson: 美国分子生物学家，DNA双螺旋结构的共同发现者）和**弗朗西斯·克里克**（Francis Crick: 英国分子生物学家、物理学家，DNA双螺旋结构的共同发现者）发现**DNA双螺旋结构**。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The third example is James Watson and Francis Crick discovering the DNA double helix structure.</p>
</details>

20世纪50年代，科学家们已经知道DNA是遗传物质，但是不知道它的结构是什么样的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In the 1950s, scientists already knew that DNA was genetic material, but its structure remained unknown.</p>
</details>

沃森和克里克没有依赖复杂的仪器，而是通过构建3D分子模型来寻找答案。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Watson and Crick did not rely on complex instruments but sought answers by building 3D molecular models.</p>
</details>

他们用金属板代表DNA的碱基，用金属丝代表连接它们的化学键，不断调整这些组件的空间排列，直到找到符合所有实验数据的结构。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They used metal plates to represent DNA bases and metal wires to represent the chemical bonds connecting them, continuously adjusting the spatial arrangement of these components until they found a structure that matched all experimental data.</p>
</details>

最终，他们发现DNA是由两条反向平行的链组成的双螺旋结构。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Ultimately, they discovered that DNA is a double helix structure composed of two antiparallel strands.</p>
</details>

这个发现揭开了生命遗传的奥秘，为现代分子生物学奠定了基础。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This discovery unveiled the mysteries of genetic inheritance and laid the foundation for modern molecular biology.</p>
</details>

这三个例子跨越了几千年的历史，但是都指向了同一个结论：空间智能是人类理解世界、改造世界的核心能力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These three examples span thousands of years of history, but all point to the same conclusion: spatial intelligence is humanity's core ability to understand and transform the world.</p>
</details>

无论是探索自然规律、发明新工具，还是推动科学革命，都离不开对空间关系的感知、推理和创造。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Whether exploring natural laws, inventing new tools, or driving scientific revolutions, all depend on the perception, reasoning, and creation of spatial relationships.</p>
</details>

而当前的AI之所以无法在这些领域发挥作用，正是因为它缺乏这种能力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The reason current AI cannot function in these areas is precisely because it lacks this capability.</p>
</details>

### 构建世界模型：AI的未来解决方案

那么，要想让AI拥有空间智能，我们需要做什么？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So, what do we need to do to enable AI to possess spatial intelligence?</p>
</details>

李飞飞和她的团队提出了一个关键的解决方案：构建世界模型。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Li Feifei and her team proposed a key solution: building world models.</p>
</details>

这种模型的核心能力是能够理解、推理、生成并且与语义、物理、几何和动态上都极为复杂的世界进行交互，这远远超出了当前大语言模型的能力范围。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The core capability of such models is to understand, reason, generate, and interact with a world that is extremely complex semantically, physically, geometrically, and dynamically, far exceeding the capabilities of current large language models.</p>
</details>

那么，一个真正的世界模型需要具备哪些核心能力？李飞飞在文章中明确提出了三点。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So, what core capabilities must a true world model possess? Li Feifei clearly outlined three points in the article.</p>
</details>

第一个核心能力是**生成式**（Generative）：它能根据语义或者感知指令，生成无穷无尽、多样化的模拟世界，而且这些世界必须满足感知一致性、几何一致性和物理一致性。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The first core capability is generative: it must be able to generate endless, diverse simulated worlds based on semantic or perceptual instructions, and these worlds must satisfy perceptual, geometric, and physical consistency.</p>
</details>

在这个方面，目前学术界正在探索的一个关键问题是：世界模型应该如何表示这些空间信息？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In this regard, a key question currently being explored in academia is: how should world models represent this spatial information?</p>
</details>

是用隐式表示，通过神经网络的参数间接存储空间信息，还是用显式表示，直接存储物体的3D坐标、形状、物理属性呢？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Should it use implicit representation, indirectly storing spatial information through neural network parameters, or explicit representation, directly storing objects' 3D coordinates, shapes, and physical properties?</p>
</details>

李飞飞对此的观点是，两者都需要。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Li Feifei's view on this is that both are needed.</p>
</details>

第二个核心能力是**多模态**（Multimodal）：我们人类在理解世界的时候，会同时使用视觉、嗅觉、触觉、听觉等多种感官。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The second core capability is multimodal: when humans understand the world, we simultaneously use multiple senses such as sight, smell, touch, and hearing.</p>
</details>

这种多模态融合的能力是我们理解世界的关键。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This ability to integrate multiple modalities is crucial for our understanding of the world.</p>
</details>

同样，世界模型也需要具备这种能力。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Similarly, world models also need to possess this capability.</p>
</details>

当前的多模态模型虽然也能处理多种输入，但是它的核心还是语言模型，图像、视频等模态只是辅助输入，并没有真正与语言模态深度融合。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Although current multimodal models can process various inputs, their core remains a language model, with modalities like images and videos serving only as auxiliary inputs, not truly deeply integrated with the language modality.</p>
</details>

而世界模型的设计理念是原生多模态，也就是说，各种模态在模型中是平等的，它们共同服务于理解和构建世界这个核心目标。
