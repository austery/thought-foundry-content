### 服装：塑造感知的无声语言

是什么让一个人变得**邪恶**？是他们的肤色吗？是我们被告知的故事吗？还是他们所穿的衣服？我们是如何做出这些判断的？我们依赖哪些线索？我们又带着哪些假设，有时甚至没有意识到？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">What makes someone wicked? Is it the color of their skin? Is it the story we've been told? Or is it what they wear? How do we make those judgments? What clues do we rely on? What assumptions do we carry, sometimes without even realizing it?</p>
</details>

作为一名**服装设计师**，我的工作就是利用这些假设，无论是好是坏。人们常以为我的工作就是布料、亮片、连衣裙、纽扣，也许还有一顶漂亮的帽子。但实际上，我的工作关乎**感知**。我是一名**讲故事的人**，而我的媒介，我的语言，就是服装。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As a costume designer, it's my job to use those assumptions, for better or for worse. People often assume my job is all fabric and sequins, dresses, buttons, maybe a good hat. In truth, my job is about perception. I am a storyteller, and my medium, my language, is clothing.</p>
</details>

通过轮廓、色彩和质地，我在他们开口说话之前就塑造了你对一个人的看法。我决定你是会好奇地前倾，还是会带着怀疑退缩。当我让一个角色穿上猩红色，或用天鹅绒包裹他们，或用锐利的黑色线条裁剪他们的服装时，我是在要求你瞬间、无声地对他们产生某种感觉。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Through silhouette, color and texture. I shape how you see someone before they speak a word. I decide whether you lean forward with curiosity or pull back with suspicion. When I put a character in a shade of scarlet, or wrap them in velvet, or cut them up in sharp black lines, I'm asking you to feel something about them, instantly, silently.</p>
</details>

这正是让我着迷的地方：简单的布料如何能告诉我们谁是英雄，谁是邪恶的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That's what fascinates me. How simple fabric can tell us who is a hero and who is wicked.</p>
</details>

### 服装中的平行叙事与潜意识语言

在每个项目中，我都会寻找一个**主题钩子**。一些人性化的东西，一些能让我在故事中找到自己的东西。我不仅仅是在装饰一个角色，我是在讲述一个平行的故事，一个存在于服装中的故事。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I seek out a thematic hook in every project. Something human, something that allows me to find myself in the story. I'm not just decorating a character, I'm telling a parallel story, one that lives in the clothing.</p>
</details>

服装并非静止不变。它们随身体而动，它们会演变。有时它们甚至会撒谎。它们反映着成长、冲突和解决。我可以通过一件夹克的合身度、下摆的磨损、布料在压力下破裂的方式，来传达一个完整的情感弧线。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Costumes are not static. They move with the body. They evolve. Sometimes they even tell lies. They reflect growth, conflict, resolution. I can telegraph an entire emotional arc in the fit of a jacket, the fraying of a hem, the way fabric breaks down under stress.</p>
</details>

非凡之处在于：即使你没有意识到，你也能感受到它。服装是一种**潜意识语言**。我正在利用你的感知能力来引导你穿越故事。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">What's extraordinary is this: you feel it, even if you don’t realize it. Costume is a subconscious language. I'm using your power of perception to lead you through the story.</p>
</details>

### 《汉密尔顿》：通过现代视角重塑历史

这种对感知的操控将我带到了**《汉密尔顿》**（Hamilton: 一部关于美国开国元勋亚历山大·汉密尔顿生平的著名音乐剧）。其核心是“我们与他们”的故事：殖民者对抗帝国，移民对抗既有体制。但**林-曼努尔·米兰达**（Lin-Manuel Miranda: 《汉密尔顿》的创作者）交给了我一个激进的任务。他说：“我希望观众能通过今天的视角来看待我们的**开国元勋**（Founding Fathers: 指美国独立战争时期建立美国的领导人）。”

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This manipulation of perception carried me to "Hamilton." At its core, "Hamilton" is a story of us versus them. Colonists versus empire. Immigrants versus the establishment. But Lin-Manuel Miranda handed me something radical. He said, "I want the audience to see our Founding Fathers through today's lens."</p>
</details>

所以想象一下这个挑战：黑人和棕色人种穿着18世纪的外套和马裤，说唱着革命。历史在实时中被重新构想。我设计了那个时代的轮廓，是的，但有所克制。大部分服装采用中性色调，同时让他们的肤色显露出来，让他们的声音力量在舞台中央真实回响。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So imagine that challenge. Black and brown bodies dressed in 18-century coats and breeches, rapping about revolution. History reimagined in real time. I design period silhouettes, yes, but with restraint. Neutral tones on much of the ensemble, while allowing their skin to show through, and the power of their voice to ring true in center stage.</p>
</details>

然后，作为对比，**乔治三世国王**（King George III: 英国国王，在美国独立战争期间统治英国）身着全套盛装出现，戴着假发，佩戴珠宝，由他的肖像画家描绘得完美无瑕。他是旧世界，他们是新世界。这种视觉上的二元性——现代身体穿着时代服装，与一个被完整保留其所有浮华的男人形成对比——正是让民主故事的展开如此引人入胜的原因。它利用历史上的熟悉事物来构建我们国家如何形成的现代观念。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And then, in contrast, King George III appears in full regalia, bewigged, jewelled, painted in perfect detail from his portraitist. He is the Old World, they are the New. That visual duality, modern bodies in period shape, versus one man preserved in all his pomp, is what makes the story of democracy unfolding so compelling. It takes the historically familiar to frame a modern perception of how our nation was formed.</p>
</details>

然后，**托马斯·杰斐逊**（Thomas Jefferson: 美国第三任总统，独立宣言主要起草人）突然出现了。我们的导演**汤米·凯尔**（Tommy Kail: 《汉密尔顿》导演）和我决定，让他成为一个摇滚明星。于是杰斐逊身着华丽的紫色服装，模仿流行偶像的形象大步走上舞台。这并非偶然，而是一种宣言。历史并非枯燥或静止的，它是鲜活的，富有魅力的，复杂的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And suddenly, there's Thomas Jefferson. Tommy Kail, our director, and I, decided, let's make him a rock star. So on struts Jefferson in flamboyant purple, modeled after a pop icon. Not by chance. That was a statement. History isn't dry or static. It's alive. It's charismatic. It's complicated.</p>
</details>

即使是微妙的对比，比如汉密尔顿鲜艳的绿色西装与伯尔柔和的葡萄干色调服装。我是在埋下伏笔，设置一场视觉上的对决。直到最后，两人并肩而立，都穿着黑色斗篷，力量均等，悲剧均等，在历史的记忆中达到平衡，而非在生活中。服装不仅仅是装饰，它们是评论，是一种提问：这是谁的历史？谁有权拥有它？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Even in subtle contrast, like Hamilton's vibrant green suits versus Burr's muted raisin palette. I was planting seeds, setting up a visual duel. Until finally, at the end, the two men stand together, both in black capes, equal in power, equal in tragedy, balanced, not in life, but in history's memory. The costumes weren't decoration. They were commentary, a way to ask whose history is this? And who gets to own it?</p>
</details>

### 《西区故事》：瓦解刻板印象，揭示城市悲剧

从开国元勋的故事，我跳到了斯皮尔伯格的**《西区故事》**（West Side Story: 一部设定在1950年代纽约的经典音乐剧和电影）中的1950年代纽约。同样是“我们与他们”：**喷气帮**（Jets: 《西区故事》中白人帮派）对抗**鲨鱼帮**（Sharks: 《西区故事》中波多黎各裔帮派），白人对抗波多黎各人。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">From the Founding Fathers, I jumped to 1950s New York with Spielberg's "West Side Story." Again, us versus them, Jets versus Sharks, white versus Puerto Rican.</p>
</details>

对于喷气帮，我倾向于展现蓝领的硬朗。牛仔布、Polo衫、运动鞋，这是扎根于城市水泥丛林中男孩们的制服。对于鲨鱼帮，我转向了拉丁纺织品，鲜艳的花卉图案，灵感来自阳光和大海的色彩，但剪裁优雅，充满抱负。这些年轻人正在为他们尚未拥有的生活而着装。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For the Jets, I leaned into blue-collar toughness. Denim, polos, sneakers, the uniform of boys rooted in concrete. For the Sharks, I turned to Latin textiles, vibrant florals, colors inspired by sun and sea, but cut with elegance and aspiration. These young men and women were dressing for a life they were yet to claim.</p>
</details>

我拒绝了认为这两个帮派只是彼此的镜像，仅仅因语言和肤色而分离的冲动。同时，我坚持了那些标志性的元素，比如**安妮塔**（Anita: 《西区故事》中的主要女性角色）的裙子，但又赋予了它我自己的特色。然后是体育馆里的舞蹈，那个两个世界碰撞的标志性场景。我让色彩相互融合，暖色调与冷色调交织，布料的线条相互呼应。因为即使在冲突中，文化也会交融，边界从不像我们假装的那样固定不变。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I rejected the impulse that these two gangs are just mirror images of each other, separated by language and skin color. At the same time, I clung to what was iconic, like Anita's dress, but gave it my own twist. And then the dance at the gym, that iconic scene where the two worlds collide. I let the colors bleed together, warm tones crossing cool, fabric echoing across lines. Because even in conflict cultures mix, the borders are never as fixed as we pretend.</p>
</details>

在这里，设计本可以延续刻板印象，但我的目标是**瓦解它们**，赋予双方尊严、真实性和复杂性。《西区故事》的悲剧并非在于这两方是如此不同。真正的**反派**是城市本身，一个发展和**位移**（displacement: 指人口因城市发展等原因被迫迁离原居住地）的系统，它正在撕裂他们的社区。两个帮派都在为他们无论如何都会失去的土地而战。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Design here could have perpetuated stereotypes, but my goal was to dismantle them, to give both sides dignity, authenticity, complexity. The tragedy of "West Side Story" isn't that these two sides were so different. The real antagonist was the city itself, a system of development and displacement that was tearing their neighborhoods apart. Both gangs were fighting for ground they would lose regardless.</p>
</details>

服装让我能够强调这个真相。美不在于分离，而在于融合。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Costume allowed me to underline that truth. The beauty wasn't in the separation, it was in the blend.</p>
</details>

### 《魔法坏女巫》：挑战“邪恶”的定义

接着是**《魔法坏女巫》**（Wicked: 一部基于《绿野仙踪》角色创作的著名音乐剧和电影）。**艾芙芭**（Elphaba: 《魔法坏女巫》中被误解的“西方坏女巫”）拥有绿色的皮肤，她穿着黑色。我们对**《绿野仙踪》**（The Wizard of Oz: 经典的儿童文学作品和电影）的故事非常熟悉，它告诉我们西方坏女巫是邪恶的。在她旁边，**格林达**（Glinda: 《魔法坏女巫》中善良的“北方好女巫”）闪耀着粉色和亮片。这些颜色似乎确认了谁是好人，谁是坏人。或者我们是这样认为的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Then came "Wicked." Elphaba, the Wicked Witch of the West, has green skin. She wears black. We're very familiar with the "Wizard of Oz" story that tells us the Wicked Witch of the West is evil. Beside her, Glinda sparkles in pink and glitter. These colors confirm who is good and who is wicked. Or so we think.</p>
</details>

但当我更仔细地阅读剧本时，我意识到艾芙芭是聪明的，富有同情心的，她被误解了。而格林达，另一方面，并不总是那么善良。我不是在设计刻板印象，我是在设计问题。谁决定谁值得尊重，谁被排斥？谁决定谁被珍爱，谁被孤立？谁决定谁属于这里，谁被驱逐？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But when I read the script more closely, I realized Elphaba is intelligent. She’s compassionate. She’s misunderstood. Glinda, on the other side, isn't always kind. I wasn't designing stereotypes. I was designing questions. Who decides who is worthy of respect and who is shunned? Who decides who is cherished and who is ostracized? Who decides who belongs and who is cast out?</p>
</details>

作为一个进入一个并非总是乐于接受我存在的领域的黑人同性恋者，我深知被**异化**（othered: 指将某人或某群体视为异类或局外人）的感觉，深知被第一眼误判的感觉。也许这就是为什么我会被边缘人物的故事所吸引。《魔法坏女巫》不仅仅是关于一个女巫，它是关于任何一个从未开口就被评判过的人。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As a black gay man entering into a field that wasn't always comfortable with my presence, I know what it feels like to be othered, to be misjudged at first glance. Maybe that's why I'm drawn to stories about marginalized characters. "Wicked" isn't just about a witch. It's about anyone who has ever been judged without speaking a word.</p>
</details>

### 设计的力量：重塑感知与“邪恶”

为什么要如此精心设计服装？因为服装承载着记忆。一件连衣裙、一把椅子、一盏灯，这些物品都留下了制造者的指纹。它们捕捉了特定时刻的文化，它们体现了我们的愿望和偏见。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Why put so much care into costumes? Because clothing carries memory. A dress, a chair, a lamp, these things hold the fingerprints of their makers. They capture a culture at a particular moment in time. They embody our aspirations and our biases.</p>
</details>

我们穿的衣服，我们坐的家具，我们挂在墙上的艺术品，这些也都是**服装**。它们塑造了我们的身份，它们创造了文化。设计从不是中立的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The clothes we wear, the furniture we sit on, the art we hang on our walls, these things are costumes too. They shape our identity. They create culture. Design is never neutral.</p>
</details>

所以我再次提出这个问题：是什么让一个人变得邪恶？不是他们的肤色，不是我们被告知的故事，也不是他们所穿的衣服。是我们的**感知**。是我们被赋予的“服装”，以及我们是否选择相信它。我的工作，我的乐趣，在于让你重新构想这些问题的答案。因为邪恶可以被设计，如果它可以被设计，那么也许我们可以一起重新设计它。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So I ask the question again. What makes someone wicked? It isn't the color of their skin. It isn't the story that we've been told. And it isn't what they wear. It's our perception. It's the costume we've been handed and whether we choose to believe it. My job, my joy, is in making you reimagine the answers to those questions. Because wickedness can be designed. And if it can be designed, then maybe together, it can be redesigned.</p>
</details>

谢谢大家。（掌声）
