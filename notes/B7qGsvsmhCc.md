### 中国内战：二十世纪的关键事件

回溯历史，**中国内战**（Chinese Civil War: 1945年至1949年中国共产党与中国国民党之间进行的内战）或许是20世纪发生的最重要事件之一，甚至可以说是最重要的事件。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In retrospect, maybe one of the most important things, if not the most important thing that happened in the 20th century, is the Chinese Civil War.</p>
</details>

对于中国内战，当时人们普遍认为这是一件非常重大的事情。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">On the Chinese Civil War, there was an understanding that it was a really big deal.</p>
</details>

正因如此，**罗斯福**（Franklin D. Roosevelt: 时任美国总统）一直试图将中国视为一个大国，尽管英国人坚持认为：“富兰克林，他们根本不是什么大国。”

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And that's why Roosevelt, he keeps trying to treat China like a great power in the... The British are going, "They're not a great power, Franklin, not remotely."</p>
</details>

但他希望将中国带入**开罗会议**（Cairo Conference: 1943年第二次世界大战期间，中美英三国首脑在埃及开罗举行的会议）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But he wants to bring them into the Cairo conference.</p>
</details>

他还希望中国成为**联合国**（United Nations: 成立于1945年的国际组织）的**否决权成员**（Veto Member: 联合国安理会常任理事国拥有的一票否决权），然而以当时中国的军事实力来看，这根本不可能实现。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And he also wants them to be a veto member of the United Nations, which by China's military status, there's just no way.</p>
</details>

但罗斯福的考量是：“日本已经不在了。我们需要在亚洲拥有某种力量来制衡**苏联**（Soviets: 指苏维埃社会主义共和国联盟）。”

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But Roosevelt is looking, "There's no Japan out there. We want to have something in Asia to counterbalance the Soviets."</p>
</details>

然而，当谈到中国内战时，那些曾参与过重大战争的美国人认为，他们无法干预并改变这一结果。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But then when you get to the Chinese Civil War, Americans who have fought some big wars, they're looking at it going, not feasible for us. to alter this outcome.</p>
</details>

当时普遍的看法是，如果他们（指国共两党）共存足够长时间，最终还是会互相攻击。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The thinking was that if they lived together long enough, they would go at each other eventually.</p>
</details>
