### 好莱坞并购案的惊人反转

**Rachel Abrams**: 几个月来，一出足以搬上大银幕的好莱坞大戏一直在上演。这是一个充斥着企业“三角恋”的故事，角色阵容包括亿万富翁后代、科技巨头，甚至还有美国总统。现在，这个故事似乎进入了尾声，但却带有一个反转的结局。今天，我的同事 Nicole Sperling、Lauren Hirsch 和 Jonathan Mahler 将解释为什么**派拉蒙（Paramount）**与**华纳兄弟（Warner Brothers）**的合并让好莱坞陷入震惊，以及这笔交易为何可能重塑我们的政治和文化版图。

<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: For months, a Hollywood drama has been unfolding that's fit for the big screen. It's a corporate love triangle with a cast of characters including a billionaire scion, a tech giant, and the president of the United States. Now, that story appears to be coming to a close, but with a twist ending. Today, my colleagues Nicole Sperling, Lauren Hirsch, and Jonathan Mahler explain why the merger between Paramount and Warner Brothers has left Hollywood in a state of shock, and why the deal could reshape our political and cultural landscape.

</details>

**Rachel Abrams**: Lauren、Nicole、Jonathan，很高兴你们能来。关于谁会收购华纳兄弟的悬念笼罩了好莱坞好几个月，现在我们终于有了答案：派拉蒙以最高出价胜出。Jonathan，我想先问你，为什么这笔交易如此重要？

<details>

And his son David, who was at one point an aspiring actor who then went on to become a film producer and financier and then bought Paramount. With this new deal, he's going to become one of the most powerful media moguls in the world almost overnight. They are the new Murdoch, but quite possibly a lot more powerful.

### 派拉蒙如何“虎口夺食”

**Rachel Abrams**: 我们待会再详谈埃里森家族的影响力。但首先，Lauren，上次你来节目时，看起来**Netflix**才是那个志在必得的买家，对吧？当时派拉蒙正试图半路杀出。请告诉我们后来发生了什么。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: So we're going to talk more about the Ellison's influence. But first, Lauren, the last time you were on the show, it looked at the time like Netflix was going to be the winning bidder, right? And at the time, Paramount was in the middle of trying to swoop in and outbid them. So, catch us up on what has happened since then.

</details>

**Lauren Hirsch**: 在华纳兄弟宣布与 Netflix 达成协议后，派拉蒙对华纳董事会发起了一场**敌意收购**，直接告诉股东：“你们的董事会没为你们争取最大利益，我们有一个更好的选择。”这引起了巨大反响，迫使华纳董事会不得不开启与派拉蒙的对话。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Lauren Hirsch**: Shortly after Warner Brothers announced that it had signed a deal with Netflix, Paramount did something called a hostile bid, which they went to Warner Brothers shareholders and said, "Your board isn't doing right by you. We're giving you a better option." And it became so loud and so frankly compelling that the Warner Brothers board was forced to open a dialogue with Paramount.

</details>

**Lauren Hirsch**: 最终，派拉蒙的方案变得如此优厚，华纳认为它已经超越了 Netflix。派拉蒙不仅给出了他们想要的一切，还额外承诺如果监管机构阻止交易，他们将支付高达 **70 亿美元**的违约金。于是，Netflix 选择了退出。派拉蒙同意以每股 **31 美元**的价格收购华纳，这比并购消息传出前的股价溢价了 **150%**，在交易史上几乎是闻所未闻的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Lauren Hirsch**: It basically got to the point where the Paramount deal was so good in Warner Brothers' mind. They're like, "Not only did they give us everything we wanted, but they threw on a $7 billion fee that they would pay if regulators blocked it." And Netflix said, "Actually, we're just going to walk away." Paramount has agreed to buy Warner Brothers for $31 a share. That is a 150% premium, which is nearly unheard of in deals.

</details>

### 高昂代价与巨额债务

**Rachel Abrams**:  Lauren 描述的这些加起来，总收购价达到了 **1110 亿美元**。许多评论认为派拉蒙买贵了。Nicole，你能解释一下为什么会有这种批评吗？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: I have read that what you just described adds up to a total purchase price of $111 billion. And a lot of the coverage has basically made it seem like that is way too much to pay. Nicole, can you just explain that? Why is that the criticism?

</details>

**Nicole Sperling**: 去年春天华纳的股价仅为 8 美元，派拉蒙的出价一路从 19 美元涨到 31 美元。此外，他们还向 Netflix 支付了 **28 亿美元的“分手费”**。合并后的公司将背负接近 **800 亿美元的债务**。虽然他们全球将拥有 2 亿订阅用户，能更有效地竞争，但代价极其高昂。而且现在的电影行业并不比 2019 年迪士尼收购福克斯时更健康。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nicole Sperling**: Last spring the stock was worth $8 a share and Paramount's bid went up to 31. They also added a $2.8 billion breakup fee to send to Netflix. The combined company is going to be carrying a debt load that's close to $80 billion. Yes, they will have 200 million subscribers, but it still cost a lot of money to get there. The movie business is not in a healthier state than it was back then pre-COVID.

</details>

**Jonathan Mahler**: 这是一个面临严重结构性挑战的行业。观众的注意力被 **TikTok** 和社交媒体分散，院线票房逐年下降。一年前，华纳兄弟看起来还像一艘正在下沉的船。CEO 大卫·扎斯拉夫（David Zaslav）当时正竭尽全力削减开支，甚至下架电影和《芝麻街》剧集以节省版税支出。那是真正的“斤斤计较”。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jonathan Mahler**: This is a business that is facing serious structural challenges. There's so much competition for eyeballs even beyond streaming, just TikTok and social media. And yet the box office numbers are consistently down. A year ago Warner Brothers Discovery seemed like it was a sinking ship. David Zaslav was doing everything in his power to cut costs, removing movies and episodes of Sesame Street from the platform. It was really penny-pinching.

</details>

**Rachel Abrams**: 听你们这么说，我怀疑这对 Netflix 来说反而是一次重大的胜利？他们迫使竞争对手多掏钱买入资产，自己还白赚了 28 亿美元。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: Hearing you describe all of this, I'm wondering if there's actually a way to think about this as kind of like a huge coup for Netflix because it sounds like they essentially forced a huge competitor to overpay for one asset and walk away with a $2.8 billion breakup fee.

</details>

**Lauren Hirsch**: Netflix 肯定会向投资者这样宣传。他们不仅拿到了钱，还避开了一家背负 800 亿债务的公司。当你背负巨额债务时，犯错的空间会变得非常小。Netflix 确实向市场证明了他们不当“冤大头”的明智。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Lauren Hirsch**: They are certainly going to market it like that. Not only are they getting money to walk away, but they're walking away from a company that's going to have about 80 billion in debt. When you have a lot of debt, your margin for error is much more slim. So Netflix is surely going to tell the market we made the smart deal in not overpaying.

</details>

### 埃里森家族的野心与政治博弈

**Rachel Abrams**: 那么问题来了，既然这笔交易风险如此之大，埃里森家族为什么要这么做？

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: And all of this I think begs the question, why are the Ellison's doing all of this?

</details>

**Nicole Sperling**: 派拉蒙目前的规模还不足以与顶级流媒体平台抗衡。他们看中了 **HBO Max** 拥有的 1.32 亿全球订户。通过合并，他们将成为一个能够真正参与“订户大战”的巨人。这是目前每家娱乐公司都梦寐以求的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nicole Sperling**: Paramount wasn't big enough to compete with the big streamers. They look at HBO Max, which has a subscriber base of 132 million people, and they see an opportunity to combine forces. Now they will be a behemoth that can actually fight in this battle for subscribers.

</details>

**Jonathan Mahler**: 我认为还有一个更高层面的计划。拉里·埃里森是否想通过控制媒体，让自己在华盛顿处于更有利的位置，从而获得更多政府合同？这是否意味着 CBS 甚至 CNN 以后会向政治中轴线或右翼倾斜？大卫是否需要调整新闻口径来配合他父亲在华盛顿的业务运营？这是我们都在关注的。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jonathan Mahler**: I think there's a bigger plan here. How much of this is about Larry Ellison wanting to put himself in a more favorable position with the administration to get more government contracts? And is that going to entail moving these news outlets, CBS and now CNN, toward the center or to the right? And is that what is going to be expected of David in order to keep his father's business operations running in Washington?

</details>

**Rachel Abrams**: 我们已经看到了一些政治影响媒体业务的迹象。大卫·埃里森去年收购 CBS 后，CBS 似乎确实做出了一些向右转的举动。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: And we've already seen some evidence of how the politics could affect the media business, right? Like look what happened to CBS after David Ellison acquired it last year.

</details>

**Jonathan Mahler**: 是的。大卫掌控 CBS 后，任命了**巴里·怀斯（Bari Weiss）**，她立即开始对新闻网络进行调整。在一个著名的事件中，她暂时撤下了一篇关于特朗普政府将遣返者送往萨尔瓦多拘留所的报道。此外，斯蒂芬·科尔伯特（Stephen Colbert）的节目在埃里森接手后很快就停播了。现在，人们担心 CNN 也会发生类似的变化。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jonathan Mahler**: That's exactly right. When David Ellison took control of CBS, he put Bari Weiss in charge, and she immediately started making changes. She temporarily shelved a report about a detention facility in El Salvador where the Trump administration was sending deportees. Of course, the Stephen Colbert show was terminated pretty quickly. There is a lot of anxiety that something similar is going to come to CNN.

</details>

**Rachel Abrams**: 那么好莱坞对此有什么反应？

**Nicole Sperling**: 很有趣。当去年 12 月 Netflix 似乎要赢得交易时，人们感到恐惧，觉得这个科技巨头会彻底摧毁院线电影业务。但随后 Netflix 的联席 CEO 们展现了极佳的姿态，承诺保留 HBO 的完整性，并将电影保留在电影院上映 45 天。人们一度感到欣慰。但现在换成了派拉蒙收购，没人清楚谁能留下来。

<details>

**Lauren Hirsch**: 为了应对债务，他们必须削减成本。他们预计通过此次交易实现约 **60 亿美元的“协同效应”**——这是“裁员”的企业化术语。虽然派拉蒙表示协同效应主要不来自裁员，但根据我报道这类交易的经验，裁员通常是合并中最容易实现成本削减的方式。

</details>

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Rachel Abrams**: What does all of this mean for us, the consumers?

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nicole Sperling**: One would assume that these are going to merge together. We will probably have one app that we can all access all this content on. As for the movie studio, David Ellison is a big believer in the theatrical release. He says that movies that debut in theaters first have a much greater impact culturally than movies that debut in streaming first.

</details>

**Nicole Sperling**: 大卫过去的投资偏好是《变形金刚》、《碟中谍》和《壮志凌云》这类大制作。因为背负巨债，公司可能会倾向于做更“安全”的决策，投资那些拥有固定受众的知名 IP，而不是像华纳过去一年那样制作一些具有风险但赢得口碑的电影。这可能不是因为政治献媚，而是单纯为了财务避险。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Nicole Sperling**: In the past, he has been a big fan of Transformers, Mission Impossible, and Top Gun. Because the studio has a lot of debt, they have to make safe decisions. It is maybe safer to make movies that are based on well-known franchises than to do the kind of risky things Warner Brothers has done in the past year. It might be less about political kowtowing and more just about making sure bets.

</details>

### 数据驱动的新帝国：不仅仅是电影

**Jonathan Mahler**: 这个时刻确实不同寻常。我们虽然在大谈大卫·埃里森，但背后的财力支持是他父亲拉里。我们需要从更大的背景看这笔交易。这与拉里在甲骨文构建 **AI 基础设施**的计划高度契合。他们现在拥有 TikTok 15% 的股份，并可能负责处理所有美国数据。拉里希望通过电影、新闻、社交媒体获得海量数据，用来**训练 AI 模型**。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jonathan Mahler**: This moment does seem different. The principal backer of all this is his father. You have to see the Hollywood piece of this dovetailing with what Larry Ellison is up to at Oracle in terms of building out this AI infrastructure. They now own 15% of TikTok. This is part of Larry Ellison's desire to have access to data to train AI models and to build out this new AI infrastructure.

</details>

**Jonathan Mahler**: 我们正在见证一个全新的、不同类型的**全球媒体帝国**的诞生。在一个由谷歌、亚马逊少数几家公司控制一切的时代，拉里·埃里森也在玩同样的游戏。默多克家族曾在过去几十年里塑造了多个国家的政治版图，而现在，埃里森家族正在为这个数据驱动的数字时代积聚力量。

<details>
<summary>Original English</summary>

**Jonathan Mahler**: We need to see this through the lens of the creation of a very new and different kind of global media empire. That's the game that Larry Ellison is playing in. Instead of Rupert and Lachlan, it's Larry and David, and they are amassing a media empire for the data-driven digital age.

</details>

**Nicole Sperling**: 我只是在想，HBO 会不会拍一部《继承之战》的续集，以埃里森家族为主角？或者这现在已经是禁忌了？
